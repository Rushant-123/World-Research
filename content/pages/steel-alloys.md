# Specialty Steel Alloys Manufacturing Process

**Top Market Leaders:**

## Raw Material Reception and Preparation

1. Receive iron ore shipments at the steel mill's rail terminal from mining operations.
2. Unload iron ore using rotary car dumpers capable of handling 100-ton railcars.
3. Transport ore to primary storage yards using conveyor belt systems spanning 2 kilometers.
4. Sample incoming ore batches for chemical composition analysis (Fe content, impurities).
5. Store iron ore in segregated stockpiles based on grade and origin.
6. Receive metallurgical coal shipments dedicated for coking operations.
7. Blend different coal grades to achieve optimal coking properties (volatile matter, ash content).
8. Crush coal to uniform particle size of 3-6mm using hammer mills.
9. Transport crushed coal to coke ovens via enclosed conveyor systems.
10. Load coal into coke oven batteries containing 50-100 individual chambers.

## Coke Production

11. Heat coal in oxygen-free coke ovens at 1000-1100°C for 18-24 hours.
12. Monitor chamber temperatures using thermocouples to ensure uniform coking.
13. Collect coal tar and coke oven gas as valuable byproducts.
14. Push red-hot coke from ovens using hydraulic ram mechanisms.
15. Quench coke with water spray to cool from 1000°C to handling temperature.
16. Screen coke to separate usable sizes (25-80mm) from fines and breeze.
17. Transport coke to blast furnace charging systems via conveyor belts.
18. Store coke in weather-protected bunkers maintaining low moisture content.
19. Test coke quality for strength (CSR), reactivity (CRI), and ash content.
20. Blend coke batches to maintain consistent blast furnace feed properties.

## Blast Furnace Operations

21. Prepare iron ore by crushing and screening to 10-30mm lump size.
22. Create sinter by agglomerating fine ore with limestone and coke breeze.
23. Heat sinter mixture on traveling grate at 1200-1300°C to fuse particles.
24. Cool sintered ore and crush to 5-50mm sizing for blast furnace charging.
25. Calculate burden recipe: 70% sinter, 25% lump ore, 5% pellets by weight.
26. Weigh limestone flux additions to balance gangue and form slag.
27. Load skip cars with alternating layers of ore burden and coke.
28. Charge materials through double bell system maintaining furnace pressure.
29. Inject preheated air blast (1200°C) through tuyeres at furnace base.
30. Supplement with pulverized coal injection to reduce coke consumption by 30%.
31. Monitor furnace descent rate and adjust charging frequency (every 3-5 minutes).
32. Maintain furnace top pressure at 2.5-3.0 bar for optimal gas utilization.
33. Control oxygen enrichment in blast (23-25% O2) to increase productivity.
34. Allow burden to descend through shaft zone (reduction at 800-1000°C).
35. Enable bosh zone reactions where carbon dissolves into iron at 1400°C.
36. Collect molten iron and slag in hearth at bottom of furnace.
37. Tap molten iron every 2-3 hours through tapping hole into torpedo ladle cars.
38. Measure hot metal temperature (1450-1500°C) using immersion thermocouples.
39. Sample hot metal for chemistry: 4.0-4.5% C, 0.5-1.0% Si, 0.3% S, 0.1% P.
40. Transport 300-ton torpedo ladles to basic oxygen furnace or desulfurization station.

## Hot Metal Pretreatment

41. Inject calcium carbide or magnesium into hot metal for desulfurization.
42. Stir ladle using argon gas injection to promote slag-metal reactions.
43. Reduce sulfur content from 0.030% to below 0.005% in 15 minutes.
44. Skim sulfur-rich slag from ladle surface using mechanical rakes.
45. Add ferrosilicon to adjust silicon content for downstream processing.
46. Measure temperature drop (typically 30-50°C) during desulfurization treatment.
47. Transfer treated hot metal to EAF charging crane position.
48. Preheat scrap metal in auxiliary scrap preheater using waste heat.
49. Sort scrap by grade: plate scrap, busheling, shredded automotive scrap.
50. Analyze scrap for tramp elements (copper, tin) that cannot be removed.

## Electric Arc Furnace Melting

51. Charge EAF with 30% hot metal and 70% scrap steel mixture (120 tons total).
52. Position water-cooled roof and lower graphite electrodes (24-inch diameter).
53. Initiate arc striking at 600V between electrodes and scrap charge.
54. Ramp power input to 100 MVA creating 3500°C arc temperatures.
55. Inject oxygen lance through roof to oxidize carbon and silicon.
56. Add lime and burnt dolomite flux to form basic slag (60 kg/ton steel).
57. Monitor melt-down progression using acoustic sensors and electrode current.
58. Complete initial melting phase in 35-45 minutes with 350 kWh/ton energy input.
59. Measure bath temperature reaching 1650°C using sublance probes.
60. Sample metal for chemistry analysis: C, Mn, P, S, Cr, Ni content.
61. Adjust chemistry by adding ferroalloys and carbon based on target grade.
62. Foaming slag with carbon injection to bury arcs and improve efficiency.
63. Refine steel for 15 minutes to reduce phosphorus below 0.015%.
64. Tap furnace by tilting EAF body and pouring steel into ladle.
65. Measure tap temperature (1650-1680°C) and steel weight (115 tons liquid).
66. Leave 5-10 tons of slag in furnace as heel for next heat.
67. Close tapping hole and prepare furnace for next scrap charge cycle.
68. Clean furnace sidewalls and roof using robotic lancing systems.
69. Replace worn refractory bricks in high-wear zones every 200 heats.
70. Calibrate electrode positioning systems for consistent arc length.

## Ladle Refining and Alloying

71. Transfer steel ladle to ladle metallurgy furnace (LMF) station.
72. Install porous plug in ladle bottom for argon stirring gas injection.
73. Lower LMF electrodes and provide trim heating (5-10 MW) to compensate heat loss.
74. Inject argon gas at 300-500 liters/minute for homogenization stirring.
75. Add aluminum wire (2-3 kg/ton) for deoxidation and oxygen removal.
76. Add lime and fluorspar to condition slag (reduce FeO and MnO content).
77. Calculate alloy additions for target steel grade composition using mass balance.
78. Add ferrochromium (18% Cr target) in 500 kg increments through chute.
79. Wait 3 minutes between additions allowing chromium dissolution and mixing.
80. Add nickel metal chunks (8% Ni target) sized 20-50mm for rapid melting.
81. Inject molybdenum ferroalloy (0.5% Mo) using cored wire feeding system.
82. Add manganese ferroalloy to adjust Mn content to 1.5% for hardenability.
83. Introduce vanadium additions (0.1%) for grain refinement if specified.
84. Stir vigorously for 12 minutes ensuring alloy homogeneity within ±0.05%.
85. Sample steel using vacuum sampling system drawing metal into quartz tube.
86. Perform rapid spectroscopic analysis (OES) giving results in 90 seconds.
87. Make trim additions of specific alloys to hit chemistry targets precisely.
88. Adjust final carbon content using oxygen lancing or carbon injection.
89. Control sulfur level (0.005% max) with calcium treatment if necessary.
90. Heat steel to target tapping temperature of 1560°C using LMF electrodes.
91. Float out non-metallic inclusions into slag layer during stirring period.
92. Measure steel cleanliness by inclusion counting in rapid solidification samples.
93. Verify final chemistry meets ASTM A182 F316 or equivalent grade specification.
94. Cease argon stirring 2 minutes before casting to allow inclusion separation.
95. Transfer ladle to continuous casting machine turret position.

## Continuous Casting

96. Preheat tundish refractory to 1200°C using natural gas burners for 6 hours.
97. Install ladle shroud connection and slide gate refractory components.
98. Position ladle above tundish using overhead casting crane.
99. Open slide gate slowly controlling initial steel flow into tundish.
100. Fill tundish (25-ton capacity) allowing steel level to reach sensors.
101. Install stopper rods controlling steel flow to four individual molds.
102. Coat mold copper plates with boron nitride-based lubricant.
103. Initiate water cooling circulation in mold at 2000 liters/minute per strand.
104. Install dummy bar with head in mold connected to withdrawal system.
105. Open stopper allowing steel to flow into mold forming meniscus.
106. Add mold powder (lime-silica based) floating on steel surface in mold.
107. Oscillate mold vertically at 180 cycles/minute with 6mm stroke.
108. Monitor mold level using eddy current or radioactive sensors.
109. Control steel flow rate matching casting speed of 1.2 meters/minute.
110. Allow steel shell to solidify to 8-10mm thickness in mold (900mm long).
111. Withdraw strand continuously through mold support and alignment system.
112. Apply water spray cooling in support zones below mold.
113. Control spray water flow rates to achieve 100°C/m cooling gradient.
114. Support strand using containment rolls every 400mm to prevent bulging.
115. Monitor strand surface temperature using infrared pyrometers (900-1100°C).
116. Continue withdrawal through curved section to straightening point.
117. Straighten strand gradually over 6-meter length from curved to horizontal.
118. Monitor strand for surface defects using online inspection cameras.
119. Achieve final solidification at metallurgical length (25 meters from meniscus).
120. Measure strand cross-section (250mm x 250mm bloom) using laser scanners.
121. Cut strand into 12-meter long blooms using traveling oxygen torch cutter.
122. Mark blooms with heat number using automated stamping system.
123. Transfer hot blooms (850°C) to cooling bed using roller conveyors.
124. Inspect bloom surface for cracks, slivers, or other surface defects.
125. Grind surface defects using portable grinders if depth less than 3mm.

## Reheating and Hot Rolling

128. Control atmosphere to slightly reducing conditions preventing scale formation.

140. Control roll speed increases maintaining constant material flow.
