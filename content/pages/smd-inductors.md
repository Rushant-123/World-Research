# SMD Inductors Manufacturing Process

## Ferrite Material Formulation (Steps 1-25)

1. Source iron oxide (Fe2O3) powder with 99.5% purity for ferrite base material
2. Obtain zinc oxide (ZnO) powder as magnetic property modifier
3. Acquire nickel oxide (NiO) for permeability adjustment in high-frequency applications
4. Source manganese oxide (MnO) for temperature stability enhancement
5. Procure cobalt oxide (CoO2) for high saturation flux density formulations
6. Receive copper oxide (CuO) as sintering aid and loss reduction additive
7. Conduct incoming material purity testing using X-ray fluorescence spectroscopy
8. Verify particle size distribution of raw powders (0.5-2.0 microns target)
9. Weigh iron oxide at 48-52 mol% according to ferrite composition specifications
10. Measure zinc oxide at 25-30 mol% for optimal permeability characteristics
11. Add nickel oxide at 15-20 mol% for frequency response optimization
12. Incorporate manganese oxide at 3-5 mol% for temperature coefficient control
13. Include small amounts of cobalt and copper oxides (0.5-2 mol%) as performance additives
14. Load raw materials into planetary ball mill with zirconia grinding media
15. Add deionized water or ethanol as milling medium (40-50% solid content)
16. Mill ferrite powder mixture for 12-24 hours to achieve submicron particle size
17. Monitor particle size reduction using laser diffraction particle analyzer
18. Drain milled slurry into stainless steel trays for drying
19. Dry milled powder at 120-150°C in forced air oven for 8-12 hours
20. Break up dried powder cakes using mortar and pestle or hammer mill
21. Sieve powder through 200-mesh screen to remove agglomerates
22. Perform pre-calcination at 800-900°C for 2-4 hours to form spinel phase
23. Cool calcined powder slowly to room temperature in furnace
24. Crush and re-mill calcined powder to break up sintered particles
25. Conduct X-ray diffraction analysis to verify ferrite phase formation

## Core Pressing and Green Body Formation (Steps 26-45)

26. Add organic binder system (PVA or acrylic) at 2-5% by weight to calcined powder
27. Mix plasticizer (glycerin or PEG) at 1-2% for green body flexibility
28. Incorporate lubricant (stearic acid or zinc stearate) at 0.5-1% for die release
29. Granulate powder mixture through spray drying to create free-flowing granules
30. Adjust granule moisture content to 1-2% for optimal pressing characteristics
31. Sieve granules to 40-100 mesh size range for uniform die filling
32. Load granulated powder into automated hopper system of press
33. Program hydraulic press for SMD core geometry (drum core, shield type, multilayer chip)
34. Set pressing pressure at 100-200 MPa depending on core size and density requirements
35. Fill precision hardened steel die cavity with measured powder charge
36. Apply uniaxial pressure to compact powder into green body core shape
37. Hold pressure for 2-5 seconds to ensure uniform density distribution
38. Release pressure and eject green core from die using mechanical ejector pins
39. Inspect green cores for surface defects, cracks, or laminations
40. Measure green density (50-60% of theoretical density target)
41. Check dimensional accuracy of green cores using optical comparator
42. Place green cores on alumina or mullite setter plates for sintering
43. Arrange cores with adequate spacing (3-5mm) to prevent sticking during firing
44. Load setter plates onto sintering furnace kiln cars
45. Record green core lot numbers and pressing parameters for traceability

## Sintering and Core Densification (Steps 46-65)

46. Program tunnel kiln or batch furnace sintering profile with controlled heating rate
47. Set initial heating ramp at 50-100°C/hour to 300°C for binder burnout
48. Maintain 300°C hold for 2-4 hours to completely remove organic binders
49. Continue heating at 100-200°C/hour to 600°C for gradual densification
50. Ramp temperature to peak sintering temperature of 1100-1300°C
51. Hold at peak temperature for 2-6 hours for grain growth and densification
52. Control oxygen partial pressure in furnace atmosphere (air, N2, or reducing atmosphere)
53. Monitor furnace temperature uniformity across hot zone (±5°C tolerance)
54. Begin controlled cooling at 100-200°C/hour to 800°C
55. Allow furnace cooling to room temperature over 12-24 hours
56. Unload sintered cores from furnace and remove from setter plates
57. Inspect sintered cores for warping, cracking, or surface defects
58. Measure final sintered density (95-98% of theoretical density)
59. Check dimensional shrinkage (15-20% linear shrinkage from green state)
60. Test hardness using Vickers or Knoop microhardness tester
61. Perform visual inspection under magnification for surface quality
62. Conduct magnetic permeability testing on sample cores using impedance analyzer
63. Measure saturation flux density using DC magnetic property tester
64. Verify core loss at specified frequencies (100kHz-10MHz range)
65. Sort cores by magnetic properties into performance bins for matching

## Wire Preparation and Winding (Steps 66-90)

66. Source ultra-fine copper magnet wire (AWG 42-50) with polyurethane insulation
67. Verify wire diameter tolerance (±0.002mm) using laser micrometer
68. Test insulation breakdown voltage (>500V for bondable polyurethane)
69. Check wire tensile strength (>400 MPa) and elongation properties
70. Inspect wire for surface defects or insulation damage under microscope
71. Load wire spools onto tension-controlled payoff stands of winding machine
72. Thread wire through ceramic or carbide wire guides to prevent insulation damage
73. Set up automated CNC coil winding machine for SMD inductor geometry
74. Program winding parameters (turns count, pitch, layer arrangement)
75. Mount sintered ferrite core on precision mandrel or winding fixture
76. Adjust wire tension to 10-30 grams-force for consistent winding
77. Position wire start point at designated terminal area of core
78. Initiate automated winding sequence with servo motor control
79. Wind first layer of coil with precise turn spacing (0.02-0.05mm pitch)
80. Monitor wire position using machine vision system for alignment verification
81. Complete first layer and transition to second layer if multilayer design
82. Apply thin insulation layer between winding layers if required
83. Continue winding subsequent layers with controlled interlayer spacing
84. Maintain constant tension throughout winding process to prevent loose turns
85. Complete final winding layer and position wire end at second terminal
86. Cut wire using automated cutter with clean shear action
87. Verify total turn count using automated counter system
88. Inspect wound coil for wire crossovers, gaps, or misalignment
89. Measure coil DC resistance (DCR) using precision milliohm meter
90. Check preliminary inductance value using LCR meter at 100kHz

## Terminal Formation and Attachment (Steps 91-110)

91. Prepare terminal electrode paste containing silver or copper powder
92. Mix conductive powder with organic binder and solvent to proper viscosity
93. Add glass frit to electrode paste for adhesion enhancement
94. Adjust paste rheology for screen printing or dipping application
95. Position wound cores in fixture exposing wire end termination areas
96. Apply electrode paste to both ends of component using automated dispenser
97. Ensure wire ends are fully embedded in wet electrode paste
98. Allow paste to air dry for 10-30 minutes at room temperature
99. Load components into belt furnace for electrode firing
100. Heat electrodes at 600-800°C for 30-60 minutes in controlled atmosphere
101. Cool fired electrodes gradually to prevent thermal shock cracking
102. Inspect fired electrodes for complete wire attachment and coverage
103. Prepare nickel plating bath with nickel sulfate solution (200-300 g/L)
104. Add boric acid and brighteners to plating bath for smooth deposit
105. Adjust bath pH to 3.5-4.5 and temperature to 45-55°C
106. Mount components on fixtures for barrel or rack plating
107. Apply nickel barrier layer (2-5 microns thick) using electroplating
108. Rinse nickel-plated components in deionized water cascade
109. Transfer components to tin plating bath or solder dipping station
110. Apply tin or tin-lead solder coating (3-10 microns) for solderability

## Epoxy Molding and Encapsulation (Steps 111-130)

111. Prepare epoxy molding compound (EMC) with appropriate viscosity
112. Select epoxy resin system with high thermal conductivity and low moisture absorption
113. Mix epoxy with ferrite filler particles for magnetic shielding enhancement
114. Add flame retardant additives to meet UL94 V-0 flammability rating
115. Incorporate pigments for component color coding (black or custom colors)
116. Degas epoxy mixture under vacuum to remove entrapped air bubbles
117. Load epoxy compound into heated reservoir of transfer molding press
118. Preheat molding dies to 165-180°C for optimal flow and cure
119. Position wound and terminated cores in lower mold cavity with precision
120. Close mold and inject epoxy under pressure (5-15 MPa) into cavity
121. Fill mold completely ensuring no voids around coil and core
122. Hold injection pressure during initial cure (1-2 minutes)
123. Continue mold cure at elevated temperature for 2-5 minutes
124. Open mold and eject molded SMD inductor component
125. Remove excess flash and runner material from molded parts
126. Post-cure molded components in oven at 150-175°C for 2-4 hours
127. Allow components to cool gradually to room temperature
128. Inspect molded bodies for voids, incomplete fill, or surface defects
129. Verify dimensions of molded package using automated vision system
130. Check for proper terminal exposure and coplanarity

## Laser Marking and Identification (Steps 131-140)

131. Set up laser marking system with appropriate wavelength for epoxy surface
132. Program marking pattern with part number, value code, and date code
133. Adjust laser power (3-15 watts) and frequency for clear marking without damage
134. Position components on indexed fixture under laser marking head
135. Apply laser marking to top surface of molded inductor body
136. Verify marking legibility and contrast using vision inspection system
137. Ensure marking depth does not compromise structural integrity
138. Check that marking is positioned within designated area per specification
139. Apply optional color fill to laser marks for enhanced readability
140. Cure color fill ink under UV light or heat if applied

## Electrical Testing and Characterization (Steps 141-165)

141. Set up automated test handler with pick-and-place system
142. Configure precision LCR meter for inductance measurement at multiple frequencies
143. Test inductance value at 100kHz or specified frequency (±10-20% tolerance)
144. Measure DC resistance (DCR) using four-wire Kelvin connection method
145. Verify DCR is within specification range (typically 0.05-2 ohms)
146. Calculate Q factor (quality factor) from inductance and resistance measurements
147. Test inductance vs. frequency response from 10kHz to 10MHz
148. Measure self-resonant frequency (SRF) where impedance peaks
149. Set up DC bias test fixture with controlled current source
150. Apply increasing DC current while monitoring inductance drop
151. Determine saturation current (Isat) at 20% or 30% inductance drop point
152. Measure temperature rise current rating using thermal camera
153. Record current level where component reaches 40°C temperature rise
154. Test inductance variation over temperature range (-40°C to +125°C)
155. Verify temperature coefficient is within specification
156. Conduct insulation resistance testing between terminals and core
157. Apply 100-500V DC and measure leakage current (>100 MΩ required)
158. Perform dielectric withstanding voltage test at 500-1000V AC for 1 second
159. Test terminal solderability using solder dip or wetting balance method
160. Verify terminals meet J-STD-002 wetting requirements
161. Measure terminal pull strength to verify electrode adhesion
162. Test component moisture sensitivity level (MSL) per J-STD-020
163. Subject samples to thermal shock testing (-40°C to +125°C, 100 cycles)
164. Perform vibration testing per MIL-STD-202 to verify mechanical robustness
165. Conduct accelerated life testing at elevated temperature and humidity

## High-Frequency Performance Verification (Steps 166-180)

166. Set up vector network analyzer (VNA) for S-parameter measurement
167. Calibrate VNA using short-open-load-through (SOLT) standards
168. Mount SMD inductor on microstrip test fixture with 50-ohm impedance
169. Measure S11 (reflection) and S21 (transmission) parameters to 6GHz
170. Extract equivalent series inductance (ESL) from S-parameter data
171. Determine equivalent series resistance (ESR) across frequency range
172. Calculate parasitic capacitance from self-resonant frequency measurement
173. Generate impedance vs. frequency plot (Bode plot) for datasheet
174. Measure insertion loss in power supply filter configuration
175. Test common-mode rejection ratio in differential mode applications
176. Verify EMI suppression performance in conducted emissions test setup
177. Characterize inductor behavior under fast current transients (di/dt testing)
178. Measure core losses at various AC flux densities and frequencies
179. Test power handling capability with RF signal injection
180. Validate thermal performance under continuous high-frequency operation

## Final Inspection and Packaging (Steps 181-195)

181. Perform automated optical inspection (AOI) of all surfaces and terminals
182. Check for cracks, chips, contamination, or other visual defects
183. Measure component dimensions using coordinate measuring machine (CMM)
184. Verify height, length, width within tolerance (±0.1-0.2mm typical)
185. Test terminal coplanarity using shadow gauge or 3D inspection
186. Ensure coplanarity is within 0.1mm for reliable SMT placement
187. Sort tested components into performance bins by inductance value
188. Apply dry pack or vacuum-sealed moisture barrier bags for MSL control
189. Include desiccant and humidity indicator card in moisture barrier bags
190. Place components in embossed carrier tape (8mm, 12mm, or 16mm pitch)
191. Apply cover tape and wind onto 7-inch or 13-inch reels
192. Verify tape dimensions and sprocket hole spacing per EIA-481 standard
193. Print reel labels with part number, quantity, lot code, and date code
194. Pack reels in cardboard cartons with protective foam inserts
195. Generate certificate of conformance and test data for shipment to customers
