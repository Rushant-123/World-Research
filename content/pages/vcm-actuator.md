Voice coil motor (VCM) actuators provide precise linear motion control for autofocus mechanisms in smartphone and tablet camera modules. These electromagnetic actuators move the lens assembly along the optical axis with response times under 50 milliseconds and positioning accuracy within 2 micrometers. Manufacturing requires precision coil winding, magnet magnetization, spring suspension fabrication, and careful calibration of the electromagnetic force characteristics.

## Neodymium Magnet Preparation (Steps 1-18)

1. Receive sintered neodymium-iron-boron (NdFeB) magnet blanks with N52 grade specification and 14.8 kOe coercivity
2. Inspect magnet blanks for dimensions: 4.2mm outer diameter, 2.8mm inner diameter, 1.5mm height with ±10μm tolerance
3. Measure magnet density at 7.5-7.6 g/cm³ using helium pycnometry
4. Load magnet blanks into precision grinding fixture with vacuum holding
5. Grind outer diameter to 4.15mm ±5μm using diamond grinding wheel at 2800 RPM
6. Grind inner diameter to 2.85mm ±5μm using cylindrical grinding mandrel
7. Grind magnet height to 1.48mm ±3μm with surface finish Ra 0.4μm
8. Clean ground magnets in ultrasonic bath with isopropyl alcohol for 3 minutes
9. Dry magnets in vacuum chamber at 60°C for 15 minutes
10. Apply nickel-copper-nickel (Ni-Cu-Ni) plating: 8μm Ni, 12μm Cu, 3μm Ni layers
11. Cure plating in oven at 150°C for 2 hours to improve adhesion
12. Mount magnets in magnetization fixture with precise radial alignment
13. Apply pulsed magnetic field of 30 kOe using capacitor discharge magnetizer
14. Magnetize magnets with radial polarization creating north pole on outer surface
15. Measure magnetic flux density at 0.45-0.48 Tesla using gaussmeter probe
16. Verify flux uniformity around circumference within ±3% variation
17. Test magnetic field strength at 3mm distance: 280-320 Gauss specification
18. Store magnetized magnets in magnetically shielded containers to prevent demagnetization

## Copper Coil Winding (Steps 19-38)
