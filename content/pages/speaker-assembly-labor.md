# Speaker Assembly Technicians

**Market Leader:** [GoerTek](/companies/goertek)



Speaker assembly technicians at GoerTek facilities perform precision manufacturing operations to construct high-quality acoustic transducers for consumer electronics, automotive systems, and professional audio equipment. This specialized labor combines mechanical assembly, adhesive application, electrical soldering, and acoustic testing to produce speakers meeting stringent performance specifications.

## Assembly Process Overview

### Pre-Assembly Preparation (Steps 1-15)

1. Review work order specifications including speaker model, frequency response requirements, and impedance ratings
2. Verify availability of all component parts including baskets, magnets, voice coils, cones, surrounds, and terminals
3. Inspect incoming basket frames for dimensional accuracy using go/no-go gauges
4. Check magnet assemblies for proper magnetization strength using gaussmeters
5. Examine voice coils for correct wire gauge, coil diameter, and winding consistency
6. Verify cone material type, thickness, and moisture content specifications
7. Inspect surround components for uniform thickness and proper durometer hardness
8. Check lead wire specifications including gauge, insulation type, and tinning quality
9. Verify adhesive materials are within shelf life and stored at proper temperature
10. Calibrate dispensing equipment for adhesive viscosity and temperature
11. Prepare workstation with all required tools including tweezers, spatulas, and fixtures
12. Set up work area lighting to minimum 1000 lux for precision visual inspection
13. Configure anti-static work surface and grounding straps for ESD protection
14. Position microscope or magnification equipment for detail inspection
15. Initialize assembly tracking system with batch number and quality control parameters

### Magnet Assembly Installation (Steps 16-30)

16. Select magnet assembly matching work order specifications for flux density
17. Clean basket mounting surface with isopropyl alcohol to remove contaminants
18. Apply precision amount of structural adhesive to basket center pole piece
19. Position magnet assembly concentrically on basket using alignment fixture
20. Verify gap between pole piece and basket is within 0.05mm tolerance
21. Apply pressure to magnet assembly using calibrated pneumatic press
22. Check for adhesive squeeze-out and remove excess with lint-free cloth
23. Verify magnet assembly perpendicularity using digital angle gauge
24. Measure air gap between pole piece and top plate with feeler gauges
25. Confirm gap uniformity around entire circumference within 0.02mm
26. Place assembly in curing fixture to maintain alignment during adhesive set
27. Transfer to temperature-controlled curing oven at specified temperature
28. Monitor cure cycle time according to adhesive manufacturer specifications
29. Remove assembly from oven and allow controlled cooling to room temperature
30. Perform post-cure inspection of bond line for voids or separation

### Voice Coil Preparation (Steps 31-45)

31. Select voice coil matching impedance specification from component inventory
32. Inspect voice coil former for cracks, deformations, or manufacturing defects
33. Verify voice coil winding uniformity under magnification for layer consistency
34. Measure voice coil DC resistance with precision milliohm meter
35. Check voice coil diameter using precision micrometers at multiple points
36. Verify voice coil height matches specification within 0.1mm tolerance
37. Inspect lead wire attachment points for secure crimping or welding
38. Test lead wire flexibility and insulation integrity through flex testing
39. Apply positioning marks on voice coil for alignment reference
40. Prepare voice coil surface for adhesive bonding with plasma treatment
41. Verify former material compatibility with selected adhesive system
42. Check voice coil for proper tinning of lead wire termination points
43. Measure voice coil weight to ensure consistency within batch specifications
44. Inspect for foreign material contamination that could affect performance
45. Stage prepared voice coils in ESD-protected containers for assembly

### Cone and Voice Coil Bonding (Steps 46-70)

46. Select cone matching diameter, profile, and material specifications
47. Inspect cone surface for defects including tears, wrinkles, or delamination
48. Verify cone apex diameter matches voice coil former outer diameter
49. Clean cone bonding surface with appropriate solvent or plasma treatment
50. Apply precision bead of adhesive to voice coil former using dispensing system
51. Monitor adhesive bead width, height, and consistency throughout application
52. Position cone apex over voice coil former using centering fixture
53. Align cone corrugations or pleats for uniform radial distribution
54. Apply controlled pressure to bond zone using pneumatic tooling
55. Verify adhesive wetting on both cone and former surfaces
56. Check for gaps, voids, or inconsistencies in adhesive bond line
57. Remove excess adhesive from visible areas using precision tools
58. Verify cone concentricity with voice coil using optical measurement
59. Measure cone angle relative to voice coil axis with angle gauges
60. Place assembly in holding fixture maintaining alignment during cure
61. Transfer to controlled temperature environment for adhesive curing
62. Monitor cure conditions including temperature, humidity, and time
63. Perform mid-cure inspection if required by process specifications
64. Allow complete cure cycle based on adhesive technical data sheet
65. Remove assembly from fixture and inspect bond integrity
66. Test bond strength through gentle manipulation and visual examination
67. Verify cone remains concentric with voice coil after fixture removal
68. Check for any spring-back or distortion in cone geometry
69. Measure cone to voice coil alignment within specified tolerances
70. Document assembly batch number and cure parameters in quality system

### Surround Attachment (Steps 71-90)

71. Select surround component matching cone diameter and material specifications
72. Inspect surround for uniform thickness, correct profile, and freedom from defects
73. Verify surround outer diameter matches basket mounting surface diameter
74. Check surround inner diameter for proper fit with cone outer diameter
75. Pre-fit surround to cone and basket assembly to verify dimensional compatibility
76. Clean surround bonding surfaces with appropriate cleaning agent
77. Apply adhesive bead to cone outer edge using precision dispensing
78. Monitor adhesive flow rate and pattern consistency during application
79. Position surround inner diameter over cone edge using alignment tools
80. Ensure uniform overlap between surround and cone around entire circumference
81. Apply controlled pressure to inner bond zone using custom tooling
82. Verify adhesive distribution and wetting on both mating surfaces
83. Remove excess adhesive from visible areas without disturbing bond
84. Apply adhesive bead to basket outer rim for surround attachment
85. Position surround outer diameter onto basket rim maintaining concentricity
86. Ensure surround seats properly in basket mounting channel or surface
87. Apply uniform pressure around outer diameter bond zone
88. Check for consistent bond line thickness around entire perimeter
89. Verify surround profile maintains specified roll geometry after bonding
90. Place assembly in curing fixture maintaining proper surround shape

### Lead Wire Attachment (Steps 91-110)

91. Route voice coil lead wires from coil termination to basket terminals
92. Verify lead wire length provides adequate slack for cone excursion
93. Position lead wires to minimize interference with cone or surround movement
94. Apply small amounts of adhesive to secure lead wires at strategic points
95. Allow lead wire adhesive tack time before proceeding with soldering
96. Clean basket terminal lugs with isopropyl alcohol and brass brush
97. Apply flux to terminal lugs appropriate for lead wire solder composition
98. Heat terminal lug with temperature-controlled soldering iron
99. Apply solder to heated terminal ensuring proper wetting and flow
100. Insert prepared voice coil lead wire into molten solder pool
101. Maintain heat for specified time to ensure thorough solder joint formation
102. Remove soldering iron and allow joint to cool without movement
103. Inspect solder joint for proper fillet formation and surface appearance
104. Verify mechanical strength of joint through gentle pull testing
105. Check for cold solder joints, insufficient wetting, or excess solder
106. Repeat soldering process for second lead wire connection
107. Verify polarity markings on terminals match voice coil lead identification
108. Inspect for solder bridges, flux residue, or contamination around terminals
109. Clean terminal area with appropriate flux removal solvent if required
110. Perform continuity test between terminals to verify electrical connection

### Dust Cap Installation (Steps 111-125)

111. Select dust cap matching cone center diameter and aesthetic requirements
112. Inspect dust cap for defects, proper dome shape, and surface finish
113. Verify dust cap material compatibility with cone and adhesive system
114. Clean dust cap mounting surface on cone apex area
115. Apply adhesive bead to dust cap mounting rim using precision dispenser
116. Monitor adhesive quantity to prevent excess squeeze-out onto cone
117. Position dust cap concentrically over cone apex and voice coil area
118. Align dust cap orientation for visual consistency if printed or branded
119. Apply controlled downward pressure to seat dust cap properly
120. Verify adhesive wetting on both dust cap and cone surfaces
121. Remove any excess adhesive from visible areas using precision tools
122. Check dust cap concentricity and perpendicularity to cone axis
123. Verify dust cap does not contact voice coil or interfere with movement
124. Allow adhesive to tack before releasing assembly from fixture
125. Transfer to curing area maintaining dust cap alignment

### Voice Coil Centering and Alignment (Steps 126-145)

126. Install assembled speaker into centering fixture with precision mandrels
127. Insert centering shims into voice coil gap around entire circumference
128. Verify shim thickness matches designed air gap specification
129. Distribute shims evenly around voice coil circumference at multiple heights
130. Check that shims do not interfere with cone or surround movement
131. Apply slight downward pressure to speaker assembly in fixture
132. Verify voice coil centers uniformly in magnetic gap using visual inspection
133. Measure gap uniformity at multiple angular positions with feeler gauges
134. Adjust shim positioning if gap variation exceeds specified tolerance
135. Apply adhesive to spider mounting surface on voice coil former
136. Position spider concentrically over voice coil using alignment features
137. Ensure spider corrugations are symmetric and properly oriented
138. Apply adhesive to spider outer diameter for basket attachment
139. Position spider against basket mounting surface maintaining concentricity
140. Apply uniform pressure around spider outer diameter bond zone
141. Verify spider plane is perpendicular to voice coil axis
142. Check that spider does not create binding or excessive force on coil
143. Maintain centering shims in position during spider adhesive cure
144. Monitor cure time according to adhesive specifications and environmental conditions
145. Perform visual inspection of spider bonds before shim removal

### Final Assembly and Adhesive Cure (Steps 146-160)

146. Verify all adhesive bonds have achieved minimum handling strength
147. Carefully remove centering shims from voice coil gap
148. Inspect voice coil for freedom of movement and absence of binding
149. Gently press cone center to verify smooth coil travel in both directions
150. Listen for any scraping, rubbing, or mechanical interference sounds
151. Check voice coil return to rest position without offset or sticking
152. Inspect all adhesive bonds visually for completeness and integrity
153. Verify surround profile maintains proper shape and compliance
154. Check lead wire routing and adhesive attachment points
155. Ensure dust cap remains concentric and properly bonded
156. Clean any adhesive residue or contamination from visible surfaces
157. Remove assembly from centering fixture using proper handling techniques
158. Place completed speaker in final cure rack maintaining proper orientation
159. Transfer to controlled environment for final adhesive cure cycle
160. Document assembly completion time and environmental conditions

### Electrical Testing (Steps 161-175)

161. Connect speaker terminals to impedance measurement equipment
162. Measure DC resistance across terminals and record value
163. Verify DC resistance falls within specified tolerance range
164. Perform impedance sweep across specified frequency range
165. Verify impedance curve matches reference profile for speaker model
166. Check for anomalies indicating voice coil defects or connection issues
167. Measure resonant frequency and compare to specification limits
168. Perform polarity check using known polarity signal source
169. Verify cone moves outward with positive voltage applied to correct terminal
170. Mark speaker with polarity indicators if required by specifications
171. Test for voice coil rubs by monitoring impedance during cone excursion
172. Apply low-frequency signal while monitoring for impedance fluctuations
173. Listen for mechanical sounds indicating voice coil contact with gap
174. Perform insulation resistance test between voice coil and metal frame
175. Document all electrical test results in quality control system

### Acoustic Testing (Steps 176-190)

176. Mount speaker in standardized test baffle or enclosure
177. Position measurement microphone at specified distance and angle
178. Calibrate acoustic measurement system with reference source
179. Apply pink noise or swept sine wave signal at specified input level
180. Measure frequency response across full operational bandwidth
181. Verify response curve falls within tolerance limits at all frequencies
182. Check for peaks, dips, or irregularities indicating assembly defects
183. Measure sensitivity at reference frequency and input power level
184. Verify sensitivity meets minimum specification requirements
185. Perform harmonic distortion measurement at multiple frequency points
186. Check that THD levels remain below maximum specified limits
187. Test maximum input power handling at rated conditions
188. Monitor for thermal failures, voice coil rubbing, or mechanical breakdown
189. Perform listening test for subjective quality assessment if required
190. Document all acoustic test results and flag any out-of-specification units

### Final Inspection and Packaging (Steps 191-195)

191. Perform comprehensive visual inspection of completed speaker assembly
192. Verify all components are properly installed and secured
193. Check for cosmetic defects, contamination, or damage requiring rework
194. Apply product labels, serial numbers, or identification markings
195. Package speaker in protective materials and prepare for shipment or next assembly stage

## Quality Control Standards

Speaker assembly technicians maintain rigorous quality standards throughout the manufacturing process. Critical control points include voice coil centering accuracy within 0.05mm, adhesive bond integrity verification, electrical parameter validation, and acoustic performance testing. All assemblies undergo 100% inspection with statistical process control monitoring to ensure consistent product quality.

## Skills and Training Requirements

Technicians require comprehensive training in precision mechanical assembly, adhesive material handling, soldering techniques, acoustic measurement principles, and quality control methodologies. Manual dexterity, attention to detail, and ability to work with microscopic tolerances are essential for successful speaker assembly operations.
