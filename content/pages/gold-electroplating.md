## Manufacturing Process: Gold Electroplating Service for Electronics

### Bath Preparation (Steps 1-25)

1. Fill primary plating tank with 500 liters of deionized water at 18.2 megohm-cm resistivity
2. Heat water to 60°C using immersion heaters with temperature control within ±1°C
3. Add potassium gold cyanide (KAu(CN)2) solution containing 4.0 grams per liter gold content
4. Mix solution using air sparging at 5 liters per minute for uniform distribution
5. Add potassium citrate buffer at 75 grams per liter to stabilize pH
6. Adjust pH to 4.5 using citric acid with continuous monitoring via pH electrode
7. Add cobalt chloride hardener at 0.5 grams per liter for hard gold applications
8. Install titanium basket anodes with platinized coating for inert electrode service
9. Load dummy cathodes for bath conditioning cycle of 2 hours at 0.5 ASF
10. Circulate solution through 0.5 micron cartridge filters at 20 liters per minute
11. Add wetting agent at 0.1 grams per liter to reduce surface tension to 30 dynes/cm
12. Install temperature probe with digital controller maintaining ±0.5°C stability
13. Connect power rectifier capable of 500 amperes at 12 volts DC output
14. Calibrate Hull cell test apparatus for plating distribution analysis
15. Prepare nickel strike bath with 240 grams per liter nickel sulfamate solution
16. Adjust nickel bath pH to 4.0 using sulfamic acid with precision electrode
17. Heat nickel bath to 50°C for optimal activation performance
18. Set up soft gold bath with 2.0 grams per liter gold content for bonding applications
19. Add thallium carbonate brightener at 0.01 grams per liter to soft gold bath
20. Install continuous gold concentration monitor using atomic absorption spectroscopy
21. Connect automated pH controller with acid/base dosing pumps
22. Set up conductivity meter monitoring at 15-20 mS/cm range
23. Install carbon treatment system for organic contamination removal
24. Prepare electrocleaner bath with 50 grams per liter sodium carbonate at 70°C
25. Establish rinse cascade system with four-stage counterflow configuration

### Surface Preparation (Steps 26-45)

26. Receive copper PCB substrates or connector pins requiring gold plating service
27. Inspect incoming parts for surface defects using 10X magnification optical inspection
28. Degrease parts in alkaline cleaner at 60°C for 5 minutes with ultrasonic agitation
29. Rinse in hot deionized water spray at 55°C for 2 minutes
30. Apply cathodic electrocleaning at 6 volts for 30 seconds in carbonate solution
31. Rinse in ambient temperature deionized water spray for 1 minute
32. Perform anodic electrocleaning at 6 volts for 15 seconds to remove final oxides
33. Rinse in deionized water cascade with overflow maintaining fresh water quality
34. Activate copper surface in 10% sulfuric acid dip for 30 seconds
35. Measure surface roughness using profilometer confirming Ra less than 0.5 microns
36. Rinse in deionized water spray ensuring no acid carryover into plating bath
37. Perform Wood's nickel strike at 6 ASF for 2 minutes to ensure adhesion layer
38. Deposit nickel underlayer at 30 ASF for 10 minutes achieving 2.5 micron thickness
39. Rinse in deionized water spray removing all nickel bath chemistry
40. Perform mild acid activation in 5% sulfuric acid for 15 seconds
41. Rinse thoroughly in deionized water with conductivity monitoring below 5 microsiemens
42. Visually inspect nickel layer for uniform bright appearance and complete coverage
43. Measure nickel thickness using XRF analyzer at five points per part
44. Transfer parts to gold plating tank within 30 seconds to prevent oxidation
45. Immerse parts ensuring complete submersion without air entrapment

### Hard Gold Plating Process (Steps 46-75)

46. Apply initial plating current at 1 ASF for strike layer formation over 30 seconds
47. Ramp current density to 3 ASF over 60 seconds for uniform deposit initiation
48. Maintain plating at 3 ASF with bath temperature at 60°C for hard gold deposition
49. Monitor gold concentration maintaining 4.0 ±0.2 grams per liter via titration
50. Check pH every 15 minutes ensuring stability at 4.5 ±0.1 throughout plating
51. Observe cathode surface for uniform gold color development without burning
52. Calculate plating time for target thickness using 0.5 microns per minute rate
53. Plate for 10 minutes to achieve 5.0 micron hard gold thickness on wear surfaces
54. Monitor current density distribution ensuring uniformity across complex geometries
55. Use auxiliary anodes or shields to improve throwing power in recessed areas
56. Sample bath solution every 2 hours for gold content verification by AAS
57. Adjust gold concentration by adding replenishment solution as needed
58. Monitor cobalt co-deposition maintaining 0.15-0.25% for hardness of 150-200 HK
59. Check solution temperature stability within ±1°C to control deposit properties
60. Perform Hull cell test every 4 hours to verify plating distribution quality
61. Inspect deposit color for bright yellow appearance indicating proper chemistry
62. Remove parts from plating bath using smooth withdrawal to avoid marking
63. Immerse immediately in stagnant deionized water rinse for 30 seconds
64. Transfer to agitated deionized water rinse for 2 minutes with fresh water flow
65. Perform final rinse in ultra-pure deionized water spray for 1 minute
66. Dry parts using filtered warm air at 50°C without water spotting
67. Visually inspect gold deposit for uniform coverage and bright appearance
68. Check for any blistering, peeling, or adhesion defects under magnification
69. Measure deposit thickness at specified locations using XRF analyzer
70. Verify thickness meets specification of 5.0 ±0.5 microns for contact applications
71. Test surface hardness using microhardness tester confirming 150-200 Knoop
72. Perform adhesion test using tape test method per ASTM B571 standard
73. Conduct thermal shock test cycling between -55°C and 125°C for 10 cycles
74. Measure contact resistance using four-point probe confirming less than 10 milliohms
75. Document plating parameters and measurements in batch production records

### Soft Gold Plating Process (Steps 76-100)

76. Transfer wire bonding substrates to soft gold bath after nickel underlayer
77. Verify soft gold bath concentration at 2.0 grams per liter gold content
78. Check bath pH at 6.5 optimal for pure gold deposition without hardeners
79. Maintain bath temperature at 65°C for improved deposit purity
80. Apply current density of 1.5 ASF for soft gold bonding pad applications
81. Monitor plating rate at 0.25 microns per minute for controlled thickness
82. Plate for 4 minutes to achieve 1.0 micron soft gold thickness on bond pads
83. Observe deposit formation for matte finish characteristic of pure gold
84. Ensure gold purity exceeds 99.7% for optimal wire bonding performance
85. Check deposit hardness maintaining below 90 Knoop for bondability
86. Sample bath for metallic contamination using ICP analysis every 8 hours
87. Maintain iron, copper contamination below 5 ppm to preserve softness
88. Add gold replenishment maintaining consistent concentration during production
89. Monitor brightener concentration at 0.01 grams per liter for surface leveling
90. Verify absence of cobalt or nickel co-deposition that would harden deposit
91. Check throwing power ensuring uniform thickness across complex pad geometries
92. Remove parts from soft gold bath with steady motion preventing drag-out
93. Rinse in deionized water spray removing all plating solution
94. Perform second rinse in flowing deionized water for 2 minutes
95. Apply final ultra-pure water rinse removing any residual contaminants
96. Dry parts in clean filtered air avoiding any particulate contamination
97. Measure soft gold thickness using XRF at multiple bond pad locations
98. Verify thickness uniformity within ±10% across all bonding sites
99. Test wire bondability using pull test achieving minimum 5 gram pull strength
100. Inspect bond pad surface using SEM confirming smooth grain structure

### Thickness Measurement and Control (Steps 101-120)

102. Select measurement parameters for gold on nickel on copper substrate stack

108. Plot thickness data on control charts identifying trends or shifts
109. Verify thickness meets customer specification of 5.0 +0.5/-0.0 microns
110. Perform destructive cross-section analysis on one part per lot
111. Mount sample in epoxy resin and grind to reveal plating cross-section
112. Polish cross-section to 0.05 micron finish using diamond suspension
113. Etch lightly to reveal gold-nickel and nickel-copper interfaces
114. Measure layer thickness using calibrated optical microscope at 1000X
115. Verify XRF measurements correlate with cross-section within ±5% accuracy
116. Document measurement data in quality control database system
