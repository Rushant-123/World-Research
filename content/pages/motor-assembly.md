Motor assembly combines precision electromechanical components into a complete electric motor unit. This process integrates the electromagnetic stator, permanent magnet rotor, precision bearings, structural housing, and electronic control systems into a balanced, efficient rotating machine. Manufacturing requires exact tolerances for mechanical alignment, careful handling of magnetized components, precise torque application, and comprehensive electrical and dynamic testing to ensure performance specifications are met.

## Stator Preparation and Inspection

1. Receive completed stator assembly from winding department with documentation
2. Verify stator part number matches work order and production batch
3. Inspect stator lamination stack for deformation or damage
4. Check stator outer diameter with precision micrometer (tolerance ±0.02mm)
5. Measure stator stack height to verify compression (target 25.4mm ±0.1mm)
6. Inspect copper winding coils for proper formation and wire routing
7. Check for wire insulation damage or exposed copper conductors
8. Verify phase lead wires are correctly color-coded and labeled
9. Measure resistance between each phase winding (target 0.8Ω ±5%)
10. Test insulation resistance between windings and stator core (minimum 100MΩ)
11. Inspect slot insulation paper for proper positioning and no tears
12. Check winding impregnation coating for complete coverage
13. Verify stator mounting holes align with housing specifications
14. Clean stator surfaces of dust and manufacturing residue with compressed air
15. Apply light coating of corrosion inhibitor to exposed steel surfaces

## Housing Preparation and Component Fitment

16. Retrieve aluminum motor housing from inventory matching batch number
17. Inspect housing bore for scratches, burrs, or dimensional irregularities
18. Measure housing inner diameter with bore gauge (specification 45.00mm +0.03/-0.00mm)
19. Check housing mounting flange flatness with dial indicator (maximum 0.05mm deviation)
20. Verify mounting hole positions and thread quality (M4 x 0.7 threads)
21. Clean housing bore with isopropanol to remove machining oils
22. Inspect bearing seat surfaces for proper finish and perpendicularity
23. Measure bearing seat diameter (specification 12.00mm +0.01/-0.00mm)
24. Check bearing seat depth consistency (target 8.5mm ±0.05mm)
25. Verify housing ventilation slots are clear of obstructions
26. Inspect electrical cable entry points for proper grommet installation
27. Apply thermal adhesive to thermal dissipation surfaces if specified
28. Position housing in assembly fixture with controlled orientation
29. Set housing temperature to 80°C in thermal expansion chamber for press fit
30. Monitor housing expansion for 10 minutes to achieve uniform temperature

## Stator Installation into Housing

31. Retrieve heated housing from thermal chamber using insulated gloves
32. Position stator above housing bore maintaining vertical alignment
33. Check stator-to-housing clearance gap (specification 0.015-0.035mm interference fit)
34. Apply alignment pins to locate stator rotation position relative to housing
35. Lower stator into housing bore using precision press with force monitoring
36. Monitor press force during insertion (target 1200-1800N insertion force)
37. Verify stator seats fully against housing shoulder with depth gauge
38. Check stator centering in housing with radial runout measurement (maximum 0.03mm)
39. Measure stator face protrusion from housing end (target flush ±0.1mm)
40. Allow housing and stator assembly to cool to room temperature (20 minutes)
41. Re-verify stator retention in housing after thermal contraction
42. Inspect for any gaps between stator laminations and housing wall
43. Route phase lead wires through designated cable channels
44. Secure lead wires with cable ties to prevent interference with rotor
45. Apply threadlocker compound to stator retention screws if mechanical fastening used

## Bearing Preparation and Pressing

46. Retrieve precision ball bearings from controlled environment storage
47. Verify bearing part number matches motor specification (example: 608ZZ)
48. Inspect bearing for proper grease fill (30-40% fill volume)
49. Check bearing outer race diameter with micrometer (8.000mm ±0.005mm)
50. Measure bearing inner race diameter (4.000mm ±0.003mm)
51. Verify bearing width dimension (3.0mm ±0.05mm)
52. Test bearing rotation by hand for smoothness and absence of noise
53. Inspect bearing seals for proper seating and no damage
54. Clean bearing outer surface with lint-free cloth
55. Apply light press-fit compound to bearing outer race if specified
56. Position front bearing in housing bearing seat with orientation mark aligned
57. Use bearing press tool with controlled force application
58. Press bearing into housing seat until flush with shoulder (force not exceeding 500N)
59. Verify bearing seating depth with depth micrometer (specification 8.5mm ±0.02mm)
60. Check bearing rotation after installation for proper freedom of movement

## Rotor Inspection and Preparation

61. Retrieve permanent magnet rotor assembly matching motor build specification
62. Verify rotor part number and magnet grade documentation (N42 NdFeB magnets)
63. Inspect rotor shaft for straightness using V-blocks and dial indicator (maximum 0.01mm runout)
64. Measure rotor shaft diameter at bearing journals (3.995mm +0.000/-0.008mm)
65. Check shaft surface finish for scoring or damage (Ra < 0.4μm)
66. Verify rotor magnet ring is securely bonded to rotor core
67. Measure rotor outer diameter at multiple points (24.95mm ±0.02mm)
68. Check rotor stack length dimension (20.0mm ±0.1mm)
69. Inspect magnet pole boundaries for consistent spacing and alignment
70. Test magnet field strength with gaussmeter (minimum 3500 Gauss surface field)
71. Verify rotor balance marking from balancing operation
72. Clean rotor shaft bearing surfaces with isopropanol
73. Apply thin film of bearing grease to shaft journals
74. Check rotor end play allowance in bearing configuration
75. Position rotor for insertion maintaining magnetic pole orientation marking

## Rotor Installation and Alignment

76. Carefully insert rotor shaft through front bearing inner race
77. Guide rotor through stator bore maintaining concentricity to prevent magnet contact
78. Monitor air gap clearance during insertion (specification 0.3-0.5mm radial gap)
79. Advance rotor slowly while rotating to verify smooth passage
80. Ensure rotor advances fully until shaft protrudes from rear bearing location
81. Verify rotor does not contact stator teeth at any rotation angle
82. Check for magnetic cogging as rotor is rotated by hand
83. Position rear bearing on protruding shaft end
84. Align rear bearing with housing bearing seat using guide tool
85. Press rear bearing onto shaft while simultaneously pressing into housing
86. Monitor shaft position during pressing to maintain centering
87. Verify rear bearing seats against housing shoulder (depth 8.5mm ±0.02mm)
88. Check bearing pressing force did not exceed specification (maximum 500N)
89. Measure total shaft end play between bearings (specification 0.05-0.15mm)
90. Verify rotor rotates freely by hand with consistent torque

## Air Gap Verification and Centering

91. Insert feeler gauge between rotor magnets and stator teeth at 12 positions
92. Measure radial air gap at each position (specification 0.35mm ±0.05mm)
93. Record maximum and minimum air gap measurements
94. Verify air gap variation does not exceed 0.08mm total range
95. Check rotor concentricity with stator bore using dial indicator on shaft
96. Measure radial runout of rotor surface (maximum 0.04mm TIR)
97. Verify rotor is not axially shifted toward one end (centering ±0.2mm)
98. Confirm magnetic pole centerlines align with stator slot centers
99. Document air gap measurements on assembly traveler
100. If air gap is non-uniform, disassemble and investigate alignment issue
101. Re-verify stator perpendicularity to housing if concentricity problems found
102. Check bearing seat positioning if rotor shows excessive runout
103. Ensure stator laminations are not deformed or shifted
104. Confirm rotor shaft straightness if runout exceeds tolerance
105. Final verification that all gap measurements are within specification

## End Bell and Shaft Seal Installation

106. Retrieve motor end bell matching housing assembly specification
107. Inspect end bell bearing seat and shaft seal pocket
108. Measure end bell bore diameter (12.00mm +0.01/-0.00mm)
109. Check end bell flatness against housing mating surface (maximum 0.05mm)
110. Clean end bell surfaces with isopropanol
111. Install shaft seal in end bell pocket if external shaft extension required
112. Press seal into position using seal installation tool (depth 3.0mm ±0.1mm)
113. Verify seal lip orientation faces inward toward bearing
114. Apply thin film of grease to seal lip for initial lubrication
115. Position end bell gasket or O-ring seal on housing mating surface
116. Verify gasket is properly seated with no twisting or gaps
117. Align end bell to housing using dowel pins or alignment features
118. Lower end bell onto housing while guiding shaft through seal
119. Verify end bell seats flush against housing face
120. Insert end bell retention screws (M3 x 0.5 x 12mm length)
121. Apply threadlocker to screw threads (medium strength formulation)
122. Tighten screws in star pattern to ensure even seating
123. Torque screws to specification: 0.8 N⋅m ±0.1 N⋅m using calibrated driver
124. Verify end bell does not show gaps at any point around circumference
125. Check shaft protrusion from end bell (if external shaft: 15.0mm ±0.5mm)

## Electrical Lead Wire Connection

126. Route stator phase lead wires to terminal connection area
127. Strip wire insulation 6mm from wire ends using precision strippers
128. Inspect stripped conductors for strand damage or insulation nicks
129. Tin wire ends with 63/37 tin-lead solder using 280°C iron
130. Clean tinned leads with isopropanol to remove flux residue
131. Position controller board in designated mounting location
132. Align board mounting holes with housing standoffs
133. Verify board clearance from rotating components (minimum 2mm)
134. Install board mounting screws (M2.5 x 0.45 x 6mm)
135. Torque board screws to 0.4 N⋅m to secure without PCB stress
136. Connect phase A lead wire to board terminal pad A
137. Solder connection using 260°C iron with eutectic solder
138. Inspect solder joint for proper wetting and fillet formation
139. Connect phase B lead wire to terminal pad B with same procedure
140. Connect phase C lead wire to terminal pad C
141. Verify no solder bridges between adjacent terminals
142. Check mechanical pull strength of each solder connection
143. Connect hall effect sensor wires to board sensor inputs
144. Verify sensor polarity and signal wire assignments (VCC, GND, Signal A, B, C)
145. Route power supply wires to board power input terminals

## Hall Sensor Installation and Alignment

146. Retrieve hall effect sensor PCB assembly for commutation sensing
147. Verify sensor part number matches motor electrical specification
148. Inspect sensor IC for proper soldering and component orientation
149. Test sensor output with magnet proximity (should switch states)
150. Position sensor board in housing sensor mounting pocket

152. Verify sensor-to-magnet air gap (specification 0.8-1.2mm)
153. Rotate rotor to multiple positions and verify sensor state changes
154. Confirm sensor electrical angle corresponds to rotor physical position
155. Secure sensor board with screws (M2 x 0.4 x 4mm) torqued to 0.25 N⋅m
156. Connect sensor signal wires to controller board inputs

## Dynamic Balancing and Vibration Testing

176. Transfer motor to dynamic balancing machine with precision spindle
177. Mount motor in balancing fixture using shaft or housing mounting
178. Secure motor to prevent movement during balancing test
179. Initialize balancing machine and enter motor specifications
180. Spin motor to balancing test speed (5000 RPM)
181. Measure dynamic unbalance in two correction planes

186. Re-run balancing test to verify correction effectiveness

189. Transfer motor to vibration test station

## Run-In Testing and Thermal Stabilization

196. Transfer motor to run-in test station with load application capability
197. Mount motor in test fixture with torque arm and speed sensor
198. Connect motor to load dynamometer or magnetic brake
199. Apply 50% of rated load torque (0.015 N⋅m)
200. Operate motor continuously for 2-hour run-in period
201. Monitor motor current during run-in (specification 0.8A ±0.15A)
202. Record motor winding temperature using thermocouple probe

204. Monitor motor speed stability (specification 4000 RPM ±50 RPM)
205. Check for abnormal noise development during run-in period
206. Listen for bearing noise or magnetic buzzing
207. Measure sound pressure level at 1 meter distance (maximum 45 dBA)

210. Increase load to 100% rated torque (0.03 N⋅m)
211. Operate at full load for 30 minutes
212. Measure loaded current draw (specification 1.5A ±0.2A)
213. Record maximum winding temperature (specification < 85°C)
214. Calculate motor efficiency: (output power / input power) x 100%
215. Target efficiency > 80% at rated load conditions
