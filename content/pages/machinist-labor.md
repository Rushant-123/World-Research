# CNC Machinist Training: Comprehensive Apprenticeship Program

## Phase 1: Foundation and Safety (Steps 1-25)

### Week 1: Introduction to Manufacturing and Safety

1. Complete workplace safety orientation and review OSHA regulations for machine shops
2. Learn proper personal protective equipment (PPE) requirements including safety glasses, steel-toed boots, and hearing protection
3. Study machine shop hazards including rotating equipment, sharp tools, and hot materials
4. Practice lockout/tagout (LOTO) procedures for machine maintenance and setup
5. Review emergency procedures including location of fire extinguishers, first aid kits, and emergency exits
6. Learn proper lifting techniques and material handling procedures to prevent injury
7. Study machine shop housekeeping standards and 5S methodology (Sort, Set in Order, Shine, Standardize, Sustain)
8. Complete hazard communication training and review Safety Data Sheets (SDS)
9. Learn proper disposal procedures for cutting fluids, metal chips, and hazardous materials
10. Practice safe operation around moving machinery and understand pinch point hazards

### Week 2: Machine Shop Tools and Equipment Overview

11. Identify and learn the purpose of various manual machine tools including drill presses, band saws, and grinders
12. Study the components and operation of manual lathes including headstock, tailstock, carriage, and tool post
13. Learn the parts and function of manual milling machines including table, spindle, quill, and feed mechanisms
14. Understand the differences between vertical and horizontal milling machines
15. Study the operation of surface grinders and their role in precision finishing
16. Learn about abrasive cutting methods and proper grinding wheel selection
17. Understand the purpose and operation of deburring equipment
18. Study various workholding devices including vises, chucks, collets, and fixtures
19. Learn proper tool storage and organization systems in the machine shop
20. Practice basic housekeeping and maintenance of hand tools and measuring instruments

### Week 3: Introduction to CNC Technology

21. Study the evolution from manual to computer numerical control (CNC) machining
22. Learn the basic components of CNC machines including controller, servo motors, and feedback systems
23. Understand the advantages of CNC machining over manual operations including repeatability and complexity
24. Study different types of CNC machines including mills, lathes, routers, and multi-axis machining centers
25. Learn the coordinate system used in CNC programming including X, Y, Z axes and rotary axes

## Phase 2: Blueprint Reading and Technical Drawing (Steps 26-50)

### Week 4: Fundamentals of Technical Drawing

26. Learn to read and interpret orthographic projections including top, front, and side views
27. Study isometric and pictorial views to understand three-dimensional part representation
28. Understand section views and how they reveal internal features of parts
29. Learn auxiliary views used to show true size and shape of angled features
30. Study detail views and their application in highlighting specific part features
31. Understand assembly drawings and how individual parts relate to finished products
32. Learn to identify hidden lines, center lines, and dimension lines on blueprints
33. Study line weights and their meanings in technical drawings
34. Practice sketching basic geometric shapes and machine features
35. Learn proper dimensioning practices and standards according to ASME Y14.5

### Week 5: Dimensions and Tolerances

36. Study basic dimensioning including linear, angular, and radial dimensions
37. Learn tolerance specification methods including bilateral, unilateral, and limit dimensions
38. Understand the concept of nominal size versus actual size and manufacturing variation
39. Study standard tolerance blocks and general tolerance specifications
40. Learn to read and interpret geometric dimensioning and tolerancing (GD&T) symbols
41. Understand datum reference frames and their role in part inspection
42. Study form tolerances including flatness, straightness, circularity, and cylindricity
43. Learn orientation tolerances including perpendicularity, parallelism, and angularity
44. Understand location tolerances including position, concentricity, and symmetry
45. Study runout tolerances including circular and total runout specifications

### Week 6: Material Specifications and Surface Finish

46. Learn to identify material specifications on blueprints including alloy designations
47. Study common engineering materials including aluminum alloys, steel grades, and stainless steels
48. Understand material properties including hardness, tensile strength, and machinability ratings
49. Learn to read heat treatment specifications including hardening, tempering, and annealing requirements
50. Study surface finish symbols and specifications including roughness average (Ra) values

## Phase 3: Precision Measurement and Metrology (Steps 51-80)

### Week 7: Basic Measuring Tools

51. Learn proper use and care of steel rules for linear measurement to 1/64 inch accuracy
52. Study vernier calipers operation for measurements to 0.001 inch (0.02mm) accuracy
53. Practice measuring outside diameters, inside diameters, depths, and steps with calipers
54. Learn proper use of micrometers for measurements to 0.0001 inch (0.0025mm) accuracy
55. Study outside micrometers for measuring external dimensions with high precision
56. Practice using inside micrometers and telescoping gauges for internal measurements
57. Learn depth micrometer operation for measuring hole depths and recess dimensions
58. Study dial indicators and their application in measuring runout, flatness, and perpendicularity
59. Practice indicator setup using magnetic bases and proper contact point positioning
60. Learn proper care, calibration, and storage of precision measuring instruments

### Week 8: Advanced Metrology Equipment

61. Study digital measuring instruments including digital calipers and micrometers
62. Learn the operation of height gauges for precision layout and measurement
63. Practice using bore gauges for accurate internal diameter measurement
64. Study pin gauges and gauge block sets for high-precision calibration work
65. Learn thread measurement using thread micrometers, wires, and optical comparators
66. Understand gauge theory including go/no-go gauges and their application in quality control
67. Study surface roughness measurement using profilometers and comparison specimens
68. Learn coordinate measuring machine (CMM) basics and their role in complex part inspection
69. Practice optical measurement techniques using toolmaker's microscopes and video systems
70. Study measurement uncertainty and proper documentation of inspection results

### Week 9: Measurement Best Practices

71. Learn proper workpiece cleaning procedures before measurement to ensure accuracy
72. Study the effects of temperature on precision measurement and thermal expansion
73. Practice measurement repeatability and reproducibility (R&R) studies
74. Understand measurement force and its impact on soft materials and thin-walled parts
75. Learn statistical process control (SPC) basics and control charting for manufacturing
76. Study measurement system analysis (MSA) and gauge capability studies
77. Practice proper documentation of inspection results using inspection reports
78. Learn to create inspection plans based on blueprint specifications and critical features
79. Understand first article inspection (FAI) procedures and documentation requirements
80. Study traceability requirements for measuring equipment and calibration certificates

## Phase 4: Manual Machining Operations (Steps 81-105)

### Week 10: Manual Lathe Operations

81. Learn manual lathe setup including workpiece mounting in three-jaw and four-jaw chucks
82. Study tool post setup and proper cutting tool height adjustment using tooling references
83. Practice facing operations to create smooth, perpendicular end surfaces
84. Learn straight turning operations for reducing outside diameter with proper depth of cut
85. Study taper turning using compound rest angle settings and tailstock offset methods
86. Practice shoulder turning and undercutting operations for creating stepped shafts
87. Learn drilling operations on the lathe using tailstock-mounted drill chucks
88. Study boring operations for enlarging existing holes to precise diameters
89. Practice threading operations including single-point thread cutting and die threading
90. Learn knurling techniques for creating grip surfaces on cylindrical parts

### Week 11: Manual Milling Operations

91. Study manual milling machine setup and workpiece fixturing in machine vises
92. Learn edge finding techniques for establishing work coordinate reference points
93. Practice face milling operations for creating flat surfaces with fly cutters and face mills
94. Study end milling techniques for machining slots, pockets, and profile features
95. Learn drilling and reaming operations on the milling machine for precision hole making
96. Practice angular milling using rotary tables and angle vises for creating angled features
97. Study slitting saw operations for cutting narrow slots and parting operations
98. Learn T-slot milling for creating workholding channels in fixtures and tables
99. Practice keyway milling using Woodruff cutters and end mills
100. Study radius milling techniques using rotary tables and coordinate positioning

### Week 12: Grinding and Finishing Operations

101. Learn surface grinding setup and proper workpiece magnetic chuck mounting
102. Study grinding wheel selection based on material hardness and surface finish requirements
103. Practice surface grinding operations achieving flatness within 0.0005 inch tolerance
104. Learn grinding wheel dressing and truing procedures for maintaining wheel geometry
105. Study manual deburring techniques using files, scrapers, and abrasive materials

## Phase 5: CNC Programming Fundamentals (Steps 106-135)

### Week 13: G-Code Programming Basics

106. Learn the structure of CNC programs including program numbers, blocks, and commands
107. Study coordinate systems and work offset assignment (G54-G59) for part positioning
108. Understand absolute positioning (G90) versus incremental positioning (G91) programming
109. Learn rapid positioning (G00) commands for non-cutting tool movements
110. Study linear interpolation (G01) for straight-line cutting movements with feedrate control
111. Practice circular interpolation (G02/G03) for arc and circular cutting paths
112. Learn tool length compensation (G43) and tool diameter compensation (G41/G42) programming
113. Study spindle control commands (M03/M04/M05) for spindle start, stop, and direction
114. Understand coolant control commands (M08/M09) for cutting fluid management
115. Learn program structure including program start, tool changes, and program end sequences

### Week 14: CNC Mill Programming

116. Study CNC mill work coordinate system setup and part zero reference point establishment
117. Learn tool library creation including tool numbers, lengths, and diameter specifications
118. Practice writing simple face milling programs with rectangular cutting patterns
119. Study pocket milling strategies including roughing and finishing passes
120. Learn contouring operations for machining complex curved profiles
121. Practice drilling cycle programming (G81-G89) for automated hole-making operations
122. Study peck drilling cycles (G83) for deep hole drilling with chip clearing
123. Learn boring cycle programming (G85-G89) for precision hole finishing
124. Practice tap cycle programming (G84) for internal thread cutting operations
125. Study subprogram and macro programming for repetitive feature patterns

### Week 15: CNC Lathe Programming

126. Learn CNC lathe coordinate system with Z-axis along spindle and X-axis for diameter control
127. Study tool nose radius compensation (G41/G42) for accurate profile programming
128. Practice OD turning cycles (G71) for automated rough and finish turning operations
129. Learn face grooving and OD grooving cycle programming (G75/G74)
130. Study threading cycles (G76/G92) for single-point thread cutting on CNC lathes
131. Practice parting-off operations and length control programming
132. Learn boring cycle programming for internal diameter machining
133. Study constant surface speed (G96) versus constant RPM (G97) spindle control
134. Practice combined turning and milling operations on mill-turn machines
135. Learn live tooling programming for radial drilling and milling on CNC lathes

## Phase 6: CNC Machine Setup and Operation (Steps 136-160)

### Week 16: CNC Mill Setup Procedures

136. Learn machine startup procedures including hydraulic, pneumatic, and lubrication system checks
137. Study machine homing and reference point return operations for axis calibration
138. Practice work offset measurement using edge finders and probe systems
139. Learn tool presetting using offline tool presetters and tool length measurement
140. Study tool holder selection including CAT, BT, and HSK taper standards
141. Practice workpiece fixturing using vises, clamps, and custom fixture plates
142. Learn to load and verify CNC programs through RS-232, USB, or network transfer
143. Study program simulation and dry run procedures to verify tool paths before cutting
144. Practice single block execution for step-by-step program verification
145. Learn feedrate override and spindle speed override functions for process optimization

### Week 17: CNC Lathe Setup Procedures

146. Study CNC lathe chuck mounting and jaw setup for workpiece holding
147. Learn workpiece mounting and runout checking using dial indicators
148. Practice tool turret setup and tool station assignment for multi-tool operations
149. Study tool offset measurement using touch-off methods and tool setting probes
150. Learn part catcher and bar feeder setup for automated production runs
151. Practice tailstock setup and programming for long part support
152. Study steady rest and follow rest setup for flexible workpiece machining
153. Learn coolant system setup and through-spindle coolant programming
154. Practice chip management and chip conveyor operation for continuous production
155. Study part unloading and inspection procedures during production runs

### Week 18: Process Optimization and Troubleshooting

156. Learn cutting parameter optimization including speed, feed, and depth of cut relationships
157. Study tool life management and tool wear monitoring techniques
158. Practice vibration analysis and chatter elimination through parameter adjustment
159. Learn surface finish troubleshooting and improvement strategies
160. Study dimensional accuracy troubleshooting including thermal effects and tool deflection

## Phase 7: Cutting Tools and Tooling Systems (Steps 161-180)

### Week 19: Cutting Tool Materials and Geometry

161. Study high-speed steel (HSS) cutting tools and their applications in low-speed operations
162. Learn carbide insert grades and their selection based on workpiece material
163. Study coated carbide tools including TiN, TiCN, and TiAlN coating benefits
164. Practice ceramic and CBN cutting tool selection for hardened material machining
165. Learn cutting tool geometry including rake angle, clearance angle, and nose radius
166. Study chip breaker design and selection for effective chip control
167. Practice insert shape selection including round, square, triangle, and diamond geometries
168. Learn insert size selection based on depth of cut and feed rate requirements
169. Study tool edge preparation including honing, chamfering, and edge treatments
170. Practice tool identification using ISO coding systems for inserts and tool holders

### Week 20: Milling Cutters and Tool Holders

171. Learn face mill design and insert selection for efficient surface milling
172. Study end mill types including square, ball, corner radius, and roughing styles
173. Practice helical end mill selection for improved chip evacuation and surface finish
174. Learn high-efficiency milling (HEM) techniques using smaller radial engagement
175. Study drill design including twist drills, center drills, and spot drills
176. Practice reamer selection and usage for precision hole finishing operations
177. Learn tap types including spiral point, spiral flute, and forming taps
178. Study boring bar selection for internal diameter finishing operations
179. Practice tool holder selection and proper tool mounting for rigidity
180. Learn tool balancing requirements for high-speed machining operations

## Phase 8: Advanced Machining and Quality Control (Steps 181-195)

### Week 21: Advanced Machining Techniques

181. Study high-speed machining (HSM) strategies and toolpath optimization
182. Learn adaptive clearing algorithms for efficient material removal
183. Practice rest machining techniques for multi-setup operations
184. Study 3+2 positioning and continuous 5-axis machining strategies
185. Learn trochoidal milling for machining difficult materials and deep slots

### Week 22: Quality Control and Production Management

186. Study statistical process control (SPC) implementation in production environments
187. Learn to create and maintain control charts for critical part dimensions
188. Practice root cause analysis for non-conforming parts and process deviations
189. Study corrective and preventive action (CAPA) systems in manufacturing
190. Learn production documentation including setup sheets, process sheets, and quality plans

### Week 23: Final Certification and Career Development

191. Complete comprehensive written examination covering machining theory and safety
192. Perform practical machining test demonstrating setup, programming, and operation skills
193. Create a portfolio of machined parts demonstrating various processes and tolerances
194. Study industry certifications including NIMS (National Institute for Metalworking Skills) credentials
195. Complete career development planning and job placement assistance for entry-level CNC machinist positions
