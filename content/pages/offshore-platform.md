## Phase 1: Design and Engineering (Steps 1-25)

1. Conduct seabed survey using multibeam sonar to map ocean floor topology and identify suitable foundation locations.

2. Perform geotechnical investigation by drilling boreholes 100 meters into seabed to assess soil bearing capacity and pile penetration resistance.

3. Measure environmental conditions including maximum wave height (30m), wind speed (200 km/h), and current velocity (3 knots) for 100-year storm design.

4. Analyze historical weather data from past 50 years to determine platform design criteria for extreme conditions.

5. Calculate water depth at installation site (150 meters) and determine required jacket leg height plus air gap (180 meters total).

6. Design jacket foundation structure using finite element analysis software to model stress distribution under operational and storm loads.

7. Specify structural steel grades: 355 MPa yield strength for jacket legs, 450 MPa for critical joints and nodes.

8. Engineer jacket leg dimensions: 2.5 meter diameter tubular members with 50mm wall thickness for main corner legs.

9. Design horizontal bracing system with K-braces and X-braces to provide lateral stability and wave load resistance.

10. Calculate required pile penetration depth (80 meters into seabed) based on soil parameters and platform dead load of 25,000 tonnes.

11. Design pile sleeves within jacket legs: 2.8 meter internal diameter to accommodate 2.6 meter diameter driven piles.

12. Engineer deck structure to support topside facilities: four-level integrated deck spanning 80m × 60m footprint.

13. Calculate deck load capacity requirements: 15,000 tonnes dead load plus 5,000 tonnes live load and equipment.

14. Design drilling derrick support structure to withstand 2,500 tonne hook load and dynamic drilling forces.

15. Engineer helideck structure for 15-tonne helicopter including emergency landing specifications and fire protection.

16. Design flare boom extending 100 meters from platform to safely burn excess gas away from facilities.

17. Calculate wind loads on topside structures using computational fluid dynamics modeling of 60m/s sustained winds.

18. Engineer living quarters module for 200 personnel including bedrooms, galley, recreation areas, and emergency shelter.

19. Design process equipment layout for oil-gas-water separation, gas compression, and oil storage tanks.

20. Engineer firewater deluge system with seawater pumps providing 6,000 m³/hr flow rate for fire suppression.

21. Design electrical power generation system: three 5 MW gas turbine generators plus emergency diesel backup.

22. Engineer control room with safety systems, process automation, and emergency shutdown capabilities.

23. Calculate steel material requirements: 18,000 tonnes for jacket, 12,000 tonnes for deck and modules.

24. Prepare detailed fabrication drawings with 15,000 unique structural components and connection details.

25. Obtain regulatory approvals from classification societies (ABS, DNV) and governmental agencies for design compliance.

## Phase 2: Jacket Fabrication (Steps 26-60)

26. Establish fabrication yard on coastal site with 500m × 300m area, heavy-lift cranes, and deepwater access.

27. Procure structural steel plates in 12-meter lengths: 3,000 tonnes of 50mm thick plate for jacket legs.

28. Receive tubular steel sections: pre-fabricated 2.5m diameter pipes in 15-meter lengths from steel mill.

29. Set up automated plate cutting facility with plasma torches cutting beveled edges for welding preparation.

30. Roll flat steel plates into cylindrical sections using pyramid plate rolls with 3,000 tonne bending force.

31. Weld longitudinal seams of jacket leg sections using submerged arc welding at 600 amps, 35 volts.

32. X-ray inspect 100% of longitudinal welds to detect internal defects, porosity, and lack of fusion.

33. Machine bevel ends of tubular sections using automatic pipe beveling equipment for circumferential field welds.

34. Fabricate node cans (connection hubs) by rolling 80mm thick plate into conical sections for multi-member joints.

35. Cut penetrations in node cans for inserting jacket leg and brace members at precise angles.

36. Fit jacket leg sections into node cans, maintaining tolerance of ±2mm on member centerline positions.

37. Weld tubular members to node cans using gas metal arc welding with 1.2mm diameter wire electrode.

38. Perform magnetic particle inspection on external welds to detect surface cracks and incomplete penetration.

39. Ultrasonic test critical welds in node regions using phased array probes to ensure structural integrity.

40. Fabricate horizontal bracing members: 800mm diameter tubes in X-brace and K-brace configurations.

41. Assemble jacket sections in horizontal orientation on fabrication bed using surveying equipment for alignment.

42. Weld brace members to jacket legs, completing one 40-meter-tall jacket section weighing 4,500 tonnes.

43. Install pile sleeves inside jacket legs: 2.8m diameter tubes extending through jacket nodes.

44. Weld pile sleeves to jacket structure with full-penetration welds tested by radiographic inspection.

45. Fabricate conductor guide frames for drilling risers: structural steel frames with guide funnels.

46. Install anodes for cathodic protection: 500 aluminum alloy anodes weighing 250 kg each bolted to jacket.

47. Apply protective coating to jacket structure: three-coat system with epoxy primer, intermediate, and polyurethane topcoat.

48. Fabricate three additional jacket sections, each 40 meters tall, using parallel assembly bays.

49. Install lifting trunnions and padeyes for loadout and transportation: 100-tonne capacity forged steel fittings.

50. Weld mudmats to jacket base: 20m × 20m steel plates to distribute load during installation phase.

51. Fabricate launch rails for jacket installation: 60-meter-long steel beams for controlled launch into water.

52. Assemble complete jacket structure by welding four sections together in vertical orientation on assembly tower.

53. Survey completed jacket structure: 180 meters tall, 60m × 60m base footprint, total weight 18,000 tonnes.

54. Install J-tubes through jacket legs for routing electrical and hydraulic umbilical lines from seabed.
