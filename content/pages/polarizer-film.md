# Polarizer Film Manufacturing Process

**Market Leader:** [Nitto Denko](/companies/nitto-denko)



Polarizer film is a critical optical component in LCD displays, converting unpolarized light into polarized light. This manufacturing process produces high-performance polarizer films specifically designed for Mac LCD displays, with precise control over optical properties, thickness, and durability.

## Raw Material Preparation and Inspection

1. Receive PVA (polyvinyl alcohol) film rolls from certified suppliers in cleanroom environment
2. Inspect PVA film for width uniformity, targeting 1300-2500mm standard widths
3. Measure initial PVA film thickness using precision optical micrometers (75-85μm base thickness)
4. Verify PVA film moisture content using Karl Fischer titration (target 4-6%)
5. Check PVA film polymerization degree through viscosity measurements (1700-2200)
6. Examine PVA film surface for defects using automated optical inspection systems
7. Test PVA film tensile strength in machine direction (MD) and transverse direction (TD)
8. Measure PVA film light transmittance using spectrophotometers (>92% initial)
9. Verify PVA film haze levels using haze meters (target <0.5%)
10. Store PVA film rolls in temperature-controlled warehouse (20-25°C, 50-60% RH)

## TAC Film Preparation

11. Receive triacetyl cellulose (TAC) protective film rolls from suppliers
12. Inspect TAC film thickness uniformity (target 80μm ±2μm)
13. Measure TAC film retardation values using birefringence analyzers
14. Verify TAC film moisture permeability (target 800-1200 g/m²/day)
15. Check TAC film optical isotropy and clarity specifications
16. Test TAC film adhesion properties for subsequent lamination
17. Examine TAC film surface energy using contact angle measurements
18. Store TAC films in controlled humidity environment to prevent curling
19. Prepare TAC film surfaces through corona treatment or plasma activation
20. Verify TAC film dimensional stability under thermal stress testing

## Iodine Dye Solution Preparation

21. Prepare ultrapure water through reverse osmosis and deionization systems
22. Measure water purity to ensure >18 MΩ·cm resistivity
23. Weigh precise quantities of iodine crystals in clean weighing chambers
24. Dissolve iodine in water with potassium iodide to form triiodide complex
25. Prepare iodine solution concentration targeting 0.1-0.5% by weight
26. Mix solution using magnetic stirrers at controlled temperature (25-30°C)
27. Monitor solution pH levels (maintain at 6.5-7.5 range)
28. Filter iodine solution through 0.2μm membrane filters
29. Test solution concentration using UV-Vis spectrophotometry
30. Store prepared iodine solutions in dark containers to prevent degradation

## PVA Film Swelling Process

31. Mount PVA film roll onto precision unwinding system with tension control
32. Thread film through multi-stage processing line at controlled speed
33. Guide film into first water bath for initial swelling treatment
34. Control water bath temperature precisely (20-30°C for swelling)
35. Monitor film residence time in swelling bath (30-60 seconds)
36. Maintain water purity in swelling bath through continuous filtration
37. Allow PVA molecular chains to absorb water and expand uniformly
38. Monitor film width expansion during swelling process (10-15% typical)
39. Control tension on film to prevent wrinkles or distortions
40. Use submerged guide rollers to maintain consistent film path

## Iodine Dyeing Process

41. Transfer swollen PVA film into iodine dyeing bath without air exposure
42. Control dyeing bath temperature precisely (25-35°C optimal range)
43. Maintain iodine concentration uniformity throughout dyeing bath
44. Monitor film residence time in dyeing bath (60-180 seconds)
45. Ensure uniform iodine absorption across entire film width
46. Control dyeing bath pH to optimize iodine complex formation
47. Use multi-stage dyeing process with gradually increasing concentrations
48. Monitor film color development from clear to dark blue-gray
49. Measure optical density in real-time using inline sensors
50. Adjust dyeing parameters based on continuous quality feedback

## First Stage Stretching Process

51. Transfer dyed film into first stretching bath containing boric acid
52. Prepare boric acid solution at 2-4% concentration
53. Control stretching bath temperature (40-50°C for initial stretch)
54. Begin mechanical stretching using precision roller speed differential
55. Apply longitudinal stretching force gradually to prevent film breakage
56. Monitor stretch ratio in real-time (target 2-3x in first stage)
57. Ensure uniform molecular orientation during stretching process
58. Control film tension distribution across width to prevent edge effects
59. Use multiple roller pairs with precisely controlled speed ratios
60. Monitor film thickness reduction during stretching process

## Intermediate Stretching Stages

61. Guide film through second stretching bath with adjusted temperature (50-60°C)
62. Increase stretch ratio progressively (cumulative 3-4x at this stage)
63. Maintain boric acid concentration to stabilize PVA structure
64. Add crosslinking agents (zinc chloride or other metal salts) to bath
65. Control crosslinking degree to enhance dimensional stability
66. Monitor dichroic properties development through inline polarimetry
67. Adjust stretching speed based on film mechanical properties
68. Prevent excessive thinning in central regions using edge holders
69. Control water content in film during stretching (critical for uniformity)
70. Use temperature gradients to optimize molecular orientation

## Final High-Ratio Stretching

71. Transfer film to final stretching zone with elevated temperature (60-70°C)
72. Apply maximum stretching force to achieve total 5-6x stretch ratio
73. Monitor film for signs of necking or tearing at critical stress points
74. Control stretching rate (typically 5-15%/second final rate)
75. Achieve maximum molecular chain alignment along stretch direction
76. Monitor iodine-polyiodide complex orientation in real-time
77. Verify dichroic ratio development (target >40 for high performance)
78. Control film width using tenter frame edge holders if required
79. Maintain uniform thickness across film width (target ±2μm variation)
80. Complete stretching process without introducing optical defects

## Dichroic Ratio Optimization

81. Measure polarization efficiency using crossed polarizer testing
82. Calculate dichroic ratio from parallel and perpendicular transmittance
83. Target dichroic ratio values >45 for premium Mac display applications
84. Verify absorption axis alignment parallel to stretching direction
85. Check for uniform dichroic properties across entire film width
86. Test polarization efficiency at multiple wavelengths (400-700nm)
87. Optimize iodine-PVA complex orientation through process adjustments
88. Measure degree of polarization (DOP) targeting >99.9%
89. Verify extinction coefficient in perpendicular direction
90. Document optical anisotropy characteristics for quality records

## Fixing and Stabilization Process

91. Guide stretched film through fixing bath containing boric acid
92. Control fixing bath temperature (50-60°C optimal)
93. Maintain higher boric acid concentration (4-6%) for crosslinking
94. Allow sufficient residence time (30-90 seconds) for stabilization
95. Form borate crosslinks between PVA molecular chains
96. Add potassium iodide to fixing bath to stabilize iodine complex
97. Control pH of fixing bath (maintain at 4-5 range)
98. Monitor film dimensional stability during fixing process
99. Prevent shrinkage or relaxation of stretched molecular orientation
100. Ensure uniform crosslinking density across film width

## Washing and Cleaning Process

101. Transfer film into multi-stage washing system with pure water
102. Remove excess boric acid and unreacted chemicals from film
103. Control washing water temperature (20-30°C)
104. Use counter-current washing flow for efficient cleaning
105. Monitor conductivity of washing water to verify removal of ions
106. Adjust washing time based on residual chemical concentration
107. Maintain film tension during washing to prevent dimensional changes
108. Use gentle water flow to avoid surface damage or contamination
109. Perform final rinse with ultrapure water (>18 MΩ·cm)
110. Prepare film surface for subsequent drying and lamination

## Drying Process

111. Guide washed film into precision drying chamber with controlled airflow
112. Apply gentle heating starting at 30°C and gradually increasing
113. Control drying temperature profile (maximum 80-100°C)
114. Monitor moisture content reduction using inline sensors
115. Maintain uniform air velocity across film width for even drying
116. Control relative humidity in drying chamber (target <30% RH)
117. Prevent over-drying which could cause brittleness or cracking
118. Target final moisture content of 2-4% in dried polarizer
119. Monitor film flatness and prevent curling during drying
120. Control drying time (typically 3-5 minutes depending on conditions)

## Thickness Measurement and Control

121. Install precision thickness gauges at drying chamber exit
122. Measure film thickness continuously across entire width
123. Target final polarizer thickness of 25-30μm after drying
124. Monitor thickness uniformity (specification ±1μm across width)
125. Adjust upstream process parameters if thickness deviations detected
126. Create thickness profile maps for quality documentation
127. Verify thickness stability over extended production runs
128. Calibrate thickness measurement systems using standard references
129. Monitor for edge effects or center thinning patterns
130. Implement feedback control for real-time thickness optimization

## Adhesive Layer Preparation

131. Prepare pressure-sensitive adhesive (PSA) solutions in clean mixers
132. Select acrylic-based adhesives with appropriate optical properties
133. Control adhesive viscosity through solvent content adjustment
134. Filter adhesive solution through fine mesh to remove particles
135. Test adhesive refractive index matching to polarizer (target n≈1.50)
136. Verify adhesive transmittance >92% in visible spectrum
137. Check adhesive thermal stability (must withstand 80°C continuous)
138. Test adhesive peel strength on TAC substrates
139. Verify adhesive does not degrade optical properties over time
140. Prepare adhesive coating systems with precision metering pumps

## TAC Protective Layer Adhesive Coating

141. Mount TAC film rolls on precision unwinding systems
142. Clean TAC film surfaces using ionized air or web cleaning systems
143. Apply adhesive layer to TAC film using precision coating methods
144. Use gravure coating, microgravure, or slot-die coating systems
145. Control adhesive layer thickness (target 15-25μm wet thickness)
146. Maintain coating uniformity across entire TAC film width
147. Control coating speed (typically 20-60 m/min)
148. Monitor adhesive flow properties during coating process
149. Prevent air bubble entrapment in adhesive layer
150. Verify coating weight using beta ray thickness gauges

## Adhesive Drying on TAC Films

151. Guide coated TAC film through drying ovens with controlled zones
152. Apply temperature gradient from 40°C to 80°C along oven length
153. Control solvent evaporation rate to prevent surface defects
154. Monitor residual solvent content using sensors or sampling
155. Prevent adhesive skinning or uneven drying across width
156. Maintain proper tension on TAC film during drying
157. Control air velocity to ensure uniform drying conditions
158. Target final adhesive thickness of 3-5μm after drying
159. Cool dried adhesive layer gradually before lamination
160. Verify adhesive tack properties before lamination step

## Polarizer-TAC Lamination Process

161. Align dried polarizer film with adhesive-coated TAC film
162. Control lamination roller pressure (typically 0.2-0.5 MPa)
163. Maintain precise temperature at lamination nip (40-60°C)
164. Ensure bubble-free contact between polarizer and TAC layers
165. Control lamination speed matching film transport rates
166. Use heated lamination rollers for optimal adhesive flow
167. Monitor alignment accuracy (target <0.5mm offset)
168. Apply second TAC protective layer on opposite polarizer side
169. Create symmetric sandwich structure: TAC-adhesive-polarizer-adhesive-TAC
170. Verify complete adhesion without delamination or voids

## Post-Lamination Processing

171. Guide laminated structure through post-heating zone for adhesive curing
172. Control curing temperature (60-80°C) and time (1-3 minutes)
173. Monitor adhesive bond strength development during curing
174. Allow laminated film to cool gradually to room temperature
175. Prevent thermal stress or dimensional changes during cooling
176. Check for any delamination or bubble formation after cooling
177. Verify total laminated film thickness (target 180-220μm)
178. Measure thickness uniformity of complete laminated structure
179. Inspect surface quality for scratches or contamination
180. Prepare laminated polarizer for final quality testing

## Optical Quality Testing

181. Measure single plate transmittance using spectrophotometers (target 42-44%)
182. Measure crossed polarizer transmittance (target <0.01% for high contrast)
183. Calculate polarization efficiency: PE = (T∥ - T⊥)/(T∥ + T⊥) × 100%
184. Target polarization efficiency >99.9% for Mac LCD applications
185. Measure transmittance across visible spectrum (400-700nm wavelength range)
186. Verify neutral color characteristics without color shift
187. Test haze levels in final laminated film (target <1%)
188. Measure specular transmittance and diffuse transmittance separately
189. Check for optical defects using polariscope inspection
190. Document chromaticity coordinates in CIE color space

## Durability and Environmental Testing

191. Perform accelerated aging tests at 80°C and 80% RH for 500 hours
192. Monitor polarization efficiency degradation over aging period
193. Test thermal cycling resistance from -40°C to 80°C (100 cycles)
194. Verify dimensional stability with less than 0.5% shrinkage
195. Measure adhesion strength between layers after environmental exposure
