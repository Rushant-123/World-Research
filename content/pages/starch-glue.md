## Raw Material Preparation

1. Receive corn starch in 50 lb bags or bulk pneumatic delivery systems
2. Inspect starch bags for moisture damage, tears, or contamination
3. Check batch certificates for starch specifications: 12% max moisture, 88% minimum starch content
4. Verify starch viscosity grade (typically 50-80 Brabender Units for corrugating adhesive)
5. Store starch in climate-controlled warehouse at 60-75°F and below 65% relative humidity
6. Rotate starch inventory using first-in-first-out (FIFO) system
7. Sample incoming starch lots for moisture content using Karl Fischer titration
8. Test starch pH which should be 5.0-7.0 for optimal adhesive performance
9. Measure starch particle size distribution using laser diffraction (15-25 micron average)
10. Check starch for foreign material using 100 mesh screen test

## Caustic Soda Solution Preparation

11. Receive sodium hydroxide as 50% liquid solution in totes or bulk tanks
12. Verify caustic concentration using acid-base titration (49-51% NaOH)
13. Check caustic solution for carbonate contamination which reduces effectiveness
14. Store liquid caustic in polyethylene or stainless steel tanks with proper venting
15. Install caustic metering pumps with chemical-resistant seals and wetted parts
16. Calibrate caustic flow meters to ±0.5% accuracy for precise gelatinization control
17. Prepare caustic dilution water using softened or deionized water to prevent scaling
18. Set up caustic dilution system to achieve 10-15% working concentration
19. Install pH monitoring probes in caustic lines with automatic data logging
20. Implement caustic safety systems including eyewash stations and neutralization tanks

## Borax Solution Preparation

21. Receive borax (sodium tetraborate) as decahydrate powder in 50 lb bags
22. Verify borax purity (99.5% minimum) and check for caking or moisture pickup
23. Dissolve borax in hot water (160-180°F) to create 20-25% solution
24. Heat borax solution using jacketed dissolving tanks with agitation
25. Filter borax solution through 50 micron bags to remove undissolved particles
26. Cool borax solution to 120-140°F before transferring to day tanks
27. Monitor borax solution concentration using refractometer or density measurement
28. Prepare fresh borax solution weekly as it can crystallize on standing
29. Install borax metering pumps for precise addition during adhesive cooking
30. Maintain borax solution temperature above 100°F to prevent crystallization

## Carrier Starch Preparation

41. Weigh carrier portion of starch (typically 20-25% of total starch) on calibrated scale
42. Transfer carrier starch to slurry tank with cold water
43. Mix carrier starch and water at ratio of 1:3 to 1:4 by weight
44. Agitate carrier slurry for 5-10 minutes to achieve complete wetting
45. Check carrier slurry for lumps using 20 mesh screen test
46. Measure carrier slurry temperature which should be 70-80°F
47. Add caustic solution to carrier slurry slowly with continuous agitation
48. Target caustic addition of 1.0-2.5% based on carrier starch weight
49. Mix caustic and carrier starch for 2-3 minutes ensuring uniform distribution
50. Monitor pH of caustic-starch mixture (pH 11-12 typical)

## Carrier Cooking Process

51. Transfer caustic-treated carrier starch to cooking vessel (jacketed kettle or jet cooker)

## Technical Specifications

**Typical Stein-Hall Formula Parameters:**
- Carrier starch: 20-25% of total starch
- Raw starch: 75-80% of total starch
- Caustic soda: 1.0-2.5% on carrier starch
- Borax: 0.5-2.0% on total starch
- Total solids: 20-25%
- Viscosity: 25-40 seconds (Zahn #5 cup at 100°F)
- pH: 9.5-11.0
- Gel temperature: 125-140°F
- Application rate: 30-50 gsm

**Bond Strength Requirements:**
- Pin adhesion: 50-80 lbs/in width (dry)
- Pin adhesion: 15-30 lbs/in width (wet)
- Peel strength: 100-200 g/inch width
- Bond coverage: >85% of flute tips
- ECT retention: >95% versus unglued components
