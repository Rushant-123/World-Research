Steel EMI (Electromagnetic Interference) shield cans are precision-stamped metal enclosures that protect sensitive electronic components from electromagnetic interference and radio frequency interference (RFI). These shields are critical components in smartphones, tablets, laptops, and other electronics where proper signal isolation prevents malfunctions. Manufacturing involves progressive die stamping of thin stainless steel followed by plating and rigorous testing to ensure 20-60 dB of shielding effectiveness across specific frequency ranges.

14. Create 3D CAD models of all die components and tooling

20. Surface grind all die components to maintain flatness within 0.01mm

23. Install die in stamping press (typically 25-100 ton capacity)

## Quality Specifications

**Material**: 301 or 304 stainless steel, thickness 0.10-0.30mm, hardness HRB 85-95

**Dimensional Tolerances**: ±0.05mm for critical features, ±0.10mm for non-critical dimensions

**Plating Thickness**: 2.5-7.5 μm tin or nickel, uniform coverage >95% of surface area

**Shielding Effectiveness**: Minimum 20-40 dB attenuation at 100 MHz to 6 GHz (specification varies by design)

**Spring Clip Force**: 50-200 grams depending on design, must retain lid during 5G vibration

**Contact Resistance**: <10 milliohms between lid and base at all contact points

**Solderability**: >95% wetting coverage at 260°C peak temperature per IPC-J-STD-002

**Surface Finish**: No scratches, dents, burrs, or plating defects visible at 10x magnification
