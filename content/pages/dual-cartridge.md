## Dual Cartridge Packaging Manufacturing Process

This page details the manufacturing of dual cartridge dispensing systems used for two-component adhesives, sealants, and coatings. The process covers side-by-side and coaxial cartridge formats with precise ratio control, static mixing technology, and dispensing force optimization.

### Raw Material Reception and Preparation (Steps 1-15)

1. Receive polypropylene resin pellets in 25 kg bags, verify manufacturer lot number and material certification documents for FDA compliance
2. Test polypropylene melt flow index at 230°C using capillary rheometer, confirm 8-12 g/10 min specification for barrel molding
3. Pre-dry polypropylene resin in desiccant hopper at 80°C for 4 hours to reduce moisture content below 0.02%
4. Receive high-density polyethylene pellets for piston manufacturing, verify density 0.95-0.96 g/cm³ for proper sealing
5. Test HDPE shore hardness using durometer, confirm Shore D 65-70 for effective piston seal performance
6. Pre-condition HDPE resin at 60°C for 3 hours to eliminate moisture and prevent molding defects
7. Receive polypropylene copolymer for static mixer elements, verify flexural modulus 1400-1600 MPa for mixing efficiency
8. Inspect raw material storage silos, verify nitrogen blanketing system maintains positive pressure to prevent moisture ingress
9. Sample each resin lot for ash content analysis, confirm less than 0.03% inorganic contamination
10. Test polypropylene chemical resistance with common adhesive components including epoxy, polyurethane, and acrylic formulations
11. Verify resin color consistency using spectrophotometer, maintain L*a*b* values within ±1.5 delta E for brand recognition
12. Check polyethylene lubricity by measuring coefficient of friction, confirm 0.15-0.20 for smooth dispensing action
13. Pre-blend color masterbatch at 2% concentration with natural polypropylene for corporate branding requirements
14. Gravimetrically dose resin additives including UV stabilizers at 0.3% and antioxidants at 0.2% for shelf life extension
15. Validate resin feedstock traceability system, scan QR codes linking raw materials to production batches

### Barrel Injection Molding - Side-by-Side Configuration (Steps 16-35)

16. Load dried polypropylene into 180-ton injection molding machine with hot runner system for 50 mL dual barrel production
17. Set barrel zone temperatures at 200°C, 215°C, 230°C, and 240°C from feed to nozzle for optimal melt viscosity
18. Heat mold cavity to 40°C using circulating water channels, control temperature ±2°C for dimensional stability
19. Configure injection parameters: 850 bar pressure, 2.5 second fill time, 15 second cooling cycle for 18-second total cycle
20. Inject molten polypropylene through hot runner gates at base of dual barrel cavity, filling both chambers simultaneously
21. Apply 600 bar packing pressure for 8 seconds to compensate for material shrinkage during solidification
22. Monitor cavity pressure sensors, verify pressure profile reaches 800 bar at switchover point from fill to pack phase
23. Cool barrel walls to 60°C through conduction to mold surface, achieve sufficient rigidity for ejection
24. Activate ejector pins at partition wall and rear surface, remove dual barrel with 3.2 mm wall thickness
25. Trim hot runner sprue using automated gate cutting system, recycle polypropylene runners into regrind hopper
26. Measure critical barrel dimensions: 27.5 mm diameter per chamber, 115 mm overall length, wall thickness 3.2±0.1 mm
27. Inspect partition wall integrity, verify no flash or thin spots that could compromise component separation
28. Check front flange diameter of 48 mm and thread profile M32x1.5 for static mixer attachment compatibility
29. Test barrel squareness by measuring parallelism between front flange and back surface, maintain within 0.15 mm
30. Verify barrel transparency for material level visibility, measure light transmission above 85% at 550 nm wavelength
31. Inspect barrel surface finish, confirm Ra < 0.8 μm for smooth piston travel and minimal dispensing friction
32. Apply barcode label to barrel exterior encoding volume capacity, ratio, and production date information
33. Nest molded barrels in anti-static trays with protective dividers, stack maximum 50 units for handling
34. Sample one barrel per 500 units for comprehensive dimensional inspection using CMM with 5 μm resolution
35. Store molded barrels in temperature-controlled warehouse at 20±3°C and 50±10% relative humidity for 24 hours

### Barrel Injection Molding - Coaxial Configuration (Steps 36-50)

36. Load two-shot injection molding machine for coaxial cartridge production with inner 25 mL and outer 25 mL chambers
37. Set first shot parameters for inner barrel: 190°C melt temperature, 750 bar injection pressure, 2.2 second fill time
38. Inject inner cylinder with 18 mm outer diameter and 1.8 mm wall thickness, forming central component chamber
39. Rotate mold core 180 degrees to position inner barrel for second shot overmolding operation
40. Set second shot parameters for outer barrel: 210°C melt temperature, 800 bar pressure to ensure proper bonding
41. Inject outer barrel forming annular chamber around inner barrel, create 32 mm outer diameter coaxial structure
42. Verify molecular bonding between inner and outer barrel layers, no delamination at interface under 50 N/mm peel force
43. Cool coaxial assembly to 55°C over 20-second cycle, longer than side-by-side due to increased thermal mass
44. Eject coaxial cartridge using core pins and sleeve ejectors to prevent damage to thin-walled inner barrel
45. Measure wall concentricity between inner and outer chambers, maintain radial variation below 0.2 mm for consistent ratios
46. Check coaxial barrel front flange, verify 10:1 ratio geometry with 10x larger outer annular dispensing area
47. Inspect rear piston interfaces, confirm stepped diameter design with 18 mm inner and 32 mm outer piston seats
48. Test coaxial barrel pressure resistance by applying 6 bar pneumatic pressure to each chamber independently
49. Verify chamber volume accuracy: inner barrel 25±0.5 mL, outer annular barrel 250±5 mL for 1:10 ratio dispensing
50. Package coaxial barrels separately from side-by-side format, label clearly with ratio designation and orientation markings

### Piston Manufacturing and Design (Steps 51-70)

51. Inject HDPE pistons using 50-ton molding machine with cavity pressure monitoring for consistent seal lip formation
52. Set HDPE processing temperature at 180°C barrel and 30°C mold temperature for optimal shore hardness in seal area
53. Mold piston body with 26.8 mm diameter for 0.35 mm interference fit in 27.5 mm barrel, ensuring effective sealing
54. Form triple-lip seal geometry on piston perimeter with 0.8 mm lip height and 45-degree contact angle
55. Include central push rod receptacle in piston design with 6 mm diameter socket for dispensing gun plunger engagement
56. Add venting channels on piston rear surface, allow air escape during filling while preventing material backflow
57. Texture piston seal lips with 20 μm micro-grooves to retain thin lubricant film and reduce break-loose force
58. Measure piston diameter using laser micrometer at 8 circumferential points, confirm 26.80±0.03 mm for proper sealing
59. Test piston seal effectiveness by pressurizing barrel to 4 bar with water, verify zero leakage past piston over 24 hours
60. Measure piston break-loose force by installing in barrel and pulling with force gauge, confirm 8-15 N initial force
61. Verify piston sliding force remains below 6 N throughout barrel travel for comfortable manual dispensing
62. Inspect piston seal lips for flash, surface defects, or geometry irregularities using automated vision system
63. Test piston chemical compatibility with common adhesive systems including epoxy hardeners and isocyanates
64. Measure piston compression set by applying 25% deflection at 70°C for 168 hours, verify recovery above 90%
65. Verify piston color consistency, maintain natural HDPE white appearance for material level visibility
66. Apply release agent to piston seal lips using micro-dosing system, deposit 2 mg silicone-free lubricant per piston
67. Package pistons in sealed bags with desiccant, prevent moisture absorption that could affect seal performance
68. Test piston sealing performance after thermal cycling from -20°C to +60°C for 5 cycles, verify maintained seal integrity
69. Measure piston weight consistency at 3.2±0.1 grams to ensure proper material distribution in molding process
70. Conduct piston shelf life testing over 36 months at 23°C, monitor hardness drift and seal compression force changes

### Static Mixer Nozzle Manufacturing (Steps 71-90)

71. Injection mold static mixer housing from polypropylene copolymer using 80-ton press with precision core pins
72. Set molding parameters: 220°C melt temperature, 900 bar injection pressure, 35°C mold temperature for tight tolerances
73. Form mixer housing with M32x1.5 male thread for barrel attachment and tapered dispensing tip with 3 mm orifice
74. Mold mixer body with 16 mm internal diameter and 75 mm active mixing length for effective component blending
75. Inject helical mixing elements using multi-cavity tool, create right-hand and left-hand 180-degree helical baffles
76. Mold mixing elements with 15.5 mm diameter for 0.25 mm clearance in housing, prevent bypass flow around elements
77. Create 12 mixing elements per standard mixer, alternating right and left helix orientation for chaotic flow pattern
78. Form each element with 0.8 mm web thickness, balance mixing efficiency against dispensing pressure drop
79. Design element pitch at 24 mm (1.5x diameter ratio) for optimal mixing within 300 mm dispense length
80. Stack mixing elements in housing, verify alternating orientation creates proper flow splitting at each element interface
81. Ultrasonically weld end cap to mixer housing, seal elements inside with 35 kHz frequency and 2.5 second weld time
82. Test weld strength by applying 150 N axial pull force, verify end cap remains attached without deformation
83. Measure mixer pressure drop by flowing glycerin at 100 mL/min, confirm pressure drop of 2.5-3.5 bar for design
84. Verify mixing efficiency by dispensing colored dye with clear resin, achieve 95% homogeneity after 12 mixing elements
85. Test mixer performance with actual adhesive components, confirm less than 5% variation in mixed material properties
86. Inspect mixing elements for flash or incomplete filling that could reduce mixing efficiency or increase pressure drop
87. Verify mixer housing thread engagement, test assembly torque on barrel at 15 N⋅m without thread stripping
88. Apply tamper-evident seal to mixer-barrel connection, indicate if cartridge has been previously opened or used
89. Measure dispensed bead uniformity using inline thickness sensor, verify ±3% variation in cross-sectional area
90. Package static mixers individually in sealed pouches, include instructions for proper attachment and first-shot waste

### Cartridge Filling Equipment Setup (Steps 91-110)

91. Position automated filling line with dual material reservoir tanks, temperature control at 23±2°C for viscosity consistency
92. Install cartridge holder fixtures that secure side-by-side barrels in precise vertical orientation during filling
93. Connect filling nozzles to each barrel chamber through pneumatic descent mechanism with force sensing
94. Prime filling system by dispensing material into waste container until bubble-free flow achieved from both nozzles
95. Calibrate volumetric piston pumps for each component, set dispense volume to 48 mL per 50 mL barrel (96% fill)
96. Program filling sequence: descend nozzles to barrel bottom, dispense at 40 mL/min, retract slowly to prevent dripping
97. Configure vacuum chamber surrounding barrels, reduce pressure to 50 mbar during filling to eliminate entrapped air
98. Set up vision system to detect piston presence in barrel and verify correct orientation before filling operation
99. Install inline viscosity sensors on material supply lines, monitor component A at 8,000 cP and component B at 12,000 cP
100. Program PLC with filling parameters including dispense volume, flow rate, vacuum level, and nozzle positioning
101. Connect material conditioning system maintaining epoxy resin at 40°C and hardener at 23°C for optimal flow properties
102. Install precision scales under filling station, weigh cartridges before and after filling with 0.1 gram resolution
103. Set up nitrogen blanketing on moisture-sensitive materials in reservoir tanks, maintain positive pressure of 0.2 bar
104. Configure automatic nozzle cleaning cycle using solvent spray and compressed air purge between fill operations
105. Install drip collection trays under filling nozzles, prevent cross-contamination between component A and B materials
106. Verify filling pump accuracy by dispensing into graduated cylinders, confirm ±0.5 mL deviation over 48 mL target volume
107. Test filling system with water to verify simultaneous dispense, check flow rate matching within 5% between chambers
108. Calibrate vacuum sensor and pump controller, verify rapid evacuation to 50 mbar within 8 seconds of cycle start
109. Program material pre-circulation to eliminate settling in supply lines, activate 2 minutes before production start
110. Validate filling system throughput capacity of 450 cartridges per hour with current configuration and parameters

### Cartridge Filling Operation (Steps 111-130)

111. Load empty dual barrels into filling station indexing conveyor, machine vision confirms proper orientation
112. Automatic system inserts pistons into rear of both barrel chambers, positions flush with barrel rim
113. Advance cartridge into vacuum chamber, engage sealing gasket around barrel rim for effective air evacuation
114. Activate vacuum pump, reduce chamber pressure from atmosphere to 50 mbar over 8 seconds
115. Descend dual filling nozzles through piston vent holes, position tips 5 mm from barrel bottom
116. Initiate synchronized filling of both chambers at 40 mL/min flow rate, maintain vacuum throughout dispense
117. Monitor filling by weight measurement, component A target 46.2 grams at 0.96 g/mL density for 48 mL volume
118. Monitor component B filling weight at 49.9 grams at 1.04 g/mL density, achieving matched 48 mL volume
119. Slowly retract filling nozzles as material level rises, maintain tip 5-10 mm below surface to minimize bubble entrainment
120. Complete filling cycle in 72 seconds per cartridge, achieve 96% fill level leaving 2 mL air space
121. Purge residual material from nozzle tips using 3 bar compressed air pulse after retraction from barrel
122. Release vacuum gradually over 3 seconds to prevent material foaming or piston displacement from rapid pressure change
123. Weigh filled cartridge on integral scale, compare to target weight of 96.1±1.0 grams total fill weight
124. Reject cartridges outside weight specification to separate accumulation station for manual inspection and correction
125. Advance accepted cartridges to piston insertion station where pneumatic rams press pistons into contact with material
126. Apply 25 N downward force on both pistons simultaneously, compress air space to less than 1 mm height
127. Verify piston seating by measuring exposed piston height above barrel rim, confirm 3±0.5 mm uniform protrusion
128. Install protective caps over piston tops, snap-fit design shields from contamination during storage and shipment
129. Apply front closure caps to barrel dispensing outlets, threaded design with O-ring seal prevents material leakage
130. Transfer filled and capped cartridges to packaging line conveyor, maintain upright orientation to prevent air migration

### Ratio Verification and Quality Control (Steps 131-145)

131. Sample one filled cartridge every 30 minutes, perform gravimetric ratio verification by weighing each chamber independently
132. Carefully remove piston from component A chamber, drain material into pre-weighed container on analytical balance
133. Weigh drained component A material, calculate actual volume using measured density, verify 48±0.5 mL dispensed
134. Repeat draining process for component B chamber, weigh material and calculate volume confirmation at 48±0.5 mL
135. Calculate volumetric ratio between chambers A and B, confirm 1:1 ratio within ±2% for side-by-side cartridge
136. Test coaxial 10:1 ratio cartridges by draining 250 mL outer chamber and 25 mL inner chamber independently
137. Verify coaxial ratio accuracy of 10:1±3% by gravimetric analysis, critical for proper adhesive curing stoichiometry
138. Install test cartridge in dispensing gun, manually dispense 5 gram sample through static mixer into collection cup
139. Analyze mixed sample using FTIR spectroscopy, verify component peaks at correct intensity ratios indicating proper mixing
140. Cure mixed sample according to adhesive technical data sheet, measure cured hardness with shore D durometer
141. Compare cured properties to specification: shore D 75-85 for epoxy indicating stoichiometric component ratio
142. Perform visual color check on dispensed mixed material, verify consistent color with no streaking or swirls
143. Test dispensing force by installing cartridge in manual gun, measure trigger force required for steady material flow
144. Confirm dispensing force below 60 N for comfortable manual operation throughout cartridge discharge cycle
145. Document all quality control measurements in batch record, release lot only when all parameters meet specifications

164. Repeat pressure hold test on component B chamber independently, confirm seal integrity on both sides
165. Cycle pistons through full travel distance of 115 mm using automated dispensing gun, repeat 50 complete strokes
166. Measure dispensing force throughout travel, confirm break-loose force 8-15 N and sliding force maintains below 6 N
167. Inspect piston seal lips after cycling test, verify no wear, cutting, or deformation of sealing geometry
168. Apply simulated storage conditions by holding pressurized cartridge at 40°C for 168 hours, test seal performance
169. Verify maintained seal integrity after thermal conditioning with pressure hold test showing less than 0.2 bar decay
170. Test piston seals with actual aggressive adhesive components including amine hardeners and organic solvents
171. Store chemical compatibility test samples at 23°C for 90 days, periodically inspect for seal swelling or degradation
172. Measure piston diameter change after chemical exposure, confirm dimensional stability within ±0.1 mm tolerance

175. Validate piston material selection by comparing HDPE performance to alternative materials including LDPE and TPE
