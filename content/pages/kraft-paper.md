## Manufacturing Process

### Raw Material Preparation (Steps 1-15)

1. **Receive Wood Chips**: Accept softwood chips (pine, spruce, fir) with 3-5 cm length, moisture content 40-50%
2. **Screen Chips**: Pass through vibrating screens with 8mm and 45mm openings to remove oversized/undersized material
3. **Remove Contaminants**: Use magnetic separators and density cleaners to eliminate metal, sand, and bark pieces
4. **Measure Chip Size**: Sample chips every 2 hours, verify 80% fall within 10-35mm length specification
5. **Store Chips**: Maintain chip pile at 5,000-15,000 bone dry tons with 7-14 day inventory turnover
6. **Meter Chip Flow**: Feed chips via screw conveyors at controlled rate of 180-220 tons/day (bone dry basis)
7. **Pre-Steam Chips**: Heat chips to 100-120°C in pre-steaming vessel for 10-15 minutes to remove air
8. **Prepare White Liquor**: Mix sodium hydroxide (NaOH) at 80 g/L and sodium sulfide (Na2S) at 25 g/L
9. **Measure Effective Alkali**: Verify white liquor has 100-120 g/L effective alkali as Na2O equivalent
10. **Check Sulfidity**: Confirm Na2S/(NaOH+Na2S) ratio is 25-30% for optimal delignification
11. **Heat White Liquor**: Preheat cooking liquor to 140-150°C using steam heat exchangers
12. **Calculate Liquor Charge**: Apply liquor-to-wood ratio of 3.5-4.5:1 based on chip bulk density
13. **Meter Chemical Charge**: Add 18-22% effective alkali on bone dry wood weight for softwood pulping
14. **Monitor pH**: Verify white liquor pH is 13.5-14.0 before charging to digester
15. **Pre-Impregnate Chips**: Soak chips in dilute white liquor for 20-30 minutes before main cook

### Kraft Pulping Process (Steps 16-45)

16. **Load Continuous Digester**: Feed chips and liquor into top of vertical digester vessel (capacity 1,500-2,500 m³)
17. **Establish Co-Current Flow**: Introduce liquor and chips together flowing downward in impregnation zone
18. **Heat to Cooking Temperature**: Raise temperature gradually to 170°C over 60-90 minutes using indirect steam
19. **Maintain H-Factor**: Target H-factor of 800-1200 (integrated time-temperature relationship) for softwood
20. **Monitor Temperature Profile**: Maintain 165-175°C in cooking zone with ±3°C control precision
21. **Control Residence Time**: Keep chips in digester for 90-150 minutes total cooking time
22. **Apply Counter-Current Washing**: Extract black liquor from lower sections while fresh liquor enters top
23. **Measure Kappa Number**: Target residual lignin content of 28-32 kappa number for unbleached kraft
24. **Discharge Cooked Pulp**: Blow pulp from digester bottom at 1.5-2.0% consistency into blow tank
25. **Cool Blow Steam**: Condense flash steam in foul condensate system, recover thermal energy
26. **Dilute Pulp**: Add white water to reduce consistency to 1.2-1.5% for pumping and washing
27. **Screen Cooked Pulp**: Pass through pressure screens with 0.15-0.20mm slots to remove knots and uncooked chips
28. **Collect Rejects**: Send screenings (2-4% of throughput) to reject refiner or back to digester
29. **Wash Pulp Stage 1**: Use rotary drum washer to remove 60-70% of residual black liquor
30. **Measure Wash Efficiency**: Verify dilution factor of 10-15:1 using conductivity measurement
31. **Wash Pulp Stage 2**: Apply second drum washer with fresh water counter-current to pulp flow
32. **Wash Pulp Stage 3**: Use third washing stage to achieve final pulp cleanliness of 0.5-1.0 kg dissolved solids/ton
33. **Thicken Pulp**: Concentrate washed pulp to 10-12% consistency on vacuum drum or belt thickener
34. **Collect Black Liquor**: Gather weak black liquor at 15-18% dissolved solids from washing stages
35. **Evaporate Black Liquor**: Concentrate to 65-80% solids using multi-effect evaporators (4-7 effects)
36. **Oxidize Black Liquor**: Blow air through concentrated liquor to improve combustion characteristics
37. **Spray Recovery Boiler**: Atomize heavy black liquor into furnace at 1,050-1,100°C combustion temperature
38. **Recover Inorganic Chemicals**: Collect molten smelt (Na2CO3 and Na2S) at furnace bottom
39. **Dissolve Smelt**: Mix with weak white liquor to form green liquor with 120-150 g/L total titratable alkali
40. **Clarify Green Liquor**: Remove dregs (insoluble impurities) using settling tank or pressure filters
41. **Causticize Green Liquor**: React Na2CO3 with Ca(OH)2 to regenerate NaOH: Na2CO3 + Ca(OH)2 → 2NaOH + CaCO3
42. **Separate Lime Mud**: Filter CaCO3 precipitate from white liquor using rotary drum filters
43. **Recalcine Lime**: Burn CaCO3 at 900-1,000°C in rotary kiln to regenerate CaO for causticizing
44. **Close Chemical Loop**: Return regenerated white liquor to chip cooking, achieving 95-98% chemical recovery
45. **Monitor Cooking Yield**: Verify pulp yield of 45-50% based on oven-dry wood weight for softwood kraft

### Bleaching Process (Optional, Steps 46-60)

46. **Dilute to Medium Consistency**: Adjust pulp to 10-12% consistency for bleaching stages
47. **Oxygen Delignification**: React pulp with O2 at 90-105°C, 500-600 kPa for 45-60 minutes
48. **Reduce Kappa Number**: Lower residual lignin from 30 to 12-15 kappa using oxygen stage
49. **Wash After O2 Stage**: Remove dissolved lignin on drum washer, measure 50-60% lignin removal
50. **Apply Chlorine Dioxide**: Add ClO2 at 0.8-1.2% on pulp in D0 stage at 60-70°C for 30-45 minutes
51. **Extract Alkaline**: Treat with 1.5-2.0% NaOH at 70°C for 60 minutes (E stage) to dissolve oxidized lignin
52. **Wash After Extraction**: Remove dissolved material to prevent redeposition on pulp fibers
53. **Apply Second ClO2 Stage**: Use 0.3-0.5% ClO2 in D1 stage at 70-75°C for 2-3 hours
54. **Final Extraction**: Apply dilute alkali (0.5-1.0% NaOH) at 70°C for 45 minutes in E stage
55. **Apply Final ClO2**: Use 0.2-0.3% ClO2 in D2 stage at 70-75°C to achieve target brightness
56. **Measure Brightness**: Verify ISO brightness of 88-92% (unbleached brown kraft targets 30-40% brightness)
57. **Wash Final Stage**: Remove all residual chemicals to achieve < 10 ppm ClO2 in final pulp
58. **Check Pulp Viscosity**: Measure cellulose chain length, target 900-1,100 mL/g for strength properties
59. **Test Cleanliness**: Count dirt specks < 10 per m² for premium grades using optical scanner
60. **Thicken Bleached Pulp**: Concentrate to 10-12% consistency for stock preparation

### Stock Preparation (Steps 61-80)

61. **Dilute to Low Consistency**: Reduce pulp to 3-4% consistency in pulper chest with agitation
62. **Refine Pulp**: Pass through disk or conical refiner at 40-80 kWh/ton to develop fiber bonding
63. **Measure Freeness**: Target Canadian Standard Freeness of 350-450 mL for kraft paper grades
64. **Control Refining Energy**: Adjust refining intensity based on desired tear/tensile strength balance
65. **Add Internal Sizing**: Blend 0.1-0.3% alkyl ketene dimer (AKD) or rosin size for water resistance
66. **Add Wet Strength Resin**: Mix 0.5-1.5% polyamide-epichlorohydrin resin if wet strength grade required
67. **Add Dry Strength Additives**: Include 0.3-0.8% cationic starch to improve internal bond strength
68. **Add Retention Aid**: Blend 0.01-0.03% polyacrylamide to retain fines and additives on wire
69. **Adjust pH**: Control stock pH to 7.0-8.5 using alum or caustic for optimal additive performance
70. **Clean Stock**: Pass through pressure screens with 0.15mm slots at 15-20 m/s approach velocity
71. **Remove Contraries**: Use centrifugal cleaners to eliminate high-density particles (sand, metal)
72. **Deaerate Stock**: Apply vacuum deaerator to reduce air content to < 3% to prevent foam and spots
73. **Blend Furnish**: Mix virgin kraft pulp with 0-30% recycled fiber depending on grade requirements
74. **Store in Machine Chest**: Maintain 30-60 minute inventory at consistent consistency and additives
75. **Dilute to Headbox Consistency**: Reduce to 0.3-0.8% consistency using white water dilution
76. **Control Stock Temperature**: Maintain 40-50°C for optimal drainage and formation on wire
77. **Add Formation Aid**: Include 10-50 ppm microparticle or polymer to improve fiber dispersion
78. **Final Screening**: Pass through fine screens with 0.10-0.15mm slots immediately before headbox
79. **Meter Flow Rate**: Control pulp flow to match paper machine speed and basis weight target
80. **Monitor Consistency**: Verify headbox consistency within ±0.02% using online optical sensors

### Sheet Formation (Steps 81-100)

81. **Distribute in Headbox**: Spread stock uniformly across machine width (4-10 meters) using tapered header
82. **Control Slice Opening**: Adjust headbox lip opening to 8-15mm for target jet-to-wire speed ratio
83. **Jet Stock onto Wire**: Form continuous jet at 800-1,200 m/min machine speed for kraft paper
84. **Form on Fourdrinier**: Deposit fibers on moving plastic wire mesh with 60-80 openings per cm
85. **Dewater by Gravity**: Allow 70-80% of water to drain through wire in first 2-3 meters of table
86. **Apply Table Rolls**: Use rotating bars to support wire and enhance drainage in forming zone
87. **Apply Vacuum Boxes**: Draw water through wire using 20-40 kPa vacuum in flat box sections
88. **Control Formation**: Adjust slice opening profile and jet angle to achieve uniform basis weight (±2%)
89. **Apply Dandy Roll**: Use light cylindrical roll with wire cover to improve formation and surface
90. **Measure Dry Weight**: Monitor basis weight continuously using nuclear gauge, target 60-150 g/m²
91. **Detect Wet Streaks**: Scan sheet with infrared sensors to identify drainage variations
92. **Trim Sheet Edges**: Cut 5-10 cm from each side to remove irregular edge material
93. **Transfer to Press Section**: Lift sheet from wire at 18-22% dryness (solids content)
94. **Support Wet Web**: Carry on felt fabric to prevent tearing during pressing operations
95. **Recirculate White Water**: Collect drainage water for reuse in stock dilution and cleaning
96. **Clean Wire**: Spray forming wire with high-pressure water jets to prevent fiber buildup
97. **Oscillate Wire**: Apply side-to-side shake motion at 100-200 cycles/min to improve formation
98. **Monitor Wire Wear**: Inspect mesh for damage every 8-12 hours, replace after 2-6 weeks service
99. **Control Wire Tension**: Maintain 30-50 kN total tension across width for flat, stable running
100. **Measure Two-Sidedness**: Verify minimal formation difference between wire and top side using optical scanner

### Press Section (Steps 101-115)

101. **Enter First Press Nip**: Pass sheet between two rolls at 2-4 MPa linear pressure
102. **Apply Felt Support**: Sandwich paper between wet felts to absorb expressed water
103. **Control Nip Pressure**: Adjust hydraulic loading to achieve 40-45% dryness after first press
104. **Remove Water to Felts**: Transfer 8,000-12,000 kg water/hour to press felts in first nip
105. **Transfer to Second Press**: Carry sheet on felt to second nip with press roll configuration
106. **Apply Reverse Press**: Use grooved roll against smooth roll to enhance water removal
107. **Increase Dryness**: Achieve 48-52% solids content after final press nip for kraft paper
108. **Monitor Press Loading**: Verify total nip load of 400-800 kN/m of machine width
109. **Control Felt Moisture**: Suction water from felts using vacuum rolls between press nips
110. **Shower Press Felts**: Clean felts continuously with oscillating high-pressure water sprays
111. **Condition Felt Surface**: Apply felt conditioner chemicals to prevent pitch and stickies buildup
112. **Replace Press Felts**: Change felts every 2-6 weeks depending on furnish and operating conditions
113. **Maintain Roll Crowning**: Use controlled deflection rolls to compensate for roll bending and ensure uniform nip
114. **Measure Sheet Caliper**: Monitor thickness continuously with laser or isotope gauges, target 80-200 micrometers
115. **Transfer to Dryer Section**: Release sheet from felt onto dryer fabric at 48-52% consistency

### Drying Section (Steps 116-140)

116. **Contact First Dryer**: Wrap sheet around steam-heated cylinder at 80-90°C surface temperature
117. **Heat by Conduction**: Transfer thermal energy from 400-600 kPa steam inside dryer cylinder
118. **Support on Dryer Fabric**: Carry sheet on permeable fabric that holds paper against hot cylinder
119. **Evaporate Water**: Remove moisture at rate decreasing from 80 kg/hour per dryer initially
120. **Progress Through Dryer Groups**: Pass over 30-60 dryer cylinders arranged in serpentine path
121. **Increase Steam Pressure**: Raise pressure progressively from 200 kPa in early dryers to 600 kPa in final groups
122. **Apply Pocket Ventilation**: Exhaust humid air from spaces between cylinders using hood system
123. **Monitor Dryer Temperature**: Control each cylinder surface at 85-160°C depending on position
124. **Prevent Sheet Fluttering**: Maintain fabric tension and use stabilizer rolls to keep sheet contact
125. **Apply Single-Tier Drying**: Use configuration where sheet alternates between top and bottom of dryers
126. **Control Moisture Profile**: Adjust steam pressure across machine width to achieve uniform dryness
127. **Measure Moisture Content**: Monitor continuously with infrared or microwave sensors, target 6-8% final moisture
128. **Manage Drying Rate**: Control to prevent overdrying (< 4% moisture causes brittleness) or underdrying
129. **Recover Condensate**: Return condensed water from dryer cylinders to steam plant for efficiency
130. **Clean Dryer Surfaces**: Apply doctor blades to remove accumulated deposits from cylinder surfaces
131. **Condition Dryer Fabrics**: Wash fabrics continuously with shower sprays and vacuum extraction
132. **Replace Dryer Fabrics**: Change fabrics every 4-12 weeks based on wear and contamination
133. **Monitor Air Emissions**: Capture and treat volatile organic compounds from drying process
134. **Control Drying Shrinkage**: Allow 2-4% machine direction shrinkage to develop bulk and stiffness
135. **Measure Curl Tendency**: Monitor sheet flatness, adjust moisture and temperature profiles to minimize curl
136. **Apply Size Press**: Optionally apply surface starch coating (2-8 g/m²) at mid-dryer for surface strength
137. **Redry After Size Press**: Remove coating water in remaining dryer sections to final moisture target
138. **Cool Sheet**: Allow sheet temperature to decrease to 40-60°C before calendering
139. **Detect Dryer Defects**: Monitor for wrinkles, holes, and edge cracks using camera systems
140. **Calculate Drying Efficiency**: Verify steam consumption of 1.5-2.2 kg steam per kg water evaporated

### Calendering and Surface Treatment (Steps 141-155)

141. **Enter Calender Stack**: Pass sheet through nip between multiple steel rolls in vertical stack
142. **Apply Calendering Pressure**: Use 100-300 kN/m linear load to compress and smooth sheet surface
143. **Control Caliper Reduction**: Reduce thickness by 5-15% to achieve target bulk and smoothness
144. **Heat Calender Rolls**: Maintain roll surface at 60-80°C to enhance smoothing effect
145. **Monitor Smoothness**: Measure using air leak method (Sheffield units), target 150-300 for kraft paper
146. **Control Gloss Development**: Adjust nip pressure and temperature to achieve matte to semi-gloss surface
147. **Minimize Two-Sidedness**: Use soft-nip or shoe calender if uniform two-sided properties required
148. **Measure Caliper Profile**: Verify thickness uniformity across width using scanning gauge (±3% variation)
149. **Adjust Crown Control**: Modify roll bending to compensate for deflection and thermal expansion
150. **Monitor Roll Surface**: Inspect for scoring, roughness, or deposits that transfer to paper surface
151. **Regrind Calender Rolls**: Refinish roll surfaces every 3-6 months to maintain smoothness
152. **Control Sheet Tension**: Maintain 150-300 N/m tension through calender to prevent wrinkles
153. **Measure Density**: Verify paper density of 0.7-0.9 g/cm³ depending on grade and calendering intensity
154. **Check Porosity**: Test air permeability using Gurley seconds method, typical range 10-40 seconds
155. **Trim Final Width**: Cut edges to final width specification using rotary slitter knives

### Winding and Finishing (Steps 156-170)

156. **Transfer to Reel**: Guide sheet onto reel drum for winding into parent roll
157. **Control Reel Tension**: Apply 200-400 N/m winding tension using center-driven or surface-driven system
158. **Build Parent Roll**: Wind continuously until roll diameter reaches 2.5-3.5 meters
159. **Monitor Roll Hardness**: Control nip pressure and tension to achieve uniform roll density
160. **Detect Winding Defects**: Identify wrinkles, telescoping, or starring using ultrasonic or visual inspection
161. **Measure Roll Weight**: Weigh completed parent roll, typical weight 15-30 tons
162. **Splice New Roll**: Join end of sheet to new core using adhesive tape to start next parent roll
163. **Remove Parent Roll**: Transport using overhead crane or roll handling equipment to unwind stand
164. **Condition Parent Roll**: Allow to equilibrate at 23°C, 50% RH for 12-24 hours before slitting
165. **Slit to Customer Width**: Cut parent roll into smaller rolls of 0.5-2.0 meters width
166. **Control Slitting Quality**: Use sharp blades and proper tension to achieve clean, dust-free edges
167. **Wind Finished Rolls**: Create rolls of specified diameter (0.8-1.5 meters) and core size (76-152mm ID)
168. **Label Rolls**: Apply tags identifying grade, basis weight, width, length, roll number, and manufacturing date
169. **Wrap for Protection**: Cover rolls with kraft paper or plastic film to prevent moisture pickup and damage
170. **Palletize or Stack**: Arrange rolls on pallets or standing on end for storage and shipment

### Quality Testing - Physical Properties (Steps 171-185)

171. **Measure Basis Weight**: Cut 100 cm² samples, weigh to ±0.01g, calculate g/m² (target ±3% of specification)
172. **Test Caliper**: Measure thickness using micrometer under 50 kPa pressure, typical range 80-200 micrometers
173. **Calculate Apparent Density**: Divide basis weight by caliper, verify 0.7-0.9 g/cm³ for kraft paper
174. **Test Tensile Strength**: Pull 15mm wide strip at 10 mm/min, measure breaking force (3-10 kN/m typical)
175. **Measure Elongation**: Record extension at break, verify 1.5-4.0% for machine direction kraft paper
176. **Test Burst Strength**: Apply hydraulic pressure through rubber diaphragm, measure kPa to rupture
177. **Verify Burst Index**: Calculate burst strength / basis weight, target 3.0-6.0 kPa·m²/g for kraft paper
178. **Measure Tear Strength**: Test using Elmendorf pendulum method, record mN·m²/g (tear index)
179. **Verify Machine Direction Tear**: Confirm 8-15 mN·m²/g tear index in machine direction
180. **Test Cross Direction Tear**: Measure 10-18 mN·m²/g tear index perpendicular to machine direction
181. **Measure Folding Endurance**: Count double folds until failure under 1 kg tension (MIT fold test)
182. **Test Stiffness**: Measure bending resistance using 15° deflection method in both directions
183. **Check Porosity**: Time required for 100 mL air to pass through sample (Gurley test), 10-40 seconds typical
184. **Measure Smoothness**: Use Sheffield air leak test, verify 150-300 Sheffield units for kraft paper
185. **Test Friction**: Measure coefficient of friction paper-to-paper and paper-to-metal (0.3-0.6 typical)

### Quality Testing - Surface and Moisture (Steps 186-195)

186. **Measure Moisture Content**: Dry sample at 105°C to constant weight, verify 6-8% moisture content
187. **Test Water Resistance**: Apply water droplet, measure contact angle or absorption time if sized
188. **Measure Surface pH**: Extract with cold water, test pH 6.5-8.5 for neutral kraft paper
189. **Check Cleanliness**: Count visible dirt specks per m² using transmitted or reflected light (< 50 per m²)
190. **Test Brightness**: Measure ISO brightness using 457nm light, unbleached kraft typically 30-40%
191. **Measure Opacity**: Test light transmission at 550nm, verify > 95% opacity for most kraft grades
192. **Check Color Uniformity**: Measure L*a*b* values across roll width, verify ΔE < 2.0 within roll
193. **Test Curl**: Measure upward or downward curl tendency on 70mm x 200mm samples at 50% RH
194. **Verify Dimensional Stability**: Measure hygroexpansion coefficient, kraft paper shows 0.2-0.4% per 10% RH change
195. **Package and Ship**: Bundle rolls according to customer specifications, load onto trucks or containers for delivery

## Technical Specifications

**Basis Weight Range**: 60-150 g/m² (standard kraft paper grades)
**Caliper**: 80-200 micrometers depending on basis weight and calendering
**Density**: 0.7-0.9 g/cm³
**Burst Strength**: 200-700 kPa (basis weight dependent)
**Burst Index**: 3.0-6.0 kPa·m²/g
**Tensile Strength MD**: 4-10 kN/m
**Tear Index MD**: 8-15 mN·m²/g
**Tear Index CD**: 10-18 mN·m²/g
**Moisture Content**: 6-8% at 23°C, 50% RH
**ISO Brightness**: 30-40% (unbleached), 88-92% (bleached)
**Porosity**: 10-40 Gurley seconds
**Smoothness**: 150-300 Sheffield units
