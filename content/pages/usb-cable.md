# How to Make USB-C Cable

**Top Market Leaders:**
- [Luxshare](/companies/luxshare)
- [Foxconn](/companies/foxconn)
- [Amphenol](/companies/amphenol)



Universal Serial Bus Type-C cable for data transfer and power delivery. Reversible 24-pin connector supports USB 2.0 (480 Mbps), USB 3.2 Gen 2 (10 Gbps), USB4 (40 Gbps), Thunderbolt 3/4 (40 Gbps), and USB Power Delivery (USB-PD) up to 240W. Modern Macs use USB-C exclusively for charging, data, video (DisplayPort Alt Mode). Cable types: USB 2.0 only (4 wires), USB 3.2 (12+ wires), Full-Featured (24 wires, all protocols). Construction: precision twisted pairs, multi-layer shielding, embedded E-Marker chip (identifies cable capabilities), overmolded connectors. Global production: 3+ billion USB-C cables/year.

**Step 1-15: [Copper Wire](copper-wire) Multi-Gauge Conductor Production**
USB-C cables require multiple conductor gauges for different functions. **Power wires (VBUS/GND):** 28-20 AWG depending on power rating (28 AWG = 3A/60W, 24 AWG = 5A/100W, 20 AWG = 5A/240W with EPR). Start with OFC copper rod, draw to diameter: 28 AWG = 0.321mm, 24 AWG = 0.511mm, 20 AWG = 0.812mm. Strand configurations: 28 AWG uses 7/36 (7 strands of 36 AWG), 24 AWG uses 10/34 or 16/38, 20 AWG uses 26/36. **High-speed data pairs (SuperSpeed):** 32 AWG (0.202mm) for TX/RX differential pairs, typically 7/40 stranding (flexibility). Four pairs required: TX1+/TX1–, TX2+/TX2–, RX1+/RX1–, RX2+/RX2– (USB 3.2 Gen 2, 10 Gbps capable). Tight impedance control 90Ω±10% differential. **Low-speed data (USB 2.0):** 28 AWG for D+/D– pair, 7/36 stranding. **Configuration Channel (CC):** 28-30 AWG for CC1/CC2 wires (cable orientation detection, USB-PD communication). **Sideband Use (SBU):** 30-32 AWG for SBU1/SBU2 (DisplayPort AUX channel in Alt Mode). Wire drawing process: anneal at 500°C, draw through carbide dies with soap lubricant, intermediate anneal every 5 passes, final anneal 250°C for 100% IACS conductivity. Tinning optional for high-end cables: dip in lead-free tin at 240°C (prevents oxidation, improves solderability, adds $0.30/meter cost).

**Step 16-35: Insulation and Impedance-Controlled Pairing**
Each conductor requires dielectric insulation matched to function. **Power wires:** PVC or XLPE insulation 0.20-0.35mm thick, εᵣ 3.0-4.0 (voltage rating 30V, not impedance critical). Extrude at 180-200°C, cool in water bath. **High-speed pairs:** Foamed polyethylene (FPE) or solid FEP insulation, εᵣ 1.8-2.1 (low dielectric constant = lower capacitance = better signal integrity at 5+ GHz). Insulation diameter precisely controlled: for 32 AWG core 0.202mm, insulate to 0.50-0.55mm OD (±0.02mm tolerance, achieved via laser diameter feedback to extruder). **Differential pair twisting:** Automated pair twister takes two insulated wires (TX+ and TX–), twists with 12-20 twists per inch (tight twist improves differential coupling, reduces skew <10 ps between +/– within pair). Four high-speed pairs twisted separately, each with slightly different twist rate (10, 12, 15, 18 TPI - reduces crosstalk between pairs, different pitch prevents resonance). **USB 2.0 pair:** D+/D– twisted 8-12 TPI, 90Ω differential impedance. **Pair shielding (optional, premium cables):** Each pair wrapped with aluminum/polyester foil (7 µm Al + 12 µm PET, drain wire 36 AWG bare copper). Individual pair shields improve crosstalk isolation (Near-End Crosstalk NEXT < –40 dB at 5 GHz per USB 3.2 spec). **Color coding:** TX1 pair (orange/orange-white), TX2 (green/green-white), RX1 (blue/blue-white), RX2 (brown/brown-white), USB 2.0 (red/black or white/green), VBUS (red 20-28 AWG thick), GND (black), CC (yellow/white thin), SBU (purple/gray thin). Extrusion rates: 100-300 m/min for power wires, 50-150 m/min for precision high-speed pairs (slower for tighter tolerances).

**Step 36-58: Cable Core Assembly and Inner Shielding**
Bundle all wire groups into cable core. **Full-featured USB-C cable wire count:** 2× VBUS power (20-28 AWG), 2× GND return (20-28 AWG), 4× SuperSpeed TX pairs (8 wires, 32 AWG), 4× SuperSpeed RX pairs (8 wires, 32 AWG), 1× USB 2.0 D+ (28 AWG), 1× USB 2.0 D– (28 AWG), 2× CC configuration (28-30 AWG), 2× SBU sideband (30-32 AWG). **Total: 24 conductors.** USB 2.0-only cable: just VBUS (2), GND (2), D+/D– (2), CC (2) = 8 conductors (lower cost $3-5 vs $15-25 full-featured). **Core assembly:** Cabling machine bundles groups in layers: (1) Center: 4× SuperSpeed pairs + USB 2.0 pair + CC/SBU (12-14 wires, helical lay 15-25 mm pitch). (2) Outer: VBUS/GND power wires (4 large gauge, helical lay 30-50 mm pitch opposite direction - counter-rotating cancels torque). Filler: nylon or PP yarn (150-300 denier, fills voids, maintains round cross-section 3-5mm diameter before shielding). **Drain wire:** Bare tinned copper 30-32 AWG runs parallel to bundle (connects inner shield to connector shell ground). **Inner foil shield:** Aluminum/polyester laminate foil (25 µm Al + 25 µm PET, 100% coverage) wrapped around core (overlap 10-20%, drain wire positioned in overlap seam). Serve helically or longitudinally. Some designs use copper foil (35 µm, better conductivity, heavier, more expensive $0.80/m vs $0.40/m for aluminum).

**Step 59-78: Braided Shield and Outer [TPE Jacket](thermoplastic-elastomer)**
**Braided shield (optional, high-quality cables):** Tinned copper braid over inner foil. Braid specs: 96-128 carrier braiding machine, 40-42 AWG individual copper wire ends (hair-fine), 16-32 ends per carrier. Weave angle: 30-45° (balance between flexibility and coverage). Coverage: 85-95% (small diamond-shaped openings between crossovers). Braid improves: EMI shielding effectiveness (SE 70-90 dB vs 40-60 dB foil alone), mechanical durability (protects foil from tearing during flexing), ground continuity (multiple conductive paths). Braid adds $1.50-3.00/meter cost and 1.5-2.0mm to cable OD. **Outer jacket extrusion:** Thermoplastic elastomer (TPE) or thermoplastic polyurethane (TPU), Shore A 65-85 hardness (softer than PVC = more flexible, better feel). Compound: TPE pellets (styrenic SEBS or olefinic TPO base, plasticizers, UV stabilizers, colorants). Extruder: 90mm screw, 180-220°C barrel temperature, crosshead die coats entire shielded core. Jacket thickness: 0.6-1.2mm (final OD 4.5-6.5mm for typical USB-C cable). TPE properties: elongation 400-600%, tensile strength 10-20 MPa, tear resistance 50-100 kN/m, cold flex –40°C (maintains flexibility vs PVC which stiffens <0°C). **Color options:** Black (standard, carbon black pigment 3-5%), white (TiO₂), gray, blue, red (retail consumer preference). **Jacket printing:** Inkjet or pad printing applies specifications on jacket: "USB 3.2 Gen 2, 10Gbps, 5A/100W, E-Marker, USB-IF Certified" plus logo, length marking every 30cm. **Premium braided sleeve (optional):** Woven nylon or polyester braid over TPE jacket (decorative, adds durability, premium feel). Braided sleeve: 32-48 carrier, 150 denier nylon multifilament, 92-96% coverage. Colors: solid, two-tone, reflective thread patterns. Adds $2-4/meter, increases OD to 5-7mm. Heat-shrink tube at cable ends secures braid to connector (before overmolding).

**Step 79-100: [USB-C Connector](usb-c-connector) Shell and Contact Manufacturing**
**Connector shell:** Stainless steel (301 or 304, stamped 0.3-0.5mm sheet) or zinc alloy die-cast (Zamak 3). Steel advantages: strength, EMI shielding, durability 10,000+ mating cycles. Zinc advantages: lower cost, easier complex shapes (side cutouts, mounting features). **Shell stamping:** Progressive die stamping press (200-600 ton, 50-200 strokes/min), coil-fed stainless steel (0.3mm thick, 20-50mm wide), 10-20 stations progressively blank, pierce, bend, form final shell shape. Shell dimensions per USB-C spec: 8.34mm × 2.56mm outer, 6.65mm × 1.85mm inner cavity (tight tolerance ±0.05mm for plug-receptacle fit). **Plating:** Electroless nickel (5-10 µm, corrosion barrier), then decorative: chrome (0.3 µm, silver appearance), gold (0.05-0.3 µm, premium connectors, no tarnish), or matte/sandblasted nickel (minimalist aesthetic). **24-pin contact terminals:** Phosphor bronze (C5191, temper H08 hard, spring temper) or beryllium copper (C17200, higher strength, more expensive). **Contact design:** Blade-type contacts, 12 pins on top row (A1-A12), 12 on bottom row (B1-B12). Pin assignments: A1/B1, A12/B12 = GND, A4/B4, A9/B9 = VBUS, A5/B5 = CC1/CC2, A6/B6 = D+/D–, A8/B8 = SBU1/SBU2, A2/A3/A10/A11 = RX1/RX2 (SuperSpeed), B2/B3/B10/B11 = TX1/TX2. **Stamping and forming:** Progressive die cuts contact profile from 0.15mm phosphor bronze strip (contact beam, retention barbs, solder tail or crimp barrel). Contact length 8-12mm, width 0.30-0.45mm (narrow for 0.65mm pitch, 24 contacts in 15.6mm width). Spring beam design: cantilever deflects 0.2-0.4mm during mating (normal force 30-80 grams per contact, 0.3-0.8 N, ensures low contact resistance <30 mΩ). **Plating:** Electroplate nickel 1-2 µm (barrier, prevents copper migration), then gold 0.3-1.0 µm over contact area (30-50 µin standard, 50-100 µin premium). Gold hardness: hard gold (99.0% Au, Knoop 130-200, wear resistant) for receptacles, soft gold (99.7-99.9% Au, Knoop 90-120, low friction) for plugs. Selective plating: only contact mating area gold-plated, solder tail tin-plated (reduces cost, gold $60-80/gram).

**Step 101-120: Contact Insert Molding into Connector Housing**
**Housing material:** Liquid crystal polymer (LCP, high-temperature thermoplastic, HDT 240-280°C, UL94 V-0 flame rating, dimensional stability), high-temp nylon PA9T or PA6T (lower cost than LCP), or polycarbonate PC (lowest cost, HDT 125-145°C - adequate for USB 2.0, marginal for high-speed/high-power). LCP advantages: tight tolerances (mold shrinkage <0.1%), high heat resistance (reflow soldering 260°C peak, no deformation), low moisture absorption (<0.02%, stable dimensions). **Insert molding process:** (1) Pre-form contact strips: 24 contacts stamped but still connected in carrier strip (lead frame, 50-100mm wide, contacts held by thin tabs). (2) Load lead frame into injection mold (vertical press, contacts positioned by mold pins, precision ±0.02mm). (3) Close mold, inject molten LCP (320-360°C barrel, 340-380°C nozzle, 80-150 MPa injection pressure). (4) Plastic flows around contacts, encapsulates retention barbs, fills housing cavity (tongue shape 6.65×1.85×6.5mm). (5) Hold pressure 40-60 seconds (LCP solidifies quickly, crystalline structure forms). (6) Cool, eject molded housing with contacts embedded. (7) Trim carrier strip (separate individual connector, punch or laser cut). **Contact pitch verification:** Measure pin-to-pin spacing (0.65mm ±0.03mm per USB Type-C spec). Automated optical inspection (AOI) camera checks alignment, missing pins, flash (excess plastic). **Housing features:** Stiffener ribs (prevent tongue flexing during insertion), PCB mounting holes or surface-mount pads (if receptacle), cable entry funnel (guides cable wires during assembly), strain relief catch (locks onto overmold).

**Step 121-142: [E-Marker Chip](usb-pd-controller) Integration (Full-Featured Cables)**
USB-C cables >3m or supporting >3A/60W or USB 3.x/4 speeds require electronically marked cable (E-Marker chip, per USB-PD spec). **E-Marker IC:** Small microcontroller (die size 1-3 mm², 8-16 pin package), examples: TI HD3SS460, Cypress CYPD2103, ON Semi FUSB302. Functions: (1) Store cable parameters in OTP (one-time programmable) memory or EEPROM: cable current rating (3A, 5A), data speed (USB 2.0, 3.2, 4.0, Thunderbolt), cable length, VCONN power requirement, manufacturer VID/PID. (2) Communicate via USB-PD protocol on CC wire (BMC encoding, 300 kHz carrier, packet-based). (3) Respond to Source/Sink discovery messages (host queries cable, chip replies with capabilities, enables appropriate charging/data modes). **E-Marker PCB:** Tiny flex PCB (10×5×0.2mm, 2-layer, polyimide substrate 50 µm). Components: E-Marker IC (QFN-16 or DFN-10 package 2×2 mm), bypass capacitor (0201 size 0.1 µF), resistors (0201, 5.1 kΩ pulldown on CC for cable identification), sometimes MOSFET for VCONN power switching. **Assembly:** Solder paste stencil print (Type 4 solder paste, SAC305 alloy), pick-and-place machine (0201 accuracy ±0.03mm), reflow oven (245-260°C peak, nitrogen atmosphere), AOI inspection. **Chip programming:** During test, E-Marker chip programmed via SWD (serial wire debug) or I2C interface (load cable parameters, burn OTP fuses, irreversible). **PCB location:** E-Marker PCB embedded in connector housing (near CC pins), or within cable strain relief near connector. Wires soldered: CC (bidirectional communication + 5V VCONN power), GND, sometimes VBUS sense. **Cost:** E-Marker chip $0.30-0.80 (volume pricing), PCB assembly $0.20-0.40, total adds $0.50-1.20 per cable end (two connectors = $1.00-2.40 per cable). E-Marker-free cables limited: 480 Mbps USB 2.0 speed, 60W power max (3A only), <1m length - acceptable for basic charging cables, not for Pro laptops or displays.

**Step 143-160: Cable Termination and Soldering to Contacts**
**Cable preparation:** Cut cable to length (0.5m, 1m, 2m standard lengths), strip TPE jacket 15-25mm (thermal stripper or rotary blade, expose shielded core). Peel back braid shield (if present, fold over jacket). Cut foil shield, fold back, tin drain wire. Strip 8-12mm of individual conductor insulation (precision wire stripper, 24 blades for 24 wires). Tin conductor ends (flux-core solder, 320-350°C iron, 2-3 seconds, coat strands). **Pin assignment verification:** Match wire colors to USB-C pinout schematic (critical: reverse TX/RX on opposite ends for "turnaround" in cable - connector A's TX pins connect to cable TX pairs, connector B's TX pins connect to cable RX pairs, vice versa - enables bidirectional link). Use continuity tester or color code chart. **Soldering:** (1) Manual (low volume): Operator uses microscope (10-20× magnification), fine-tip soldering iron (0.5mm chisel or conical tip, 350-380°C), tweezers to hold wire. Position tinned wire on contact solder tail (cup or pad), apply iron 2-4 seconds, solder flows and wets. Inspect for bridging (adjacent pins shorted, 0.65mm pitch = easy to bridge), cold joints (dull/grainy appearance), insufficient solder. (2) Automated (high volume): Solder robot or selective soldering machine, contact pins pre-tinned, wires positioned by fixture, hot air or laser pulse (3-5 seconds, 300-400°C localized heat), solder reflows. AOI camera verifies all 24 connections. **E-Marker connection:** Solder E-Marker PCB's CC, GND, VBUS wires to corresponding contact pins. Secure PCB in connector housing (adhesive or snap-fit into molded pocket). **Shield termination:** Solder braid + drain wire to connector shell or dedicated ground pad (360° shield bond, critical for EMI). Crimp ferrule over braid/foil, then solder ferrule to shell (mechanically strong + electrically conductive).

**Step 161-175: Overmolding Connector and Strain Relief**
**Strain relief boot molding:** Connector assembly (shell + molded housing + soldered cable) placed in injection mold (aluminum or steel mold, two halves, cavity shaped as boot/strain relief). Cable protrudes from one end, connector from other. Inject TPE or TPU (same or similar material to cable jacket, 180-240°C, 50-120 MPa). Overmold encapsulates cable-connector junction (covers solder joints, shield connection, seals entry point), extends 25-50mm along cable (tapers from connector OD to cable OD, flexible strain relief, distributes bending stress). Cycle time: 30-90 seconds (cool, solidify, eject). **Material bonding:** Molten TPE chemically bonds to cable jacket TPE (if same polymer family, cohesive bond, inseparable). If cable jacket is PVC, use adhesion promoter (prime PVC surface with solvent or plasma treatment before overmolding, or use TPE with adhesive layer). **Color matching:** Overmold color matches cable jacket (black, white, gray) for seamless appearance, or contrasting color (e.g., red overmold on black cable) for visual accent. **Connector identification:** Overmold can be shaped to indicate function: smooth cylinder (data + power), ribbed or ridged pattern (Thunderbolt 3/4 cable), logo embossed (Apple logo, USB-IF trident). **Pull-tab (optional):** Some designs integrate pull-tab into overmold (protrusion or loop, user grips to extract connector from tight port, prevents pulling cable). **IP rating (optional, rugged cables):** Gasket or O-ring embedded in overmold (seals connector-cable junction, achieves IP54 or IP67 rating, waterproof/dustproof for industrial, outdoor, military applications). Requires mating receptacle with gasket too. Cost adds $1-3 per connector.

**Step 176-185: Testing and [USB-IF Certification](usb-compliance-testing)**
**In-process testing (100% production):** (1) Continuity: Automated bed-of-nails tester or flying probe (applies voltage to each pin A1-A12, B1-B12, verifies connection to cable conductors, checks all 24 conductors plus shield. Resistance <0.5Ω per conductor, <1.0Ω end-to-end 2-meter cable). (2) Isolation: Measure resistance between adjacent pins (>10 MΩ, no solder bridges). (3) E-Marker function: Query E-Marker chip via USB-PD tester (emulates Source device, reads cable VDO Vendor Defined Object, verifies correct parameters programmed: current rating, speed, length). E-Marker must respond within 50 ms, VCONN current <15 mA. (4) Hi-pot: 500V DC 1 second between conductors and shield (dielectric withstand, no breakdown). **Sample testing (per lot):** (1) Insertion/extraction force: Force gauge measures plug/receptacle mating force (10-35 N insertion per USB-C spec, <10 N extraction). Verify 10,000 mating cycles (accelerated life, mechanical durability, contact spring retention). (2) Cable flexing: Clamp cable near connector, bend ±90° or ±180°, 5,000-20,000 cycles (IEC 62680-1-3 Annex F). Monitor continuity (no intermittent opens >1 µs). Premium cables target 30,000+ flex cycles. (3) High-speed signal integrity: Vector network analyzer (VNA) measures S-parameters DC-10 GHz. Insertion loss: <6 dB at 5 GHz for 1m cable (USB 3.2 Gen 2×2), <8 dB at 10 GHz (USB4). Return loss: >10 dB (impedance matching). Differential-to-common mode conversion: <–30 dB. Eye diagram: PRBS31 at 10 Gbps, eye height >150 mV, eye width >70 ps, jitter <20 ps. (4) USB-PD power delivery: Apply 5A at 20V (100W), 1 hour continuous. Cable voltage drop <0.5V (wire gauge adequate, I²R loss minimal). Connector temperature rise <30°C (contact resistance <30 mΩ). **USB-IF Certification:** Optional but highly recommended for premium cables. Submit samples to USB-IF authorized test lab (Granite River Labs, Allion, TÜV). Tests: TID (Test ID) compliance (electrical, protocol, interoperability, 50+ test cases). Cost: $5,000-12,000 per cable type, 4-8 weeks. Upon passing, cable earns USB-IF Certified logo (use in marketing, packaging). Certification validity: specific design/vendor, any change (wire gauge, length, E-Marker chip) requires re-test. Non-certified cables cannot legally use "USB-C" trademark (generic "Type-C" allowed).

**Step 186-195: Market, Applications, and Economics**
Global USB-C cable market: 3-5 billion cables/year (2023), growing 15-25% annually (USB-C adoption in smartphones, laptops, tablets, accessories). **Segments:** (1) Charging-only (40-50%): USB 2.0 data, 3A/60W max, no E-Marker, $3-8 retail. (2) Standard data + charging (30-35%): USB 3.2 Gen 2 (10 Gbps), 3A/60W, E-Marker optional if <1m, $10-20 retail. (3) Full-featured (15-20%): USB 3.2/USB4/Thunderbolt, 5A/100-240W, E-Marker required, DisplayPort Alt Mode, $20-50 retail. (4) Specialty (5%): Active optical cables (AOC, 10-50m, fiber optic, $100-500), Thunderbolt 4 Certified ($30-80), MagSafe 3 to USB-C ($40-60, Apple proprietary connector + USB-C). **Pricing examples:** Apple USB-C Charge Cable (2m, USB 2.0, 60W): $19 retail (OEM cost ~$3-5). Anker PowerLine III USB-C to USB-C (6ft, USB 3.1 Gen 2, 100W, braided): $18-25 retail. Cable Matters Thunderbolt 4 (2m, 40 Gbps, 100W, certified): $35-50 retail. AmazonBasics USB-C to USB-C (3ft, USB 2.0, 60W): $7-10 retail (economy). **Manufacturing cost (1m full-featured cable, USB 3.2 Gen 2, 5A/100W, E-Marker):** Copper wire (24 conductors, 2m total length) $1.50, insulation/pairing $0.80, foil shield $0.40, braid shield $0.60, TPE jacket $0.50, two USB-C connectors (shells + contacts) $3.00, two E-Marker chips + PCBs $1.50, assembly labor (20-30 min per cable, semi-automated) $1.20, testing $0.30, packaging $0.40. **Total cost: $10.20-11.50.** Wholesale $15-18 (35-50% margin). Retail $25-35 (70-100% markup). Lower-cost USB 2.0 cable: $2.50-3.50 cost, $5-8 wholesale, $8-15 retail. **Production locations:** China (75-80%, Luxshare, Foxconn, Lotes, Deren, JAE, 100+ million cables/year capacity in large factories). Vietnam (10%), Taiwan (5%), USA/Europe (<3%, high-end Thunderbolt/specialty). **Profit margins:** Manufacturers 20-40% (commodity cables 15-25%, certified/branded 35-50%). Brands (Anker, Belkin, Cable Matters) 40-60%. Retailers 30-50%.
