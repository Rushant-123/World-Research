# Polypropylene Production Supply Chain

**Market Leader:** [LyondellBasell](/companies/lyondellbasell)



## Phase 1: Propylene Feedstock Preparation (Steps 1-25)

1. Receive propylene from fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) units
2. Obtain propylene from steam cracking of naphtha or ethane
3. Source propylene from propane dehydrogenation (PDH) plants
4. Store propylene in refrigerated spherical tanks at -42°C
5. Maintain propylene storage pressure at 5-10 bar
6. Vaporize liquid propylene using steam heat exchangers
7. Compress propylene gas to 30-40 bar for purification
8. Remove propane impurities through pressure swing adsorption
9. Eliminate sulfur compounds using zinc oxide beds
10. Remove moisture to <5 ppm using molecular sieves
11. Purify propylene through cryogenic distillation
12. Filter propylene through activated carbon beds
13. Achieve polymer-grade purity (>99.5% propylene)
14. Analyze propylene quality using online GC systems
15. Store purified propylene in intermediate feed tanks
16. Prepare hydrogen gas for molecular weight control
17. Purify nitrogen for reactor inerting and purging
18. Blend propylene with ethylene for random copolymers
19. Prepare ethylene-propylene comonomer ratios
20. Filter all gas streams through coalescers
21. Remove oxygen traces to prevent catalyst poisoning
22. Monitor carbon monoxide levels (<1 ppm)
23. Control propylene feed temperature at 20-30°C
24. Measure propylene flow rates using mass flow meters
25. Transfer propylene to polymerization reactor feed system

## Phase 2: Catalyst System Preparation (Steps 26-50)

26. Synthesize Ziegler-Natta catalyst from TiCl4 and MgCl2
27. Mill catalyst support to 5-20 micron particle size
28. Activate catalyst with electron donors (ethyl benzoate)
29. Add internal donor for isotacticity control
30. Suspend catalyst in mineral oil for handling
31. Analyze catalyst morphology using SEM imaging
32. Measure catalyst surface area (>200 m²/g)
33. Test catalyst activity in pilot-scale reactor
34. Prepare cocatalyst solution (triethylaluminum)
35. Add external donor (dicyclopentyl dimethoxysilane)
36. Control donor/cocatalyst ratio for stereoregularity
37. Synthesize metallocene catalysts on silica supports
38. Activate metallocene with methylaluminoxane (MAO)
39. Handle air-sensitive catalysts in glove box
40. Dilute catalyst suspensions to target concentration
41. Filter catalyst streams through 10-micron filters
42. Store catalyst under inert nitrogen atmosphere
43. Monitor catalyst shelf life and activity decay
44. Prepare multiple catalyst injection points
45. Calibrate catalyst feed pumps for precise dosing
46. Maintain catalyst temperature at 5-15°C
47. Analyze cocatalyst aluminum content
48. Test catalyst stereoselectivity (isotactic index >95%)
49. Document catalyst lot numbers for traceability
50. Transfer catalyst systems to reactor feed vessels

## Phase 3: Isotactic Polypropylene - Bulk Process (Steps 51-85)

51. Design loop reactor with 50-100 m³ volume
52. Charge reactor with liquid propylene medium
53. Establish reactor temperature at 70-80°C
54. Pressurize reactor to 30-40 bar
55. Inject Ziegler-Natta catalyst continuously
56. Add cocatalyst and external donor simultaneously
57. Feed propylene to maintain constant pressure
58. Introduce hydrogen for molecular weight control
59. Control H2/propylene ratio for target melt flow rate
60. Monitor polymerization heat removal through cooling jackets
61. Maintain propylene circulation at 5-10 m/s
62. Control residence time at 30-60 minutes
63. Achieve isotactic content >95% for crystallinity
64. Monitor slurry concentration (30-40 wt% polymer)
65. Sample polymer for melt flow rate determination
66. Flash unreacted propylene from reactor effluent
67. Recover and recycle propylene monomer
68. Separate polymer powder from liquid propylene
69. Deactivate catalyst residues with steam treatment
70. Purge polymer powder with nitrogen
71. Add primary antioxidant (Irganox 1010)
72. Add secondary antioxidant (Irgafos 168)
73. Incorporate acid scavenger (calcium stearate)
74. Mix nucleating agent for faster crystallization
75. Dry polymer powder at 80-90°C
76. Cool powder to ambient temperature
77. Screen powder to remove agglomerates
78. Analyze isotactic index via xylene extraction
79. Measure crystallinity using DSC (55-65%)
80. Test melting point (160-165°C for iPP)
81. Determine molecular weight distribution via GPC
82. Assess catalyst residue levels (<50 ppm Ti)
83. Blend polymer batches for consistency

85. Prepare for extrusion and pelletization
