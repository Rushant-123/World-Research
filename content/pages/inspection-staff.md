## INSPECTOR RECRUITMENT AND QUALIFICATION

### Candidate Screening
1. Post job openings for quality inspection positions across technical colleges and job boards
2. Screen applications for minimum education requirements (technical diploma or equivalent)
3. Review candidate work history for prior manufacturing or quality control experience
4. Verify educational credentials and employment references for shortlisted candidates
5. Schedule initial phone screening interviews to assess communication skills and interest
6. Evaluate candidate availability for shift work including nights and weekends
7. Conduct background checks for security clearance and employment verification
8. Review candidates' manual dexterity and fine motor skills during initial assessment
9. Screen for color vision deficiencies using Ishihara color blindness test plates
10. Assess basic mathematical skills for dimensional measurement and statistical calculations

### Visual Acuity Testing
11. Administer Snellen chart visual acuity test requiring minimum 20/20 corrected vision
12. Test near vision acuity using Jaeger eye chart at typical inspection distances (12-16 inches)
13. Evaluate depth perception using stereoscopic vision tests for 3D inspection tasks
14. Assess color discrimination ability using Farnsworth-Munsell 100 Hue Test
15. Test visual contrast sensitivity for detecting subtle cosmetic defects on various surfaces
16. Verify candidates can distinguish fine details at magnification levels up to 40X
17. Screen for astigmatism or visual distortions that could affect defect detection
18. Test peripheral vision to ensure adequate field of view during inspection scanning
19. Evaluate eye-hand coordination through practical manipulation tests with small components
20. Document baseline visual acuity measurements for annual re-certification tracking

### Basic Skills Assessment
21. Conduct practical hand-eye coordination test manipulating small electronic components
22. Test fine motor control through assembly and disassembly of sample products
23. Assess attention to detail using hidden defect detection exercises on sample boards
24. Evaluate sustained concentration ability through 30-minute inspection simulation
25. Test pattern recognition skills by identifying anomalies in repetitive component arrays
26. Measure reaction time and decision-making speed for high-volume inspection roles
27. Assess ability to follow detailed written procedures and work instructions
28. Test basic computer literacy for quality management system data entry
29. Evaluate understanding of measurement concepts using basic dimensional gauging exercises
30. Screen candidates for physical stamina to stand or sit for extended inspection periods

## IPC-A-610 CERTIFICATION TRAINING

### IPC Standards Introduction
31. Enroll new inspectors in IPC-A-610 Acceptability of Electronic Assemblies training course
32. Provide overview of IPC organization and its role in electronics quality standards
33. Introduce three-class system: Class 1 (general), Class 2 (dedicated service), Class 3 (high reliability)
34. Explain target conditions, acceptable conditions, and defect conditions for each class
35. Review differences in acceptance criteria across the three product classes
36. Study IPC-A-610 document structure and how to navigate specification sections
37. Learn terminology for electronic assembly components, processes, and defects
38. Understand visual inspection principles including lighting, magnification, and viewing angles
39. Review importance of cleanliness and handling procedures during inspection
40. Study documentation requirements and nonconformance reporting procedures

### Soldering Inspection Training
41. Train on through-hole solder joint inspection criteria including fillet shape and coverage
42. Learn to identify acceptable wetting characteristics and solder flow patterns
43. Study solder joint defects: insufficient solder, excess solder, cold joints, disturbed joints
44. Practice identifying voids, blowholes, and porosity in solder joints at various magnifications
45. Learn to assess solder wicking height on through-hole component leads
46. Train on surface mount solder joint inspection for various package types (QFP, BGA, 0201 chips)
47. Study heel, toe, and side fillet requirements for gull-wing leaded components
48. Learn to identify solder bridging, tombstoning, and Manhattan defects on SMT components
49. Practice measuring solder joint dimensions using calibrated microscope reticles
50. Study acceptable vs. defective conditions for solder balls, solder splatters, and flux residue

### Component and Board Inspection
51. Train on component placement criteria including position, orientation, and alignment tolerances
52. Learn to measure component body standoff heights and lead coplanarity
53. Study acceptable conditions for component marking legibility and orientation indicators
54. Practice identifying damaged components including cracked bodies, bent leads, and lifted pads
55. Learn to inspect PCB substrate conditions: scratches, delamination, warpage, discoloration
56. Train on trace and pad inspection including conductor width, spacing, and damage assessment
57. Study via fill requirements and acceptable conditions for via tenting and plugging
58. Learn to identify PCB defects: measling, weave exposure, haloing, and crazing
59. Practice conformal coating inspection for coverage, thickness, and defect identification
60. Train on hardware installation criteria for screws, standoffs, and mechanical fasteners

### Certification Examination
61. Review all training materials and sample defect images before certification exam
62. Complete written theory examination covering IPC-A-610 criteria and requirements
63. Take practical hands-on exam inspecting sample assemblies and identifying defects
64. Classify identified defects as target, acceptable, or process indicator for each class level
65. Demonstrate proper use of inspection tools including microscopes, magnifiers, and gauges
66. Achieve minimum 90% score on written exam and 95% agreement on practical exam
67. Receive IPC-A-610 CIS (Certified IPC Specialist) certification upon passing both exams
68. Document certification credentials including certification number, issue date, and expiration
69. Schedule two-year recertification training to maintain current IPC-A-610 certification status
70. Maintain certification records in quality management system for audit traceability

## ADVANCED INSPECTION TECHNIQUES

### Microscope Operation Training
71. Train on stereoscopic microscope operation including focus, zoom, and illumination controls
72. Practice proper illumination techniques: brightfield, darkfield, and oblique lighting
73. Learn to adjust magnification levels (10X-40X typical) based on inspection requirements
74. Study depth of field limitations and focus stacking techniques for 3D features
75. Practice using calibrated reticles for dimensional measurements at various magnifications
76. Train on digital microscope systems with image capture and annotation capabilities
77. Learn proper cleaning and maintenance procedures for microscope optics
78. Practice ergonomic positioning to minimize eye strain and fatigue during extended use
79. Study polarized light techniques for stress analysis in transparent materials
80. Train on UV fluorescence inspection for conformal coating coverage verification

### Dimensional Measurement Skills
81. Learn to use digital calipers for measuring component dimensions, lead spacing, and board thickness
82. Practice micrometer operation for precision measurements to 0.001mm resolution
83. Train on height gauge usage for measuring component standoff and solder joint height
84. Learn pin gauge sets for verifying hole diameters and alignment tolerances
85. Study coordinate measuring machine (CMM) operation for complex 3D measurements
86. Practice using optical comparators for profile inspection and dimensional verification
87. Train on laser measurement systems for non-contact dimensional inspection
88. Learn statistical analysis of measurement data including capability studies (Cp/Cpk)
89. Study measurement uncertainty and gauge repeatability and reproducibility (GR&R)
90. Practice proper documentation of measurement results in quality records

### Cosmetic Defect Detection
91. Train on surface finish inspection for scratches, dents, burrs, and tool marks
92. Learn to assess severity of cosmetic defects based on size, location, and visibility criteria
93. Practice identifying molding defects: flow lines, weld lines, sink marks, short shots
94. Study anodizing and coating defects: color variation, orange peel, runs, and sags
95. Learn to detect contamination including dust, oils, adhesive residue, and fingerprints
96. Train on glass and display inspection for optical defects, inclusions, and surface damage
97. Practice label and marking inspection for legibility, position, and adhesion
98. Study acceptable cosmetic limits for A-surface (customer-visible) vs. B-surface areas
99. Learn to use defect comparison standards and limit samples for consistent judgment
100. Practice photography techniques for documenting cosmetic defects for engineering review

## INCOMING INSPECTION OPERATIONS

### Supplier Material Receiving
101. Review incoming inspection schedule and prioritize urgent or critical material receipts
102. Verify packing slip matches purchase order for part numbers, quantities, and revision levels
103. Check shipping container for damage, proper sealing, and moisture indicator status
104. Inspect for proper ESD packaging and moisture-sensitive device (MSD) labeling
105. Verify traceability documentation including certificates of conformance and material certifications
106. Check date codes on components to ensure shelf life and storage requirements are met
107. Scan barcodes or RFID tags to log material receipt into inventory management system
108. Quarantine received material in hold area pending completion of inspection activities
109. Generate incoming inspection work orders specifying sampling plans and test requirements
110. Coordinate with expeditors on hot-lot materials requiring immediate inspection priority

### First Article Inspection (FAI)
111. Retrieve first article inspection package including drawings, specifications, and control plan
112. Conduct 100% dimensional verification of all critical-to-quality characteristics on first samples
113. Verify material specifications through visual inspection, testing, or certification review
114. Inspect surface finish, coating thickness, and cosmetic appearance against requirements
115. Perform functional testing per test procedures to verify operational performance
116. Document all measurements, test results, and observations on FAI report forms
117. Capture digital photographs of first article samples showing key features and characteristics
118. Compare inspection results against drawing tolerances and specification limits
119. Identify any nonconformances and initiate supplier corrective action requests as needed
120. Obtain quality manager approval signature before releasing supplier for production shipments

### Sample Inspection Plans
121. Apply ANSI/ASQ Z1.4 sampling standard to determine sample sizes for lot inspection
122. Select appropriate Acceptable Quality Limit (AQL) levels: 0.065% for critical, 0.65% for major, 6.5% for minor
123. Use inspection level II (normal) for standard incoming inspection activities
124. Calculate sample size 'n' and acceptance number 'c' from sampling table based on lot size
125. Use single sampling plan for efficient inspection of homogeneous lots
126. Apply double sampling when first sample results are borderline to reduce inspection cost
127. Implement reduced inspection after 10 consecutive lots accepted with normal inspection
128. Switch to tightened inspection if 2 of 5 consecutive lots rejected under normal inspection
129. Return to normal inspection after 5 consecutive lots accepted under tightened inspection
130. Document sampling plan parameters and rationale in incoming inspection procedures

## IN-PROCESS INSPECTION ACTIVITIES

### First-Off Inspection
131. Inspect first unit produced after setup, changeover, or significant process adjustment
132. Verify all product identification, labels, and markings are correct and legible
133. Check dimensional conformance of critical features using gauges and measurement tools
134. Inspect process-specific characteristics such as solder quality, coating coverage, or assembly torque
135. Verify proper component installation, orientation, and values match build documentation
136. Check for cosmetic defects including scratches, contamination, or handling damage
137. Perform functional tests if specified in first-off inspection procedure
138. Compare inspection results against specification limits and customer requirements
139. Obtain production supervisor approval to release process for continued production
140. Document first-off results and retain inspection records for lot traceability

### Roving Inspection Patrols
141. Follow planned patrol route through production area inspecting multiple workstations
142. Observe operator techniques and adherence to work instructions during production
143. Conduct random sampling of in-process work-in-progress (WIP) material
144. Verify process controls are functioning: temperatures, pressures, speeds, and settings
145. Check calibration status and due dates on measurement equipment at workstations
146. Inspect for 5S housekeeping compliance: organization, cleanliness, and discipline
147. Verify proper use of ESD controls including wrist straps, mats, and grounded equipment
148. Monitor environmental conditions: temperature, humidity, particulate levels in controlled areas
149. Identify potential quality issues and alert production supervisors for immediate corrective action
150. Document patrol findings in inspection log with timestamps and corrective actions taken

### Statistical Process Control (SPC) Monitoring
151. Collect measurement data from designated control characteristics per sampling frequency plan
152. Enter measurements into SPC charting software or manually plot on control charts
153. Monitor X-bar and R charts for process mean and variation trends
154. Identify out-of-control conditions: points beyond control limits, runs, or trends
155. Apply Western Electric rules to detect non-random patterns indicating process shifts
156. Calculate process capability indices (Cp and Cpk) to assess process performance
157. Alert process owners when statistical signals indicate potential process problems
158. Participate in investigation of special causes when out-of-control conditions occur
159. Verify effectiveness of corrective actions through continued SPC monitoring
160. Archive control charts and capability studies in quality records for audit trail

## FINAL INSPECTION AND AUDIT

### 100% Final Inspection
161. Receive completed products in final inspection area with build documentation and travelers
162. Verify product identification and serial numbers match work order and customer requirements
163. Conduct visual inspection for cosmetic defects on all customer-visible surfaces
164. Check completeness of assembly including all components, labels, and accessories
165. Verify proper installation of gaskets, seals, and protective films or covers
166. Inspect packaging components for damage and verify correct packaging materials
167. Perform dimensional checks on final product using gauges and measurement tools
168. Conduct electrical testing or functional verification per final test procedures
169. Apply final inspection acceptance stamps or labels to approved products
170. Quarantine nonconforming products for material review board (MRB) disposition

### Final Audit Sampling
171. Apply final audit sampling plan using AQL 1.0 for major defects and AQL 4.0 for minor defects
172. Randomly select samples from accepted production lots for comprehensive final audit
173. Conduct detailed inspection covering all critical, major, and minor characteristics
174. Perform accelerated life testing or destructive testing on audit samples as required
175. Verify packaging integrity, labeling accuracy, and inclusion of all required documentation
176. Check final product against customer specifications and quality agreement requirements
177. Document all audit findings including pass/fail results and defect descriptions
178. Calculate defect rates and trending metrics for management review and customer reporting
179. Initiate corrective action for systemic issues identified during final audit sampling
180. Release audited lots to shipping or place on hold pending corrective action completion

### Nonconformance Management
181. Document nonconformances using standardized forms capturing defect details and quantities
182. Classify defects as critical (safety/compliance), major (functionality), or minor (cosmetic)
183. Photograph nonconforming material showing defect characteristics for engineering review
184. Segregate nonconforming material in designated hold area with clear rejection tags
185. Convene material review board (MRB) with engineering, quality, and operations representatives
186. Evaluate disposition options: use-as-is, rework, repair, scrap, or return to supplier
187. Document MRB decision rationale and obtain required approval signatures
188. Track rework operations to ensure corrective actions are properly implemented
189. Conduct re-inspection of reworked material to verify conformance to requirements
190. Close nonconformance records in quality system and update metrics dashboards
