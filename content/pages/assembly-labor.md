## Recruitment and Selection

1. Post job openings for electronics assembly positions on local job boards and recruitment agencies with specific requirements for manual dexterity and visual acuity
2. Screen applications for minimum education level (typically high school diploma or equivalent) and any prior manufacturing experience
3. Conduct initial phone interviews to assess communication skills, availability for shift work, and basic understanding of manufacturing environments
4. Schedule in-person interviews with HR and production supervisors to evaluate candidate suitability and cultural fit
5. Administer manual dexterity tests requiring candidates to assemble small components within time limits (typically 15-20 minutes)
6. Perform visual acuity tests to ensure candidates can identify small components and defects (20/30 vision or correctable to this standard)
7. Conduct color blindness tests using Ishihara plates as workers must distinguish color-coded wires and components
8. Review candidates' ability to stand for extended periods (8-12 hour shifts) and perform repetitive motions
9. Check references from previous employers focusing on attendance, quality performance, and teamwork capabilities
10. Conduct background checks and verify right-to-work documentation according to local labor laws
11. Make job offers including wage rates ($2.50-4.00/hour base rate in China), shift schedules, and benefits package
12. Schedule new hires for orientation sessions within 2 weeks of acceptance, organizing cohorts of 15-20 workers
