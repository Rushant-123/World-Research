## Crude Oil Selection and Initial Processing

1. Source naphthenic base crude oil from specific fields in California, Venezuela, or Texas
2. Verify crude oil has appropriate naphthenic ring structure content (25-40%)
3. Test crude oil for sulfur content, targeting less than 1% for easier processing
4. Measure crude oil viscosity index to ensure proper base stock characteristics
5. Transport crude oil via pipeline to refinery receiving facilities
6. Store crude oil in temperature-controlled storage tanks at 40-60°C
7. Sample incoming crude oil batches for comprehensive compositional analysis
8. Perform ASTM D2007 clay-gel analysis to determine aromatic content
9. Heat crude oil to 120°C for initial separation preparation
10. Pump crude oil through pre-heat exchangers using process heat recovery

## Atmospheric Distillation

11. Feed heated crude oil into atmospheric distillation column at controlled rate
12. Maintain column bottom temperature at 350-370°C under atmospheric pressure
13. Separate light ends (gases, naphtha) from overhead at 40-180°C
14. Extract kerosene fraction from middle section at 180-250°C
15. Collect diesel range material at 250-350°C from lower trays
16. Draw atmospheric gas oil (AGO) from section above bottom residue
17. Monitor tray temperatures throughout column using 50+ measurement points
18. Control reflux ratio to optimize separation efficiency (typically 2:1 to 4:1)
19. Remove atmospheric residue (reduced crude) from column bottom
20. Cool overhead products through air-fin coolers and water exchangers
21. Separate vapor and liquid phases in overhead accumulator drums
22. Route light gases to fuel gas system after amine sweetening
23. Send naphtha to gasoline blending or petrochemical feedstock
24. Direct atmospheric residue to vacuum distillation unit
25. Analyze residue properties: viscosity, density, carbon residue content

## Vacuum Distillation

26. Transfer atmospheric residue to vacuum distillation feed surge tank
27. Heat residue to 380-400°C in vacuum column feed furnace
28. Maintain careful temperature control to prevent thermal cracking
29. Introduce heated feed into vacuum column operating at 20-40 mmHg absolute
30. Generate vacuum using steam ejector system (3-5 stage configuration)
31. Extract light vacuum gas oil (LVGO) from upper section at 350-450°C
32. Collect heavy vacuum gas oil (HVGO) from middle section at 450-520°C
33. Remove vacuum residue (asphalt) from column bottom
34. Monitor vacuum levels continuously to maintain separation efficiency
35. Use wash oil circulation to prevent coking in hot sections
36. Cool vacuum gas oil fractions through heat exchange network
37. Separate the desired transformer oil base stock fraction (typically HVGO)
38. Measure kinematic viscosity at 40°C and 100°C for each fraction
39. Verify pour point characteristics (should be below -40°C for Type I oils)
40. Route non-transformer oil fractions to other processing units

## Solvent Extraction (Aromatics Removal)

41. Design extraction unit to remove aromatic compounds using furfural or N-methylpyrrolidone
42. Pre-heat vacuum gas oil feed to extraction temperature (80-120°C)
43. Mix vacuum gas oil with solvent in rotating disc contactor (RDC) column
44. Maintain solvent-to-oil ratio between 1.5:1 and 3:1 by volume
45. Allow counter-current contact between oil and solvent over 60-90 minutes
46. Extract aromatic compounds into solvent phase (extract phase)
47. Separate paraffinic/naphthenic oil as raffinate phase
48. Monitor extract and raffinate quality through online analyzers
49. Remove solvent from raffinate by flashing in reduced pressure tower
50. Strip final traces of solvent using steam at 150-180°C
51. Recover solvent from extract phase in separate recovery column
52. Cool and condense solvent vapors for recycle to extraction unit
53. Purify recovered solvent to maintain extraction efficiency
54. Store aromatic extract for other uses (rubber processing oils, etc.)
55. Verify raffinate has less than 10% aromatic content by mass
56. Test raffinate oxidation stability using ASTM D2440 method
57. Measure raffinate color on ASTM scale (should be L0.5 or better)
58. Check interfacial tension (IFT) of raffinate/water interface
59. Adjust extraction severity if product specifications not met
60. Cool raffinate to 60°C for downstream processing

## Hydrotreating Process

61. Pump raffinate through feed filters to remove particulates
62. Mix hydrogen gas with oil feed at 200-300 SCFH per barrel ratio
63. Heat hydrogen-oil mixture to reaction temperature (300-380°C)
64. Pass mixture through fixed-bed catalytic reactor containing CoMo or NiMo catalyst
65. Maintain reactor pressure at 500-1500 psig for optimal hydrogenation
66. Remove sulfur compounds as hydrogen sulfide (hydrodesulfurization)
67. Saturate olefinic compounds to improve oxidation stability
68. Reduce nitrogen compounds through hydrodenitrogenation reactions
69. Monitor catalyst bed temperatures using multiple thermowells
70. Control reaction severity to avoid excessive saturation of naphthenic rings
71. Maintain hydrogen partial pressure above 300 psi throughout reactor
72. Separate hydrogen-rich gas from liquid product in high-pressure separator
73. Recycle hydrogen after removal of H2S in amine scrubber
74. Flash dissolved light gases from liquid product in low-pressure separator
75. Route flash gases to fuel system after sulfur removal
76. Strip remaining light ends from hydrotreated oil using steam
77. Analyze hydrotreated product for sulfur content (target <5 ppm)
78. Verify nitrogen content reduced to less than 1 ppm
79. Check oxidation stability improvement compared to feed
80. Monitor catalyst activity and plan regeneration or replacement cycles

## Clay Treatment / Filtration

81. Heat hydrotreated oil to 90-120°C for clay contact
82. Add activated fuller's earth or bentonite clay at 1-5% by weight
83. Mix oil and clay thoroughly in agitated contact vessel for 30-60 minutes
84. Allow clay to adsorb polar compounds, trace metals, and color bodies
85. Maintain gentle agitation to maximize clay surface area contact
86. Monitor oil color improvement during treatment process
87. Allow settling time of 2-4 hours to facilitate clay separation
88. Pump oil through bag filters to remove suspended clay particles
89. Use multi-stage filtration with progressively finer media (50→10→1 micron)
90. Maintain filter differential pressure below 30 psi to prevent media damage
91. Monitor filtrate clarity using turbidity meters
92. Replace filter elements when pressure drop exceeds design limits
93. Polish filter oil through final 0.5 micron absolute filters
94. Verify particulate cleanliness meets ISO 4406 code 16/14/11 or better
95. Analyze filtered oil color on ASTM D1500 scale (should be 0.5 or less)
96. Test dielectric breakdown voltage on filtered sample (preliminary check)
97. Dispose of spent clay according to environmental regulations
98. Reclaim clay oil content through solvent washing if economical
99. Cool filtered oil to ambient temperature in plate heat exchangers
100. Store filtered base oil in clean, nitrogen-blanketed tanks

## Additive Blending

101. Transfer filtered oil to blending tank equipped with high-shear mixer
102. Verify tank is clean and free from contaminants or water
103. Sample base oil for final quality verification before blending
104. Calculate required additive dosages based on batch volume and specifications
105. Add 2,6-ditertiary-butyl-para-cresol (DBPC) oxidation inhibitor at 0.08-0.40%
106. Introduce tert-butylphenol (TBP) as supplementary antioxidant if specified
107. Add passivator compounds like benzotriazole to neutralize copper catalyst effects
108. Include pour point depressant if low-temperature performance required
109. Meter additives accurately using calibrated dosing pumps
110. Mix additives into base oil using high-speed agitator for 2-4 hours
111. Maintain mixing temperature at 50-70°C to ensure complete dissolution
112. Circulate blended oil through external pump loop for homogenization
113. Sample blended oil from multiple tank locations to verify uniformity
114. Analyze additive concentration using chromatography or spectroscopy
115. Verify oxidation inhibitor content meets ASTM D4768 requirements
116. Check that metal passivator concentration is within specification
117. Measure blended oil kinematic viscosity at 40°C and 100°C
118. Verify viscosity index (VI) is appropriate for application (typically 90-105)
119. Test pour point on blended product (should be -45°C or better for Type I)
120. Allow blended oil to equilibrate at mixing temperature for 4-8 hours

## Degassing / Dehydration

121. Transfer blended oil to vacuum degassing vessel
122. Apply vacuum of 1-5 mmHg absolute using mechanical vacuum pumps
123. Heat oil to 65-75°C to promote moisture and gas release
124. Spray oil through nozzles to create large surface area for degassing
125. Allow oil droplets to fall through vacuum space onto collection surface
126. Remove released gases and water vapor continuously via vacuum system
127. Condense water vapor in chilled condenser before vacuum pump
128. Monitor vacuum level and adjust pump speed to maintain target pressure
129. Measure dissolved gas content using portable gas-in-oil analyzer
130. Continue degassing until total gas content below 0.5% by volume
131. Test water content using Karl Fischer titration (target <5 ppm)
132. Verify interfacial tension (IFT) above 40 mN/m at 25°C
133. Check acidity level using ASTM D974 (should be <0.01 mg KOH/g)
134. Re-process oil through degassing if moisture or gas targets not met
135. Cool degassed oil to ambient temperature under vacuum or nitrogen blanket
136. Transfer degassed oil to final storage tanks via closed system
137. Maintain nitrogen pad on storage tanks to prevent air ingress
138. Purge all transfer lines with dry nitrogen before and after use
139. Verify all storage vessels are completely dry before receiving oil
140. Install desiccant breathers on tank vents to exclude moisture
