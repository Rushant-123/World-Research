# Natural Gas Compressor Station Manufacturing Process

**Market Leader:** [Siemens](/companies/siemens)



## Initial Design and Engineering (Steps 1-25)

1. Conduct site survey and geological assessment to determine foundation requirements and environmental conditions.

2. Analyze gas composition from the pipeline to determine compression requirements and material compatibility.

3. Calculate required compression ratio based on inlet pressure, outlet pressure, and flow rate specifications.

4. Determine optimal number of compression stages needed to achieve target pressure while maintaining efficiency.

5. Select turbine engine model based on power requirements, fuel availability, and efficiency targets.

6. Design centrifugal compressor impeller geometry using computational fluid dynamics simulations.

7. Calculate thermodynamic properties at each compression stage including temperature rise and gas density changes.

8. Design intercooler heat exchangers to reduce gas temperature between compression stages.

9. Specify turbine-to-compressor gearbox ratio to match rotational speeds and torque requirements.

10. Develop piping and instrumentation diagrams showing all process flows, valves, and measurement points.

11. Design foundation specifications including concrete thickness, reinforcement, and anchor bolt patterns.

12. Calculate acoustic emissions and design noise suppression enclosures to meet regulatory limits.

13. Develop emergency shutdown system logic including overspeed protection and vibration monitoring.

14. Design fuel gas conditioning system including filtration, pressure regulation, and heating.

15. Specify lubrication oil system including reservoir capacity, pump specifications, and filtration requirements.

16. Design seal gas system to prevent process gas leakage through compressor shaft seals.

17. Calculate thermal expansion of piping systems and specify expansion joints and support locations.

18. Develop electrical distribution plan including transformer sizing and cable routing.

19. Design fire detection and suppression systems including gas detectors and deluge systems.

20. Specify instrumentation including pressure transmitters, temperature sensors, and flow meters.

21. Develop control philosophy document defining normal operation, startup, and shutdown sequences.

22. Design vibration monitoring system including accelerometer placement and alarm setpoints.

23. Calculate cooling water requirements for intercoolers, lube oil coolers, and turbine systems.

24. Develop maintenance access platforms and walkways ensuring safe access to all equipment.

25. Complete hazard and operability study identifying potential failure modes and mitigation strategies.

## Turbine Engine Manufacturing (Steps 26-50)

26. Cast turbine housing from high-temperature nickel alloy using investment casting process.

27. Machine turbine housing bore to precise tolerances for bearing installation and rotor clearances.

28. Manufacture hot gas path components including combustion liner from high-temperature alloys.

29. Cast turbine blades using directional solidification to create columnar grain structure.

30. Apply thermal barrier coating to turbine blades using plasma spray deposition process.

31. Machine turbine blades to final airfoil profile using 5-axis CNC machining centers.

32. Drill cooling air passages through turbine blades using electrical discharge machining.

33. Balance individual turbine blade sets to minimize vibration during high-speed rotation.

34. Manufacture turbine rotor shaft from vacuum-melted steel billet using precision turning and grinding.

35. Install turbine blades onto rotor disk using dovetail or fir-tree root attachment method.

36. Balance completed turbine rotor assembly in vacuum spin pit to achieve precise dynamic balance.

37. Manufacture compressor section for turbine air intake including inlet guide vanes and casing.

38. Assemble multi-stage axial compressor for turbine air supply including rotor and stator blades.

39. Install journal bearings in turbine housing using precision alignment and clearance verification.

40. Install thrust bearing to absorb axial loads from turbine and compressor pressure differentials.

41. Assemble combustion system including fuel nozzles, igniters, and flame detectors.

42. Install turbine rotor into housing with precise axial positioning and radial clearance verification.

43. Connect fuel gas manifold to combustion system with leak-tight connections.

44. Install exhaust diffuser and transition piece to direct hot gases away from turbine.

45. Assemble lube oil system including pumps, filters, coolers, and accumulator tank.

46. Install control system sensors including speed pickups, temperature sensors, and pressure transmitters.

47. Connect hydraulic system for fuel valve actuation and inlet guide vane positioning.

48. Perform cold rotation test to verify bearing alignment and freedom of rotation.

49. Conduct leak test of fuel system using nitrogen pressure to verify joint integrity.

50. Perform initial turbine firing test in factory to verify combustion stability and control response.

## Centrifugal Compressor Manufacturing (Steps 51-75)

51. Cast compressor casing from ductile iron or steel using sand casting or fabrication methods.

52. Machine compressor casing internal surfaces to precise tolerances for impeller clearances.

53. Machine inlet and discharge nozzle flanges to API standard dimensions and surface finish.

54. Manufacture compressor impellers from titanium or aluminum alloy using 5-axis machining.

55. Design impeller blade geometry to achieve high efficiency while avoiding surge and choke conditions.

56. Polish impeller surfaces to minimize aerodynamic losses from surface roughness.

57. Balance each impeller individually to minimize vibration at operating speeds.

58. Manufacture compressor shaft from high-strength alloy steel with integral thrust collar.

59. Machine shaft journals to precise diameter and surface finish for bearing installation.

60. Install shrink-fit or keyed impellers onto compressor shaft with precise axial positioning.

61. Balance complete compressor rotor assembly including all impellers and shaft as a unit.

62. Manufacture diffuser vanes to convert kinetic energy to pressure rise efficiently.

63. Machine volute scroll passages to collect gas from impeller and direct flow to discharge.

64. Install labyrinth seals between stages to minimize internal leakage and maintain efficiency.

65. Manufacture and install dry gas seals on compressor shaft to prevent process gas leakage.

66. Install magnetic bearings or journal bearings depending on size and speed requirements.

67. Install thrust bearing system to absorb axial loads from pressure differential across stages.

68. Assemble compressor internals including diaphragms, seals, and bearing housings.

69. Install instrumentation ports for pressure and temperature measurement at each stage.

70. Perform mechanical run test of compressor in factory using air or nitrogen as test gas.

71. Verify compressor performance curve matches design predictions for flow and pressure ratio.

72. Conduct vibration analysis during mechanical run test to verify rotor dynamics.

73. Test dry gas seal system for leak rate using helium leak detection methods.

74. Verify bearing oil flow rates and temperatures meet design specifications.

75. Package compressor for shipment with protective covers and shock-absorbing mounting.
