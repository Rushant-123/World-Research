# Battery Separator Membrane Production

## Raw Material Preparation and Quality Control

1. Receive ultra-high molecular weight polyethylene (UHMWPE) resin in powder form with molecular weight 2-6 million g/mol
2. Verify polyethylene resin specifications including molecular weight distribution, particle size (50-200 μm), and purity >99.9%
3. Test resin melt flow index (MFI) at 190°C under 2.16 kg load to confirm processing characteristics
4. Store UHMWPE resin in moisture-controlled silos at <0.01% humidity and 20-25°C temperature
5. Receive polypropylene (PP) resin with molecular weight 200,000-400,000 g/mol for multilayer applications
6. Analyze PP resin isotacticity (>95%) and crystallinity (50-60%) using differential scanning calorimetry
7. Check PP resin ash content (<0.03%) and volatile matter (<0.5%) for purity verification
8. Receive mineral oil (liquid paraffin) as pore-forming agent with viscosity 100-200 cSt at 40°C
9. Test mineral oil purity, distillation range, and compatibility with polyolefin resins
10. Store mineral oil in temperature-controlled tanks at 40-60°C to maintain optimal viscosity
11. Receive aluminum oxide (Al2O3) ceramic powder with particle size 50-500 nm for coating applications
12. Characterize Al2O3 powder using scanning electron microscopy (SEM) for particle size distribution
13. Measure Al2O3 surface area (50-200 m²/g) using BET nitrogen adsorption method
14. Verify Al2O3 purity (>99.5%) and crystalline phase (alpha or gamma) using X-ray diffraction
15. Receive polymeric binder (PVDF or acrylic) for ceramic coating slurry preparation
16. Test binder molecular weight, viscosity, and adhesion properties on test substrates
17. Receive surfactants and dispersants for stabilizing ceramic coating slurry
18. Prepare deionized water with conductivity <1 μS/cm for coating slurry and cleaning operations
19. Install inline particle filters (0.5-1 μm) on all liquid feed lines to prevent contamination
20. Calibrate all analytical instruments including DSC, SEM, XRD, and viscometers

## Dry Process - Resin Compounding and Mixing

21. Set up twin-screw extruder with barrel diameter 65-90 mm and L/D ratio 40-48
22. Configure extruder temperature profile: feed zone 180°C, compression zone 200-220°C, metering zone 240-260°C
23. Install vacuum degassing port at 60-70% barrel length to remove volatiles and moisture
24. Weigh UHMWPE resin according to batch formulation (typically 30-40% by weight)
25. Weigh mineral oil pore-forming agent (60-70% by weight) for target porosity of 40%
26. Add antioxidant stabilizers (0.1-0.5% by weight) including phenolic and phosphite types
27. Add processing aids (0.1-0.3% by weight) such as fluoropolymer or silicone-based additives
28. Pre-mix solid components in high-speed mixer at 500-800 rpm for 3-5 minutes
29. Feed pre-mixed materials into extruder hopper using gravimetric dosing system
30. Meter liquid mineral oil into extruder barrel using precision pump at controlled rate
31. Monitor extruder torque, pressure (50-150 bar), and melt temperature (240-270°C) continuously
32. Verify homogeneous mixing through die pressure stability (±2 bar variation)
33. Extract melt samples for optical microscopy to confirm oil droplet dispersion (<5 μm size)
34. Extrude compound through strand die and cool in water bath at 20-40°C
35. Pelletize compounded material using underwater or strand pelletizer
36. Dry pellets in fluidized bed dryer at 80-100°C for 2-4 hours to remove surface moisture
37. Screen pellets to remove fines and oversized particles (target size 3-5 mm)
38. Store compounded pellets in sealed containers with desiccant until film extrusion
39. Sample and test pellet composition using thermogravimetric analysis (TGA) to verify oil content
40. Measure pellet density (0.90-0.95 g/cm³) and melt flow characteristics

## Film Extrusion and Casting

41. Load compounded pellets into film extrusion line feed hopper with nitrogen blanketing
42. Configure cast film extrusion line with sheet die width 1500-3000 mm
43. Set up three-zone extruder (feed, transition, metering) with screw diameter 90-150 mm
44. Program extruder temperature profile: feed 180-200°C, transition 220-240°C, metering 250-270°C
45. Heat sheet die to uniform temperature 250-270°C using multi-zone heating elements
46. Adjust die lip gap to 0.8-1.5 mm for target film thickness control
47. Install automatic die lip control system for cross-direction thickness uniformity
48. Set up primary chill roll at 80-120°C with mirror-polished chrome surface
49. Install secondary and tertiary cooling rolls at progressively lower temperatures (60-40°C)
50. Configure thickness measurement system using laser or beta-ray gauges across full web width
51. Start extruder with gradual temperature ramp and screw speed increase to 60-120 rpm
52. Purge extruder until melt flow is stable and free of contamination or degradation
53. Initiate melt flow through sheet die at output rate 200-500 kg/hour
54. Adjust die bolt heaters to eliminate cross-direction temperature variations (±2°C)
55. Contact extruded melt with primary chill roll using air knife or electrostatic pinning
56. Control chill roll temperature to achieve rapid quenching and optimal crystalline structure
57. Monitor cast film for surface defects including gels, die lines, and thickness variations
58. Measure online thickness profile with target uniformity ±5% across web width
59. Wind cast film onto core with tension control 20-50 N/m at line speed 10-50 m/min
60. Record process parameters including temperatures, pressures, speeds, and thickness data

## Annealing and Crystallization Control

61. Transfer cast film roll to annealing oven with precise temperature control zones
62. Mount film roll on unwinder with automatic tension control system
63. Thread film through multi-zone annealing oven length 10-30 meters
64. Set annealing temperature profile: preheat 90-110°C, main anneal 120-130°C, cooling 70-90°C
65. Control residence time in annealing zones for total treatment 2-10 minutes
66. Maintain air circulation velocity 2-5 m/s for uniform heat transfer
67. Monitor film temperature using infrared sensors at multiple positions
68. Control film tension at 10-30 N/m throughout annealing process
69. Achieve beta-crystal formation in PP or optimized PE crystallinity for mechanical strength
70. Cool film gradually to prevent thermal stress and dimensional instability
71. Measure crystallinity change using DSC analysis on samples before and after annealing
72. Test mechanical properties including tensile strength (>100 MPa) and elongation (>50%)
73. Verify dimensional stability by measuring shrinkage (<5%) after heat treatment at 105°C for 8 hours
74. Rewind annealed film with consistent tension and alignment
75. Store annealed film rolls in climate-controlled area at 20-25°C and <40% RH

## Machine Direction (MD) Stretching

76. Mount annealed film roll on tenter frame stretching line unwinder
77. Thread film through MD stretching section with precision edge guiding system
78. Install infrared or hot air preheating zone to raise film temperature to 90-130°C
79. Configure MD stretching rolls with speed ratio 3:1 to 5:1 for target draw ratio
80. Set preheating zone length 2-5 meters with temperature uniformity ±3°C
81. Control film tension in preheating zone at 20-40 N/m to prevent premature stretching
82. Engage MD stretching rolls rotating at differential speeds to induce elongation
83. Monitor stretching force and film stress using load cells on draw rolls
84. Achieve target MD stretch ratio 4-5x original length over stretching zone 1-3 meters
85. Control stretching temperature profile for optimal micropore formation and orientation
86. Install online optical microscopy or imaging system to monitor pore formation
87. Measure film thickness reduction to 30-40 μm after MD stretching
88. Test MD tensile strength increase to >150 MPa due to molecular orientation
89. Verify film width reduction (necking) of 20-40% during MD stretching
90. Cool stretched film using air knives or cooling rolls to set molecular orientation
91. Monitor for stretching defects including breaks, thickness variations, and uneven pores
92. Adjust stretching parameters based on real-time feedback from sensors and cameras
93. Wind MD-stretched film with precise tension control to prevent deformation
94. Sample stretched film for microscopy analysis of pore structure in MD direction
95. Measure air permeability (Gurley seconds) to verify initial porosity development

## Transverse Direction (TD) Stretching

96. Mount MD-stretched film roll on TD tenter frame unwinder
97. Thread film edges into motorized clip chains or pin tenter system
98. Engage film edges with clips spaced 10-50 mm apart along tenter rails
99. Preheat film in TD preheating zone at 110-140°C using hot air impingement
100. Control preheating zone length 3-8 meters for uniform temperature distribution
101. Set TD stretching zone with rail divergence angle 3-15 degrees
102. Program clip chain speed and rail separation for TD stretch ratio 5-7x original width
103. Achieve biaxial stretching with combined MD and TD ratios for 20-30x area expansion
104. Monitor TD stretching force through rail tension sensors and clip load monitoring
105. Control film temperature during TD stretching at 115-135°C for optimal pore formation
106. Generate microporous structure through polymer chain separation and void formation
107. Target porosity 35-45% with pore diameter 20-100 nm after biaxial stretching
108. Measure thickness uniformity achieving 20-25 μm target with ±1 μm variation
109. Enter heat-setting zone at 120-145°C while maintaining film constraint in tenter
110. Hold film at constant width for 10-60 seconds to set molecular orientation and dimensions
111. Apply controlled relaxation (2-5% width reduction) in heat-setting zone to reduce internal stress
112. Cool film gradually to 40-60°C before releasing from tenter clips
113. Trim film edges (selvage) containing clip marks using rotary knives or laser cutters
114. Wind finished microporous film with tension control 15-30 N/m at line speed 30-100 m/min
115. Inspect film surface for defects using automated optical inspection system
116. Measure final thickness profile, air permeability (100-300 Gurley seconds), and porosity
117. Test puncture strength (>300 gf/25 μm), tensile strength (MD >150 MPa, TD >100 MPa)
118. Verify thermal shrinkage <5% in both MD and TD at 105°C for 8 hours
119. Analyze pore size distribution using mercury intrusion porosimetry or bubble point method
120. Examine microporous structure using SEM imaging at 5000-50000x magnification

## Wet Process - Oil Extraction and Pore Formation

121. Transfer biaxially stretched film containing mineral oil to extraction line
122. Mount film roll on unwinder with edge guiding and tension control system
123. Thread film through multi-stage solvent extraction bath system
124. Prepare methylene chloride or other hydrocarbon solvent extraction baths at 20-40°C
125. Configure extraction line with 3-5 consecutive solvent bath tanks, each 2-5 meters long
126. Set film residence time in each extraction bath for 30-120 seconds
127. Maintain solvent purity and cleanliness using continuous filtration and circulation
128. Immerse film in first extraction bath to begin mineral oil dissolution and removal
129. Monitor oil extraction rate through solvent analysis using gas chromatography
130. Pass film through subsequent extraction baths with progressively cleaner solvent
131. Achieve >98% oil removal to form open microporous structure throughout film thickness
132. Control film tension at 10-25 N/m during extraction to prevent dimensional changes
133. Install air knives or vacuum slots to remove excess solvent from film surfaces
134. Pass film through warm air drying zone at 40-80°C to evaporate residual solvent
135. Configure solvent recovery system using distillation or activated carbon adsorption
136. Recycle extracted mineral oil after purification for reuse in compounding process
137. Test extracted film for residual oil content (<0.5%) using solvent extraction analysis
138. Measure air permeability increase to 150-250 Gurley seconds after oil extraction
139. Verify pore opening and interconnectivity using liquid intrusion porosimetry
140. Examine cross-sectional morphology using cryo-fractured samples in SEM
141. Confirm uniform pore distribution throughout 20-25 μm film thickness
142. Test electrolyte wettability using contact angle measurement with battery electrolyte
143. Measure ionic conductivity of electrolyte-saturated separator at 25°C
144. Verify electrical insulation resistance >1000 MΩ·cm for dry separator film
145. Wind extracted and dried separator film onto cores with clean room protocol

## Ceramic Coating Preparation

146. Prepare ceramic coating slurry in high-shear mixing tank with temperature control
147. Weigh Al2O3 ceramic particles targeting 40-60% solids content in final slurry
148. Add polymeric binder (PVDF, acrylic, or CMC) at 3-10% of ceramic weight
149. Disperse ceramic particles in water or NMP solvent using high-speed disperser at 2000-4000 rpm
150. Add dispersing agents (0.5-2% by weight) to prevent particle agglomeration
151. Control slurry viscosity to 100-1000 cP using rheology modifier additives
152. Mix slurry for 2-4 hours to achieve stable, homogeneous dispersion
153. Degas slurry under vacuum to remove entrained air bubbles
154. Filter slurry through 10-50 μm screens to remove agglomerates and contamination
155. Measure slurry particle size distribution using laser diffraction (target d50 200-400 nm)
156. Verify slurry stability over 24-48 hours with <5% sedimentation
157. Adjust slurry pH to 7-9 using ammonia or alkaline additives for optimal coating
158. Transfer prepared slurry to coating station with continuous circulation and agitation
159. Maintain slurry temperature at 20-30°C to control viscosity and coating quality
160. Monitor slurry properties including viscosity, solids content, and pH during coating operation

## Ceramic Coating Application

161. Mount microporous separator film roll on coating line unwinder
162. Thread film through tension control system and accumulator for continuous operation
163. Clean film surface using corona or plasma treatment at 38-42 dyne/cm surface energy
164. Configure gravure coating system with engraved roll pattern 80-200 lines per inch
165. Immerse gravure roll partially in ceramic slurry bath with doctor blade metering
166. Transfer controlled slurry thickness from gravure roll to separator film surface
167. Adjust gravure roll speed and line speed for target coating weight 2-6 g/m² (dry basis)
168. Apply ceramic coating to one or both sides of separator film as specified
169. Install precision coating weight control using online beta-ray or infrared sensors
170. Pass coated film through drying oven with multiple temperature zones
171. Set drying profile: preheat 60-80°C, main drying 100-130°C, final drying 140-160°C
172. Control drying air velocity 2-5 m/s and humidity <20% RH for uniform solvent evaporation

178. Measure air permeability increase to 200-350 Gurley seconds after ceramic coating
