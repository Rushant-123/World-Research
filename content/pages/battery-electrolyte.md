## Phase 1: Raw Material Preparation and Quality Control (Steps 1-25)

### Raw Material Reception and Inspection

1. Receive LiPF6 salt shipment in moisture-proof sealed drums with nitrogen atmosphere protection
2. Verify LiPF6 purity specification (minimum 99.99% battery grade) through supplier certificates
3. Inspect drum seals for integrity and any signs of moisture contamination or physical damage
4. Transfer LiPF6 drums to dry room (dew point -45°C, relative humidity <0.5%)
5. Record batch numbers, manufacturing dates, and expiration dates in material tracking system
6. Sample LiPF6 from each drum using sealed sampling tools in glove box environment
7. Analyze LiPF6 moisture content using Karl Fischer titration (target <50 ppm H2O)
8. Measure LiPF6 free acid content through acid-base titration (target <50 ppm HF)
9. Check LiPF6 particle size distribution to ensure proper dissolution characteristics
10. Test LiPF6 thermal stability through differential scanning calorimetry (DSC) analysis

### Organic Solvent Reception and Testing

11. Receive EC (ethylene carbonate) solvent in stainless steel drums with nitrogen blanket
12. Verify EC purity (>99.95%) and moisture content (<20 ppm) through COA review
13. Measure EC melting point (36-38°C) to confirm quality and proper storage conditions
14. Receive DMC (dimethyl carbonate) solvent with moisture specification <20 ppm
15. Test DMC purity using gas chromatography (GC) analysis (target >99.95%)
16. Check DMC density at 20°C (1.063-1.073 g/cm³) for quality verification
17. Receive EMC (ethyl methyl carbonate) solvent in sealed containers under inert atmosphere
18. Analyze EMC moisture content using Karl Fischer titration (specification <20 ppm)
19. Verify EMC purity through GC-MS analysis (target >99.95% with minimal impurities)
20. Test all solvents for metal ion contamination (Fe, Cu, Ni, Cr <1 ppm each)

### Additive Preparation and Verification

21. Receive vinylene carbonate (VC) additive in amber bottles under argon atmosphere
22. Verify VC purity (>99.5%) and confirm absence of polymerization through NMR spectroscopy
23. Receive fluoroethylene carbonate (FEC) additive with moisture control packaging
24. Test FEC purity and fluorine content through 19F NMR and elemental analysis
25. Receive prop-1-ene-1,3-sultone (PS) additive and verify purity through HPLC analysis

## Phase 2: Environmental Control and Equipment Setup (Steps 26-45)

### Dry Room Environment Establishment

26. Activate molecular sieve dehumidification system to achieve -45°C dew point
27. Monitor dry room temperature control at 20-25°C with ±2°C stability
28. Verify HEPA filtration system providing Class 1000 (ISO 6) cleanroom conditions
29. Check nitrogen supply system pressure and purity (99.999% N2, <5 ppm O2)
30. Calibrate humidity sensors at multiple locations throughout production area
31. Test emergency backup dehumidification systems for redundancy compliance
32. Verify oxygen concentration monitors and alarm systems functioning correctly
33. Check differential pressure between dry room and adjacent areas (>5 Pa positive)
34. Inspect door interlocks and airlock entry/exit procedures for contamination prevention
35. Validate dry room environmental data logging system with real-time monitoring

### Production Equipment Preparation

36. Clean stainless steel (316L) mixing reactor with ultra-pure water and nitrogen drying
37. Inspect reactor interior surface for corrosion, pitting, or contamination deposits
38. Verify reactor heating/cooling jacket temperature control system (±1°C accuracy)
39. Test reactor agitator motor and variable speed controller (10-500 RPM range)
40. Calibrate reactor pressure sensors for vacuum and positive pressure operations
41. Install fresh molecular sieve filter cartridges in recirculation purification loop
42. Connect nitrogen purge lines with flow rate controllers and pressure regulators
43. Set up in-line moisture analyzer probe in reactor with continuous monitoring
44. Install temperature probes at multiple reactor positions for thermal mapping
45. Verify reactor seal integrity through helium leak detection testing

## Phase 3: Solvent Blending and Purification (Steps 46-70)

### Solvent Pre-Treatment

46. Transfer EC solvent from storage drum to pre-heating vessel at 40°C for liquefaction
47. Maintain EC temperature above melting point (36°C) throughout handling process
48. Pass EC through 0.1 micron absolute filter to remove particulate contamination
49. Degas EC solvent under vacuum (10-50 mbar) for 30 minutes with nitrogen sparging
50. Measure EC moisture content post-treatment to verify <10 ppm H2O specification
51. Transfer DMC solvent through stainless steel transfer lines under nitrogen pressure
52. Pass DMC through molecular sieve column (3A type) for deep moisture removal
53. Verify DMC moisture reduction to <10 ppm through in-line Karl Fischer monitoring
54. Filter DMC through 0.1 micron membrane filter for particulate removal
55. Degas DMC under vacuum while stirring to remove dissolved oxygen and moisture

### Ternary Solvent Mixture Preparation

56. Calculate precise solvent ratios for EC:DMC:EMC formulation (typically 1:1:1 volume ratio)
57. Adjust ratios based on target electrolyte specifications (may range 20-40% EC, 30-50% DMC, 20-40% EMC)
58. Add weighed quantity of EC (heated to 40°C liquid state) to clean mixing reactor
59. Record EC mass with precision balance (±0.1 gram accuracy for batch size control)
60. Add DMC solvent to reactor while maintaining nitrogen blanket and temperature control
61. Monitor reactor temperature and adjust cooling to maintain 20-25°C during exothermic mixing
62. Add EMC solvent in controlled manner with continuous agitation at 100 RPM
63. Mix ternary solvent system for 60 minutes at 150 RPM for complete homogenization
64. Sample solvent mixture and verify composition through GC analysis (±0.5% tolerance)
65. Measure mixed solvent density and compare to theoretical calculation for quality check

### Solvent Purification and Final Treatment

66. Circulate mixed solvent through packed bed molecular sieve column for 2 hours
67. Monitor moisture content reduction with target <5 ppm H2O in purified solvent
68. Pass solvent through activated alumina column to remove trace acidic impurities
69. Filter solvent through 0.05 micron final polish filter for ultra-high purity
70. Verify final solvent mixture moisture content <5 ppm before salt dissolution step

## Phase 4: LiPF6 Salt Dissolution (Steps 71-95)

### Salt Transfer and Addition System

71. Transfer mixing reactor containing purified solvent to glove box-integrated system
72. Maintain glove box atmosphere at <0.1 ppm H2O and <10 ppm O2 during operations
73. Calculate LiPF6 quantity required for target molarity (1.0-1.2 M concentration)
74. Weigh LiPF6 salt in sealed weighing chamber with high-precision balance (±0.01 g)
75. Account for LiPF6 purity assay results in final quantity calculation
76. Transfer weighed LiPF6 to sealed addition hopper with nitrogen purge
77. Connect addition hopper to reactor via sealed transfer system
78. Verify reactor agitation at 150 RPM before initiating salt addition
79. Open reactor inlet valve and pressurize hopper with nitrogen for salt transfer
80. Add LiPF6 salt in controlled portions (10-20% total quantity per addition)

### Controlled Dissolution Process

81. Monitor reactor temperature during exothermic dissolution (target <30°C maximum)
82. Activate cooling jacket if temperature rises above 28°C to prevent decomposition
83. Increase agitation speed to 200 RPM after first LiPF6 addition for mixing enhancement
84. Wait 10 minutes between additions to allow complete dissolution and temperature stabilization
85. Add second portion of LiPF6 (10-20% of total) through sealed transfer system
86. Monitor dissolution progress through visual inspection via reactor sight glass
87. Continue incremental LiPF6 additions with 10-minute intervals between portions
88. Add third portion of LiPF6 salt and maintain temperature control <30°C
89. Monitor conductivity increase as indication of salt dissolution progress
90. Add fourth portion of LiPF6 and extend mixing time to 15 minutes

### Complete Dissolution and Verification

91. Add final portion of LiPF6 salt to achieve target 1.0-1.2 M concentration
92. Increase agitation to 250 RPM for enhanced mixing and dissolution completion
93. Mix electrolyte solution for minimum 2 hours at controlled temperature (22-25°C)
94. Sample electrolyte through sealed sampling port into moisture-free vial
95. Verify complete LiPF6 dissolution through visual clarity inspection (no particles)

## Phase 5: Electrolyte Quality Analysis (Steps 96-120)

### Physical Property Testing

96. Measure electrolyte density at 20°C using precision density meter (±0.0001 g/cm³)
97. Compare measured density to theoretical value for concentration verification
98. Measure electrolyte viscosity at 25°C using rotational viscometer
99. Record viscosity data for batch quality documentation (typical 3-5 cP at 25°C)
100. Test electrolyte appearance for clarity, color, and absence of particulates
101. Verify electrolyte is water-clear with no turbidity or suspended solids
102. Measure electrolyte refractive index at 20°C for additional composition verification
103. Check electrolyte freezing point to confirm proper solvent mixture ratio
104. Test electrolyte flash point for safety classification and transportation compliance
105. Record all physical property data in batch quality control documentation

### Chemical Composition Analysis

106. Analyze LiPF6 concentration through lithium atomic absorption spectroscopy (AAS)
107. Verify molarity within specification range (1.0-1.2 M with ±0.02 M tolerance)
108. Perform ion chromatography to measure fluoride ion concentration
109. Calculate free HF content from fluoride analysis (specification <50 ppm)
110. Analyze solvent composition through gas chromatography with FID detection
111. Verify EC:DMC:EMC ratio within ±1% of target formulation
112. Measure water content through Karl Fischer coulometric titration
113. Confirm moisture specification <20 ppm H2O (typically achieve <10 ppm)
114. Test for metal contamination through ICP-MS analysis (Fe, Cu, Ni, Cr, Zn)
115. Verify all metal ions <1 ppm each for high purity specification

### Electrochemical Property Testing

116. Measure ionic conductivity at 25°C using calibrated conductivity cell
117. Verify conductivity within specification range (typically 9-11 mS/cm at 25°C)
118. Test conductivity temperature dependence from -20°C to +60°C
119. Measure electrolyte conductivity at -20°C (minimum 2 mS/cm requirement)
120. Record conductivity vs. temperature profile for batch characterization

## Phase 6: Additive Formulation and Incorporation (Steps 121-145)

### Film-Forming Additive Addition (VC)

121. Calculate vinylene carbonate (VC) quantity for target concentration (1-2 wt%)
122. Weigh VC additive in glove box environment using analytical balance
123. Transfer VC to sealed addition vessel with nitrogen atmosphere protection
124. Connect VC addition vessel to reactor through sealed transfer line
125. Begin VC addition slowly while maintaining agitation at 150 RPM
126. Add VC over 15-minute period to ensure uniform distribution
127. Monitor temperature during VC addition (maintain 20-25°C)
128. Increase agitation to 200 RPM after VC addition completion
129. Mix electrolyte with VC for 30 minutes for complete homogenization
130. Sample electrolyte to verify VC concentration through GC analysis

### FEC Additive Incorporation

131. Calculate fluoroethylene carbonate (FEC) quantity for specification (2-5 wt%)
132. Weigh FEC additive in controlled environment with moisture exclusion
133. Transfer FEC to clean stainless steel addition vessel under nitrogen
134. Connect FEC vessel to reactor via sealed transfer system
135. Add FEC to electrolyte while maintaining agitation at 200 RPM
136. Complete FEC addition over 15-minute period with temperature monitoring
137. Mix electrolyte with FEC for 30 minutes to ensure uniform distribution
138. Verify FEC concentration through 19F NMR or GC-MS analysis
139. Check electrolyte clarity and absence of phase separation after FEC addition
140. Record FEC addition data including quantity, time, and temperature profile

### Sultone Additive Addition (PS)

141. Calculate prop-1-ene-1,3-sultone (PS) quantity for target concentration (0.5-2 wt%)
142. Weigh PS additive using precision balance in dry atmosphere environment
143. Transfer PS to sealed addition container with nitrogen purge
144. Add PS to electrolyte through sealed system while agitating at 150 RPM
145. Mix electrolyte with all additives for 60 minutes at 200 RPM for final homogenization

## Phase 7: Final Purification and Conditioning (Steps 146-165)

### Advanced Filtration

146. Circulate finished electrolyte through 0.5 micron pre-filter to remove large particles
147. Monitor pressure differential across pre-filter (replace if >2 bar pressure drop)
148. Pass electrolyte through 0.1 micron absolute membrane filter for fine filtration
149. Recirculate electrolyte through filter system for minimum 3 passes
150. Monitor filtration flow rate and adjust if needed to maintain optimal conditions
151. Perform final filtration through 0.05 micron PTFE membrane for ultra-high purity
152. Collect filtered electrolyte in clean stainless steel holding tank
153. Verify particulate count through laser particle counter (<100 particles/mL >1 micron)
154. Check filter integrity post-filtration through bubble point or pressure hold test
155. Document filtration parameters including pressure, flow rate, and particle counts

### Final Moisture Removal

156. Circulate electrolyte through activated molecular sieve column (4A type)
157. Monitor moisture content reduction with in-line Karl Fischer analyzer
158. Continue circulation until moisture content stabilizes <15 ppm H2O
159. Apply gentle vacuum (100-200 mbar) to reactor while stirring for final degassing
160. Maintain vacuum for 30 minutes to remove dissolved gases and residual moisture
161. Break vacuum with ultra-pure nitrogen and establish positive pressure
162. Sample electrolyte for final moisture content verification through Karl Fischer
163. Confirm moisture specification <20 ppm (target achievement <15 ppm)
164. Test for dissolved oxygen content (target <20 ppm O2)
165. Document final purification results in batch quality record

## Phase 8: Final Quality Testing and Validation (Steps 166-185)

### Comprehensive Electrochemical Testing

166. Prepare test cells with standard electrodes for electrochemical window measurement
167. Assemble coin cells with lithium metal anode and stainless steel cathode
168. Fill test cells with electrolyte sample under controlled dry room conditions
169. Perform linear sweep voltammetry (LSV) from OCP to 6.0V vs Li/Li+
170. Measure anodic stability limit (oxidation potential >4.5V required)
171. Determine cathodic stability limit through LSV in negative direction
172. Verify electrochemical window >5.0V for high voltage application compatibility
173. Test electrolyte oxidation current at 4.2V (<0.1 mA/cm² specification)
174. Measure lithium ion transference number through polarization and impedance methods
175. Calculate transference number from current decay and impedance measurements

### Battery Performance Validation

176. Assemble full test cells using standard LiFePO4 cathode and graphite anode
177. Fill test cells with electrolyte batch sample in dry room environment
178. Seal cells and allow wetting period of 12-24 hours before testing
179. Perform initial cell formation cycling at C/20 rate for first 3 cycles
180. Measure formation cycle efficiency (>85% first cycle efficiency required)
181. Conduct rate capability testing at C/5, C/2, 1C, 2C discharge rates
182. Record discharge capacity at each rate for performance validation
183. Perform cycle life testing for minimum 50 cycles at 1C rate
184. Measure capacity retention after 50 cycles (>95% retention required)
185. Analyze test cell impedance through electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS)

## Phase 9: Filling, Packaging, and Storage (Steps 186-195)

### Electrolyte Filling Operations

186. Transfer qualified electrolyte to automated filling station in dry room environment
187. Connect filling station to holding tank via sealed stainless steel lines
188. Calibrate volumetric filling pumps for accurate dispensing (±0.5% accuracy)
189. Purge filling lines with nitrogen before initiating electrolyte flow
190. Fill HDPE or aluminum bottles (1L, 5L, 20L sizes) using automated filling nozzles

### Packaging and Documentation

191. Seal filled bottles immediately with tamper-evident caps and nitrogen headspace
192. Apply batch labels including product code, concentration, date, and QC approval
193. Pack bottles in moisture-barrier bags with desiccant packets for shipping protection
194. Generate certificate of analysis (COA) with all test results and specifications
195. Store finished electrolyte in climate-controlled warehouse (15-25°C, <30% RH) with maximum 12-month shelf life before use
