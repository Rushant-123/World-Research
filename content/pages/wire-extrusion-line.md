# Wire Insulation Extrusion Line

**Market Leader:** [Maillefer](/companies/maillefer)



A comprehensive plastic insulation extrusion line for applying PVC, PE, or XLPE insulation onto copper or aluminum conductors for electrical cables. This industrial system integrates precise material handling, thermal processing, concentricity control, and quality assurance testing.

## Raw Material Preparation

1. Receive plastic resin pellets (PVC, PE, or XLPE) in 25kg bags or bulk containers
2. Inspect resin lot numbers and verify material specifications match production requirements
3. Check moisture content of resin using moisture analyzer (target less than 0.05% for PE/XLPE)
4. Store resin in climate-controlled warehouse at 20-25 degrees Celsius with relative humidity below 60%
5. Transfer required quantity of base resin to blending area using pneumatic conveying or manual handling
6. Weigh base resin on industrial scale with 0.1kg accuracy
7. Add carbon black pigment at 2-3% by weight for black insulation compounds
8. Incorporate heat stabilizers (0.5-1.0%) to prevent thermal degradation during extrusion
9. Mix in processing aids (0.3-0.8%) to improve melt flow and reduce die buildup
10. Add antioxidants (0.2-0.5%) to prevent oxidative degradation during service life
11. Incorporate UV stabilizers (0.3-0.6%) for outdoor cable applications
12. Add flame retardant additives (10-15%) for fire-resistant cable grades
13. Blend all components in high-intensity mixer for 8-12 minutes until homogeneous
14. Discharge blended compound into storage hoppers with dust extraction
15. Label each batch with formulation number, date, and operator identification
16. Take sample of blended compound for quality control testing
17. Test melt flow index (MFI) to verify processing characteristics (target range specific to grade)
18. Measure bulk density of compound to ensure proper hopper feeding
19. Screen compound through 5mm mesh to remove any contamination or agglomerates
20. Transfer approved compound to day hoppers above extruder feed throat

## Conductor Preparation and Payoff

21. Select appropriate conductor spool based on production schedule (copper or aluminum)
22. Verify conductor size matches production specification (typical range 0.5mm to 50mm diameter)
23. Inspect conductor surface for oxidation, oil contamination, or mechanical damage
24. Mount conductor spool on motorized payoff stand with adjustable tension control
25. Thread conductor through dancer arm tension sensing system
26. Set payoff tension to 2-5 kg for small conductors, up to 50 kg for large conductors
27. Route conductor through accumulator system (optional for continuous operation during spool changes)
28. Pass conductor through pre-heating unit to raise temperature to 80-120 degrees Celsius
29. Install conductor through straightener with multiple rollers to remove casting effects
30. Thread conductor through brush cleaning station to remove surface contaminants
31. Apply optional sizing fluid or lubricant to conductor surface for improved die flow
32. Route conductor through diameter measuring gauge to verify size within tolerance
33. Pass conductor through metal detector to identify any ferrous contamination
34. Thread conductor into extruder crosshead die entrance guide tube
35. Verify conductor path alignment through optical centering system

## Extruder Setup and Startup

36. Open extruder hopper gate and inspect feed throat for residual material from previous run
37. Clean feed throat and hopper surfaces with vacuum and compressed air
38. Check screw rotation direction (typically clockwise viewed from feed end)
39. Verify gear reducer oil level and temperature (should be at proper mark on sight glass)
40. Inspect barrel cooling water connections and open inlet valves
41. Check all barrel heater band connections and insulation condition
42. Verify temperature controller setpoints for each barrel zone based on material type
43. Set feed zone temperature to 160-180 degrees Celsius for PVC, 180-200 degrees for PE
44. Set compression zone temperature 5-10 degrees higher than feed zone
45. Set metering zone temperature 5-10 degrees higher than compression zone
46. Set adapter and die temperatures to match metering zone or slightly higher
47. Activate barrel heating and monitor temperature ramp-up on control panel
48. Wait for all zones to reach setpoint and stabilize (typically 45-60 minutes)
49. Check screw cooling water flow and temperature (maintain 15-25 degrees Celsius)
50. Verify screen pack and breaker plate are properly installed in adapter
51. Ensure proper mesh size screen pack based on material (typically 40-80 mesh)
52. Fill extruder hopper with prepared compound to 70-80% capacity
53. Start screw rotation at minimum speed (5-10 RPM)
54. Monitor drive motor current during startup (should gradually increase as screw fills)
55. Gradually increase screw speed to 20-30 RPM over 5-10 minutes
56. Observe material discharge from die lips and verify consistent melt flow
57. Check melt temperature with handheld pyrometer (target 180-200 degrees Celsius for PVC, 200-220 for PE)
58. Adjust screw speed and barrel temperatures to achieve target melt temperature
59. Monitor discharge pressure on melt pressure transducer (typical range 100-300 bar)
60. Purge extruder for 10-15 minutes to clear any degraded material or contamination

## Crosshead Die Setup

61. Select appropriate crosshead die set based on conductor size and insulation thickness
62. Inspect die body, tip, and die plate for wear, scratches, or buildup
63. Clean all die components with brass brush and approved solvent
64. Measure die tip outer diameter with micrometer for proper size
65. Measure die plate inner diameter to ensure correct clearance
66. Calculate die gap for target insulation thickness (typically 1.2-1.5 times final thickness)
67. Install die tip into crosshead body and secure with locking ring
68. Install die plate and adjust concentricity using dial indicator
69. Achieve concentricity within 0.02mm total indicator reading
70. Secure die plate with mounting bolts torqued to specification
71. Install conductor guide tube through center of die tip
72. Verify guide tube alignment and smooth internal finish
73. Connect die heater bands and temperature control thermocouples
74. Set die body temperature to match extruder adapter temperature
75. Activate die heating and wait for thermal stabilization
76. Install vacuum port connection for die venting if equipped
77. Connect die to extruder adapter flange with mounting bolts
78. Torque die mounting bolts to specification in star pattern
79. Verify die angle alignment (typically 60-90 degrees from conductor path)
80. Install safety guards around die area per safety requirements

## Cooling System Setup

81. Inspect cooling trough for cleanliness and verify water level
82. Fill cooling trough with filtered water to proper level
83. Add water treatment chemicals to prevent algae and corrosion
84. Set cooling water temperature to 10-20 degrees Celsius using chiller unit
85. Start circulation pump and verify water flow through trough
86. Install appropriate conductor guide tubes in trough entrance and exit
87. Adjust trough length based on line speed and cooling requirements (typically 3-8 meters)
88. Position spray nozzles for initial contact cooling of hot extrudate
89. Adjust water spray pressure and flow rate for uniform cooling
90. Install air wipe or water wipe at trough exit to remove surface water
91. Position infrared temperature sensor to monitor wire temperature at cooling exit
92. Set target wire exit temperature to 40-60 degrees Celsius
93. Install dryer section after cooling trough (hot air or absorbent pads)
94. Verify proper drainage of cooling trough to prevent overflow
95. Check cooling water filtration system and clean filters if needed

## Measuring and Testing Equipment Setup

96. Position laser diameter measurement gauge 1-2 meters after cooling exit
97. Calibrate diameter gauge using precision reference standards
98. Set diameter measurement tolerance limits in control system
99. Configure diameter gauge to measure insulation thickness at multiple points around circumference
100. Install optical concentricity measurement system to monitor insulation uniformity
101. Set concentricity tolerance to maximum 10% deviation from nominal
102. Position infrared camera for thermal imaging of hot extrudate at die exit
103. Mount high-voltage spark tester electrode array over moving wire
104. Set spark test voltage based on insulation thickness (typically 4-6 kV per mm thickness)
105. Calibrate spark tester with known defect samples
106. Install capacitance measurement system for dielectric constant verification
107. Position ultrasonic thickness gauge as backup to laser measurement
108. Install marking printer for continuous footage marking if required
109. Configure data logging system to record all measurement parameters
110. Set up automatic alarm and rejection system for out-of-specification product

## Capstan and Takeup Setup

111. Select capstan size appropriate for wire diameter (typically 20-40 times wire diameter)
112. Clean capstan surface and inspect for wear or damage
113. Install capstan drive motor and verify rotation direction
114. Connect capstan speed control to line master speed controller
115. Install dancer arm between capstan and takeup for tension control
116. Set capstan pulling tension to balance extrusion pressure and prevent stretching
117. Thread wire around capstan with 2-3 wraps for adequate grip
118. Install optional second capstan for dual-pull configuration on large conductors
119. Mount takeup reel spindle and verify proper alignment
120. Install empty take-up reel with correct flange size for product
121. Set takeup tension control to maintain consistent back tension
122. Configure traverse system for proper wire layering on takeup reel
123. Set traverse pitch to prevent wire overlap and crossover on reel
124. Install revolution counter for footage tracking
125. Connect emergency stop circuits for capstan and takeup drives

141. Take physical samples of insulated wire at regular intervals
142. Cut sample sections for laboratory testing of insulation properties

144. Check insulation concentricity calculation from measurement data

## Shutdown Procedure

189. Gradually reduce line speed to minimum (5-10 meters per minute)
190. Cut and secure finished wire end to takeup reel
191. Stop conductor payoff drive and extruder screw rotation simultaneously
192. Purge extruder with purging compound or next production material
193. Reduce barrel temperatures to 100 degrees Celsius
194. Stop barrel heaters and allow cooling water to continue circulating
195. Clean die face and surrounding area while still warm, document production run data including total footage, average measurements, any quality issues, and material consumption for records
