1. Surface water intake from river or lake
2. Screening removes large debris (leaves, sticks)
3. Coarse screens with 25-50mm openings
4. Fine screens remove particles >1mm
5. Pumping station lifts water to treatment plant
6. Coagulation adds aluminum sulfate (alum)
7. Flash mixing for 1-3 minutes
8. Flocculation basins with gentle mixing
9. Floc particles aggregate over 20-30 minutes
10. Sedimentation tanks allow settling
11. Settled water moves to filters
12. Sand filters (0.5-1.0mm sand depth: 0.6-1.2m)
13. Filtration removes particles >10 microns
14. Backwashing filters every 24-72 hours
15. Disinfection with chlorine or UV light
16. Chlorine dose: 1-3 mg/L
17. Contact time: 30 minutes minimum
18. pH adjustment to 7-8 for corrosion control
19. Fluoridation for dental health (optional)
20. Storage in clear well or reservoir
21. Final pumping to distribution system
22. Water quality testing every 4 hours
23. Turbidity target: <0.3 NTU
24. Microbiological testing daily
25. Heavy metals analysis monthly
26. Organic compound screening quarterly
27. Treatment capacity: 50-500 million liters per day
28. Operational cost: $0.20-0.50 per 1,000 liters
29. Energy consumption: 0.3-0.7 kWh per 1,000 liters
30. Residuals (sludge) management and disposal
