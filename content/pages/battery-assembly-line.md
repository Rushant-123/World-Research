## Overview

The Battery Assembly Line is an advanced automated manufacturing system designed by Hirata Corporation for high-volume production of lithium-ion battery cells. This comprehensive line integrates multiple specialized stations to transform electrode materials into fully tested, production-ready battery cells suitable for electric vehicles, energy storage systems, and consumer electronics applications.

## Process Steps

### Stage 1: Pre-Assembly Preparation (Steps 1-15)

1. Receive electrode stacking machine output with cathode and anode layers
2. Verify electrode stack alignment using vision inspection system
3. Check stack height and layer count against specification
4. Measure stack compression force to ensure proper density
5. Scan electrode stack barcode for traceability tracking
6. Transfer stack to pre-cleaning station using vacuum gripper
7. Apply compressed air cleaning to remove loose particles
8. Perform electrostatic discharge treatment on electrode surfaces
9. Position stack in humidity-controlled holding buffer
10. Monitor ambient humidity level (maintain below 1% RH)
11. Temperature stabilize stack to 25°C ± 2°C
12. Verify electrode tab positions using high-resolution camera
13. Check cathode tab for any coating defects or tears
14. Check anode tab for copper foil integrity
15. Transfer stack to tab preparation station

### Stage 2: Tab Preparation and Alignment (Steps 16-30)

16. Position electrode stack in tab alignment fixture
17. Extend cathode tab to specified length (typically 15-20mm)
18. Apply tension control to prevent tab deformation
19. Clean cathode tab surface with isopropyl alcohol
20. Dry cathode tab using heated nitrogen gas flow
21. Extend anode tab to specified length
22. Apply tension control to anode tab
23. Clean anode tab surface with isopropyl alcohol
24. Dry anode tab using heated nitrogen gas flow
25. Verify tab separation distance meets safety requirements
26. Apply positioning marks on tabs using laser marking
27. Measure tab thickness using precision micrometer
28. Check for tab surface oxidation using optical inspection
29. Record tab dimensions in manufacturing database
30. Transfer stack to welding preparation area

### Stage 3: Tab Welding Setup (Steps 31-45)

31. Load electrode stack into welding fixture
32. Secure stack with pneumatic clamps (0.5 MPa pressure)
33. Position cathode tab in ultrasonic welding horn
34. Align tab with current collector terminal
35. Load nickel-plated copper terminal from feeder
36. Verify terminal material certification and lot number
37. Clean terminal surface with plasma treatment
38. Position terminal against cathode tab
39. Apply pre-weld pressure (50N force)
40. Verify welding horn temperature is within range
41. Check ultrasonic generator frequency (20 kHz ± 50 Hz)
42. Calibrate welding energy setting (target: 800-1200 J)
43. Enable weld quality monitoring system
44. Verify argon gas purge flow rate (5 L/min)
45. Initialize welding sequence for cathode tab

### Stage 4: Cathode Tab Welding (Steps 46-60)

46. Activate ultrasonic welding horn on cathode tab
47. Apply welding pressure ramping profile (50N to 200N)
48. Monitor welding amplitude (target: 25-35 microns)
49. Control welding duration (target: 500-800 milliseconds)
50. Measure weld temperature using infrared sensor
51. Monitor acoustic emission signals for weld quality
52. Complete cathode tab welding cycle
53. Allow cooling period (3 seconds minimum)
54. Release welding pressure gradually
55. Retract ultrasonic welding horn
56. Inspect weld zone visually using magnification camera
57. Measure weld nugget dimensions (length and width)
58. Perform pull test on sample weld (every 50 units)
59. Record weld parameters in quality control database
60. Verify electrical continuity across weld joint

### Stage 5: Anode Tab Welding (Steps 61-75)

61. Rotate fixture to position anode tab in welding station
62. Load nickel terminal from automated feeder
63. Clean terminal with ultrasonic cleaning solution
64. Dry terminal using compressed air
65. Position terminal against anode tab
66. Apply pre-weld pressure (50N force)
67. Verify welding parameters for anode material
68. Adjust ultrasonic frequency if needed for copper foil
69. Enable protective gas atmosphere (argon)
70. Activate ultrasonic welding on anode tab
71. Monitor welding current and voltage in real-time
72. Control welding energy delivery (700-1000 J)
73. Complete anode tab welding cycle
74. Allow cooling period under protective atmosphere
75. Release welding pressure and retract horn

### Stage 6: Weld Quality Inspection (Steps 76-90)

76. Transfer welded stack to inspection station
77. Perform automated optical inspection of cathode weld
78. Check for weld spatter, cracks, or incomplete fusion
79. Measure weld penetration depth using ultrasonic probe
80. Perform automated optical inspection of anode weld
81. Verify weld dimensions against acceptance criteria
82. Conduct electrical resistance measurement (target: <1 mΩ)
83. Apply mechanical stress test at 80% of specification
84. Check for any tab deformation during stress test
85. Verify terminal alignment and positioning
86. Scan weld zones with thermal imaging camera
87. Document all inspection results with images
88. Apply pass/fail decision based on criteria matrix
89. Mark rejected units and route to rework station
90. Transfer accepted units to pouch preparation

### Stage 7: Pouch Material Preparation (Steps 91-105)

91. Retrieve laminated aluminum pouch material from storage
92. Verify pouch material thickness (0.15mm typical)
93. Check pouch material lot number and certification
94. Inspect pouch for any punctures or delamination
95. Cut pouch material to specified dimensions
96. Apply dimensional check using vision measurement
97. Clean pouch interior surfaces with lint-free cloth
98. Apply vacuum drying to remove moisture from pouch
99. Heat seal one side of pouch (150-170°C)
100. Verify seal width and integrity (minimum 5mm)
101. Perform seal strength test on sample pouches
102. Insert electrode stack into pouch cavity
103. Align tabs with pouch opening cutouts
104. Apply gentle vacuum to position stack correctly
105. Verify stack placement using X-ray imaging

### Stage 8: Pouch Sealing - First Phase (Steps 106-120)

106. Transfer pouch assembly to heat sealing station
107. Position pouch in precision sealing fixture
108. Ensure tabs are properly extended through openings
109. Apply protective insulation strips around tab exit points
110. Heat upper sealing bar to 160°C ± 5°C
111. Heat lower sealing bar to 160°C ± 5°C
112. Close sealing bars with controlled pressure (0.8 MPa)
113. Monitor seal temperature profile in real-time
114. Maintain seal pressure for 2.5 seconds
115. Allow cooling under pressure for 1.5 seconds
116. Release sealing pressure gradually
117. Open sealing bars and remove pouch assembly
118. Inspect seal appearance for uniformity
119. Check for any wrinkles or air bubbles in seal
120. Measure seal width using calibrated gauge

### Stage 9: Electrolyte Filling Preparation (Steps 121-135)

121. Transfer sealed pouch to dry room environment (dew point: -40°C)
122. Verify dry room conditions are stable
123. Place pouch in vacuum chamber for degassing
124. Apply vacuum to 10 mbar absolute pressure
125. Hold vacuum for 5 minutes to remove trapped air
126. Backfill chamber with dry argon gas
127. Transfer pouch to electrolyte filling station
128. Load electrolyte filling syringe with precise volume
129. Verify electrolyte composition and lot number
130. Check electrolyte moisture content (<20 ppm)
131. Measure electrolyte temperature (target: 25°C)
132. Position filling needle at pouch injection port
133. Pierce aluminum pouch at designated fill location
134. Verify needle penetration depth
135. Prepare vacuum-assisted filling sequence

### Stage 10: Electrolyte Injection (Steps 136-150)

136. Apply vacuum to pouch interior (50 mbar absolute)
137. Begin electrolyte injection at controlled rate (2 mL/min)
138. Monitor fill volume using precision flow meter
139. Inject calculated electrolyte volume (based on cell capacity)
140. Monitor for any leakage during filling process
141. Allow electrolyte wetting time (30 seconds)
142. Apply vacuum pulse to improve electrolyte penetration
143. Inject additional electrolyte if needed to compensate absorption
144. Complete final electrolyte volume injection
145. Withdraw filling needle slowly to prevent spillage
146. Apply immediate seal to injection port using heat sealer
147. Verify injection port seal integrity
148. Check final cell weight to confirm electrolyte volume
149. Inspect pouch for any electrolyte contamination on exterior
150. Transfer cell to temporary storage under inert atmosphere

### Stage 11: Final Sealing (Steps 151-160)

151. Position filled cell in final sealing fixture
152. Verify all previous seals are intact
153. Heat final sealing bars to 165°C
154. Apply sealing pressure of 1.0 MPa
155. Complete perimeter seal around entire pouch edge
156. Monitor seal temperature uniformity across length
157. Maintain seal for 3.0 seconds minimum
158. Cool seal zone under pressure
159. Release pressure and extract sealed cell
160. Perform leak test using helium mass spectrometry

### Stage 12: Formation Preparation (Steps 161-170)

161. Transfer sealed cells to formation equipment
162. Load cells into formation cycling fixtures
163. Connect cathode terminal to positive lead
164. Connect anode terminal to negative lead
165. Verify electrical connections have low resistance
166. Apply thermal coupling pads for temperature monitoring
167. Position cells in temperature-controlled chamber (25°C)
168. Verify chamber environmental conditions
169. Input cell parameters into formation control system
170. Initialize cell tracking database entry

### Stage 13: Formation Cycling (Steps 171-180)

171. Apply initial low-rate charge (C/20) for first cycle
172. Monitor cell voltage rise during initial charge
173. Limit first charge to 70% of nominal capacity
174. Hold voltage at 4.1V for 30 minutes
175. Discharge cell at C/20 rate to 3.0V cutoff
176. Record first cycle capacity and efficiency
177. Apply second formation charge at C/10 rate
178. Charge to full voltage (4.2V) with constant current
179. Switch to constant voltage mode when current drops
180. Complete formation charging when current reaches C/50

### Stage 14: Aging and Degassing (Steps 181-190)

181. Disconnect cells from formation equipment
182. Transfer cells to aging storage racks
183. Store cells at 45°C for initial aging period (24 hours)
184. Monitor cell voltage during aging process
185. Check for any voltage drop indicating self-discharge
186. Measure cell thickness to detect gas generation
187. Transfer cells requiring degassing to venting station
188. Open vent seal and extract generated gases
189. Reseal cell after degassing procedure
190. Return cells to aging storage for additional 48 hours

### Stage 15: Final Testing and Packaging (Steps 191-195)

191. Transfer aged cells to final testing station
192. Perform electrical characterization (capacity, impedance, IR)
193. Conduct high-potential insulation test (500V DC)
194. Grade cells based on capacity and performance parameters
195. Package qualified cells with protective materials and ship to customer

## Quality Control Points

Throughout the assembly process, automated quality control systems monitor critical parameters including weld strength, seal integrity, electrolyte volume accuracy, and formation cycling consistency. Statistical process control algorithms detect deviations and trigger automatic adjustments to maintain optimal production quality.

## Process Capabilities

- Production rate: 60-80 cells per hour per line
- First-pass yield: >95% for cells meeting specifications
- Welding consistency: ±5% variation in joint resistance
- Electrolyte filling accuracy: ±0.5% of target volume
- Formation efficiency: >92% coulombic efficiency on first cycle

## Safety and Environmental Controls

The battery assembly line operates under strict environmental controls with humidity management, temperature regulation, and inert gas atmosphere in critical zones. Safety interlocks prevent operation outside specified parameters, and automated fire suppression systems protect against thermal events during formation cycling.
