# Dehumidified Dry Room Construction for Battery Manufacturing

**Market Leader:** [Munters](/companies/munters)



A comprehensive guide to constructing and operating ultra-low humidity dry rooms for lithium-ion battery manufacturing, achieving -40°C dew point control.

## Phase 1: Site Selection and Preparation (Steps 1-15)

1. Conduct environmental assessment of facility location for baseline humidity and temperature conditions
2. Evaluate existing building structure for load-bearing capacity to support dehumidification equipment
3. Analyze floor vibration characteristics to ensure stability for sensitive battery manufacturing equipment
4. Survey electrical infrastructure capacity for 480V three-phase power requirements
5. Assess compressed air availability for pneumatic controls and desiccant regeneration systems
6. Map existing HVAC systems to identify potential moisture infiltration pathways
7. Review local building codes and cleanroom classifications required for battery manufacturing
8. Conduct risk assessment for fire suppression compatibility with lithium materials
9. Evaluate water drainage systems for condensate removal from dehumidifier regeneration
10. Measure ceiling height requirements for air handling unit installation (minimum 4 meters)
11. Calculate heat load from production equipment to size cooling capacity appropriately
12. Survey neighboring spaces for potential humidity migration through shared walls
13. Assess outdoor air intake locations to minimize moisture ingress during fresh air cycles
14. Map utility corridors for routing ductwork, electrical conduits, and monitoring cables
15. Document baseline particulate levels to establish cleanroom classification targets (ISO 7 or better)

19. Design sandwich panel construction with polyurethane foam core for thermal and moisture isolation
20. Plan flush-mounted LED lighting fixtures with sealed gaskets to prevent moisture leakage
21. Specify epoxy-coated flooring with chemical resistance to battery electrolytes and solvents
22. Design raised access flooring system for underfloor air distribution and cable management
23. Calculate wall thickness requirements to accommodate embedded utilities and vapor barriers
24. Plan ceiling plenum depth for laminar airflow distribution (minimum 600mm clearance)
25. Design reinforced ceiling grid to support HEPA filter housings and air distribution plenums
26. Specify hermetically sealed electrical penetrations with double O-ring gland designs
27. Plan sloped flooring toward drainage points with minimum 1% grade for condensate removal
28. Design modular wall panel system with interlocking edges and silicone compression seals
29. Specify stainless steel 316L for all metal components exposed to low humidity environment
30. Plan pressure-rated wall construction to maintain +15 Pa positive pressure differential

39. Install vapor barrier boots around vertical structural columns with multiple seal layers
