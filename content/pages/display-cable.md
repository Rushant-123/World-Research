# How to Make Display Cable (DisplayPort/HDMI)

**Top Market Leaders:**
- [Samsung Electronics](/companies/samsung-electronics)
- [Lg Display](/companies/lg-display)
- [Boe](/companies/boe)



High-speed digital video/audio cable connecting computer to display. DisplayPort (DP) or HDMI protocols transmit compressed/uncompressed video, audio, control signals. Modern MacBooks: Mini DisplayPort, USB-C with DP Alt Mode, or Thunderbolt (DP tunneling). Cable specifications: DP 1.4 (32.4 Gbps, 8K@60Hz), DP 2.0 (80 Gbps, 16K), HDMI 2.1 (48 Gbps, 10K@60Hz). Construction: shielded twisted-pair copper conductors, EMI shielding, strain relief, precision connectors. Global production: 500+ million cables/year.

**Step 1-15: [Copper Wire](copper-wire) Conductor Production**
Start with electrolytic copper rod (99.99% Cu, 8mm diameter, oxygen-free OFC grade). Wire drawing process: anneal copper (500-600°C, hydrogen atmosphere, soften), draw through tungsten carbide dies (8mm → 0.5mm, 10-20 passes, reduction ratio 15-25% per pass). Each pass: pull wire through lubricant-cooled die, diameter reduces, length increases. Intermediate annealing (every 3-5 passes, prevent work hardening, maintain ductility). Final conductor sizes for display cables: (1) Power/ground: 28-30 AWG (0.32-0.25mm diameter, carries DC power 3.3-5V, 0.5-1A per conductor). (2) High-speed differential pairs: 30-32 AWG (0.25-0.20mm, impedance-controlled, video data lanes). (3) Auxiliary/low-speed: 32-34 AWG (0.20-0.16mm, DDC/I2C control, hot-plug detect). Stranded vs solid: stranding (7-19 strands per conductor) improves flexibility, fatigue resistance. Production rate: 500-2,000 m/min (continuous process). Wire annealing final: 200-300°C (set temper, conductivity 100-102% IACS, resistance 0.017-0.018 Ω·mm²/m). Quality control: diameter measurement (laser micrometer, ±0.01mm tolerance), tensile testing (break strength 30-50 kg for 30 AWG), surface inspection (no scratches, oxidation).

**Step 16-35: Insulation and Jacketing with [PVC](pvc-polymer) or FEP**
Coat each conductor with dielectric insulation. Extrusion process: feed polymer pellets (PVC, polyethylene PE, fluorinated ethylene propylene FEP) into extruder (screw barrel, 180-220°C for PVC, 300-380°C for FEP). Molten polymer flows through crosshead die, coats moving copper wire concentrically. Insulation thickness: 0.15-0.30mm (depends on voltage rating, impedance target). **Dielectric properties:** PVC (relative permittivity εᵣ 3.0-4.0, loss tangent 0.01-0.02, low cost, flexible), PE (εᵣ 2.3, low loss 0.0002, better high-frequency performance), FEP (εᵣ 2.1, excellent thermal stability 200°C, low loss, expensive - used in premium cables). **Impedance control:** Differential pair impedance 100Ω±10% (DP/HDMI specification, matched to transmitter/receiver, minimize reflections). Formula: Z₀ = (87/√εᵣ) × ln(5.98h/(0.8w+t)), where h = dielectric thickness, w = conductor width, t = conductor thickness. For round coaxial (single-ended): Z₀ = (138/√εᵣ) × log(D/d), D = shield inner diameter, d = conductor outer diameter. Adjust insulation thickness, dielectric constant to achieve target impedance. **Cooling bath:** Water tank (20-30°C) solidifies extruded insulation, wire passes through at 50-200 m/min. **Color coding:** Add pigments to polymer (red, green, blue, white, black - identify conductors, video lanes, power, ground). **Outer jacket (optional):** Second extrusion layer over all conductors, typically PVC (1.0-2.0mm thick, mechanical protection, flexibility). Shore A hardness 70-90 (balance flexibility vs abrasion resistance).

**Step 36-58: Twisting Differential Pairs and Cable Assembly**
High-speed data requires differential signaling: two conductors (+ and –) carry inverted signals, common-mode noise cancels. Pair conductors for each video lane. **Twisting:** Pair machine twists two insulated wires (helical twist, 10-30 twists/meter, lay length 30-100mm). Tight twist improves common-mode rejection, reduces crosstalk (electromagnetic coupling between pairs). DisplayPort: 4 differential pairs (Main Link lanes 0-3, each 2.7-8.1 Gbps depending on HBR mode), plus auxiliary channel, hot-plug detect, power. HDMI: 3 TMDS differential pairs (video RGB or YCbCr data, 3.4-6.0 Gbps per pair), plus clock pair, CEC, DDC, power. **Cable structure (example DP cable):** 8 high-speed conductors (4 pairs, 30 AWG twisted), 2 auxiliary (32 AWG), 2 hot-plug detect (34 AWG), 1 power (28 AWG), 1 ground (28 AWG or shield drain). Total ~14 conductors. **Cabling:** Bundle all pairs and conductors, helical lay (counter-rotating layers, reduces overall cable twist). Filler yarns (nylon, aramid) maintain round cross-section, prevent conductor shifting. **Strain relief:** Aramid fiber (Kevlar) yarn strength member (500-1,000 denier, tensile strength 20-40 kg, prevents connector pullout). Lay aramid parallel to conductors or braid around bundle.

**Step 59-78: [EMI Shielding Foil](aluminum-foil) and Braiding**
Electromagnetic interference (EMI) shielding prevents external noise coupling, reduces radiation (FCC Class B limits, CE EMC compliance). **Foil shield:** Aluminum foil laminated to polyester film (Al 7-50 µm thick, PET 10-25 µm backing, total 20-75 µm). Wrap foil around conductor bundle (overlap or butt seam, 100% coverage). Aluminum facing inward (contacts drain wire) or outward (depends on design). Drain wire: bare tinned copper (30-34 AWG) runs along foil seam, provides low-impedance ground path (shield effectiveness SE 40-60 dB at 100 MHz). Foil advantages: lightweight, 100% coverage, low cost. Disadvantages: less flexible, poor durability with repeated flexing. **Braid shield:** Woven mesh of tinned copper wires (38-42 AWG individual ends, 16-32 carriers, 90-95% coverage). Braiding machine weaves carriers in helical pattern. Braid advantages: flexibility, mechanical strength, durability, SE 60-90 dB (higher than foil). Disadvantages: heavier, more expensive, incomplete coverage (small holes). **Combination foil + braid:** Premium cables use both (foil inner layer 100% coverage, braid outer layer mechanical protection + SE >80 dB). Triple-shielded: foil, braid, foil (achieves SE >100 dB, used in professional AV cables, harsh EMI environments). Shield termination: crimp or solder shield to connector shell (360° termination, continuous Faraday cage).

**Step 79-100: [Connector Shell](zinc-alloy-casting) Manufacturing**
DisplayPort/HDMI connectors: standardized geometry, precision alignment. **Shell material:** Zinc alloy (Zamak 3 or 5, die-cast, composition Zn-4%Al-0.04%Mg-balance, high strength, corrosion resistance). Alternative: aluminum alloy (lighter, more expensive), stainless steel (durability, higher cost), plastic (low-cost cables, less shielding). **Die casting:** Molten zinc alloy (420-450°C) injected into steel die (two halves, cavity shape = connector shell external form). Pressure 10-150 MPa, cycle time 30-90 seconds. Solidify, eject part, trim flash. Typical shell: 20-40mm long, 10-15mm wide, 5-8mm thick, weight 2-8 grams. **Machining:** CNC mill or drill holes for pins, mounting tabs, screws. Precision ±0.05mm (ensure pin alignment with mating connector). **Plating:** Clean (alkaline degrease, acid pickle), electroplate nickel (10-20 µm, corrosion barrier), then chrome (0.5-1.0 µm, hard, wear-resistant surface). Alternative: gold plate (1-3 µm over nickel, premium connectors, low contact resistance, no oxidation). **Connector types:** DisplayPort: 20-pin (standard DP, full-size), Mini DisplayPort 20-pin (smaller footprint, 7.5×4.6mm vs 16×4.8mm). HDMI Type A: 19-pin (standard, 13.9×4.45mm), Type C (Mini HDMI), Type D (Micro HDMI). USB-C with DP Alt Mode: 24-pin USB-C connector (8 high-speed pairs, multiplexed for USB 3.x or DP lanes).

**Step 101-120: Contact Pin Manufacturing and Insertion**
Connector pins: transmit signals, provide mechanical contact. **Material:** Phosphor bronze (Cu-5%Sn-0.2%P, spring temper, high conductivity 15-20% IACS, elastic modulus 110 GPa), beryllium copper (Cu-2%Be, stronger, more expensive), brass (lower cost, less spring force). **Stamping:** Coil feed precision stamping press, progressive die cuts pin shape (contact tip, retention barbs, solder cup or crimp barrel). Stamp rate: 200-600 strokes/min, 50,000-500,000 pins/hour. Pin dimensions: length 10-20mm, contact width 0.3-0.8mm, thickness 0.15-0.30mm (varies by connector type). **Plating:** Electroplate for conductivity, corrosion resistance. Standard: nickel underplate (1-2 µm, barrier), gold flash (0.1-0.5 µm over contact area, low insertion force, no oxidation). Premium: 1-3 µm gold over nickel (longer life, 1,000+ mating cycles). Low-cost: tin plate (3-10 µm, oxidizes over time, 10-50 mating cycles). **Insertion:** Automated pin insertion machine places pins into connector housing (plastic thermoplastic housing, molded polyester PBT or liquid crystal polymer LCP, UL94 V-0 flame rating). Pins press-fit or insert-molded, retention force 5-20 N per pin (prevent pullout during cable insertion/removal). **Pin arrangement:** DisplayPort: 20 pins in single row (1-4: Main Link lane 0, 5-8: lane 1, 9-12: lane 2, 13-16: lane 3, 17-18: Hot Plug Detect + Return, 19-20: Auxiliary + Ground). HDMI: 19 pins (alternating signal/ground, impedance-controlled).

**Step 121-142: [Flexible PCB](flexible-pcb) Adapter Board (Optional)**
Some connectors use internal PCB for signal routing, impedance matching, ESD protection. **Flexible PCB (FPC):** Polyimide substrate (25-50 µm thick, DuPont Kapton or equivalent), copper traces (12-35 µm, 1-2 oz/ft²), coverlay adhesive (polyimide + acrylic adhesive, 25 µm). **Fabrication:** (1) Laminate: bond copper to polyimide (heat press, 180-200°C, 2-5 MPa). (2) Photolithography: coat photoresist, expose pattern (UV 365nm, chrome photomask), develop, etch copper (ferric chloride or cupric chloride). Trace width/spacing: 0.1-0.2mm (impedance-controlled, 100Ω differential). (3) Coverlay lamination: protect traces, solder mask alternative. (4) Drill/punch vias (if multilayer), plate through-holes (electroless + electrolytic copper). (5) Surface finish: ENIG (electroless nickel immersion gold, 3-5 µm Ni, 0.05-0.1 µm Au), prevents oxidation, solderable. **Components (optional):** Surface-mount ESD protection diodes (transient voltage suppressors TVS, 0402 package, clamp voltage 6-15V, protect against electrostatic discharge ±15 kV contact, ±25 kV air per IEC 61000-4-2). Signal conditioning: resistors (49.9Ω series termination, impedance matching), capacitors (0.1-1.0 µF decoupling). **Assembly:** Reflow solder (SAC305 lead-free, peak 245-260°C), attach FPC to connector housing, wire bond or solder cable conductors to FPC pads.

**Step 143-160: Cable-to-Connector Termination**
**Preparation:** Strip outer jacket (10-30mm, precision stripping tool, expose conductors + shield). Strip individual conductor insulation (1-3mm, thermal or mechanical stripper). Tin conductor ends (solder dip or flux, prevents oxidation, improves solder joint). **Shield termination:** Fold shield braid back over jacket, crimp or solder to connector shell. Drain wire solder to shell ground pin. 360° shield coverage essential (EMI integrity, ground continuity). **Soldering:** Hand solder or automated soldering (soldering iron 300-370°C, lead-free solder SAC305 or SnAgCu, flux-core). Solder each conductor to corresponding pin cup (pad on FPC if present). Solder joint: shiny concave fillet, no cold joints, bridges, or excess solder. Inspect under magnification (10-30×, check for defects). **Crimping (alternative to solder):** Crimp ferrule or barrel over conductor + pin (pneumatic or manual crimping tool, 10-50 N force). Advantages: faster, no heat damage, better strain relief. Disadvantages: requires specialized tooling, less common for high-speed signals (solder preferred for impedance control). **Strain relief assembly:** Clamp aramid strength member to connector shell (crimp ferrule, molded strain relief boot). Overmold or assemble plastic boot (PVC, TPE, or silicone rubber, Shore A 40-70, prevents cable kink at connector junction, distributes bend stress). Boot length 20-50mm, tapers to cable diameter.

**Step 161-175: Overmolding and Final Assembly**
**Overmolding:** Injection mold thermoplastic elastomer (TPE) or rubber around connector-cable junction. (1) Place terminated connector in mold (two-piece steel or aluminum mold, cavity shape = final boot form). (2) Inject molten TPE (180-240°C, 5-20 MPa injection pressure). (3) Cool (water channels, 30-120 seconds cycle time), open mold, eject connector. **Material:** TPE (styrenic SBS, olefinic TPO, thermoplastic polyurethane TPU, Shore A 50-90, elongation 300-600%, tensile strength 5-15 MPa). Color: black (standard, UV resistant), white, gray (matches device color schemes). Overmold advantages: waterproof seal (IP54-IP67 possible with gasket integration), improved durability, professional appearance. **Label/branding:** Heat shrink sleeve or printed label (connector type, cable length, manufacturer, certification logos - HDMI Licensing LLC, VESA DisplayPort Certified). **Cable tie/management:** Velcro strap or twist tie for cable coiling. **Packaging:** Plastic bag (antistatic if ESD-sensitive), cardboard sleeve or blister pack. Label: specifications (resolution, refresh rate, cable length, gauge - e.g., "4K@60Hz, 2m, 28AWG"). Bulk packaging: coil 50-100 cables per carton (export to distribution centers).

**Step 176-185: Electrical Testing - [Signal Integrity](signal-integrity-tester) and Compliance**
**Continuity test:** Automated tester applies voltage to each conductor, verifies connection pin-to-pin (resistance <0.5Ω per conductor, open circuit = fail). Test all pins, power, ground, shield continuity. **Insulation resistance (IR):** Apply 500V DC between adjacent conductors, measure leakage current (IR >1 GΩ, ensures no insulation breakdown, shorts). High-pot test: 1,000-1,500V AC 1 minute (dielectric withstand, production 100% test or sample). **Impedance test (TDR):** Time-domain reflectometry (TDR) sends fast pulse down cable, measures impedance vs distance. Differential impedance 100Ω±10% (DP spec) or 100Ω±15% (HDMI spec). Reflections indicate impedance discontinuities (connector transitions, conductor spacing variations). Typical tolerance: impedance variation <±5Ω over cable length. **Insertion loss:** Vector network analyzer (VNA) measures S-parameters (S₂₁ forward transmission, S₁₁ return loss). Frequency sweep 100 kHz to 10 GHz. DP 1.4 HBR3 (8.1 Gbps): insertion loss <10 dB at 4.05 GHz (Nyquist frequency). HDMI 2.1 (12 Gbps lanes): <12 dB at 6 GHz. Longer cables: higher loss (copper skin effect, dielectric loss, proportional to length and √frequency). **Eye diagram test:** Transmit PRBS (pseudo-random bit sequence) at data rate (8.1 Gbps DP, 6.0 Gbps HDMI), capture waveform on oscilloscope. Eye opening (voltage margin, timing margin) must meet spec (eye height >200 mV, eye width >0.3 UI unit interval). Jitter <30 ps RMS. **Compliance certification:** VESA DisplayPort Certified (fee $5,000-10,000, logo license, test at authorized lab), HDMI Licensing (fee + per-unit royalty $0.04-0.15, certification required to use HDMI trademark).

**Step 186-195: Quality Control, Market, and Economics**
**Quality assurance:** Random sample testing (1-5% of production batch, full electrical + mechanical tests). Pull test: apply force to connector (50-100 N, verify strain relief, aramid anchoring). Bend test: flex cable 90° at connector junction, 1,000-10,000 cycles (accelerated life test, no conductor breakage, intermittent contact). Environmental: temperature cycling (–40 to +85°C, 100 cycles, solder joint integrity), humidity (85°C/85% RH, 500-1,000 hours, corrosion resistance). Salt spray (5% NaCl, 48-96 hours, shell/plating corrosion, ASTM B117). Defect rate target: <0.1% (100-1,000 ppm, depends on price tier). **Market:** Global display cable production 500+ million units/year (HDMI 60%, DisplayPort 25%, USB-C with DP Alt Mode 10%, legacy VGA/DVI 5%). Cable lengths: 1-2m (most common, desktop/laptop to monitor), 3-5m (home theater), 10-30m (commercial AV, requires active cables or signal repeaters >5m for 4K). **Pricing:** Consumer-grade 1m cable: $3-8 retail (wholesale $1-4, basic construction, 28-30 AWG, foil shield). Premium 2m: $15-30 (triple-shielded, 26-28 AWG, gold-plated, VESA/HDMI certified, braided nylon sleeve). Active cables (fiber optic hybrid or active signal boosting): $50-200 (10-30m lengths, support 4K/8K long distance, power from USB or external source). **Manufacturing cost breakdown:** Copper wire 30%, insulation/jacketing 10%, shielding 15%, connectors 25%, assembly labor 10%, testing/QC 5%, packaging 5%. Production location: China (70-80%, Luxshare, Foxconn, Lotes, JAE), Taiwan (10%), USA/Japan (5-10%, premium/specialized). Profit margin: 20-40% (commodity cables thin margin 10-20%, premium/certified higher 40-60%).
