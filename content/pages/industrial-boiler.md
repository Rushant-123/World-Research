## Manufacturing Process: Industrial Steam Boiler

### Boiler Drum Fabrication (Steps 1-35)

1. Review engineering specifications for 150,000 lb/hr steam capacity at 600 psig working pressure with 750°F superheat temperature
2. Select SA-516 Grade 70 carbon steel pressure vessel quality plate material with certified mill test reports and impact testing
3. Inspect steel plates using ultrasonic testing to detect internal laminations, inclusions, or defects before fabrication begins
4. Transfer engineering drawings to CNC plasma cutting table with nesting software to optimize material utilization and minimize waste
5. Cut drum shell courses to length accounting for longitudinal seam weld shrinkage of 3/16 inch per 10 feet
6. Bevel longitudinal seam edges to 37.5-degree angle with 1/8 inch land and 1/8 inch root opening for complete penetration welding
7. Roll steel plates in pyramid plate rolling machine with continuous monitoring to achieve 84-inch inside diameter cylindrical shape
8. Fit-up longitudinal seam with backing strip and strongbacks maintaining proper alignment and gap control throughout length
9. Preheat shell course to 200°F using ceramic pad heaters to reduce hydrogen cracking risk in thick section welding
10. Weld longitudinal seam using submerged arc welding process with Lincoln LA-100 flux and L-61 electrode wire at 32 volts and 650 amps
11. Perform radiographic examination of 100% of longitudinal seam length using iridium-192 source with interpretation per ASME Section VIII standards
12. Grind and repair any weld defects exceeding ASME acceptance criteria then re-radiograph repaired areas to verify quality
13. Machine drum head forming dies and lubricate with high-pressure forming compound for cold forming operations
14. Press form 2:1 elliptical drum heads from plate material using 3000-ton hydraulic press with progressive forming sequence
15. Trim formed heads to final diameter leaving 2-inch straight flange for attachment to drum shell courses
16. Cut nozzle openings in drum heads and shell for steam outlet, feedwater inlet, blowdown, manway, and instrumentation penetrations
17. Reinforce nozzle openings with integral reinforcing pads or increased shell thickness per ASME Section I requirements for pressure vessels
18. Fabricate and install internal baffle plates to promote proper water circulation and steam separation in drum upper section
19. Install steam separating equipment including cyclone separators and chevron-type mist eliminators to ensure 99.5% steam quality
20. Weld drum heads to shell courses using double-welded butt joints with complete penetration and radiographic examination
21. Attach lifting lugs, support lugs, and bracket attachments using full penetration welds with stress analysis verification
22. Stress relieve completed drum assembly in gas-fired furnace at 1150°F for 2 hours plus 1 hour per inch of thickness
23. Monitor stress relieving temperature using calibrated thermocouples at multiple locations with chart recorder documentation
24. Allow furnace to cool slowly at rate not exceeding 200°F per hour to prevent thermal shock and residual stress development
25. Perform post-weld heat treatment ultrasonic examination to verify no cracking occurred during thermal cycling process
26. Machine nozzle ends to proper dimensions and tolerances for flange attachment using CNC lathe with carbide tooling
27. Apply ASME Code stamp after authorized inspector reviews all documentation, material certifications, and NDE reports
28. Hydrostatic test drum at 1.5 times maximum allowable working pressure (900 psig) for minimum 10 minute hold period
29. Inspect all welds, nozzles, and drum surface during hydrostatic test for evidence of leakage, permanent deformation, or structural distress
30. Install internal feedwater distribution pipe with thermal sleeve to prevent thermal shock to drum shell from cold feedwater injection
31. Install chemical feed connection, continuous blowdown connection, and bottom blowdown connection with proper isolation valves
32. Sandblast interior drum surfaces to white metal finish and coat with FDA-approved epoxy for corrosion protection
33. Sandblast exterior drum surfaces and apply high-temperature resistant primer and finish coat paint system
34. Install drum level instrumentation including redundant level transmitters, sight glasses, and level control system connections
35. Package drum assembly with protective covering and prepare shipping documents for transport to final assembly facility

### Heat Exchanger Tube Installation (Steps 36-70)

36. Fabricate tube sheets from SA-266 Class 2 carbon steel forgings machined to precise thickness and flatness tolerances
37. Layout tube hole pattern on tube sheets using CNC drilling template with 4-inch triangular pitch spacing for optimal heat transfer
38. Drill tube holes using twist drills with cutting fluid, maintaining 0.002-inch diameter tolerance and perpendicularity to tube sheet face
39. Ream tube holes to final dimension with surface finish of 63 micro-inch Ra for proper tube-to-tubesheet seal
40. Groove tube holes using special grooving tool to create mechanical roll expansion grooves for enhanced tube joint strength
41. Inspect tube holes using go/no-go gauges and document all dimensional measurements in quality control records
42. Receive seamless carbon steel SA-178 Grade A boiler tubes in 2-inch OD x 0.120-inch wall thickness x 20-foot lengths
43. Inspect tubes for straightness, ovality, wall thickness variation, and surface defects per ASME Section I requirements
44. Perform eddy current testing on 100% of tubes to detect subsurface defects, wall thinning, or material inconsistencies
45. Cut tubes to required length using cold saw with square cut perpendicular to tube axis within 1 degree tolerance
46. Deburr tube ends inside and outside using rotary deburring tool to remove sharp edges and cutting residue
47. Clean tube interior using high-pressure water jet system to remove mill scale, rust, dirt, and contamination
48. Apply penetrant to tube ends and inspect for cracks or defects that could compromise tube-to-tubesheet joint integrity
49. Position lower tube sheet in assembly fixture and support with precision leveling system to maintain horizontal orientation
50. Insert tubes through lower tube sheet holes ensuring tubes are fully seated against tube sheet back face
51. Expand tubes into lower tube sheet using hydraulic tube expander with torque-controlled rolling mandrel
52. Roll tubes to 5-8% wall reduction creating mechanical interference fit and seal between tube and tube sheet
53. Inspect rolled joints using eddy current testing to verify proper expansion, no overrolling, and complete tube-to-tubesheet contact
54. Install spacer supports at 6-foot intervals along tube length to prevent vibration and maintain tube alignment
55. Position upper tube sheet onto tube bundle and align tubes with upper tube sheet holes using alignment guides
56. Support upper tube sheet with temporary scaffolding while expanding tubes into upper holes using same rolling procedure
57. Roll tubes into upper tube sheet with same parameters and quality verification as lower tube sheet expansion
58. Weld tube ends to tube sheet using GTAW process with ER70S-6 filler metal creating 1/8-inch seal weld
59. Grind seal welds smooth and inspect visually for cracks, porosity, or incomplete fusion defects
60. Install handhole covers on tube sheet inspection openings with Flexitallic gaskets torqued to proper specification
61. Fabricate water wall tubes from SA-210 Grade A1 seamless medium carbon steel in 3-inch OD configuration
62. Bend water wall tubes to match boiler contour using induction bending process with precise radius control
63. Install membrane bar between adjacent water wall tubes creating gas-tight furnace enclosure with welded attachment
64. Weld membrane bar to tubes using automatic GMAW process with copper backing bar and positive weld quality monitoring
65. Radiograph 10% of membrane welds per ASME Code requirements to verify weld quality and proper penetration
66. Assemble water wall panels into sections manageable for shipping and field erection, typically 20 feet wide by 30 feet high
67. Install refractory support studs on water wall outer surface for attachment of insulation and refractory materials
68. Hydrostatic test tube bundle assembly at 1.5 times working pressure checking for leakage at tube-to-tubesheet joints
69. Apply corrosion-resistant coating to external tube surfaces in areas subject to moisture or corrosive atmosphere
70. Prepare tube bundle for integration with drum assembly, documenting all quality tests and material certifications

### Pressure Testing and Quality Verification (Steps 161-180)

161. Review all fabrication documentation, material certifications, NDE reports, and quality control records before pressure testing
162. Verify all welding procedures and welder qualifications meet ASME Section I requirements for power boiler construction
163. Install temporary piping, flanges, and blank-off plates isolating boiler from auxiliary equipment during hydrostatic test
164. Fill boiler with demineralized water while venting all high points to remove trapped air from system
165. Install calibrated pressure gauge at lowest point of boiler visible to test personnel during pressurization
166. Pressurize boiler slowly to test pressure of 900 psig at rate not exceeding 100 psi per minute monitoring for leaks
167. Hold test pressure for minimum 10 minutes while inspecting all pressure parts, welds, flanges, and connections
168. Examine all welds for seepage, sweating, or leakage indicating defective weld quality requiring repair
169. Inspect tube-to-tubesheet joints for leakage at rolled connections or seal welds requiring re-rolling or weld repair
170. Check all flange connections for proper gasket seating and bolt tightness adjusting as required to eliminate leaks
171. Reduce pressure and repair any defects found during hydrostatic test using approved repair procedures
172. Retest repaired areas at full test pressure verifying leak-free performance after repair completion
173. Drain boiler completely and dry using forced air circulation preventing corrosion during storage or shipment
174. Document all test results, repairs made, and final acceptance test data in permanent boiler records
175. Apply ASME Code stamp and Data Report Form certifying boiler construction meets all Code requirements
176. Obtain Certificate of Inspection from authorized inspector and jurisdiction accepting boiler for operation
