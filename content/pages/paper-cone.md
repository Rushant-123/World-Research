# Speaker Paper Cone Manufacturing Process

**Market Leader:** [Panasonic](/companies/panasonic)



## Phase 1: Raw Material Preparation and Pulp Slurry Formulation

### Step 1: Wood Pulp Reception and Quality Inspection
Receive wood pulp bales from suppliers and verify moisture content is between 8-12%. Check for contaminants and perform visual inspection for color consistency and fiber quality.

### Step 2: Wood Pulp Storage and Conditioning
Store wood pulp in climate-controlled warehouse at 20-25°C and 50-60% relative humidity for at least 24 hours to stabilize moisture content before processing.

### Step 3: Pulp Bale Breaking and Separation
Load pulp bales onto breaking machine that mechanically separates compressed fibers into loose form suitable for hydration and mixing.

### Step 4: Initial Water Addition and Soaking
Add deionized water to separated wood pulp at ratio of 20:1 water to pulp by weight. Allow fibers to soak for 30 minutes to begin hydration process.

### Step 5: Synthetic Fiber Reception and Inspection
Receive synthetic fiber materials including aramid, polyester, or carbon fibers. Verify fiber length, diameter, and tensile strength specifications meet requirements.

### Step 6: Synthetic Fiber Pre-Treatment
Subject synthetic fibers to surface treatment with coupling agents to improve bonding with cellulose fibers and resin systems.

### Step 7: Fiber Blending Ratio Calculation
Calculate precise blending ratios based on desired cone properties: typically 60-70% wood pulp, 20-30% synthetic fibers, with 10% reserved for specialty additives.

### Step 8: Primary Pulping Process
Transfer hydrated wood pulp to industrial pulper operating at 1200-1500 RPM. Process for 15-20 minutes to separate individual fibers and create uniform suspension.

### Step 9: Synthetic Fiber Addition
Gradually introduce synthetic fibers into pulper while maintaining agitation. Add fibers slowly over 10-minute period to ensure even distribution throughout pulp mixture.

### Step 10: Defibration and Fiber Length Control
Pass fiber mixture through refiner with rotating discs to control fiber length and fibrillation level. Target average fiber length of 2-4mm for optimal acoustic properties.

### Step 11: Consistency Adjustment
Measure pulp consistency using laboratory equipment. Adjust water content to achieve target consistency of 3-5% for forming process.

### Step 12: pH Adjustment and Buffering
Test slurry pH and adjust to optimal range of 6.5-7.5 using buffering agents. Proper pH ensures fiber bonding and resin compatibility.

### Step 13: Dispersant Addition
Add dispersing agents at 0.1-0.3% by weight to prevent fiber agglomeration and ensure uniform distribution in slurry.

### Step 14: Retention Aid Incorporation
Introduce retention aid polymers at 0.05-0.15% concentration to improve fiber retention on forming screen and reduce material loss.

### Step 15: Defoaming Agent Addition
Add defoaming agents to eliminate air bubbles that could create defects in formed cone structure. Target foam level below 5% by volume.

### Step 16: Filler Material Preparation
Prepare mineral fillers such as kaolin clay or calcium carbonate if required for specific density or acoustic damping properties.

### Step 17: Filler Integration
Blend filler materials into pulp slurry at concentrations of 5-15% depending on desired cone density and acoustic characteristics.

### Step 18: Slurry Homogenization
Pass complete slurry mixture through inline homogenizer to ensure complete dispersion of all components and eliminate fiber clumps.

### Step 19: Temperature Control
Maintain slurry temperature at 18-22°C using heat exchangers to ensure optimal viscosity and forming characteristics.

### Step 20: Final Consistency Verification
Perform final consistency check using gravimetric method. Adjust if necessary to achieve precisely 4.0% ± 0.2% consistency.

### Step 21: Contaminant Screening
Pass slurry through fine mesh screens (100-200 mesh) to remove any remaining contaminants, oversized particles, or fiber bundles.

### Step 22: Stock Chest Storage
Transfer prepared slurry to agitated stock chest for temporary storage. Maintain gentle agitation to prevent settling while preserving fiber dispersion.

### Step 23: Flow Rate Calibration
Calibrate slurry feed pumps to deliver consistent flow rate of 15-25 liters per minute to forming equipment.

### Step 24: Inline Quality Monitoring
Install inline sensors to continuously monitor slurry consistency, pH, temperature, and flow rate throughout production run.

### Step 25: Slurry Degassing
Subject slurry to vacuum degassing at 0.3-0.5 bar absolute pressure to remove dissolved air that could cause voids in formed cone.

## Phase 2: Wet Forming Process

### Step 26: Forming Die Preparation
Clean forming die thoroughly using compressed air and lint-free cloths. Inspect for damage, wear, or residue from previous production.

### Step 27: Screen Installation
Install precisely manufactured forming screen onto die surface. Screen mesh size typically 60-100 mesh depending on desired cone surface finish.

### Step 28: Die Temperature Preheating
Preheat forming die to 40-50°C using integrated heating elements to facilitate water drainage and improve forming efficiency.

### Step 29: Vacuum System Verification
Test vacuum forming system to ensure proper operation at target pressure of 0.4-0.6 bar. Check all seals and connections for leaks.

### Step 30: Die Positioning and Alignment
Position forming die in precise alignment with slurry delivery system. Verify concentricity within 0.1mm tolerance.

### Step 31: Initial Slurry Application
Begin slurry flow onto rotating forming die. Start with lower flow rate and gradually increase to target rate over 30-second ramp period.

### Step 32: Vacuum Forming Initiation
Activate vacuum system as slurry contacts forming screen. Vacuum draws water through screen while retaining fibers on surface.

### Step 33: Cone Profile Development
Control slurry flow pattern and vacuum distribution to build up fiber layer following conical die profile. Monitor thickness uniformity.

### Step 34: Radial Thickness Control
Adjust slurry application pattern to achieve desired thickness distribution: typically 0.3-0.5mm at apex, 0.4-0.6mm at rim.

### Step 35: Fiber Orientation Management
Control die rotation speed (typically 60-120 RPM) and slurry application angle to optimize fiber orientation for acoustic performance.

### Step 36: Target Basis Weight Achievement
Continue forming process until target basis weight of 200-350 g/m² is achieved. Monitor using inline weight sensors.

### Step 37: Edge Formation
Use precision edge control system to form clean, uniform cone edge without fiber spillover or uneven buildup.

### Step 38: Final Water Extraction
Increase vacuum pressure to maximum (0.7-0.8 bar) for final 10-15 seconds to extract maximum water before cone removal.

### Step 39: Wet Strength Verification
Test formed cone wet strength by gentle probe. Must support own weight plus slight handling force without tearing.

### Step 40: Cone Release Preparation
Reduce vacuum gradually over 5-second period to prevent sudden pressure change that could damage delicate wet cone structure.

### Step 41: Die Rotation Cessation
Stop die rotation smoothly to avoid introducing tension or distortion into formed cone structure.

### Step 42: Automated Cone Removal
Activate pneumatic release system that gently lifts formed cone from die surface using precisely timed air pulse.

### Step 43: Transfer to Support Fixture
Transfer wet cone immediately to support fixture that maintains conical shape during subsequent processing steps.

### Step 44: Visual Defect Inspection
Inspect wet cone for obvious defects including holes, thin spots, thick areas, edge irregularities, or contamination.

### Step 45: Wet Cone Storage
Place supported wet cone in climate-controlled holding area at 22-24°C and 65% relative humidity until ready for pressing.

## Phase 3: Pressing and Consolidation

### Step 46: Pressing Die Preparation
Clean and inspect male and female pressing dies. Verify surfaces are smooth and free of residue or damage.

### Step 47: Die Temperature Setting
Heat pressing dies to optimal temperature of 80-100°C using integrated electric heaters. Monitor temperature uniformity across die surface.

### Step 48: Release Agent Application
Apply thin, uniform coating of food-grade release agent to die surfaces using automated spray system. Ensure complete coverage without excess.

### Step 49: Wet Cone Loading
Carefully position wet cone onto lower pressing die, ensuring concentric alignment and proper seating on die profile.

### Step 50: Die Closure and Alignment
Close pressing die assembly, bringing male and female dies together. Verify alignment using precision guide pins.

### Step 51: Initial Light Pressing
Apply initial light pressure of 0.5-1.0 MPa for 5 seconds to begin consolidation without damaging fragile wet structure.

### Step 52: Progressive Pressure Increase
Gradually increase pressing pressure over 15-second period, ramping from 1.0 to 4.0 MPa following programmed pressure profile.

### Step 53: Maximum Pressure Dwell
Maintain maximum pressure of 4.0-5.0 MPa for 30-45 seconds. This consolidates fiber structure and expresses additional water.

### Step 54: Water Extraction Monitoring
Monitor water extraction through drainage channels in pressing dies. Verify adequate water removal before pressure release.

### Step 55: Temperature Maintenance
Maintain die temperature at 90-95°C throughout pressing cycle to facilitate water removal and begin drying process.

### Step 56: Fiber Bonding Enhancement
Pressing heat and pressure activate natural lignin bonding and promote mechanical interlocking of fibers for improved strength.

### Step 57: Dimensional Stabilization
Pressing operation sets critical dimensions including cone angle, throat diameter, rim diameter, and thickness profile.

### Step 58: Pressure Release Sequence
Release pressing pressure gradually over 10-second period to prevent spring-back or distortion of cone shape.

### Step 59: Die Opening and Dwell
Open pressing dies slowly and allow pressed cone to remain on lower die for 5-10 seconds to stabilize before removal.

### Step 60: Pressed Cone Extraction
Use vacuum pickup or mechanical gripper to carefully remove pressed cone from die surface without applying excessive force.

### Step 61: Post-Press Inspection
Inspect pressed cone for proper consolidation, uniform density, smooth surfaces, and dimensional accuracy.

### Step 62: Edge Condition Verification
Examine cone edge for clean formation, uniform thickness, and absence of delamination or cracking.

### Step 63: Moisture Content Measurement
Measure pressed cone moisture content using moisture analyzer. Target range is 35-45% for optimal subsequent drying.

### Step 64: Dimensional Measurement
Measure critical dimensions including throat diameter, rim diameter, cone height, and wall thickness using precision instruments.

### Step 65: Transfer to Drying Fixture
Mount pressed cone onto specialized drying fixture that maintains shape and provides support during drying process.

## Phase 4: Drying Process

### Step 66: Drying System Preparation
Preheat industrial drying oven or conveyor dryer to target temperature of 120-140°C. Verify uniform temperature distribution.

### Step 67: Humidity Control Setup
Configure drying chamber humidity control to maintain 20-30% relative humidity during initial drying phase.

### Step 68: Air Flow Calibration
Set drying air flow rate to 2-4 m/s velocity across cone surfaces. Ensure laminar flow pattern for uniform drying.

### Step 69: Initial Drying Phase
Begin drying at lower temperature of 100-110°C for first 10-15 minutes to prevent surface case hardening and internal moisture trapping.

### Step 70: Progressive Temperature Increase
Gradually increase drying temperature from 110°C to 135°C over 20-minute period following programmed temperature profile.

### Step 71: Moisture Gradient Management
Control drying rate to maintain acceptable moisture gradient between cone surfaces and interior, preventing warping or cracking.

### Step 72: Rotation During Drying
Rotate drying fixtures at 2-3 RPM to ensure uniform heat exposure on all cone surfaces and prevent uneven drying.

### Step 73: Intermediate Moisture Monitoring
Measure cone moisture content at 15-minute intervals using infrared moisture sensors or periodic gravimetric sampling.

### Step 74: Dimensional Stability Monitoring
Track cone dimensions during drying to detect any shrinkage or distortion. Expected shrinkage is 2-4% in diameter.

### Step 75: High Temperature Dwell
Maintain peak drying temperature of 130-140°C for 30-40 minutes to reduce moisture content to target level.

### Step 76: Final Moisture Target Achievement
Continue drying until moisture content reaches 6-8%, measured using capacitive moisture sensors or laboratory oven method.

### Step 77: Controlled Cool-Down Initiation
Begin temperature reduction phase, decreasing oven temperature from 135°C to 80°C over 15-minute period.

### Step 78: Humidity Reintroduction
Gradually increase chamber humidity to 40-50% during cool-down to prevent excessive brittleness in dried cone.

### Step 79: Equilibrium Conditioning
Hold cones at 60-70°C and 45-50% humidity for 20-30 minutes to allow moisture redistribution and stress relief.

### Step 80: Final Cooling to Ambient
Cool cones to ambient temperature (20-25°C) gradually over 30-minute period to prevent thermal shock and dimensional change.

### Step 81: Post-Drying Moisture Verification
Measure final moisture content using laboratory oven method. Acceptable range is 6-8% for optimal processing and performance.

### Step 82: Visual Inspection for Drying Defects
Inspect dried cones for surface cracking, warping, delamination, discoloration, or other drying-related defects.

### Step 83: Dimensional Verification
Measure all critical dimensions on dried cones and compare to specifications. Verify dimensions are within tolerance ranges.

### Step 84: Acoustic Preliminary Testing
Perform preliminary acoustic testing on sample cones using tap test or laser vibrometry to verify resonant characteristics.

### Step 85: Mechanical Strength Testing
Test representative sample cones for tensile strength, tear resistance, and flexural modulus. Verify meets minimum specifications.

## Phase 5: Resin Impregnation

### Step 86: Resin System Selection
Select appropriate resin system based on application requirements. Common options include phenolic, acrylic, or polyurethane resins.

### Step 87: Resin Mixing and Preparation
Mix resin components according to manufacturer specifications. Typical formulation includes resin, hardener, and performance additives.

### Step 88: Viscosity Adjustment
Adjust resin viscosity to 50-150 cP using appropriate solvents or diluents for optimal penetration into cone structure.

### Step 89: Resin Temperature Control
Maintain resin temperature at 20-25°C using temperature-controlled mixing vessel to ensure consistent viscosity and handling properties.

### Step 90: Resin Filtration
Filter prepared resin through 50-micron filter to remove any particulate matter that could cause surface defects or clog application equipment.

### Step 91: Impregnation Bath Preparation
Fill impregnation bath with filtered resin to appropriate depth. Install overflow system to maintain consistent resin level.

### Step 92: Cone Pre-Cleaning
Clean dried cones using compressed air to remove any dust or loose fibers that could interfere with resin adhesion.

### Step 93: Initial Resin Application Method Selection
Choose between dip coating, spray application, or roll coating based on production volume and desired resin penetration depth.

### Step 94: Dip Coating Process
Slowly immerse cone into resin bath at controlled rate of 5-10 cm/second. Maintain immersion for 10-30 seconds depending on desired pickup.

### Step 95: Dwell Time Control
Control dwell time in resin bath to achieve target resin pickup of 20-40% by weight relative to dry cone mass.

### Step 96: Controlled Withdrawal
Withdraw cone from resin bath at controlled rate of 3-5 cm/second to ensure uniform coating and prevent drips or runs.

### Step 97: Excess Resin Drainage
Position coated cone in drainage fixture for 30-60 seconds to allow excess resin to drain off and return to bath.

### Step 98: Vacuum Impregnation Enhancement
Apply vacuum of 0.3-0.5 bar after initial coating to draw resin deeper into cone structure for improved penetration.

### Step 99: Pressure Impregnation Option
Alternatively, apply positive pressure of 2-3 bar to force resin into cone pores for maximum impregnation depth.

### Step 100: Resin Distribution Optimization
Rotate cone slowly during drainage period to promote even resin distribution and prevent pooling in lower areas.

### Step 101: Surface Resin Leveling
Use soft brush or air knife to level surface resin and eliminate drips, runs, or thick spots that could affect acoustic performance.

### Step 102: Staged Resin Application
For specialized applications, apply multiple resin layers with different formulations to create graded property profiles.

### Step 103: Resin Pickup Measurement
Weigh impregnated cone to verify resin pickup is within target range of 20-40% by weight of original dry cone mass.

### Step 104: Visual Inspection of Coating
Inspect resin coating for uniform coverage, absence of dry spots, appropriate gloss level, and freedom from defects.

### Step 105: Initial Resin Cure Setup
Transfer impregnated cones to curing fixtures that maintain proper geometry during resin cure process.

## Phase 6: Resin Curing and Heat Treatment

### Step 106: Curing Oven Preparation
Preheat curing oven to initial curing temperature of 60-80°C. Verify temperature uniformity throughout oven chamber.

### Step 107: Ventilation System Activation
Activate oven ventilation system to remove volatile organic compounds released during resin cure. Set exhaust rate to 10-15 air changes per hour.

### Step 108: Initial Low-Temperature Cure
Begin cure cycle at 70°C for 30-45 minutes to initiate polymerization while allowing solvent evaporation without surface skinning.

### Step 109: Progressive Temperature Ramp
Increase oven temperature gradually from 70°C to 120°C over 60-minute period following programmed cure profile.

### Step 110: Mid-Temperature Cure Phase
Maintain temperature at 120-130°C for 60-90 minutes to advance resin cure to 70-80% completion.

### Step 111: High-Temperature Post-Cure
Raise temperature to 150-170°C for final 30-60 minutes to complete cure and maximize resin cross-link density.

### Step 112: Cure Monitoring
Monitor cure progress using temperature probes embedded in sample cones or by periodic extraction and testing of cure degree.

### Step 113: VOC Emission Monitoring
Monitor volatile organic compound emissions throughout cure cycle to ensure adequate ventilation and environmental compliance.

### Step 114: Dimensional Stability During Cure
Monitor cone dimensions during cure process to detect any warping or distortion caused by resin shrinkage or thermal expansion.

### Step 115: Controlled Cool-Down
Reduce oven temperature gradually from 170°C to 60°C over 45-minute period to minimize thermal stress and prevent cracking.

### Step 116: Ambient Temperature Stabilization
Remove cones from oven when temperature reaches 40-50°C. Allow final cooling to ambient temperature over 30-60 minutes.

### Step 117: Post-Cure Conditioning
Store cured cones in controlled environment at 23°C and 50% relative humidity for 24 hours to allow stress relaxation and equilibration.

### Step 118: Cure Verification Testing
Test cure degree on representative samples using differential scanning calorimetry or acetone rub test. Target minimum 95% cure.

### Step 119: Surface Hardness Testing
Measure surface hardness using Shore D durometer. Typical target range is 65-80 Shore D depending on resin system.

### Step 120: Glass Transition Temperature Measurement
Verify glass transition temperature (Tg) of cured resin meets specifications, typically 80-120°C for speaker cone applications.

### Step 121: Visual Inspection Post-Cure
Inspect cured cones for surface defects including blisters, cracks, delamination, resin-starved areas, or excessive gloss variation.

### Step 122: Dimensional Verification Post-Cure
Measure all critical dimensions on cured cones. Account for expected cure shrinkage of 1-3% in forming die design.

### Step 123: Weight Documentation
Record final cured cone weight for process control and specification verification. Typical weight range is 3-8 grams depending on size.

### Step 124: Surface Preparation for Coating
Lightly sand or abrade cone surface if additional coating or damping treatment will be applied in subsequent operations.

### Step 125: Quality Sorting and Grading
Sort cured cones into quality grades based on dimensional accuracy, surface quality, and freedom from defects.

## Phase 7: Trimming and Edge Finishing

### Step 126: Trimming Equipment Setup
Install appropriate diameter trimming tool in precision lathe or CNC trimming machine. Verify cutting edge sharpness and condition.

### Step 127: Cone Mounting for Trimming
Mount cone securely in trimming fixture using vacuum chuck or mechanical gripper that maintains concentric alignment.

### Step 128: Throat Diameter Trimming
Trim cone throat opening to precise diameter specification using rotating cutting tool. Typical tolerance is ±0.05mm.

### Step 129: Rim Diameter Trimming
Trim outer rim of cone to final diameter specification. Ensure perpendicularity to cone axis within 0.1mm tolerance.

### Step 130: Edge Bevel Creation
Create beveled edge profile on rim using angled cutting tool. Typical bevel angle is 30-45 degrees for surround attachment area.

### Step 131: Edge Surface Finishing
Smooth trimmed edges using fine abrasive or burnishing tool to eliminate rough spots that could cause premature failure.

### Step 132: Dimensional Verification Post-Trim
Measure throat diameter, rim diameter, and cone height using precision measuring instruments. Verify all dimensions meet specifications.

### Step 133: Edge Quality Inspection
Inspect trimmed edges under magnification for chips, tears, delamination, or other defects that could compromise performance.

### Step 134: Dust and Debris Removal
Remove all trimming dust and debris using vacuum system and compressed air. Ensure cone surfaces are completely clean.

### Step 135: Trim Waste Collection
Collect trimming waste material for recycling or disposal according to environmental regulations and company policies.

## Phase 8: Surround Attachment Area Preparation

### Step 136: Adhesive Selection
Select appropriate adhesive for surround attachment based on surround material type. Common options include contact cement, hot melt, or reactive adhesives.

### Step 137: Surface Treatment Application
Apply surface treatment or primer to rim attachment area if required for specific adhesive system or surround material.

### Step 138: Treatment Drying
Allow surface treatment to dry completely, typically 15-30 minutes at ambient temperature or 5-10 minutes at 60°C.

### Step 139: Adhesive Mixing Preparation
For two-component adhesives, mix resin and hardener according to manufacturer specifications immediately before application.

### Step 140: Pot Life Monitoring
Monitor adhesive pot life carefully. Typical working time is 30-60 minutes depending on adhesive chemistry and temperature.

### Step 141: Adhesive Application Method
Apply adhesive to rim area using precision dispensing system, brush, or spray application depending on adhesive viscosity and production volume.

### Step 142: Adhesive Bead Profile Control
Control adhesive application to achieve uniform bead width of 2-4mm and appropriate thickness for optimal bonding.

### Step 143: Open Time Management
Manage adhesive open time according to manufacturer specifications. Typical open time ranges from 30 seconds to 10 minutes.

### Step 144: Tack Development
For contact adhesives, allow appropriate tack development time before surround attachment. Test tack level by light finger touch.

### Step 145: Quality Verification of Adhesive Application
Inspect adhesive application for uniform coverage, appropriate thickness, absence of voids or skips, and proper placement.

## Phase 9: Frequency Response Testing

### Step 146: Anechoic Chamber Preparation
Prepare acoustic testing environment with controlled temperature (23±2°C), humidity (50±5% RH), and minimal background noise (<20 dB SPL).

### Step 147: Test Fixture Assembly
Assemble cone into standardized test fixture that simulates final speaker assembly conditions including voice coil and spider attachment.

### Step 148: Measurement Microphone Setup
Position calibrated measurement microphone at standard distance (typically 1 meter on-axis) from cone surface.

### Step 149: Signal Generator Configuration
Configure audio signal generator to produce test signals including sine wave sweeps, pink noise, or multi-tone signals.

### Step 150: Amplifier Calibration
Calibrate test amplifier to deliver specified input power level, typically 1 watt (2.83V into 8 ohms) for sensitivity measurements.

### Step 151: Frequency Sweep Execution
Execute frequency sweep from 20 Hz to 20 kHz at octave or third-octave resolution to measure frequency response curve.

### Step 152: Sensitivity Measurement
Measure acoustic output level at 1 kHz at 1 meter distance with 1 watt input. Typical specification is 85-90 dB SPL.

### Step 153: Frequency Response Analysis
Analyze measured frequency response curve for smoothness, breakup modes, resonant peaks, and overall shape.

### Step 154: Resonance Frequency Identification
Identify fundamental resonance frequency (Fs) of cone structure. Typical range for small speakers is 80-150 Hz.

### Step 155: Q Factor Measurement
Measure mechanical and electrical Q factors by analyzing impedance curve and free-air response characteristics.

### Step 156: Breakup Mode Detection
Identify cone breakup modes that appear as resonant peaks in high frequency range, typically above 2-5 kHz.

### Step 157: Phase Response Measurement
Measure acoustic phase response across frequency range to verify time-domain behavior and minimum phase characteristics.

### Step 158: Harmonic Distortion Testing
Measure total harmonic distortion (THD) at various frequencies and input levels. Typical specification is <1% THD at rated power.

### Step 159: Intermodulation Distortion Testing
Perform intermodulation distortion testing using two-tone test signals to evaluate nonlinear behavior.

### Step 160: Impulse Response Measurement
Capture impulse response to evaluate transient behavior, ringing, and time-domain performance characteristics.

### Step 161: Waterfall Plot Generation
Generate cumulative spectral decay (waterfall) plot to visualize resonance decay behavior over time and frequency.

### Step 162: Directivity Pattern Measurement
Measure off-axis response at multiple angles (typically 0°, 15°, 30°, 45°, 60°) to characterize directivity pattern.

### Step 163: Power Handling Evaluation
Subject test samples to elevated power levels to evaluate thermal and mechanical limits. Monitor for voice coil heating and mechanical failure.

### Step 164: Frequency Response Comparison
Compare measured response to target specification and reference samples. Verify performance meets design requirements.

### Step 165: Data Logging and Documentation
Record all measurement data, test conditions, and cone identification information in database for traceability and statistical analysis.

## Phase 10: Quality Inspection and Final Testing

### Step 166: Visual Inspection Protocol
Perform systematic visual inspection of cone surface, edges, geometry, and overall appearance under controlled lighting conditions.

### Step 167: Surface Defect Detection
Inspect for surface defects including pinholes, scratches, contamination, resin voids, fiber bundles, or color variations.

### Step 168: Dimensional Inspection Setup
Set up precision measuring equipment including calipers, micrometers, optical comparators, and coordinate measuring machines.

### Step 169: Throat Diameter Measurement
Measure throat diameter at minimum four points around circumference. Calculate average and verify within specified tolerance.

### Step 170: Rim Diameter Measurement
Measure rim diameter at minimum four points around circumference. Verify concentricity relative to throat diameter.

### Step 171: Cone Height Measurement
Measure cone height from mounting plane to apex. Typical specification tolerance is ±0.1mm.

### Step 172: Wall Thickness Measurement
Measure wall thickness at specified locations using ultrasonic thickness gauge or destructive cross-sectioning of sample cones.

### Step 173: Cone Angle Verification
Verify cone angle using optical measurement or trigonometric calculation from diameter and height measurements.

### Step 174: Mass Measurement
Weigh each cone on precision balance with 0.001 gram resolution. Verify mass is within specification range.

### Step 175: Balance and Symmetry Testing
Test cone balance by mounting on precision spindle and checking for wobble or runout. Maximum allowable runout is typically 0.05mm.

### Step 176: Stiffness Testing
Measure cone stiffness by applying calibrated force and measuring deflection. Verify stiffness meets specification for intended application.

### Step 177: Flexibility Testing
Perform flex testing to evaluate cone flexibility and freedom from excessive stiffness that could limit low-frequency response.

### Step 178: Surface Hardness Verification
Measure surface hardness at multiple locations using Shore D durometer. Verify consistency across cone surface.

### Step 179: Moisture Content Verification
Verify final moisture content using moisture analyzer. Specification typically 5-7% for optimal performance and stability.

### Step 180: Adhesion Testing
Perform tape adhesion test on resin coating to verify adequate bonding between resin and fiber substrate.

### Step 181: Edge Integrity Inspection
Inspect edge region for delamination, cracking, or other defects that could initiate failure during use.

### Step 182: Optical Surface Quality Assessment
Evaluate surface finish quality using gloss meter and visual comparison to reference standards.

### Step 183: Accelerated Aging Testing
Subject sample cones to accelerated aging conditions including elevated temperature, humidity cycling, and UV exposure to verify long-term stability.

### Step 184: Environmental Stress Testing
Expose sample cones to temperature extremes (-20°C to +80°C) and humidity extremes (10% to 90% RH) to verify environmental durability.

### Step 185: Drop and Impact Testing
Perform drop testing on packaged cones to verify resistance to shipping and handling damage.

### Step 186: Statistical Process Control
Monitor production using statistical process control charts for key parameters including dimensions, weight, and acoustic performance.

### Step 187: Defect Classification and Sorting
Classify any detected defects by type and severity. Sort cones into accept, rework, or reject categories.

### Step 188: Acceptance Testing Summary
Compile results from all inspection and testing operations. Verify cone meets all specification requirements for release to inventory.

## Phase 11: Final Processing and Packaging

### Step 189: Final Cleaning
Clean accepted cones using compressed air or soft brush to remove any dust or handling residue. Ensure cleanliness for packaging.

### Step 190: Protective Coating Application
Apply optional protective coating or handling treatment if specified for particular customer or application requirements.

### Step 191: Individual Cone Packaging
Package individual cones in protective containers, typically plastic trays or foam inserts that prevent contact damage during storage and shipping.

### Step 192: Lot Identification and Labeling
Apply identification labels containing manufacturing date code, lot number, part number, and traceability information.

### Step 193: Quantity Verification and Boxing
Count packaged cones and load into shipping containers in specified quantities, typically 100-500 pieces per box depending on size.

### Step 194: Final Documentation
Complete production records including quantity produced, quality test results, material lot numbers, and equipment used for full traceability.

### Step 195: Release to Inventory
Release finished cones to inventory system for storage in climate-controlled warehouse awaiting shipment to speaker assembly operations or external customers.
