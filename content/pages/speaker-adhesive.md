# Speaker Assembly Adhesive Manufacturing Process

**Market Leader:** [Henkel](/companies/henkel)



## Phase 1: Raw Material Preparation and Quality Control

1. Receive epoxy resin shipment in 200L drums and verify batch documentation
2. Check resin viscosity at 25°C using Brookfield viscometer (target: 8000-12000 cP)
3. Measure epoxy equivalent weight (EEW) using titration method (target: 185-192 g/eq)
4. Verify resin color index is below 2 on Gardner scale
5. Test resin moisture content using Karl Fischer titration (must be <0.1%)
6. Store epoxy resin in temperature-controlled area at 20-25°C
7. Receive amine hardener in sealed containers with nitrogen blanket
8. Measure hardener amine hydrogen equivalent weight (AHEW) at 95-105 g/eq
9. Check hardener viscosity at 25°C (target: 50-200 cP)
10. Verify hardener water content is below 0.2%
11. Test hardener color to ensure clarity and absence of crystallization
12. Store hardener in dry environment with desiccant protection
13. Inspect filler material (silica or calcium carbonate) for particle size distribution
14. Measure filler median particle size at 5-15 microns using laser diffraction
15. Check filler moisture content (must be <0.5%)
16. Verify filler surface treatment with silane coupling agent
17. Test filler oil absorption number (target: 30-50 g/100g)
18. Store filler in sealed containers with humidity control
19. Receive flexibilizer (polyether or rubber modifier) and check specification sheet
20. Measure flexibilizer molecular weight using GPC (target: 2000-4000 Da)
21. Verify flexibilizer hydroxyl number by acetylation method
22. Check flexibilizer compatibility with epoxy system through miscibility test
23. Test flexibilizer viscosity at process temperature
24. Store flexibilizer away from oxidizing agents
25. Prepare deionized water for equipment cleaning and testing
26. Calibrate all weighing scales and measuring instruments
27. Verify temperature control equipment accuracy (±0.5°C)
28. Check mixing equipment cleanliness and functionality
29. Inspect all containers and vessels for contamination
30. Prepare clean room environment with controlled humidity (<50% RH)

## Phase 2: Epoxy Resin Component Formulation

31. Calculate precise stoichiometric ratio based on EEW and AHEW values
32. Determine optimal resin-to-hardener ratio (typically 100:30-50 by weight)
33. Weigh epoxy resin base component to ±0.1% accuracy
34. Transfer resin to jacketed mixing vessel with temperature control
35. Heat resin to 40-50°C to reduce viscosity for easier mixing
36. Begin slow agitation at 50-100 RPM using anchor or helical mixer
37. Calculate filler loading percentage (typically 20-40% by weight)
38. Pre-dry filler material at 110°C for 2 hours to remove moisture
39. Cool filler to room temperature in desiccator
40. Slowly add filler to resin under continuous mixing
41. Increase mixing speed to 200-300 RPM to disperse filler particles
42. Monitor mixing temperature to prevent exceeding 60°C
43. Continue mixing for 30-45 minutes until uniform dispersion achieved
44. Check mixture viscosity periodically during mixing
45. Add flexibilizer component at 5-15% by weight of resin
46. Mix flexibilizer thoroughly for 15-20 minutes
47. Deaerate mixture under vacuum (25-30 inHg) for 20 minutes
48. Check for complete removal of air bubbles visually
49. Measure final Part A viscosity at 25°C (target: 15,000-30,000 cP)
50. Verify Part A density using pycnometer method
51. Test Part A shelf stability by storing sample at 50°C for 7 days
52. Check Part A gel time inhibitor if extended pot life required
53. Add thixotropic agent (fumed silica) at 1-3% if anti-sag properties needed
54. Mix thixotropic agent using high-shear mixer for 10-15 minutes
55. Perform rheology testing to measure thixotropic index
56. Filter Part A through 50-100 micron filter to remove agglomerates
57. Transfer Part A to storage containers with moisture barrier
58. Label containers with batch number, date, and specification data
59. Store Part A component at controlled temperature until packaging
60. Record all process parameters in batch production record

## Phase 3: Hardener Component Formulation

61. Weigh hardener base material to ±0.1% accuracy
62. Transfer hardener to clean mixing vessel under inert atmosphere
63. Add accelerator (tertiary amine or imidazole) at 0.5-2% if faster cure needed
64. Mix accelerator thoroughly at 100-150 RPM for 10 minutes
65. Add reactive diluent if viscosity reduction required (5-15% by weight)
66. Mix diluent for 15-20 minutes ensuring complete dissolution
67. Check hardener mixture viscosity at 25°C
68. Add pigment or dye if color coding required (typically 0.1-1%)
69. Disperse pigment using high-shear mixer or bead mill
70. Verify color uniformity and consistency
71. Add corrosion inhibitor if metal substrate adhesion required
72. Mix inhibitor at 0.5-2% by weight for 10 minutes
73. Check pH of hardener mixture (should be in acceptable range for application)
74. Test hardener reactivity using model compound method
75. Measure amine value by titration to confirm stoichiometry
76. Deaerate hardener mixture under vacuum for 15 minutes
77. Filter hardener through 50 micron filter
78. Check hardener clarity and absence of precipitation
79. Measure final Part B viscosity at 25°C (target: 100-500 cP)
80. Verify Part B density and compare to specification
81. Test Part B stability at elevated temperature (40°C for 14 days)
82. Check for skin formation or viscosity increase during storage
83. Transfer Part B to storage containers with moisture exclusion
84. Purge container headspace with nitrogen or dry air
85. Label Part B containers with batch information
86. Store Part B in cool, dry location away from Part A
87. Perform compatibility test between Part A and Part B
88. Record all hardener formulation parameters
89. Retain samples of both components for quality verification
90. Update formulation database with batch-specific data

## Phase 4: Mixing and Application Testing

91. Prepare test mixing at specified ratio (e.g., 100:40 by weight)
92. Use precision balance to weigh both components accurately
93. Combine Part A and Part B in clean mixing container
94. Mix manually or mechanically for 2-3 minutes until homogeneous
95. Check mixed adhesive for uniform color and consistency
96. Measure mixed viscosity immediately after mixing (target: 8,000-15,000 cP)
97. Record initial mixed viscosity and temperature
98. Monitor viscosity increase over time to determine pot life
99. Measure working time until viscosity doubles (target: 15-30 minutes at 25°C)
100. Test open time by applying to substrate and monitoring tack
101. Check gel time using wooden stick test method (target: 30-60 minutes)
102. Verify no exothermic runaway during gel formation
103. Monitor maximum exotherm temperature in 100g mass (<150°C)
104. Apply mixed adhesive to speaker component test samples
105. Test application with manual dispensing gun
106. Verify pumpability through pneumatic dispensing equipment
107. Check bead formation and shape retention on vertical surfaces
108. Test automated meter-mix-dispense system performance
109. Verify correct mix ratio maintained through dispensing equipment
110. Apply adhesive to voice coil assembly test fixture
111. Bond magnet to basket assembly using adhesive
112. Apply adhesive to surround-to-frame joint
113. Test adhesive flow and wetting on different speaker materials
114. Verify adhesive does not contaminate acoustic components
115. Check for proper gap filling capability (0.1-0.5mm gaps)
116. Test adhesive penetration into porous speaker materials
117. Verify no air entrapment during application
118. Apply adhesive in various patterns (dots, beads, full coating)
119. Test low-temperature application performance (15-20°C)
120. Test high-temperature application performance (30-35°C)

## Phase 5: Curing and Performance Validation

121. Cure bonded test samples at room temperature (23°C) for 24 hours
122. Monitor cure progression using Shore hardness measurements
123. Measure hardness at 1 hour, 4 hours, 8 hours, and 24 hours
124. Record final Shore A or Shore D hardness (target: 60-80 Shore D)
125. Test accelerated cure at elevated temperature (60°C for 2 hours)
126. Compare room temperature cure vs. accelerated cure properties
127. Verify full cure using differential scanning calorimetry (DSC)
128. Check glass transition temperature (Tg) of cured adhesive (target: 60-90°C)
129. Prepare lap shear test specimens per ASTM D1002
130. Cure lap shear specimens for required time
131. Test shear strength using universal testing machine
132. Verify shear strength exceeds 5 MPa minimum requirement
133. Record typical shear strength values (target: 8-15 MPa on aluminum)
134. Test shear strength on different speaker materials (plastic, metal, composite)
135. Perform T-peel test on flexible substrates
136. Measure peel strength per ASTM D1876 (target: >30 N/cm)
137. Conduct tensile strength testing per ASTM D638
138. Record tensile strength and elongation at break
139. Test impact resistance using falling dart method
140. Verify adhesive flexibility and elongation (>10% elongation)
141. Perform environmental aging tests at 85°C for 500 hours
142. Measure property retention after heat aging
143. Test temperature cycling resistance (-40°C to +85°C, 100 cycles)
144. Check for cracking, delamination, or property loss after cycling
145. Conduct humidity resistance testing (85°C/85% RH for 500 hours)
146. Measure moisture absorption and effect on bond strength
147. Test chemical resistance to common speaker cleaning agents
148. Verify resistance to isopropyl alcohol, mild detergents, and solvents
149. Check electrical insulation properties (volume resistivity >10^12 ohm-cm)
150. Measure dielectric strength and dielectric constant

## Phase 6: Acoustic and Speaker-Specific Testing

151. Apply adhesive to complete speaker assembly
152. Bond voice coil to former using controlled adhesive amount
153. Cure voice coil assembly and measure concentricity
154. Verify no adhesive squeeze-out into magnetic gap
155. Bond spider to voice coil former and basket
156. Check spider centering after adhesive cure
157. Apply adhesive to surround-to-frame attachment
158. Cure surround assembly and verify alignment
159. Test for adhesive effect on suspension compliance
160. Measure speaker impedance curve before and after adhesive cure
161. Verify no significant impedance changes from adhesive
162. Conduct frequency response measurements
163. Check for adhesive-induced resonances or damping
164. Measure total harmonic distortion (THD) across frequency range
165. Verify THD remains below 1% at rated power
166. Test maximum SPL output with adhesive-bonded components
167. Conduct long-term power handling tests (100 hours at rated power)
168. Monitor for adhesive failure or degradation during power testing
169. Check for outgassing from curing adhesive affecting acoustic performance
170. Test adhesive odor and VOC emissions per ISO 16000
171. Verify low-odor formulation for consumer products
172. Measure cure shrinkage and dimensional stability
173. Check for stress concentration at adhesive bond lines
174. Test fatigue resistance under cyclic acoustic loading
175. Verify bond durability under continuous vibration (20-20kHz)
176. Conduct drop test on assembled speakers
177. Check adhesive shock resistance and impact absorption
178. Test adhesive performance at temperature extremes (-20°C to +70°C)
179. Verify consistent acoustic performance across temperature range
180. Measure adhesive contribution to speaker damping characteristics

## Phase 7: Quality Control and Packaging

## Technical Specifications Summary

- **Shear Strength**: >5 MPa (typically 8-15 MPa on aluminum)
- **Pot Life**: 15-30 minutes at 25°C
- **Cure Time**: 24 hours at 23°C or 2 hours at 60°C
- **Temperature Resistance**: -40°C to +85°C continuous
- **Flexibility**: >10% elongation at break
- **Viscosity (mixed)**: 8,000-15,000 cP
- **Glass Transition**: 60-90°C
- **Mix Ratio**: 100:30-50 by weight (Part A:Part B)
