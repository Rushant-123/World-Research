# SMD Resistors Manufacturing Process

**Market Leader:** [Yageo](/companies/yageo)



Surface Mount Device (SMD) resistors are critical passive components used extensively in Mac logic boards and modern electronics. This document details the complete manufacturing process for chip resistors in standard sizes (0201, 0402, 0603) with thick film technology.

## Phase 1: Alumina Substrate Preparation (Steps 1-25)

### Raw Material Reception and Quality Control

**Step 1:** Receive high-purity alumina ceramic sheets (96% Al2O3) from substrate manufacturer in cleanroom environment with ISO Class 7 certification.

**Step 2:** Inspect incoming alumina sheets using optical microscopy for surface defects, cracks, or contamination at 50x magnification.

**Step 3:** Measure substrate thickness using precision micrometers, verifying 0.30mm ±0.02mm tolerance for 0603 size components.

**Step 4:** Check surface roughness using atomic force microscopy (AFM) ensuring Ra < 0.3 micrometers for optimal film adhesion.

**Step 5:** Test ceramic density via Archimedes method confirming 3.72 g/cm³ minimum for mechanical strength requirements.

**Step 6:** Verify thermal expansion coefficient (6.5 ppm/°C) matching solder reflow profile requirements using dilatometry.

**Step 7:** Store substrates in humidity-controlled environment at 45% RH and 23°C to prevent moisture absorption.

### Substrate Cutting and Singulation

**Step 8:** Load alumina sheets onto precision dicing saw with diamond-coated blade (15 micrometers grain size).

**Step 9:** Program CNC controller for array cutting pattern yielding 10,000 individual resistor substrates per sheet.

**Step 10:** Execute cutting operation at 30,000 RPM with deionized water coolant flow to prevent thermal stress.

**Step 11:** Separate individual substrates for 0201 (0.6mm x 0.3mm), 0402 (1.0mm x 0.5mm), and 0603 (1.6mm x 0.8mm) dimensions.

**Step 12:** Measure cut edge quality using scanning electron microscopy (SEM) ensuring edge chipping < 5 micrometers.

**Step 13:** Clean diced substrates in ultrasonic bath with isopropyl alcohol (IPA) for 10 minutes at 40 kHz frequency.

**Step 14:** Rinse substrates with deionized water (18.2 MΩ·cm resistivity) in three-stage cascade system.

**Step 15:** Dry substrates in nitrogen purge oven at 120°C for 30 minutes removing all moisture content.

### Surface Treatment and Activation

**Step 16:** Perform plasma cleaning treatment using oxygen plasma at 200W power for 5 minutes to activate surface.

**Step 17:** Verify surface energy increase to >72 dynes/cm using contact angle measurement with water droplet test.

**Step 18:** Inspect plasma-treated surface for uniform activation using fluorescent dye penetration test.

**Step 19:** Load substrates onto vacuum pallet fixtures with precise positioning alignment within 10 micrometers.

**Step 20:** Transfer palletized substrates to screen printing station within 30 minutes to maintain surface activation.

**Step 21:** Verify substrate positioning using automated optical inspection (AOI) system with fiducial mark recognition.

**Step 22:** Pre-heat substrates to 80°C on thermal chuck to improve paste adhesion and reduce thermal shock.

**Step 23:** Measure substrate warpage using laser profilometry ensuring flatness within 50 micrometers across entire surface.

**Step 24:** Apply anti-static ionization to neutralize any electrostatic charge buildup on ceramic surfaces.

**Step 25:** Document substrate batch traceability with 2D matrix barcode laser marking for quality tracking.

## Phase 2: Resistive Paste Preparation (Steps 26-45)

### RuO2 Paste Formulation

**Step 26:** Receive ruthenium dioxide (RuO2) powder with particle size 0.5-2.0 micrometers and 99.9% purity from chemical supplier.

**Step 27:** Weigh RuO2 functional phase component to 15-25% by weight depending on target resistance value range.

**Step 28:** Add borosilicate glass frit (60-70% by weight) as permanent binder with softening point 550°C.

**Step 29:** Incorporate organic vehicle composed of ethyl cellulose (8-12%) dissolved in terpineol solvent system.

**Step 30:** Add bismuth ruthenate or lead ruthenate secondary phases for resistance temperature coefficient (TCR) control.

**Step 31:** Introduce rheology modifiers including dispersants and thixotropic agents to achieve proper paste viscosity.

**Step 32:** Load all components into three-roll mill for mechanical mixing and particle size reduction.

**Step 33:** Execute first milling pass with roll gap set to 20 micrometers at 100 RPM for initial dispersion.

**Step 34:** Perform second milling pass with gap reduced to 10 micrometers ensuring uniform particle distribution.

**Step 35:** Conduct third milling pass at 5 micrometer gap achieving final paste consistency and homogeneity.

### Paste Characterization and Quality Control

**Step 36:** Measure paste viscosity using Brookfield viscometer confirming 100-200 Pa·s at 25°C and 4 RPM.

**Step 37:** Test thixotropic index ensuring viscosity ratio between 2 RPM and 20 RPM falls within 2.5-4.0 range.

**Step 38:** Analyze particle size distribution using laser diffraction showing D50 = 1.0-1.5 micrometers.

**Step 39:** Check paste density via pycnometer method verifying 2.8-3.2 g/cm³ for proper printing behavior.

**Step 40:** Evaluate paste tack using texture analyzer measuring 50-150 g·cm adhesion force at 1 mm/s withdrawal speed.

**Step 41:** Determine solids content by thermogravimetric analysis (TGA) confirming 75-85% non-volatile components.

**Step 42:** Test paste shelf stability by storing sample at 5°C for 3 months and re-measuring viscosity drift.

**Step 43:** Verify paste printability on test substrates using quality screen achieving 80 micrometer line definition.

**Step 44:** Adjust paste formulation if necessary by adding solvent or modifier to meet specification requirements.

**Step 45:** Transfer qualified paste to printing station in sealed containers maintaining temperature at 20-25°C.

## Phase 3: Thick Film Screen Printing (Steps 46-70)

### Screen Preparation and Setup

**Step 46:** Select stainless steel screen mesh with 325 wires per inch and 18 micrometer wire diameter for 0603 components.

**Step 47:** Verify screen emulsion thickness at 15-20 micrometers using coating thickness gauge for proper paste release.

**Step 48:** Inspect screen pattern openings using microscope confirming 400 x 250 micrometer apertures for resistor body.

**Step 49:** Clean screen thoroughly with mesh cleaning solution removing any dried paste residue from previous runs.

**Step 50:** Mount screen on printing press with tension setting at 35 N/cm for dimensional stability during printing.

**Step 51:** Set snap-off distance between screen and substrate at 0.8-1.2mm for controlled paste release.

**Step 52:** Install tungsten carbide squeegee with 75 durometer hardness and 60-degree blade angle.

**Step 53:** Measure squeegee edge straightness ensuring deviation < 20 micrometers across entire length.

### Printing Process Execution

**Step 54:** Dispense prepared resistive paste onto screen forming bead approximately 15mm in front of pattern area.

**Step 55:** Set squeegee pressure at 4-6 kg force and speed at 50-100 mm/second for optimal paste transfer.

**Step 56:** Execute printing stroke with controlled downward force pushing paste through screen apertures onto substrate.

**Step 57:** Lift screen immediately after squeegee pass allowing paste pattern to transfer cleanly from mesh.

**Step 58:** Inspect printed pattern using automated vision system checking for smearing, bridging, or incomplete filling.

**Step 59:** Measure wet print thickness using non-contact laser profilometer confirming 20-25 micrometer deposit.

**Step 60:** Verify print alignment accuracy within ±25 micrometers of substrate centerline using registration marks.

**Step 61:** Check printed edge definition ensuring clean, sharp boundaries without feathering or bleeding effects.

**Step 62:** Monitor print consistency across production run using statistical process control (SPC) sampling every 100 pieces.

**Step 63:** Print termination electrode patterns on substrate ends using silver-palladium (Ag-Pd) conductor paste.

**Step 64:** Allow printed substrates to level for 5-10 minutes at room temperature for paste relaxation.

**Step 65:** Inspect termination overlaps ensuring 50 micrometer minimum contact with resistive film for electrical connection.

### Print Quality and Process Control

**Step 66:** Measure printed resistor dimensions using calibrated optical comparator at 50x magnification.

**Step 67:** Document print defect rate tracking skips, smears, shorts, and registration errors for yield analysis.

**Step 68:** Adjust printing parameters (pressure, speed, snap-off) based on real-time quality feedback from inspection.

**Step 69:** Clean screen every 50 prints using vacuum wipe system to prevent mesh clogging and maintain definition.

**Step 70:** Validate printed substrate batch with destructive cross-section analysis verifying paste penetration and adhesion.

## Phase 4: Drying and Firing Process (Steps 71-90)

### Initial Drying Stage

**Step 71:** Transfer printed substrates to infrared belt dryer within 15 minutes to prevent paste slumping.

**Step 72:** Set dryer temperature profile with three zones: 80°C, 120°C, and 150°C for gradual solvent evaporation.

**Step 73:** Configure belt speed at 1.5 meters/minute providing 12-15 minute total drying time.

**Step 74:** Monitor dryer atmosphere maintaining slight positive pressure to prevent contamination ingress.

**Step 75:** Measure weight loss during drying confirming 20-25% reduction corresponding to solvent removal.

**Step 76:** Inspect dried prints for surface defects including pinholes, cracking, or non-uniform appearance.

**Step 77:** Verify dried film integrity by gentle tape test ensuring adhesion without delamination.

**Step 78:** Allow dried substrates to stabilize at room temperature for 30 minutes before firing.

### High-Temperature Firing

**Step 79:** Load dried substrates onto high-purity alumina setter plates in single layer without overlap.

**Step 80:** Transfer setter plates to belt furnace with eight temperature zones for precise thermal profile control.

**Step 81:** Set peak firing temperature at 850°C with ±5°C tolerance for optimal glass frit sintering.

**Step 82:** Program heating rate at 50-80°C/minute in initial zones to prevent thermal shock damage.

**Step 83:** Maintain peak temperature dwell time of 10-15 minutes for complete organic burnout and densification.

**Step 84:** Control cooling rate at 80-120°C/minute preventing thermal stress crack formation in fired film.

**Step 85:** Monitor furnace atmosphere as air with proper ventilation for organic vehicle combustion.

**Step 86:** Measure furnace belt speed at 30-40 cm/minute providing 45-60 minute total cycle time.

**Step 87:** Install thermocouples on setter plate monitoring actual substrate temperature during firing cycle.

**Step 88:** Verify furnace temperature uniformity across belt width maintaining ±3°C variation at peak zone.

**Step 89:** Inspect fired films for proper densification showing smooth, continuous surface without porosity.

**Step 90:** Measure fired film thickness using profilometer confirming 8-12 micrometer final thickness after sintering.

## Phase 5: Laser Trimming for Precision (Steps 91-110)

### Resistance Measurement and Sorting

**Step 91:** Transfer fired substrates to automated resistance measurement station with four-wire probe configuration.

**Step 92:** Measure as-fired resistance values using precision LCR meter with 0.1% accuracy at 1 kHz test frequency.

**Step 93:** Record resistance distribution statistics calculating mean value and standard deviation for process control.

**Step 94:** Sort substrates into trim groups based on as-fired values requiring different laser trim percentages.

**Step 95:** Verify electrical contact quality between probes and terminations ensuring stable, repeatable readings.

**Step 96:** Temperature-stabilize substrates at 25°C ±1°C for 10 minutes before measurement to eliminate thermal drift.

**Step 97:** Apply automated handling with vacuum pick-and-place preventing substrate damage during measurement.

### Laser Trimming Operation

**Step 98:** Load substrates onto precision XY stage with position accuracy ±2 micrometers for laser targeting.

**Step 99:** Configure Nd:YAG laser system operating at 1064nm wavelength with 20 kHz pulse repetition rate.

**Step 100:** Set laser power at 5-15 watts peak with 100 nanosecond pulse width for controlled material removal.

**Step 101:** Focus laser beam to 25 micrometer spot size using F-theta lens assembly with focal length 100mm.

**Step 102:** Execute L-cut trim pattern starting from resistor edge cutting perpendicular to current flow direction.

**Step 103:** Monitor resistance increase in real-time using continuous measurement during laser cutting process.

**Step 104:** Control trim depth penetrating 60-80% through resistive film thickness for optimal stability.

**Step 105:** Adjust laser scanning speed at 100-500 mm/second based on required resistance change rate.

**Step 106:** Terminate laser cutting when resistance reaches target value within ±0.5% tolerance window.

**Step 107:** Implement scan-link algorithm for fine trimming making multiple small cuts for precise final adjustment.

**Step 108:** Apply plunge cut or serpentine pattern for high resistance values requiring greater material removal.

**Step 109:** Inspect trim cut quality using high-magnification camera checking for clean edges without debris.

**Step 110:** Measure final trimmed resistance verifying achievement of ±1% or ±0.5% tolerance depending on grade.

## Phase 6: Protective Glass Coating (Steps 111-125)

### Glass Overcoat Application

**Step 111:** Prepare protective glass paste composed of low-temperature glass frit with 500°C softening point.

**Step 112:** Formulate glass paste with appropriate viscosity (80-120 Pa·s) for screen printing application.

**Step 113:** Print glass overcoat using 200 mesh screen covering entire resistor body but leaving terminations exposed.

**Step 114:** Control printed glass thickness at 15-20 micrometers wet providing 8-10 micrometer final coverage.

**Step 115:** Dry printed glass coating at 150°C for 15 minutes removing organic solvents before firing.

**Step 116:** Fire glass overcoat at 600°C for 15 minute dwell time achieving complete densification and sealing.

**Step 117:** Cool coated substrates gradually at 100°C/minute preventing thermal expansion mismatch stress.

**Step 118:** Verify glass coating continuity using optical inspection at 100x magnification checking for pinholes.

**Step 119:** Test glass adhesion to resistor film using tape test method ensuring no delamination occurs.

**Step 120:** Measure glass coating thickness uniformity using cross-section SEM analysis on sample pieces.

**Step 121:** Evaluate glass surface smoothness via AFM scanning confirming Ra < 0.5 micrometers for protection.

**Step 122:** Conduct moisture resistance testing on coated samples exposing to 85°C/85% RH for 1000 hours.

**Step 123:** Monitor resistance stability of glass-coated resistors measuring drift < 0.25% after environmental stress.

**Step 124:** Inspect glass-to-termination interface ensuring proper edge coverage without shorting to electrodes.

**Step 125:** Validate glass coating thermal cycling performance through -55°C to +125°C for 1000 cycles.

## Phase 7: Termination Electrode Processing (Steps 126-145)

### Base Termination Metallization

**Step 126:** Prepare silver (Ag) or silver-palladium (Ag-Pd) termination paste with 70-80% metal content by weight.

**Step 127:** Verify termination paste compatibility with nickel plating process via adhesion testing protocols.

**Step 128:** Print termination electrodes on substrate ends using 200 mesh screen with wrap-around pattern design.

**Step 129:** Control termination thickness at 15-25 micrometers wet providing sufficient contact area for soldering.

**Step 130:** Ensure termination overlap with resistive film by minimum 100 micrometers for reliable electrical contact.

**Step 131:** Dry printed terminations at 150°C for 15 minutes allowing organic vehicle evaporation.

**Step 132:** Fire terminations at 850°C for 10 minute dwell achieving strong mechanical and electrical bond.

**Step 133:** Measure termination conductivity using four-point probe confirming sheet resistance < 10 milliohms/square.

**Step 134:** Inspect termination coverage on substrate ends ensuring complete wrap to top surface for mounting.

### Nickel Barrier Plating

**Step 135:** Clean fired terminations using plasma treatment removing any organic contamination before plating.

**Step 136:** Load substrates into electroless nickel plating bath at 85°C containing nickel sulfate solution.

**Step 137:** Control plating time at 15-20 minutes depositing 2-4 micrometer nickel layer thickness.

**Step 138:** Monitor plating bath pH at 4.5-5.5 and nickel concentration at 5-6 g/L for consistent deposition.

**Step 139:** Verify nickel layer uniformity using X-ray fluorescence (XRF) measuring thickness across termination surface.

**Step 140:** Rinse plated substrates thoroughly with deionized water removing all plating solution residue.

**Step 141:** Dry nickel-plated components in nitrogen atmosphere at 120°C preventing oxidation formation.

### Tin Finish Plating

**Step 142:** Transfer dried components to electroless tin plating bath at 70°C for final surface finish.

**Step 143:** Deposit 3-5 micrometer tin layer providing excellent solderability for surface mount assembly.

**Step 144:** Control tin plating parameters including bath pH 8-9 and stannous ion concentration 12-15 g/L.

**Step 145:** Test tin coating adhesion and solderability using wetting balance test achieving >75% coverage in 2 seconds.

## Phase 8: Singulation and Separation (Steps 146-160)

### Array Separation Process

**Step 146:** Transfer plated substrate arrays to precision dicing saw for individual component separation.

**Step 147:** Mount substrate arrays on adhesive dicing tape stretched over metal frame for secure holding.

**Step 148:** Install diamond-coated dicing blade with 50 micrometer thickness for minimal kerf width cutting.

**Step 149:** Program dicing pattern with street width 150-200 micrometers between individual components.

**Step 150:** Execute dicing operation at 30,000 RPM with deionized water coolant flow preventing thermal damage.

**Step 151:** Control cut depth penetrating through substrate and adhesive tape for complete separation.

**Step 152:** Inspect diced edges using microscope verifying clean cuts without chipping or cracking damage.

**Step 153:** Measure edge quality ensuring termination integrity maintained without electrode damage or peeling.

**Step 154:** Remove diced components from tape using automated pick-and-place system with vacuum nozzles.

**Step 155:** Transfer separated components to cleaning station for post-dicing residue removal.

### Post-Singulation Cleaning

**Step 156:** Clean diced components in ultrasonic bath with DI water removing cutting debris and particles.

**Step 157:** Rinse components in flowing DI water for 5 minutes ensuring complete contamination removal.

**Step 158:** Dry cleaned components in nitrogen purge oven at 100°C for 20 minutes eliminating moisture.

**Step 159:** Inspect dried components using automated optical inspection detecting edge defects or damage.

**Step 160:** Sort components removing any with visible defects including cracks, chips, or termination damage.

## Phase 9: Electrical Testing and Characterization (Steps 161-180)

### Resistance Value Testing

**Step 161:** Load dried components into automated test handler with capacity for 100,000 pieces per hour throughput.

**Step 162:** Configure four-wire Kelvin measurement eliminating contact resistance errors from test readings.

**Step 163:** Apply test current at 10% of rated power (typically 1-10 mA) measuring voltage drop across component.

**Step 164:** Calculate resistance using Ohm's law (R = V/I) with precision to 0.01% of measured value.

**Step 165:** Compare measured resistance against target specification sorting components into tolerance bins.

**Step 166:** Bin components into standard tolerances: ±1% (F), ±0.5% (D), ±0.25% (C), or ±0.1% (B) categories.

**Step 167:** Test resistance stability by multiple measurements over 1 second period detecting contact issues.

**Step 168:** Monitor test system calibration using NIST-traceable reference resistors every 2 hours.

**Step 169:** Record test data for each component including serial number for full traceability documentation.

**Step 170:** Reject components falling outside specified tolerance limits directing to failure analysis.

### Temperature Coefficient Testing

**Step 171:** Transfer sample components from each production lot to environmental test chamber.

**Step 172:** Measure baseline resistance at 25°C reference temperature using precision meter.

**Step 173:** Heat chamber to 125°C maximum operating temperature allowing 30 minute stabilization time.

**Step 174:** Measure hot resistance calculating temperature coefficient (TCR) using formula: TCR = (ΔR/R₀)/ΔT ppm/°C.

**Step 175:** Cool chamber to -55°C minimum operating temperature measuring cold resistance value.

**Step 176:** Verify TCR specification compliance typically ±100 ppm/°C or ±200 ppm/°C depending on grade.

**Step 177:** Document TCR values for lot acceptance ensuring consistent manufacturing process control.

### Additional Electrical Parameters

**Step 178:** Test component voltage coefficient measuring resistance change under applied voltage stress.

**Step 179:** Verify maximum working voltage (typically 25-50V for 0603 size) without breakdown or arcing.

**Step 180:** Measure parasitic capacitance at 1 MHz frequency confirming < 0.05 pF for high-frequency applications.

## Phase 10: Reliability and Environmental Testing (Steps 181-195)

### Mechanical and Environmental Tests

**Step 181:** Conduct solder heat resistance test immersing components in 260°C solder bath for 10 seconds.

**Step 182:** Measure resistance change after solder exposure ensuring drift < 1% from initial value.

**Step 183:** Perform thermal shock testing cycling components between -55°C and +125°C for 100 cycles minimum.

**Step 184:** Execute humidity resistance test exposing to 85°C/85% RH for 1000 hours measuring resistance drift.

**Step 185:** Apply power load test at rated power for 1000 hours at 70°C measuring long-term stability.

**Step 186:** Test mechanical robustness using bend test on mounted components per JESD22-B113 standard.

**Step 187:** Verify ESD resistance capability withstanding 1000V human body model discharge without damage.

### Packaging and Final Quality Control

**Step 188:** Transfer tested and sorted components to automated tape-and-reel packaging equipment.

**Step 189:** Load components into embossed carrier tape with 2mm, 4mm, or 8mm pitch depending on size.

**Step 190:** Apply cover tape sealing components in pockets protecting from handling damage and moisture.

**Step 191:** Wind carrier tape onto 7-inch or 13-inch diameter reels holding 10,000-50,000 pieces per reel.

**Step 192:** Apply reel labels with product code, resistance value, tolerance, lot number, and quantity information.

**Step 193:** Package sealed reels in moisture barrier bags with desiccant maintaining < 10% RH internal humidity.

**Step 194:** Conduct final visual inspection and dimension verification on packaged components using sampling plan.

**Step 195:** Ship qualified component reels to customers with complete certificate of compliance and test data documentation.

## Manufacturing Output and Applications

The completed SMD resistors manufactured through this 195-step process are precision components suitable for demanding applications in Mac logic boards, including:

- Power supply voltage dividers and feedback networks
- Signal conditioning and filtering circuits
- Current sensing and measurement applications
- Pull-up and pull-down resistor networks
- Impedance matching for high-speed digital signals
- ESD protection and discharge path circuits

Each resistor provides reliable performance with tight tolerance, low TCR, excellent stability, and high reliability meeting the stringent requirements of modern electronic systems. The thick film ruthenium oxide technology combined with precision laser trimming and protective coatings ensures long-term stability and performance across the full operating temperature range from -55°C to +155°C.
