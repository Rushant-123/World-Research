# How to Make Speakers (Laptop Audio)

**Top Market Leaders:**
- [AAC Technologies](/companies/aac-technologies)
- [GoerTek](/companies/goertek)
- [Knowles](/companies/knowles)



Laptop speakers are miniature dynamic drivers that convert electrical signals to sound waves. Compact design (10-20mm diameter) fits in thin laptop chassis while delivering audio for music, videos, and calls. Modern MacBooks use stereo speakers with wide frequency response and spatial audio.

## What is it?

A moving-coil dynamic speaker: electrical current through voice coil creates magnetic field that interacts with permanent magnet, causing coil and attached cone to vibrate, producing sound waves. Frequency response: 80 Hz - 20 kHz. Impedance: 4-8 ohms. Power handling: 1-3 watts RMS per speaker. Typical laptop has 2-6 speakers for stereo or spatial audio.

## Steps to Make:

**Magnet Manufacturing:**
1. Create [Speaker Magnet](neodymium-magnet) from neodymium-iron-boron (NdFeB)
2. Mix powdered neodymium, iron, and boron in precise ratios
3. Press powder in die under high pressure (100-200 tons)
4. Sinter at 1,000-1,100°C in inert atmosphere
5. Creates solid magnetic material
6. Machine to final shape: ring or disc
7. Magnetize in strong field (2-3 Tesla)
8. Neodymium magnets: strongest permanent magnets, 10× stronger than ferrite
9. Coat with nickel to prevent corrosion

**Voice Coil Winding:**
10. Wind [Voice Coil](copper-wire-coil) from copper wire
11. Wire diameter: 0.05-0.1mm (very thin)
12. Wind 50-200 turns around cylindrical former (bobbin)
13. Bobbin: aluminum or Kapton (polyimide) tube, 8-15mm diameter
14. Apply adhesive between wire layers
15. Bake coil at 120°C to cure adhesive
16. Solder lead wires to coil ends
17. Attach tinsel leads (flexible wires) for connection to terminals

**Cone Formation:**
18. Create [Speaker Cone](paper-cone) from paper pulp or polymer film
19. Paper pulp: wood fibers mixed with resins and fillers
20. Pour pulp into cone-shaped mold
21. Apply vacuum to remove water
22. Dry at 80-120°C
23. Impregnate with damping resin to control vibration
24. Cone diameter: 10-20mm for laptop speakers
25. Cone depth: 2-5mm
26. Alternative materials: polypropylene, aluminum, Kevlar
27. Cut center hole for voice coil attachment

**Spider Manufacturing:**
28. Create [Spider Suspension](fabric-spider) from woven fabric
29. Fabric: cotton, polyester, or fiberglass
30. Impregnate with phenolic resin
31. Form into corrugated shape (accordion pattern)
32. Corrugations allow coil to move in/out while centering
33. Die-cut to size
34. Spider prevents coil from rubbing on magnet pole

**Surround Manufacturing:**
35. [Surround](rubber-surround) connects cone edge to frame
36. Material: rubber, foam, or cloth
37. Mold rubber surround with half-roll or accordion shape
38. Surround provides compliance (allows cone to move)
39. Also seals air gap between cone and frame

**Frame Preparation:**
40. Injection mold [Frame Basket](plastic-frame) from ABS or polycarbonate
41. Frame provides mounting for magnet and cone assembly
42. Includes mounting tabs for installation in laptop
43. Ventilation holes allow air pressure equalization
44. For metal frame: stamp from steel sheet

**Assembly Process:**
45. [Assembly Labor](speaker-assembly-labor) in clean facility
46. Attach magnet to frame with [Adhesives](speaker-adhesive) - structural epoxy
47. Center pole piece in magnet gap (for ring magnets)
48. Glue spider to frame, ensuring centered over magnet
49. Attach voice coil to spider center
50. Position coil in magnetic gap with <0.2mm clearance
51. Coil must not touch magnet or pole piece
52. Use centering jig to align coil precisely
53. Glue surround to frame outer edge
54. Attach cone to voice coil
55. Glue surround to cone outer edge
56. Glue [Dust Cap](dome-cap) over cone center hole
57. Dust cap prevents dust from entering magnetic gap
58. Solder tinsel leads to [Terminals](solder-terminals)
59. Terminals connect to speaker wires
60. Apply adhesive at all joints
61. Cure at room temperature for 24 hours

**Testing:**
62. Connect speaker to signal generator
63. Sweep frequency from 20 Hz to 20 kHz
64. Measure frequency response with microphone
65. Target: flat response ±3 dB from 100 Hz to 15 kHz
66. Check for resonances (peaks) or nulls (dips)
67. Measure impedance: should be 4-8 ohms
68. Measure sensitivity: dB SPL at 1 meter with 1 watt input
69. Typical laptop speaker: 75-85 dB SPL/W/m
70. Test power handling: apply rated power for 1 hour
71. Monitor temperature: voice coil should stay <150°C
72. Test distortion: THD (total harmonic distortion) should be <3% at 90% power
73. Listen test: trained listeners evaluate sound quality
74. Check for: buzzing, rattling, or abnormal sounds

**Enclosure Integration:**
75. Mount speaker in [Enclosure](speaker-housing)
76. Enclosure: sealed or ported cavity machined into laptop chassis
77. Sealed enclosure: tighter bass, smaller size
78. Ported enclosure: extended bass, requires tuned port
79. Enclosure volume: 1-10 cm³ for laptop speakers
80. Port tuning: length and diameter determine bass resonance frequency
81. Acoustic stuffing: polyester fiber inside enclosure to absorb standing waves
82. Grille: perforated metal or fabric covering speaker opening

**Quality Control:**
83. 100% automated testing on production line
84. Laser interferometer measures cone motion
85. Verify no rubbing or binding
86. Impedance analyzer checks electrical characteristics
87. Acoustic chamber measures frequency response
88. Compare to reference speaker
89. Reject if: >5% impedance variation, >5 dB response variation, or audible defects
90. Typical yield: 98-99%

**Lifespan Testing:**
91. Subject samples to accelerated life testing
92. Drive at 150% rated power for 100 hours
93. Temperature cycling: -20°C to 70°C
94. Humidity exposure: 85% RH at 40°C
95. Drop test: 1-meter drop onto hard surface
96. Verify no degradation in performance
97. Target lifespan: 10,000-50,000 hours

**Matching:**
98. For stereo pairs, match speakers within 1 dB sensitivity
99. Match impedance within 5%
100. Pair speakers with similar response curves
101. Label pairs for installation together

**Packaging:**
102. Place speaker in anti-static tray
103. Protect terminals with foam
104. Package in moisture barrier bag
105. Ship to laptop assembly facility

**Speaker Specifications (MacBook):**
- Driver size: 12-18mm diameter
- Frequency response: 80 Hz - 20 kHz
- Impedance: 4 ohms
- Power handling: 1-3 watts RMS, 5-10 watts peak
- Sensitivity: 80-85 dB SPL/W/m
- Distortion (THD): <1% at normal listening levels, <3% at max volume
- Voice coil diameter: 10-14mm
- Magnet strength: 8,000-12,000 Gauss (0.8-1.2 Tesla)
- Excursion (max movement): ±1-2mm
- Resonant frequency: 400-800 Hz
- Weight: 2-5 grams per speaker

**MacBook Audio System:**
- 13" MacBook: 2 speakers (stereo)
- 14"/16" MacBook Pro: 6 speakers (woofers + tweeters)
- Woofer: handles bass/midrange (80-2,000 Hz)
- Tweeter: handles treble (2,000-20,000 Hz)
- Force-cancelling woofers: two woofers moving in opposite phase cancel vibration
- Spatial audio: simulates surround sound with head tracking

**Manufacturing Challenges:**
- Voice coil alignment: <0.2mm tolerance to prevent rubbing
- Cone shape: must be consistent for uniform response
- Adhesive curing: insufficient curing causes delamination
- Magnet strength variation: affects sensitivity
- Small size limits bass response (cannot move much air)
- Thin laptop chassis provides minimal acoustic enclosure
- Heat dissipation: voice coil gets hot at high power

**Comparison:**
- Laptop speakers: 10-20mm driver, 80 Hz - 20 kHz, 1-3W
- Phone speakers: 6-10mm driver, 200 Hz - 18 kHz, 0.5-1W
- Bookshelf speakers: 100-200mm woofer, 40 Hz - 20 kHz, 10-100W
- Studio monitors: 150-250mm woofer, 30 Hz - 40 kHz, 50-300W

Despite tiny size, modern laptop speakers achieve impressive audio quality through: neodymium magnets (strong field in small package), optimized cone geometry, force-cancelling configurations, and DSP (digital signal processing) to compensate for limitations. However, laws of physics dictate that small drivers cannot reproduce deep bass (<100 Hz) - insufficient cone area and excursion to move enough air.
