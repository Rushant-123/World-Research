# Engineer Compensation Package Manufacturing Process

**Market Leader:** [Tech Companies](/companies/tech-companies)



## Market Research and Benchmarking (Steps 1-25)

1. Conduct industry-wide salary surveys across technology sectors to establish baseline compensation data for engineering roles
2. Partner with compensation consulting firms like Radford, Mercer, and Willis Towers Watson to access proprietary market data
3. Analyze compensation trends across geographic markets including Silicon Valley, Seattle, Austin, New York, and Boston
4. Survey competitor compensation packages from FAANG companies, unicorn startups, and mid-sized technology firms
5. Gather data on equity compensation trends including stock option grants, RSU packages, and ESPP programs
6. Research benefits trends including healthcare coverage, retirement matching, and wellness programs across the industry
7. Compile data on signing bonuses, relocation packages, and retention bonuses offered by competing employers
8. Analyze compensation differences across engineering disciplines: software, hardware, data science, machine learning, and DevOps
9. Study seniority-based compensation progressions from junior engineer through principal/staff engineer levels
10. Review geographic cost-of-living adjustments and remote work compensation strategies emerging post-pandemic
11. Examine performance-based compensation models including individual bonuses, team bonuses, and company-wide profit sharing
12. Research equity refresh programs and retention-focused equity grant schedules used by top technology companies
13. Analyze total compensation packages rather than just base salary to understand complete value propositions
14. Study compensation compression issues between new hires and existing employees at similar levels
15. Investigate internal equity concerns and compensation parity across different engineering teams and departments
16. Review legal requirements including minimum wage laws, overtime exemptions, and equal pay legislation
17. Analyze historical compensation inflation rates and project future market movements for budget planning
18. Study startup compensation models balancing lower cash compensation with higher equity stakes
19. Research compensation differences between public companies with liquid stock and private companies
20. Examine variable compensation structures linking pay to individual, team, and company performance metrics
21. Analyze compensation data by education level, years of experience, and specialized technical skills
22. Study remote work compensation strategies including location-based pay scales versus uniform national rates
23. Research international compensation differences for engineers working in offshore development centers
24. Examine compensation practices for contract engineers, consultants, and temporary technical staff
25. Compile comprehensive market intelligence reports to inform compensation philosophy and strategy development
