# Insulated Copper Wire Manufacturing Process

## Overview
This document details the 195-step process for manufacturing insulated copper wire from copper rod, including wire drawing operations that reduce 8mm diameter copper rod down to fine wires ranging from 0.1mm to 2mm, followed by annealing, insulation application, stranding operations, comprehensive testing, and final spooling for distribution.

## Raw Material Reception and Preparation (Steps 1-15)

### Step 1: Copper Rod Delivery
Receive copper rod shipments from copper rod manufacturing facility in standardized coils or straight lengths.

### Step 2: Supplier Documentation Review
Verify certificates of analysis including copper purity (typically 99.95-99.99% Cu), oxygen content, and conductivity specifications.

### Step 3: Visual Inspection
Inspect copper rod surface for oxidation, scratches, dents, or dimensional irregularities that could affect wire drawing.

### Step 4: Dimensional Verification
Measure rod diameter using precision calipers to confirm 8mm (+/-0.05mm) specification compliance.

### Step 5: Surface Quality Assessment
Examine rod surface finish under magnification to detect micro-cracks, inclusions, or surface defects.

### Step 6: Hardness Testing
Perform Vickers hardness testing on sample sections to verify appropriate hardness for drawing operations.

### Step 7: Conductivity Testing
Test electrical conductivity using eddy current or four-point probe methods to confirm minimum 100% IACS specification.

### Step 8: Batch Identification
Apply unique batch identification labels with barcode or RFID tags for traceability throughout manufacturing.

### Step 9: Storage Assignment
Store accepted copper rod in climate-controlled warehouse with humidity control to prevent oxidation.

### Step 10: Drawing Schedule Planning
Input rod specifications into production planning system to determine drawing pass sequence and die selection.

### Step 11: Material Staging
Transport required copper rod quantities to wire drawing area using material handling equipment.

### Step 12: Rod Straightening
Pass copper rod through straightening rolls to remove coil set and ensure uniform geometry entering drawing dies.

### Step 13: End Preparation
Machine or grind rod ends to pointed profile for threading through first drawing die.

### Step 14: Surface Cleaning
Clean rod surface using solvent wash or ultrasonic cleaning to remove oils, dirt, and contaminants.

### Step 15: Pre-Drawing Inspection
Perform final visual inspection and dimensional check before committing rod to drawing operations.

## Wire Drawing - First Pass Series (Steps 16-35)

### Step 16: First Die Selection
Select diamond or tungsten carbide drawing die with 7.2mm diameter for first reduction pass (10% reduction).

### Step 17: Die Installation
Install first drawing die in draw block with proper alignment and secure mounting.

### Step 18: Lubricant Application
Apply drawing lubricant (soap-based or synthetic) to rod surface and die entrance to reduce friction.

### Step 19: Threading Operation
Thread pointed rod end through first die and grip with drawing capstan or bull block.

### Step 20: Drawing Speed Setting
Set capstan rotation speed to achieve appropriate drawing velocity (typically 5-10 m/s for first pass).

### Step 21: First Pass Drawing
Execute first drawing pass, reducing rod from 8mm to 7.2mm diameter with continuous monitoring.

### Step 22: Tension Control
Monitor and adjust pulling tension to maintain consistent reduction without exceeding material yield strength.

### Step 23: Die Temperature Monitoring
Monitor die temperature using infrared sensors to prevent overheating that could damage die or wire.

### Step 24: Diameter Measurement
Measure wire diameter using laser micrometer or mechanical gauge to verify 7.2mm specification.

### Step 25: Surface Inspection
Inspect drawn wire surface for die marks, scoring, or other defects introduced during drawing.

### Step 26: Second Die Selection
Install 6.5mm diameter die for second reduction pass (approximately 10% additional reduction).

### Step 27: Inter-Pass Lubricant Refresh
Apply fresh lubricant to wire surface before entering second die.

### Step 28: Second Pass Drawing
Draw 7.2mm wire through 6.5mm die with adjusted capstan speed for consistent reduction.

### Step 29: Third Die Installation
Install 5.8mm diameter die for third pass reduction continuing the drawing sequence.

### Step 30: Third Pass Execution
Execute third drawing pass reducing wire from 6.5mm to 5.8mm diameter.

### Step 31: Fourth Pass Setup
Install 5.2mm die and adjust drawing parameters for fourth reduction pass.

### Step 32: Fourth Pass Drawing
Draw wire through 5.2mm die maintaining proper tension and lubrication.

### Step 33: Fifth Pass Configuration
Set up 4.7mm die for fifth drawing pass in the continuous reduction sequence.

### Step 34: Fifth Pass Execution
Execute fifth pass drawing operation reducing wire to 4.7mm diameter.

### Step 35: First Series Quality Check
Perform comprehensive dimensional and visual inspection after completing first drawing series.

## Intermediate Annealing Process (Steps 36-50)

### Step 36: Work Hardening Assessment
Evaluate wire hardness and ductility to determine need for annealing before continuing drawing operations.

### Step 37: Annealing Furnace Preparation
Prepare continuous annealing furnace with controlled atmosphere (nitrogen or forming gas) to prevent oxidation.

### Step 38: Temperature Setting
Set furnace temperature to 400-600°C depending on copper grade and desired final properties.

### Step 39: Atmosphere Verification
Verify protective atmosphere composition and flow rate using oxygen analyzers and flow meters.

### Step 40: Wire Threading
Thread work-hardened wire through annealing furnace entrance and set up continuous feed system.

### Step 41: Feed Rate Setting
Set wire feed rate to achieve appropriate residence time in hot zone (typically 30-120 seconds).

### Step 42: Annealing Execution
Pass wire through hot zone where elevated temperature relieves internal stresses and restores ductility.

### Step 43: Cooling Zone Transit
Allow wire to pass through controlled cooling zone with gradual temperature reduction preventing thermal shock.

### Step 44: Exit Temperature Monitoring
Monitor wire temperature at furnace exit using infrared pyrometer to verify proper cooling.

### Step 45: Post-Anneal Inspection
Inspect annealed wire for discoloration, oxidation, or surface changes indicating process problems.

### Step 46: Hardness Verification
Test annealed wire hardness to confirm adequate softening for subsequent drawing operations.

### Step 47: Ductility Testing
Perform wrap test or bend test to verify restored ductility and absence of brittleness.

### Step 48: Microstructure Sampling
Extract samples for metallographic examination confirming grain recrystallization and stress relief.

### Step 49: Coil Formation
Wind annealed wire onto spools or coils for transfer to next drawing stage.

### Step 50: Inter-Stage Storage
Store annealed wire in controlled environment until ready for fine drawing operations.

## Fine Wire Drawing - Second Pass Series (Steps 51-80)

### Step 51: Fine Drawing Setup
Configure fine wire drawing machine with multiple die blocks for sequential reduction passes.

### Step 52: Die Block Assembly
Install series of progressively smaller dies: 4.2mm, 3.8mm, 3.4mm, 3.0mm, 2.7mm, 2.4mm.

### Step 53: Capstan Synchronization
Set up tandem capstans with synchronized speeds maintaining proper inter-die tension and accumulation.

### Step 54: Lubricant System Setup
Configure continuous lubrication system delivering controlled lubricant flow to each die entrance.

### Step 55: Wire Threading Through Multiple Dies
Thread annealed wire sequentially through all dies in the fine drawing machine.

### Step 56: Machine Speed Setting
Set machine operating speed (typically 10-20 m/s) based on final wire diameter and material properties.

### Step 57: Pass 6 Execution (4.7mm to 4.2mm)
Execute sixth drawing pass with 10.6% reduction in cross-sectional area.

### Step 58: Pass 7 Execution (4.2mm to 3.8mm)
Complete seventh pass achieving 3.8mm diameter with continuous tension control.

### Step 59: Pass 8 Execution (3.8mm to 3.4mm)
Draw wire through eighth pass reducing diameter to 3.4mm.

### Step 60: Pass 9 Execution (3.4mm to 3.0mm)
Execute ninth drawing pass achieving 3.0mm wire diameter.

### Step 61: Pass 10 Execution (3.0mm to 2.7mm)
Complete tenth pass reducing wire to 2.7mm diameter.

### Step 62: Pass 11 Execution (2.7mm to 2.4mm)
Draw wire to 2.4mm diameter through eleventh die.

### Step 63: Die Condition Monitoring
Monitor die wear using drawing force sensors and periodic dimensional measurements.

### Step 64: Lubricant Flow Verification
Verify adequate lubricant delivery to each die through visual inspection and flow meter readings.

### Step 65: Inter-Die Tension Measurement
Monitor tension between each die using load cells ensuring optimal drawing conditions.

### Step 66: Pass 12 Setup (2.4mm to 2.1mm)
Install 2.1mm die and adjust capstan speed for twelfth drawing pass.

### Step 67: Pass 12 Execution
Execute twelfth pass reducing wire to 2.1mm diameter.

### Step 68: Pass 13 Configuration (2.1mm to 1.9mm)
Set up thirteenth die at 1.9mm diameter with recalibrated tension settings.

### Step 69: Pass 13 Execution
Draw wire through thirteenth pass achieving 1.9mm diameter.

### Step 70: Pass 14 Setup (1.9mm to 1.7mm)
Install 1.7mm die for fourteenth reduction pass.

### Step 71: Pass 14 Execution
Complete fourteenth pass reducing wire to 1.7mm diameter.

### Step 72: Pass 15 Configuration (1.7mm to 1.5mm)
Set up fifteenth die at 1.5mm for continued reduction.

### Step 73: Pass 15 Execution
Execute fifteenth drawing pass achieving 1.5mm wire diameter.

### Step 74: Pass 16 Setup (1.5mm to 1.3mm)
Install 1.3mm die and adjust parameters for sixteenth pass.

### Step 75: Pass 16 Execution
Draw wire to 1.3mm diameter through sixteenth die.

### Step 76: Pass 17 Configuration (1.3mm to 1.2mm)
Set up seventeenth die at 1.2mm diameter.

### Step 77: Pass 17 Execution
Complete seventeenth pass achieving 1.2mm wire diameter.

### Step 78: Second Series Quality Verification
Perform dimensional measurements, visual inspection, and surface quality assessment.

### Step 79: Mechanical Properties Testing
Test tensile strength, elongation, and hardness on wire samples from second drawing series.

### Step 80: Work Hardening Re-Assessment
Evaluate wire condition to determine if additional annealing required before ultra-fine drawing.

## Ultra-Fine Wire Drawing (Steps 81-100)

### Step 81: Ultra-Fine Drawing Machine Setup
Configure specialized ultra-fine wire drawing equipment for final diameter reductions.

### Step 82: Precision Die Installation
Install precision-ground diamond dies for ultra-fine wire sizes: 1.0mm, 0.9mm, 0.8mm, 0.7mm, 0.6mm.

### Step 83: Clean Room Preparation
Ensure clean room environment to prevent particle contamination on fine wire surfaces.

### Step 84: Advanced Lubrication System
Activate mist or spray lubrication system designed for ultra-fine wire drawing applications.

### Step 85: Pass 18 Execution (1.2mm to 1.0mm)
Execute eighteenth pass reducing wire to 1.0mm diameter with careful tension control.

### Step 86: Diameter Verification
Measure 1.0mm wire using laser micrometer with 0.1 micron resolution.

### Step 87: Pass 19 Execution (1.0mm to 0.9mm)
Draw wire through nineteenth die achieving 0.9mm diameter.

### Step 88: Pass 20 Execution (0.9mm to 0.8mm)
Complete twentieth pass reducing wire to 0.8mm diameter.

### Step 89: Pass 21 Execution (0.8mm to 0.7mm)
Execute twenty-first drawing pass achieving 0.7mm wire diameter.

### Step 90: Pass 22 Execution (0.7mm to 0.6mm)
Draw wire to 0.6mm diameter through twenty-second die.

### Step 91: Optional Fine Size Dies
For applications requiring finer wire, install additional dies: 0.5mm, 0.4mm, 0.3mm, 0.2mm, 0.1mm.

### Step 92: Pass 23-27 Execution (Optional)
Execute additional passes for ultra-fine wire down to 0.1mm diameter for specialized applications.

### Step 93: Surface Cleanliness Verification
Inspect ultra-fine wire surface under microscope for contamination, die marks, or defects.

### Step 94: Dimensional Tolerance Check
Verify wire diameter meets tight tolerance specifications (typically +/-0.5% for fine wire).

### Step 95: Straightness Assessment
Evaluate wire straightness and detect any curvature or deviation from straight axis.

### Step 96: Die Wear Documentation
Record die usage cycles and measure die diameter for wear tracking and maintenance scheduling.

### Step 97: Production Batch Documentation
Document all drawing parameters, die sequences, and quality measurements for batch traceability.

### Step 98: Final Drawing Quality Inspection
Perform comprehensive inspection including dimensions, surface quality, and mechanical properties.

### Step 99: Drawing Process Completion
Confirm successful completion of all drawing passes achieving target wire diameter.

### Step 100: Wire Coiling for Insulation
Wind drawn wire onto precision spools preparing for transfer to insulation application line.

## Final Annealing Before Insulation (Steps 101-110)

### Step 101: Pre-Insulation Annealing Decision
Determine if final annealing required based on wire application and specified temper (soft, half-hard, hard).

### Step 102: Continuous Annealing Line Setup
Configure in-line annealing system integrated with insulation extrusion equipment.

### Step 103: Temperature Profile Setting
Set annealing temperature profile (typically 350-500°C) for final softening or stress relief.

### Step 104: Atmosphere Control
Establish reducing or inert atmosphere preventing copper oxidation during final annealing.

### Step 105: Wire Feed Through Annealer
Thread drawn wire through continuous annealing furnace with controlled residence time.

### Step 106: Property Optimization
Adjust time and temperature to achieve specified conductivity and mechanical properties.

### Step 107: Rapid Cooling Section
Cool wire rapidly after annealing using water quench or forced air cooling if required.

### Step 108: Final Temper Verification
Test wire hardness and ductility confirming achievement of specified temper designation.

### Step 109: Conductivity Confirmation
Measure electrical conductivity verifying minimum 100% IACS for soft annealed wire.

### Step 110: Surface Preparation for Insulation
Ensure wire surface is clean, dry, and free from oxides before insulation application.

## Insulation Material Preparation (Steps 111-125)

### Step 111: Insulation Polymer Selection
Select appropriate insulation material based on application: PVC, polyethylene (PE), cross-linked PE (XLPE), or PTFE (Teflon).

### Step 112: Polymer Receipt and Storage
Receive insulation polymer pellets or powder in moisture-proof packaging and store in climate-controlled warehouse.

### Step 113: Material Certification Review
Verify polymer specifications including dielectric strength, temperature rating, flexibility, and chemical resistance.

### Step 114: Drying Process
Dry hygroscopic polymers (especially nylon and PVC) in desiccant dryers removing moisture that could cause defects.

### Step 115: Additive Preparation
Prepare additives including stabilizers, plasticizers, colorants, flame retardants, and UV inhibitors.

### Step 116: Compound Formulation
Mix base polymer with additives according to proprietary formulation for specific wire application.

### Step 117: Compound Blending
Blend polymer compound using high-intensity mixers ensuring uniform additive dispersion.

### Step 118: Color Masterbatch Addition
Add color masterbatch for product identification (black, white, red, blue, green, yellow per standards).

### Step 119: Compound Testing
Test blended compound for melt flow index, density, and dielectric properties before extrusion.

### Step 120: Extruder Hopper Loading
Load prepared insulation compound into extruder hopper with level monitoring and automatic feeding.

### Step 121: Extruder Purging
Purge extruder with new compound material removing any residual different polymers from previous runs.

### Step 122: Temperature Profile Setting
Set extruder barrel zones to optimal temperature profile for chosen insulation material (typically 150-250°C).

### Step 123: Screw Speed Configuration
Configure extruder screw rotation speed controlling material throughput and melt homogenization.

### Step 124: Die Selection and Installation
Install wire coating die with appropriate diameter and land length for target insulation thickness.

### Step 125: Compound Flow Stabilization
Stabilize extruder operation achieving consistent melt temperature, pressure, and flow rate before starting production.

## Insulation Extrusion Process (Steps 126-145)

### Step 126: Wire-Extruder Alignment
Thread bare copper wire through center of extrusion die ensuring concentric alignment.

### Step 127: Line Speed Synchronization
Synchronize wire feed speed with extruder output rate achieving target insulation thickness.

### Step 128: Extrusion Die Temperature
Monitor and control die temperature for optimal polymer flow and wire adhesion.

### Step 129: Insulation Application
Apply molten insulation polymer concentrically around moving wire through pressure die extrusion.

### Step 130: Thickness Control
Continuously monitor insulation thickness using non-contact optical or capacitive sensors.

### Step 131: Eccentricity Monitoring
Detect and correct insulation eccentricity (off-center application) using multi-point measurement.

### Step 132: Cross-Head Pressure Monitoring
Monitor extruder cross-head pressure indicating proper polymer flow and die conditions.

### Step 133: Primary Cooling - Water Trough
Pass newly insulated wire through temperature-controlled water cooling trough solidifying polymer.

### Step 134: Cooling Water Temperature Control
Maintain cooling water temperature (typically 15-25°C) for controlled cooling rate preventing defects.

### Step 135: Secondary Cooling - Air Cooling
Continue cooling insulated wire through air cooling section with controlled air flow.

### Step 136: Diameter Measurement
Measure finished insulated wire diameter using laser micrometers verifying overall size specification.

### Step 137: Insulation Thickness Calculation
Calculate actual insulation thickness from overall diameter and known conductor diameter.

### Step 138: Surface Quality Inspection
Inspect insulation surface for lumps, voids, contamination, die lines, or other visual defects.

### Step 139: Capacitance Measurement
Monitor wire capacitance as inline quality indicator of insulation thickness and consistency.

### Step 140: Spark Test
Subject insulated wire to high-voltage spark test detecting pinholes or thin spots in insulation.

### Step 141: Marking Application
Apply product identification markings (footage marks, part numbers, specifications) using ink jet or indent marking.

### Step 142: Dual Layer Application (If Required)
For dual-insulated wire, apply second insulation layer with different color through second extruder.

### Step 143: Cross-Linking Activation (For XLPE)
Pass through cross-linking chamber (peroxide cure or radiation) for cross-linked polyethylene insulation.

### Step 144: Post-Extrusion Cooling
Complete cooling process ensuring insulation fully solidified before mechanical handling.

### Step 145: Tension Control Throughout Line
Maintain proper wire tension throughout extrusion line preventing insulation elongation or compression.

## Wire Stranding Operations (Steps 146-160)

### Step 146: Stranding Requirement Determination
Determine if wire stranding required for flexibility enhancement (particularly for larger gauge wires).

### Step 147: Strand Configuration Selection
Select stranding configuration: 7-strand, 19-strand, 37-strand, or other patterns per application requirements.

### Step 148: Individual Strand Preparation
Prepare individual wire strands of appropriate diameter for bunching or stranding operation.

### Step 149: Stranding Machine Setup
Configure stranding or bunching machine with required number of pay-off reels matching strand count.

### Step 150: Pay-Off Reel Loading
Load individual wire strands onto pay-off reels with proper tension adjustment.

### Step 151: Strand Tension Balancing
Balance tension across all strands ensuring uniform contribution to stranded conductor construction.

### Step 152: Bunching or Stranding Execution
Execute bunching (no twist) or stranding (with lay twist) operation combining multiple wires.

### Step 153: Lay Length Control
Control lay length (twist pitch) according to specification affecting flexibility and electrical properties.

### Step 154: Strand Direction Selection
Set stranding direction (left-hand or right-hand lay) per product specification.

### Step 155: Compacting Operation (Optional)
Pass stranded conductor through compacting die reducing overall diameter and improving surface smoothness.

### Step 156: Stranded Conductor Annealing
Anneal stranded conductor if required to improve flexibility and relieve stranding stresses.

### Step 157: Stranded Conductor Insulation
Apply insulation to stranded conductor using extrusion process similar to solid wire insulation.

### Step 158: Strand Integrity Verification
Verify all strands present and properly formed without broken strands or irregular lay.

### Step 159: Flexibility Testing
Perform flex testing on stranded wire confirming improved flexibility versus solid conductor.

### Step 160: Electrical Continuity Check
Test electrical continuity of all strands ensuring proper electrical performance of stranded conductor.

## Electrical Testing (Steps 161-175)

### Step 161: Conductivity Testing Setup
Set up four-point probe or bridge measurement system for accurate conductivity testing.

### Step 162: DC Resistance Measurement
Measure DC resistance per unit length verifying compliance with conductor resistance specifications.

### Step 163: Conductivity Calculation
Calculate electrical conductivity as percentage of IACS (International Annealed Copper Standard).

### Step 164: Temperature Coefficient Verification
Verify resistance temperature coefficient matches copper standard (0.393% per °C at 20°C).

### Step 165: Insulation Resistance Testing
Measure insulation resistance between conductor and ground using megohmmeter at specified voltage.

### Step 166: High-Pot Testing
Apply high voltage (2-3x rated voltage) between conductor and ground for specified duration detecting insulation weaknesses.

### Step 167: Dielectric Strength Testing
Test dielectric strength on sample sections by increasing voltage until breakdown occurs.

### Step 168: Capacitance Measurement
Measure wire capacitance per unit length verifying expected value based on geometry and insulation material.

### Step 169: Partial Discharge Testing
Perform partial discharge testing on critical applications detecting incipient insulation defects.

### Step 170: Time Domain Reflectometry
Use TDR testing to locate insulation defects or impedance discontinuities along wire length.

### Step 171: Voltage Withstand Duration
Subject wire to rated voltage for extended period (minutes to hours) proving long-term insulation integrity.

### Step 172: Corona Inception Testing
Test corona inception voltage for high-voltage applications ensuring adequate insulation margins.

### Step 173: Temperature Cycling with Electrical Load
Apply electrical load during temperature cycling testing insulation performance under thermal stress.

### Step 174: Bend Test Under Voltage
Flex wire repeatedly under applied voltage ensuring insulation integrity maintained during mechanical stress.

### Step 175: Production Testing Documentation
Document all electrical test results with automatic data logging and statistical process control tracking.

## Mechanical and Physical Testing (Steps 176-185)

### Step 176: Tensile Strength Testing
Test wire tensile strength on universal testing machine verifying minimum breaking strength specifications.

### Step 177: Elongation Measurement
Measure elongation at break determining wire ductility and conformance to temper specifications.

### Step 178: Wrap Test
Wrap wire around mandrel of specified diameter verifying no cracking or insulation defects occur.

### Step 179: Bend Test
Bend wire through specified angle and radius verifying flexibility and absence of conductor breakage.

### Step 180: Repeated Flexing Test
Subject wire to repeated flexing cycles measuring cycles to failure for fatigue resistance assessment.

### Step 181: Abrasion Resistance Test
Test insulation abrasion resistance using standardized abrasion equipment and methods.

### Step 182: Cold Bend Test
Perform bend testing at low temperature (typically -40°C) ensuring insulation flexibility in cold environments.

### Step 183: Heat Shock Test
Subject insulated wire to rapid temperature change testing insulation adhesion and crack resistance.

### Step 184: Flame Test
Conduct vertical flame testing for flame-retardant wire grades verifying self-extinguishing properties.

### Step 185: Chemical Resistance Testing
Expose insulation to specified chemicals (oils, solvents, acids, bases) verifying chemical resistance claims.

## Final Inspection and Spooling (Steps 186-195)

### Step 186: Final Visual Inspection
Perform 100% visual inspection of finished wire detecting surface defects before packaging.

### Step 187: Dimensional Verification
Verify final wire dimensions including conductor diameter, insulation thickness, and overall diameter.

### Step 188: Length Measurement
Measure and record actual wire length using precision measuring wheels or electronic counters.

### Step 189: Spool Selection
Select appropriate spool size and type based on wire diameter, length, and customer requirements.

### Step 190: Precision Spooling
Wind finished wire onto spools using precision winding equipment with controlled tension and layer spacing.

### Step 191: Traverse Width Control
Control wire traverse width preventing edge buildup and ensuring uniform spool winding.

### Step 192: Spool Labeling
Apply labels with product identification, specifications, length, batch number, and test certifications.

### Step 193: Protective Packaging
Package spools in moisture-proof wrapping or boxes protecting wire from environmental damage during storage and shipping.

### Step 194: Final Documentation
Complete manufacturing documentation including certificates of compliance, test reports, and traceability records.

### Step 195: Quality Release and Shipping
Obtain quality assurance approval for release and prepare finished wire spools for shipment to customers or distribution centers.

## Quality Control and Standards Compliance

Throughout the manufacturing process, copper wire production adheres to international standards including:
- ASTM B3 (Soft or Annealed Copper Wire)
- ASTM B8 (Concentric-Lay-Stranded Copper Conductors)
- IEC 60228 (Conductors of Insulated Cables)
- UL 83 (Thermoplastic-Insulated Wires and Cables)
- NEC (National Electrical Code) requirements

Quality control measures ensure consistent production of high-conductivity copper wire with reliable insulation suitable for diverse applications in power distribution, electronics, telecommunications, automotive wiring, and industrial control systems.

## Process Summary

The 195-step manufacturing process transforms 8mm copper rod into precisely dimensioned insulated copper wire through controlled wire drawing operations (reduction ratios of 10-15% per pass), intermediate annealing cycles restoring ductility, ultra-fine drawing achieving final diameters from 2.0mm down to 0.1mm, insulation extrusion applying PVC, PE, or PTFE protective coatings, optional stranding for enhanced flexibility, comprehensive electrical testing (conductivity, insulation resistance, high-pot), mechanical testing (tensile, flex, abrasion), and precision spooling. The result is high-quality insulated copper wire meeting stringent electrical, mechanical, and safety standards for reliable performance in electrical and electronic applications.
