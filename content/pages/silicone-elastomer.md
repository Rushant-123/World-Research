## Raw Material Preparation and Selection

1. Source polydimethylsiloxane (PDMS) base polymer with molecular weight between 60,000-400,000 g/mol depending on target elastomer properties
2. Verify PDMS viscosity specification ranging from 10,000 to 100,000 centistokes at 25°C for different grades
3. Receive vinyl-terminated PDMS chains for addition cure systems with 0.05-0.15% vinyl content
4. Obtain hydroxyl-terminated PDMS chains for condensation cure systems with specific end-group functionality
5. Select fumed silica reinforcing filler with surface area of 150-300 m²/g for mechanical property enhancement
6. Choose between hydrophobic fumed silica (treated with hexamethyldisilazane) and hydrophilic fumed silica based on application
7. Procure precipitated silica as alternative filler with surface area of 100-200 m²/g for cost-sensitive applications
8. Source platinum-based catalyst such as Karstedt's catalyst (platinum-divinyltetramethyldisiloxane complex) at 2-4% platinum concentration
9. Obtain platinum-cyclovinylmethylsiloxane complex for applications requiring reduced inhibition sensitivity
10. Receive hydrogen-terminated methylhydrosiloxane crosslinker with 25-35 mol% methylhydrosilane units

## PDMS Base Formulation

11. Transfer base PDMS polymer to stainless steel mixing vessel with temperature control and agitation capability
12. Pre-heat PDMS base to 40-60°C to reduce viscosity and improve mixing efficiency
13. Add hydrophobic fumed silica filler at 10-30 weight percent to base polymer for reinforcement
14. Initiate high-shear mixing at 500-1500 rpm using dual-blade planetary mixer with heated jacket
15. Mix PDMS and fumed silica continuously for 2-4 hours at 150-180°C to break down silica agglomerates
16. Monitor torque increase during mixing indicating proper silica dispersion and structure formation
17. Add processing aids such as low molecular weight PDMS (500-1000 centistokes) at 1-3% to improve flow
18. Incorporate pigments or colorants if required, typically iron oxide, titanium dioxide, or carbon black at 0.5-5%
19. Add functional additives such as heat stabilizers (cerium oxide at 0.1-0.5%) for high-temperature applications
20. Include flame retardants like platinum compounds or special silica at 5-15% for fire-resistant grades

## Filler Treatment and Dispersion

21. Verify fumed silica surface treatment with trimethylsilyl groups providing hydrophobic character
22. Conduct "structure" formation where silica particles create three-dimensional network in polymer matrix
23. Measure "crepe hardening" phenomenon where viscosity increases during high-shear mixing indicating good dispersion
24. Add additional silica in multiple stages to achieve target filler loading without excessive viscosity
25. Incorporate extending fillers such as ground quartz or diatomaceous earth at 10-40% for specific properties
26. Mix conductive fillers like carbon black or metal powders at 15-30% for EMI shielding applications
27. Add thermally conductive fillers such as aluminum oxide or boron nitride at 30-60% for heat dissipation
28. Ensure uniform filler dispersion verified by optical microscopy showing no agglomerates larger than 50 microns
29. Apply vacuum during mixing at 10-50 mbar to remove entrapped air and volatile compounds
30. Cool compounded base material to room temperature and transfer to storage containers

## Crosslinker and Catalyst Addition (Addition Cure)

32. Add inhibitors to Part A such as 1-ethynyl-1-cyclohexanol at 0.05-0.5% to control cure rate
33. Incorporate methylvinylcyclosiloxanes at 1-5% to increase vinyl functionality for faster cure
34. Prepare Part B formulation with methylhydrosiloxane crosslinker at stoichiometric ratio to vinyl groups
35. Optimize Si-H to vinyl ratio typically at 1.0-2.0:1 for complete cure and mechanical properties
36. Add platinum catalyst to Part B at 5-50 ppm platinum metal concentration
37. Include additional inhibitors if needed such as dimethyl maleate or dimethyl fumarate at 0.1-1%
38. Add adhesion promoters like silane coupling agents (gamma-methacryloxypropyltrimethoxysilane) at 0.5-3%
39. Package Part A and Part B separately in moisture-proof containers to prevent premature reaction
40. Label each part with mixing ratio (typically 1:1 or 10:1 by weight) and storage conditions

## Two-Part Mixing and Degassing

52. Pour components into planetary mixer bowl at specified ratio (common ratios: 1:1, 10:1, or 100:10)

## Compression Molding Process

61. Pre-heat compression mold made from aluminum or steel to 150-180°C for addition cure systems
62. Apply mold release agent such as fluoropolymer spray or PTFE film to mold surfaces
63. Weigh precise amount of mixed silicone elastomer for mold cavity with 10-20% overfill
64. Place material in center of lower mold half distributing evenly
65. Close mold platens with hydraulic press applying pressure of 50-150 bar (700-2200 psi)
66. Allow material to flow and fill mold cavity completely under pressure and heat
67. Maintain molding temperature at 150-200°C for addition cure (higher temperatures for faster cure)
68. Hold cure cycle for 2-10 minutes depending on part thickness and cure speed
69. Monitor cure by observing no material flow or flash formation after initial cycle
70. Open mold and demold cured elastomer part using manual extraction or ejector pins
