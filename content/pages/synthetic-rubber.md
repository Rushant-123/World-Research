# Synthetic Rubber Production for Mac Rubber Feet

## Phase 1: Raw Material Preparation and Quality Control

1. Receive styrene monomer shipment in temperature-controlled storage tanks at 15-20°C
2. Verify styrene monomer purity using gas chromatography (minimum 99.5% purity required)
3. Test styrene for inhibitor content (typically 10-15 ppm tert-butylcatechol)
4. Check styrene moisture content using Karl Fischer titration (must be <100 ppm)
5. Receive butadiene monomer in refrigerated pressure vessels at -5°C to 0°C
6. Analyze butadiene purity via GC-MS (minimum 99.0% purity specification)
7. Test butadiene for polymer inhibitor levels and adjust if necessary
8. Verify butadiene pressure and temperature logs for storage integrity
9. Inspect polymerization catalyst delivery and verify batch documentation
10. Analyze catalyst activity using standardized polymerization test batches
11. Check catalyst particle size distribution (typically 0.1-5 microns)
12. Verify catalyst storage conditions (dry, inert atmosphere, 2-8°C)
13. Receive vulcanizing agent (sulfur or peroxide-based) in sealed containers
14. Test vulcanizing agent purity and chemical composition via FTIR spectroscopy
15. Measure vulcanizing agent particle size (200-400 mesh for optimal dispersion)
16. Prepare deionized water for emulsion polymerization (conductivity <1 µS/cm)
17. Test water quality for trace metals, organics, and dissolved oxygen
18. Prepare emulsifier solution (typically fatty acid soap at 2-5% concentration)
19. Verify emulsifier molecular weight and hydrophilic-lipophilic balance (HLB)
20. Prepare electrolyte solution for pH control and ionic strength adjustment
21. Mix buffer solutions to maintain pH 10-11 during polymerization
22. Prepare chain transfer agent (typically dodecyl mercaptan) for molecular weight control
23. Verify chain transfer agent purity and concentration via titration
24. Prepare antioxidant solution to prevent premature degradation
25. Mix stabilizer packages including phenolic and amine-based compounds

## Phase 2: Emulsion Polymerization Reactor Preparation

26. Clean polymerization reactor using alkaline detergent wash cycle
27. Rinse reactor thoroughly with deionized water (minimum 3 rinse cycles)
28. Inspect reactor interior for residual polymer deposits or contamination
29. Check reactor jacket cooling system and verify glycol circulation
30. Test reactor agitator motor and gearbox for proper operation
31. Calibrate reactor temperature probes (±0.5°C accuracy required)
32. Verify reactor pressure transducers and safety relief valves
33. Check reactor feed lines and automated dosing pumps
34. Purge reactor with nitrogen to remove oxygen (target <50 ppm O2)
35. Pressurize reactor with nitrogen and perform leak test (hold 1 bar for 30 min)
36. Load deionized water into reactor (typically 180 parts per 100 parts monomer)
37. Add emulsifier solution while agitating at 100-150 RPM
38. Heat reactor contents to initial polymerization temperature (45-55°C)
39. Verify reactor pH and adjust to 10.5 using sodium hydroxide solution
40. Add electrolyte (potassium chloride or sodium sulfate) for ionic strength
41. Introduce chain transfer agent for molecular weight targeting (Mn 100,000-150,000)
42. Begin nitrogen sparging to maintain inert atmosphere throughout reaction
43. Verify all automated control systems and emergency shutdown procedures
44. Calibrate monomer flow meters for accurate feed rate control
45. Prepare initiator solution (potassium persulfate in water, 0.3-0.5% on monomer)
46. Cool initiator solution to 2-8°C to prevent premature decomposition
47. Load redox activator (sodium formaldehyde sulfoxylate) if using cold polymerization
48. Verify all reactor interlocks and safety systems are operational
49. Establish baseline viscosity measurement for incoming emulsion
50. Set up online conversion monitoring via density or refractive index measurement

## Phase 3: Styrene-Butadiene Rubber (SBR) Emulsion Polymerization

51. Begin continuous co-feed of styrene and butadiene monomers at 23.5:76.5 ratio
52. Control monomer feed rate to maintain 10-14 hour polymerization time
53. Add initiator solution continuously or in split additions over first 2 hours
54. Monitor exothermic reaction and control temperature at 50°C ±2°C using jacket cooling
55. Track reactor pressure (typically 3-5 bar) as indicator of monomer concentration
56. Sample latex every 30 minutes for solids content determination (gravimetric method)
57. Monitor conversion via gas chromatography of unreacted monomers
58. Adjust agitator speed (150-200 RPM) to maintain uniform particle size distribution
59. Add supplemental emulsifier if particle size begins to increase (maintain 80-150 nm)
60. Control pH throughout polymerization (maintain 10-11 for optimal reaction rate)
61. Monitor latex viscosity using online rotational viscometer
62. Add shortstop agent (hydroquinone or sodium dimethyldithiocarbamate) at 60-70% conversion
63. Allow polymerization to proceed to 95-98% conversion (typically 12 hours total)
64. Cool reactor contents to 40°C after conversion target reached
65. Add antioxidant package (phenolic and amine-based stabilizers at 1-2%)
66. Verify final latex properties: pH 10-11, solids 20-25%, particle size 100-120 nm
67. Sample final latex for residual monomer analysis (target <500 ppm total)
68. Strip residual monomers using steam stripping under vacuum if necessary
69. Add additional stabilizers and biocides for latex storage stability
70. Transfer latex to holding tank with gentle agitation

## Phase 4: Coagulation and Crumb Formation

71. Prepare coagulation tank with hot water at 60-70°C
72. Add acid coagulant solution (sulfuric acid or aluminum sulfate at 2-4%)
73. Adjust coagulation pH to 3.5-4.5 for optimal particle aggregation
74. Add calcium chloride or sodium chloride brine (1-3% solution) to enhance coagulation
75. Begin continuous addition of latex to coagulation tank with high-shear mixing
76. Control latex feed rate to maintain proper crumb size (3-8 mm particles)
77. Monitor coagulation temperature (maintain 60-70°C throughout process)
78. Observe crumb formation and particle growth in coagulation vessel
79. Allow residence time of 20-30 minutes for complete coagulation
80. Test coagulation completeness by checking clarity of serum phase
81. Separate rubber crumb from serum using vibrating screen or centrifuge
82. Wash rubber crumb with hot water (50-60°C) to remove salts and impurities
83. Perform second wash cycle with fresh water for thorough cleaning
84. Test wash water conductivity to verify salt removal (target <500 µS/cm)
85. Dewater rubber crumb using mechanical pressing or centrifugation
86. Reduce moisture content to 40-50% before drying (mechanical dewatering limit)
87. Collect serum water for treatment and recycling of chemicals
88. Screen rubber crumb to remove oversized particles or agglomerates
89. Sample wet rubber for quality analysis (bound styrene content, Mooney viscosity)
90. Transfer wet rubber crumb to drying system via conveyor

## Phase 5: Drying and Finishing

91. Feed wet rubber crumb into tunnel dryer or fluidized bed dryer
92. Set drying air temperature at 110-130°C for efficient moisture removal
93. Control residence time in dryer (15-25 minutes depending on crumb size)
94. Monitor exit moisture content continuously (target <0.5% moisture)
95. Use multi-zone drying with temperature profiling to prevent case hardening
96. Collect dried rubber crumb and allow cooling to ambient temperature
97. Screen dried rubber to remove dust and fines (<1 mm particles)
98. Recycle fines back to coagulation or compounding process
99. Sample dried rubber for final quality testing (moisture, ash, volatiles)
100. Test Mooney viscosity ML(1+4) at 100°C (target 45-55 MU for rubber feet application)
101. Measure bound styrene content via NMR or pyrolysis-GC (verify 23.5% ±1%)
102. Analyze gel content using toluene extraction method
103. Determine volatile content via thermogravimetric analysis
104. Test ash content after combustion at 550°C (typically <1%)
105. Measure specific gravity (typically 0.93-0.94 for SBR)
106. Store dried rubber crumb in polyethylene-lined bags or containers
107. Label packages with batch number, production date, and specification data
108. Store in cool, dry warehouse away from direct sunlight and heat sources
109. Maintain storage temperature below 30°C to prevent aging
110. Implement first-in-first-out (FIFO) inventory management

## Phase 6: Compound Formulation and Mixing

111. Design rubber compound formulation for Mac rubber feet application
112. Calculate recipe: 100 phr SBR base, 50 phr carbon black, 5 phr process oil
113. Add 3 phr zinc oxide as vulcanization activator
114. Include 2 phr stearic acid for processing and activation
115. Add 1.5 phr sulfur as primary vulcanizing agent
116. Include 1.5 phr accelerator package (CBS and TBBS combination)
117. Add 1 phr antioxidant (TMQ and 6PPD blend for aging resistance)
118. Include 0.5 phr antiozonant for outdoor durability
119. Weigh all ingredients accurately using calibrated scales (±0.1% accuracy)
120. Condition SBR rubber to room temperature before mixing (20-25°C)
121. Break down rubber bales or crumb into smaller pieces for easier feeding
122. Load internal mixer (Banbury type) with SBR polymer
123. Start mixer and masticate rubber for 1-2 minutes at 40-50 RPM
124. Add zinc oxide and stearic acid, mix for 30 seconds
125. Add half of carbon black reinforcing filler (N550 or N660 grade)
126. Increase mixer speed to 60-70 RPM and ram pressure to disperse carbon black
127. Monitor mixing temperature (target 140-150°C for optimal dispersion)
128. Add remaining carbon black in stages with continued mixing
129. Add process oil (paraffinic or naphthenic) to improve processability
130. Mix until compound temperature reaches 150-160°C and carbon black is well dispersed
131. Dump master batch from internal mixer onto open mill
132. Cool master batch and allow to equilibrate for 24 hours (maturation period)
133. Return master batch to internal mixer for final mixing stage
134. Add sulfur vulcanizing agent at low temperature (60-80°C to prevent scorching)
135. Add accelerator package with rapid mixing (30-60 seconds)
136. Dump final compound quickly to prevent premature vulcanization
137. Sheet compound on two-roll mill at 2-3 mm thickness for cooling
138. Cut compound into strips and stack for further cooling
139. Sample compound for quality testing before molding
140. Test Mooney viscosity ML(1+4) at 100°C (target 55-65 MU after compounding)
141. Measure Mooney scorch time ts5 at 120°C (minimum 10 minutes for safe processing)
142. Perform oscillating disk rheometer (ODR) test at 160°C for cure characteristics
143. Record scorch time ts2, optimum cure time tc90, and maximum torque
144. Verify compound specific gravity (typically 1.10-1.15 with carbon black)
145. Store compound in cool conditions wrapped in polyethylene film

## Phase 7: Molding and Vulcanization

146. Prepare compression molding press and clean mold cavities
147. Preheat mold to vulcanization temperature (150-170°C)
148. Cut compound to appropriate weight for each rubber foot cavity (typically 2-5 grams)
149. Coat mold surface with release agent (silicone-based) if necessary
150. Load pre-weighed compound pieces into each mold cavity
151. Close mold and apply pressure (50-100 bar compression pressure)
152. Allow mold to heat compound to vulcanization temperature
153. Monitor cure time based on ODR tc90 data (typically 8-12 minutes at 160°C)
154. Maintain constant temperature and pressure throughout cure cycle
155. Vent mold briefly if necessary to release trapped air or volatiles
156. Allow slight over-cure (tc95) to ensure complete crosslinking
157. Open mold carefully and remove vulcanized rubber feet
158. Inspect parts for flash, voids, or surface defects
159. Trim excess flash using cutting tools or cryogenic deflashing
160. Place molded parts on cooling rack at ambient temperature
161. Allow post-cure relaxation for 24 hours before testing
162. Inspect dimensional accuracy using calipers and templates
163. Verify part weight and thickness specifications
164. Check surface finish and appearance quality
165. Sample parts for destructive testing from each production batch

## Phase 8: Hardness Control and Testing

166. Calibrate Shore A durometer on standard reference blocks
167. Condition test specimens at 23°C ±2°C for 24 hours before testing
168. Measure Shore A hardness at 5 different locations on each rubber foot
169. Record hardness readings after 1 second and 15 second dwell time
170. Calculate average hardness and verify specification range (Shore A 40-70)
171. Adjust compound formulation if hardness is out of specification
172. Increase carbon black or reduce oil content to increase hardness
173. Decrease filler loading or increase oil to reduce hardness
174. Verify hardness uniformity across production batch (standard deviation <3 Shore A)
175. Perform compression set testing at 23°C and 70°C (ASTM D395 Method B)
176. Test for 22 hours under 25% deflection, target <35% compression set
177. Conduct tensile strength testing on die-cut specimens (ASTM D412)
178. Measure tensile strength at break (target >8 MPa for rubber feet)
179. Record elongation at break (target >300% for flexibility)
180. Test tear strength using trouser tear method (ASTM D624)
181. Measure resilience using rebound pendulum test (typically 40-60%)
182. Perform abrasion resistance testing using DIN abrader or Pico abrader
183. Test adhesion to substrate using peel test if adhesive backing applied
184. Conduct accelerated aging test at 70°C for 7 days in air oven
185. Re-measure hardness and tensile properties after aging (verify <20% change)
186. Perform ozone resistance testing in ozone chamber (50 pphm, 20% strain, 48 hours)
187. Check for cracking or crazing after ozone exposure
188. Test low temperature flexibility by cold bending at -40°C
189. Verify no brittleness or cracking at low temperature
190. Conduct coefficient of friction testing on various surfaces (glass, metal, wood)
191. Verify static and dynamic friction coefficients meet specifications
192. Test damping and vibration absorption properties using dynamic mechanical analysis
193. Package approved rubber feet in protective containers with lot traceability
194. Label with production date, batch number, and quality certification
195. Store finished products in controlled environment until shipment to Mac manufacturing facility

## Quality Assurance and Process Control

Throughout the synthetic rubber production process, JSR Corporation maintains strict quality control protocols including statistical process control (SPC) monitoring, batch traceability systems, and compliance with ISO 9001 quality management standards. All critical process parameters are recorded in real-time using distributed control systems (DCS), and automated sampling ensures consistent product quality. The rubber feet produced through this 195-step process meet Apple's stringent specifications for hardness, durability, chemical resistance, and aesthetic appearance.
