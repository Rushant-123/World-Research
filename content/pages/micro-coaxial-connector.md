Micro-coaxial connectors are ultra-miniature RF connectors used in mobile devices, laptops, and wireless modules to connect antenna cables to mainboards. These precision components maintain 50-ohm impedance across frequencies up to 6 GHz while fitting in spaces under 2mm. Manufacturing requires sub-micron tolerances, specialized tooling, and rigorous RF testing to ensure signal integrity in compact consumer electronics.

## Material Preparation and Quality Control

Receive C2680 brass strip coils in 0.15mm thickness with +/-0.005mm tolerance for contact manufacturing. Verify material certificates including tensile strength of 370-450 MPa and electrical conductivity above 27% IACS. Inspect incoming brass for surface defects, scratches, or oxidation using optical microscopy at 50x magnification. Measure strip thickness at 20 points per coil using laser micrometers with 0.001mm resolution to ensure uniformity.

Clean brass strips in alkaline degreasing solution at 65°C for 8 minutes to remove manufacturing oils and contaminants. Rinse strips in deionized water cascade with resistivity above 15 megohm-cm followed by hot air drying at 80°C. Verify surface cleanliness using contact angle measurements, targeting values below 30 degrees for proper plating adhesion. Store cleaned brass in nitrogen-purged cabinets at <30% relative humidity until processing.

Verify material purity through thermal gravimetric analysis showing decomposition onset above 500°C. Check moisture content using Karl Fischer titration, maintaining levels below 0.02% for consistent molding. Pre-dry PTFE granules in vacuum ovens at 150°C for 4 hours before processing to eliminate absorbed moisture.

Prepare 304 stainless steel strip in 0.08mm thickness for shell components with surface roughness Ra below 0.2 micrometers. Verify mechanical properties including yield strength of 215 MPa minimum and hardness of 150-200 HV. Inspect steel strips for flatness deviation, maintaining tolerance within +/-0.01mm across 100mm spans. ## Electroplating Process
