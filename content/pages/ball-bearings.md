## Raw Material Preparation

1. Receive AISI 52100 high-carbon chromium bearing steel bars, diameter 15-30mm, carbon content 0.98-1.10%, chromium 1.30-1.60%
2. Inspect incoming steel certificates for hardness uniformity and carbide distribution per ASTM A295
3. Verify steel cleanliness rating (oxide inclusion K4 max 1.0, non-metallic inclusions per ASTM E45 method A)
4. Store steel bars in temperature-controlled environment at 20±2°C, relative humidity below 60%
5. Cut steel bars to rough lengths using precision saw with carbide-tipped blade, tolerance ±0.5mm
6. Deburr cut edges with rotary tumbler using ceramic media for 30 minutes
7. Clean bar ends with alkaline degreaser solution (pH 10-12) at 60°C for 10 minutes
8. Rinse with deionized water and dry with compressed air at 0.6 MPa pressure
9. Mark each bar with batch number and heat treatment lot code using laser engraver
10. Organize bars by diameter size into production batches of 500 pieces

## Inner Race Manufacturing - Turning Operations

11. Load steel bar into CNC turning center with 12-position tool turret and 5000 rpm spindle
12. Face both ends of bar to remove saw marks, achieving surface finish Ra 3.2 μm
13. Turn outer diameter to inner race rough dimension, removing 2mm material with depth of cut 0.5mm per pass
14. Apply water-soluble cutting fluid at 15 liters/minute flow rate during all turning operations
15. Turn inner bore using carbide boring bar to diameter within 0.1mm of final dimension
16. Create raceway profile using form tool with radius matching ball size (for 3mm balls, radius 1.53-1.54mm)
17. Turn side faces to final width dimension, tolerance ±0.02mm for ABEC-5 grade
18. Machine chamfers on both inner and outer edges at 45° angle, 0.3mm x 0.3mm dimension
19. Part off completed inner race blank from bar stock
20. Inspect dimensions using coordinate measuring machine (CMM) with 0.001mm resolution
21. Check raceway profile using form tester, radius tolerance ±0.003mm
22. Verify concentricity between inner bore and outer diameter, maximum runout 0.005mm
23. Clean turned parts in ultrasonic cleaner with mild alkaline solution for 5 minutes
24. Dry parts in centrifugal dryer at 1500 rpm for 2 minutes

## Outer Race Manufacturing - Turning Operations

25. Load steel bar into second CNC turning center dedicated to outer race production
26. Face bar ends and turn to rough outer diameter, removing 3mm material total
27. Turn inner bore to raceway diameter, leaving 0.15mm grinding allowance
28. Form outer raceway groove using copy turning technique with hydraulic tracer control
29. Machine raceway to 52-54 HRC initial hardness specification
30. Turn external diameter to within 0.08mm of final dimension
31. Create snap ring groove if required for shielded bearing design, width 0.8mm, depth 0.3mm
32. Machine side face chamfers at 20° angle for shield clearance
33. Part off outer race blank and inspect raceway geometry with optical comparator at 50x magnification
34. Measure raceway oscillation using V-block setup and dial indicator, maximum 0.008mm total indicator reading
35. Check wall thickness uniformity around circumference, variation not exceeding 0.01mm
36. Deburr all edges manually using fine abrasive stone (400 grit aluminum oxide)

## Heat Treatment Process - Hardening

37. Load inner and outer races onto heat treatment fixtures in vertical orientation
38. Place fixtures into continuous mesh belt furnace with controlled atmosphere
39. Heat parts to austenitizing temperature of 840-860°C in endothermic gas atmosphere
40. Maintain carbon potential at 0.80-0.85% during heating cycle
41. Soak at austenitizing temperature for 25-35 minutes to ensure complete carbide dissolution
42. Monitor furnace atmosphere with oxygen probe, maintaining CO/CO2 ratio of 40:1
43. Transfer parts rapidly to quench tank using automated handling system (transfer time under 5 seconds)
44. Quench in fast oil (120-140°C) with vigorous agitation at 0.5 m/s flow velocity
45. Control quench rate to achieve cooling from 840°C to 200°C in 8-12 seconds
46. Remove parts when temperature reaches 60-80°C to minimize distortion
47. Immediately transfer to sub-zero treatment chamber
48. Cool parts to -70°C in liquid nitrogen bath and hold for 1 hour
49. Sub-zero treatment converts retained austenite to martensite, reducing from 8-12% to under 2%
50. Warm parts gradually to room temperature over 30-minute period

## Heat Treatment Process - Tempering

51. Place hardened races in tempering furnace with forced air circulation
52. Heat to tempering temperature of 150-180°C for precision bearings
53. Hold at temperature for 2 hours to relieve quenching stresses
54. Control temperature uniformity within furnace to ±5°C throughout load
55. Cool parts in still air to room temperature over 45 minutes
56. Perform second temper cycle at same temperature for additional 2 hours
57. Verify final hardness of 58-64 HRC using Rockwell hardness tester at multiple points per part
58. Check hardness uniformity, maximum variation 2 HRC between measurement locations
59. Inspect for heat treatment defects (cracks, soft spots) using magnetic particle inspection
60. Apply magnetic field with 1500 amp-turns and dust with fluorescent particles
61. Examine under UV light for crack indications, reject any parts showing linear indications over 0.5mm
62. Demagnetize parts using AC degaussing coil with decaying field
63. Clean parts in solvent to remove magnetic particle residue

## Race Grinding - Inner Race

64. Mount inner race on expanding mandrel with 0.002mm interference fit
65. Install mandrel in centerless grinding machine with vitrified aluminum oxide wheel (60 grit, I hardness)
66. Dress grinding wheel with single-point diamond dresser to maintain form accuracy
67. Grind inner bore diameter to final dimension with tolerance +0.000/-0.008mm for ABEC-5 grade
68. Use plunge grinding technique with infeed rate of 0.005mm per pass
69. Apply water-based grinding fluid at 40 liters/minute with 4% concentration
70. Measure bore diameter every 5 parts using air gauge with 0.0001mm resolution
71. Grind raceway surface using formed grinding wheel matching ball radius
72. Control raceway surface finish to Ra 0.2 μm maximum
73. Verify raceway roundness using roundness tester, maximum out-of-round 0.002mm
74. Grind outer diameter of inner race to achieve concentricity with bore within 0.003mm
75. Measure final dimensions and record in statistical process control system
76. Clean ground races in ultrasonic cleaner with 5% alkaline solution at 50°C for 8 minutes

## Race Grinding - Outer Race

77. Mount outer race on precision chuck with jaw pressure calibrated to 50 N
78. Install in CNC cylindrical grinding machine with CBN (cubic boron nitride) wheel
79. Grind outer diameter to final dimension, tolerance 0.000/-0.009mm for ABEC-5 specification
80. Use traverse grinding with wheel oscillation frequency of 60 cycles/minute
81. Maintain grinding wheel peripheral speed at 30 m/s
82. Control part rotation speed at 120 rpm for optimal surface finish
83. Measure outer diameter with laser micrometer after every grinding pass
84. Grind inner raceway using internal grinding spindle with 12mm diameter wheel
85. Form raceway radius to match ball size with tolerance +0.005/-0.000mm
86. Achieve raceway surface finish of Ra 0.15 μm through fine grinding passes
87. Check raceway geometry using talysurf profilometer, waviness Wa less than 0.5 μm
88. Verify cylindricity of outer diameter, maximum deviation 0.002mm per 10mm length
89. Inspect grinding burn using acid etch test (5% nital solution for 10 seconds)
90. Parts showing grinding burn (white etching areas) are rejected and scrapped

## Superfinishing Process

91. Load ground races into vibratory superfinishing machine with 8 spindle positions
92. Install fine-grit abrasive film (5 μm aluminum oxide) on oscillating shoes
93. Apply light contact pressure of 1-2 N/cm² between film and raceway surface
94. Oscillate film at 1200 cycles/minute while rotating part at 60 rpm
95. Flow superfinishing oil (low viscosity mineral oil) over contact zone at 2 liters/minute
96. Continue superfinishing for 30-45 seconds until surface finish improves to Ra 0.05 μm
97. Measure surface roughness using contact profilometer after each batch
98. Inspect surface for superfinish marks using 100x optical microscope
99. Verify that crosshatch angle of surface lay is 45-60° for optimal lubrication performance
100. Clean superfinished parts in vapor degreaser using trichloroethylene at 87°C

## Ball Manufacturing - Cold Heading

101. Receive AISI 52100 bearing steel wire, diameter 3.2mm for producing 3mm finished balls
102. Verify wire hardness of 200-240 HV suitable for cold heading operations
103. Load wire into high-speed ball heading machine capable of 300 pieces/minute
104. Form rough ball spheroids by impact heading between two die halves
105. Control heading force to avoid internal cracks while achieving 85-90% sphericity
106. Collect headed balls in collection bin, inspect random samples for heading defects
107. Separate oversized and undersized balls using vibrating screen with calibrated apertures
108. Heat treat ball blanks using same process as races (austenitize, quench, sub-zero, temper)
109. Verify ball hardness of 60-66 HRC after heat treatment
110. Sort balls by diameter using precision ball sorter with 0.0001mm discrimination

## Ball Manufacturing - Grinding and Lapping

111. Load hardened ball blanks into centerless ball grinding machine
112. Position balls between two cast iron grinding plates with spiral grooves
113. Rotate upper plate at 60 rpm while lower plate rotates at 50 rpm in opposite direction
114. Add silicon carbide abrasive compound (F320 grit) mixed with oil-based vehicle
115. Grind balls for 20 minutes, removing 0.15mm from diameter
116. Measure ball diameter periodically using ball micrometer with 0.001mm accuracy
117. Progress through finer abrasive grits: F500, F800, F1200 for 15 minutes each
118. Clean balls between grit changes using ultrasonic cleaner with isopropyl alcohol
119. Transfer balls to lapping machine with fine cast iron plates
120. Apply chromium oxide lapping compound (1 μm particle size) in mineral oil carrier
121. Lap balls for 40-60 minutes to achieve sphericity within 0.00013mm (grade 5 balls)
122. Monitor ball diameter reduction every 10 minutes using automated measuring system
123. Continue lapping until surface finish reaches Ra 0.02 μm or better
124. Clean lapped balls thoroughly in ultrasonic bath with hot detergent solution
125. Rinse with deionized water and dry in clean room environment (Class 10,000)
126. Measure final ball diameter using air gauge ball comparator, tolerance ±0.00025mm
127. Sort balls into diameter grades with 0.0001mm increments for precision matching
128. Inspect ball surface under 200x magnification for pits, scratches, or lapping marks
129. Test ball sphericity using precision V-block setup with LVDT indicator
130. Verify ball lot variation (difference between largest and smallest ball) under 0.00013mm

## Cage Manufacturing

131. Receive phosphor bronze strip (C5191 alloy) in 0.3mm thickness for pressed cage design
132. Uncoil strip and feed into progressive stamping die with 12 stations
133. Pierce pilot holes in first station for accurate strip positioning
134. Blank out cage circular form in station 3 with clearance of 3% of material thickness
135. Form ball pockets by coining operation in stations 4-6, pocket depth 1.2mm
136. Create cage rim reinforcement ribs using embossing dies in station 7
137. Pierce center hole and ball pocket openings in stations 8-9
138. Form retention tabs that hold cage to inner race in station 10
139. Bend cage into final cylindrical shape using progressive forming dies
140. Join cage ends using resistance welding with 2000 amp current for 0.1 seconds
141. Eject finished cage from die and inspect for dimensional accuracy
142. Verify pocket spacing uniformity (for 7-ball bearing, pockets at 51.43° intervals)
143. Check pocket depth using depth micrometer, tolerance ±0.02mm
144. Inspect weld quality under magnification, ensure full penetration with no voids
145. Deburr any sharp edges using vibratory tumbling with walnut shell media for 20 minutes
146. Clean cages in ultrasonic cleaner with alkaline solution to remove stamping oils
147. Apply corrosion-resistant coating (zinc plating 5-8 μm thick) via barrel plating process
148. Rinse plated cages and dry in heated air dryer at 80°C for 15 minutes

## Bearing Assembly - Ball Set Preparation

149. Select ball set for specific bearing from graded inventory, all balls within 0.00005mm diameter range
150. Count exact number of balls required (typically 7 balls for miniature deep groove bearing)
151. Clean selected balls in filtered isopropyl alcohol and dry with filtered nitrogen gas
152. Inspect balls one final time under magnifying glass for any surface defects
153. Place balls in covered tray lined with lint-free paper to prevent contamination

## Bearing Assembly - Race and Ball Installation

154. Move to clean assembly room (Class 1,000 cleanroom with HEPA filtration)
155. Clean inner and outer races with filtered compressed air to remove any particles
156. Place outer race in assembly fixture with raceway facing upward
157. Apply thin film of bearing lubricant (0.005 grams) to outer race raceway
158. Position cage into outer race, aligning retention features
159. Place first ball into cage pocket and press gently into raceway groove
160. Continue loading balls one at a time into remaining pockets, maintaining even spacing
161. Tilt outer race assembly to 30° angle to aid ball retention during installation
162. Position inner race above ball set, aligning raceway with balls
163. Apply light downward pressure while rotating inner race to engage all balls simultaneously
164. Use special assembly tool with controlled force limit (50 N maximum) to seat inner race
165. Verify inner race is fully seated by measuring assembled width with micrometer
166. Rotate bearing by hand to check for smooth operation and uniform resistance
167. Listen for any clicking or grinding sounds indicating assembly issues

## Lubrication Application

169. Calculate precise lubricant quantity based on bearing free volume (typically 25-35% fill)
170. Prepare synthetic oil lubricant (PAO base with anti-wear additives, viscosity 20 cSt at 40°C)
171. Draw measured dose of lubricant (0.008 grams) into precision dispensing syringe
172. Apply lubricant into bearing through gap between inner and outer race
173. Rotate bearing slowly by hand 10-20 revolutions to distribute lubricant evenly
174. For shielded bearings, fill cavity on both sides of bearing with grease lubricant
175. Use lithium complex grease with NLGI grade 2 consistency for long-life applications
176. Dispense grease using automated grease gun with 0.001 gram accuracy
177. Fill to 40% of bearing free volume for optimal life and low friction
178. Wipe excess grease from bearing exterior using lint-free cloth

180. Inspect shields for flatness (maximum 0.02mm deviation) and edge burrs

182. Position first shield over inner race with seal lip facing inward

185. Verify shield retention by attempting to remove with fingernail (should be secure)

## Pre-load Setting

190. Place bearing on spin test machine with precision spindle (runout under 0.0005mm)
191. Apply axial load using calibrated spring or pneumatic system
192. For contact angle bearing conversion, load to 2-5 N to create ball contact angles
193. Rotate bearing at 1000 rpm for 2 minutes under preload to establish contact pattern
194. Measure starting torque using precision torque transducer, target 0.3-0.5 N·mm
195. Measure radial play after preload setting, verify reduction to 0.001-0.003mm
196. Use eddy current displacement sensor to measure bearing deflection under load
197. Calculate axial and radial stiffness from load-deflection curve
198. For speed-rated bearings, verify preload allows target speed (30,000-50,000 rpm for miniature bearings)

## Quality Testing - Vibration Analysis

199. Install bearing on precision vibration test spindle in anechoic test chamber
200. Accelerate bearing to test speed of 1800 rpm (equivalent to 30 Hz for 3mm balls)
201. Measure vibration using piezoelectric accelerometer mounted on outer race
202. Record vibration velocity in frequency bands: V10 (50-300 Hz), V20p (300-1800 Hz)
203. Compare measured values to ABEC-5 limits: V10 under 10 μm/s, V20p under 15 μm/s
204. Analyze vibration spectrum using FFT analyzer to identify defect frequencies
205. Check for ball pass frequency outer race (BPFO = n × RPM × (1 - d/D × cos α) / 60)
206. Check for ball pass frequency inner race (BPFI = n × RPM × (1 + d/D × cos α) / 60)
207. Verify no resonant peaks exceed background noise by more than 6 dB
208. Test bearing at multiple speeds: 1800, 3600, 5400 rpm to map vibration characteristics

## Quality Testing - Noise Measurement

209. Transfer bearing to acoustic testing station with background noise under 30 dB(A)
210. Run bearing at 1800 rpm on precision air spindle (air bearing) to eliminate external vibration
211. Position precision condenser microphone 100mm from bearing at 45° angle
212. Measure sound pressure level in dB(A) weighted scale
213. Record narrow-band acoustic spectrum from 20 Hz to 20 kHz
214. Verify total noise level under 42 dB(A) for quiet running grade bearings
215. Identify any tonal components indicating waviness or ball size variations
216. Grade bearings into noise categories: Z1 (quietest), Z2, Z3, Z4 based on acoustic signature
217. Reject bearings with irregular noise patterns or discrete tones exceeding limits

## Quality Testing - Functional Performance

218. Mount bearing on endurance test rig with radial load of 50 N and axial load of 10 N
219. Rotate bearing at 10,000 rpm for 2 hours continuous operation
220. Monitor bearing temperature using IR thermometer, maximum temperature 80°C
221. Measure torque continuously during run-in period using inline torque sensor

226. Test bearing speed rating by accelerating to maximum speed (50,000 rpm for precision miniature bearings)
