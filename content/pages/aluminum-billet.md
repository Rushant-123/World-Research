# Aluminum Billet Manufacturing for Mac Unibody Enclosures

## Phase 1: Alloy Selection and Specification (Steps 1-20)

1. Evaluate material requirements for Mac unibody applications requiring high strength-to-weight ratio and excellent surface finish capability.

2. Select 6061-T6 aluminum alloy as primary material for structural components due to superior mechanical properties (yield strength 276 MPa).

3. Specify 6063-T6 aluminum alloy for decorative extrusions requiring excellent anodizing characteristics and surface aesthetics.

4. Verify alloy composition for 6061: 0.8-1.2% Mg, 0.4-0.8% Si, 0.15-0.4% Cu, 0.04-0.35% Cr with aluminum balance.

5. Confirm 6063 alloy composition: 0.45-0.9% Mg, 0.2-0.6% Si, maximum 0.1% Cu for optimal extrudability.

6. Source certified aluminum ingots from primary smelters meeting ASTM B221 specifications for extrusion alloys.

7. Review material certificates verifying chemical composition analysis by optical emission spectroscopy.

8. Inspect incoming ingots for surface defects, cracks, inclusions, and dimensional conformity to procurement specifications.

9. Verify ingot dimensions typically 6-9 meters length, 500-700mm diameter for large-scale extrusion operations.

10. Check ingot weight ranging 2000-4000 kg per unit suitable for continuous casting operations.

11. Measure ingot surface temperature uniformity ensuring variation less than 5°C across entire length.

12. Perform ultrasonic testing to detect internal voids, porosity, or inclusion clusters exceeding 2mm diameter.

13. Conduct hardness testing on ingot samples verifying as-cast hardness 40-50 HB typical for heat-treatable alloys.

14. Analyze grain structure through metallographic examination confirming dendritic arm spacing 50-100 microns.

15. Test chemical homogeneity across ingot cross-section ensuring composition variation within ±0.05% for major alloying elements.

16. Record ingot traceability data including heat number, casting date, smelter location, and quality certifications.

17. Store ingots in controlled environment maintaining temperature 15-25°C and relative humidity below 60% preventing oxidation.

18. Implement first-in-first-out inventory rotation preventing ingot age exceeding 6 months before processing.

19. Calculate material yield expectations of 75-85% from ingot to finished billet accounting for scalping and cropping losses.

20. Establish processing parameters optimized for Mac unibody requirements: dimensional tolerance ±0.5mm, surface roughness Ra < 1.6μm.

## Phase 2: Ingot Preparation and Scalping (Steps 21-40)

21. Transfer selected ingots from storage to processing area using overhead cranes with 5-ton lifting capacity.

22. Position ingot on automated scalping lathe equipped with carbide-tipped cutting tools and coolant delivery system.

23. Secure ingot in lathe chuck system with pneumatic clamping force 50-80 kN ensuring concentric rotation.

24. Set lathe spindle speed 40-80 rpm appropriate for ingot diameter and material hardness characteristics.

25. Program scalping depth 5-8mm per pass removing surface oxides, casting defects, and segregation layers.

26. Engage cutting tool with flood coolant flow rate 40-60 liters/minute preventing thermal damage to subsurface material.

27. Machine ingot surface in continuous helical pattern maintaining constant cutting speed 60-100 meters/minute.

28. Remove total surface layer thickness 15-25mm exposing clean aluminum substrate free from defects.

29. Monitor cutting tool wear replacing inserts after processing 20-30 ingots or detecting edge chipping.

30. Collect scalping chips and turnings for recycling recovery, typically representing 3-5% of ingot weight.

31. Inspect scalped surface visually for remaining defects, tool marks, or surface irregularities requiring additional passes.

32. Measure scalped ingot diameter with calibrated micrometers verifying dimensional accuracy within ±1mm tolerance.

33. Check surface finish using profilometer confirming roughness Ra < 3.2μm suitable for subsequent heating operations.

34. Clean scalped ingot surface with compressed air removing loose particles and residual coolant contamination.

35. Mark ingot with heat number and processing date using permanent industrial marking system.

36. Transfer scalped ingot to saw station for length sectioning into billet blanks.

37. Position ingot on power saw bed with carbide-tipped blade 600-800mm diameter, 4-6mm thickness.

38. Set cutting speed 1000-1500 rpm with feed rate 50-100 mm/minute for clean cut surfaces.

39. Saw ingot into billet lengths 300-600mm depending on final extrusion profile requirements and press capacity.

40. Deburr sawn ends using wire brush or abrasive pad removing sharp edges and metal fragments.

## Phase 3: Homogenization Heat Treatment (Steps 41-65)

41. Load billet blanks onto furnace charging rack maintaining 100-150mm spacing for uniform air circulation.

42. Record billet identification, quantity, alloy type, and charging time in furnace operation log.

43. Transfer rack into homogenization furnace preheated to 540°C ±5°C for 6061 alloy processing.

44. Close furnace door immediately minimizing heat loss and maintaining thermal efficiency above 85%.

45. Monitor furnace temperature using Type K thermocouples positioned at multiple zones: entry, center, and exit.

46. Maintain homogenization temperature 540°C for 6061 alloy dissolving low-melting eutectic phases and reducing microsegregation.

47. Adjust furnace setpoint to 520°C for 6063 alloy requiring lower homogenization temperature preventing grain growth.

48. Control heating rate 50-100°C/hour during ramp-up preventing thermal shock and cracking in billet mass.

49. Hold billets at homogenization temperature for 4-8 hours depending on billet diameter and alloy composition.

50. Calculate soaking time using rule: 1 hour per 25mm of billet diameter ensuring core reaches target temperature.

51. Monitor furnace atmosphere maintaining slightly oxidizing conditions preventing excessive surface oxidation or hydrogen pickup.

52. Verify temperature uniformity across furnace chamber within ±10°C ensuring consistent heat treatment throughout billet batch.

53. Record continuous temperature data using chart recorder or digital data acquisition system for quality traceability.

54. Observe billet surface through furnace viewport checking for uniform heating indicated by consistent surface appearance.

55. Test atmosphere composition periodically measuring oxygen content 1-3% and moisture level below 0.01%.

56. Inspect burner operation ensuring complete combustion without flame impingement on billet surfaces.

57. Monitor energy consumption tracking fuel input and thermal efficiency metrics for process optimization.

58. Complete homogenization cycle when metallurgical transformation achieved: Mg2Si precipitates dissolved, dendritic segregation minimized.

59. Verify homogenization effectiveness through microstructural analysis of sample billets confirming uniform grain structure.

60. Measure post-homogenization hardness expecting 10-15% reduction compared to as-cast condition indicating successful treatment.

61. Initiate controlled cooling by reducing furnace temperature 100°C/hour to 400°C preventing thermal stress.

62. Open furnace door gradually when temperature reaches 400°C allowing slow air cooling to room temperature.

63. Alternative rapid cooling: Transfer billets directly to 450-500°C holding furnace maintaining temperature for immediate extrusion.

64. Inspect homogenized billets for surface condition checking for excessive oxidation, scale formation, or thermal cracking.

65. Store homogenized billets in temperature-controlled area if not extruding immediately, maintaining temperature above 100°C preventing age hardening.

## Phase 4: Billet Preheating for Extrusion (Steps 66-85)

66. Transfer homogenized billets to induction heating system or gas-fired furnace for extrusion temperature conditioning.

67. Select target billet temperature 450°C for 6063 alloy providing optimal material flow during extrusion.

68. Set billet temperature 480-500°C for 6061 alloy requiring higher temperature due to increased alloy content.

69. Load billets into induction coil heating system providing rapid, uniform heating with minimal surface oxidation.

70. Energize induction heater operating at frequency 1-10 kHz generating electromagnetic field inducing eddy currents.

71. Monitor heating rate 200-300°C/hour significantly faster than conventional furnace heating improving productivity.

72. Rotate billets during heating ensuring circumferential temperature uniformity within ±5°C around perimeter.

73. Alternative gas furnace heating: Position billets on hearth with burners providing radiant and convective heat transfer.

74. Control furnace atmosphere using nitrogen injection or low-oxygen combustion minimizing surface oxide formation.

75. Measure billet surface temperature using infrared pyrometer or thermal imaging camera with accuracy ±2°C.

76. Verify core temperature reaching target using embedded thermocouples in test billets validating heating profile.

77. Hold billets at extrusion temperature 15-30 minutes ensuring thermal equilibrium throughout billet cross-section.

78. Calculate temperature soak time: minimum 20 seconds per mm of billet diameter for complete heat penetration.

79. Monitor temperature drop during transfer from furnace to press maintaining billet temperature within 20°C of target.

80. Use insulated transfer tongs or automated handling system minimizing heat loss during 30-60 second transfer period.

81. Preheat extrusion die to 400-450°C preventing rapid billet cooling upon contact and improving material flow.

82. Preheat extrusion container (cylinder) to 350-400°C reducing thermal gradients and extending tooling life.

83. Apply graphite-based lubricant to billet surface if using indirect extrusion reducing friction and improving surface finish.

84. Inspect heated billet surface for excessive oxidation indicated by dark gray or black appearance requiring removal.

85. Transfer heated billet immediately to extrusion press maintaining production rhythm and thermal efficiency.

## Phase 5: Hot Extrusion Process (Steps 86-120)

86. Position heated billet in extrusion press container bore diameter 200-400mm depending on press capacity 1000-5000 tons.

87. Center billet concentrically in container using automated alignment system ensuring uniform material flow pattern.

88. Insert dummy block between ram and billet providing thermal barrier and pressure distribution surface.

89. Close press frame and activate hydraulic system developing pressure 300-500 MPa for aluminum extrusion.

90. Select extrusion ratio (billet area / profile area) 10:1 to 40:1 balancing productivity and material properties.

91. Calculate ram speed 2-15 mm/second based on alloy type, exit temperature limit, and profile complexity.

92. Initiate ram advance forcing billet material through die opening shaped to final profile geometry.

93. Monitor breakthrough pressure spike 20-30% above running pressure indicating initial material entering die cavity.

94. Observe first emerging material (butt end) checking for surface defects and proper die filling before continuing.

95. Discard initial 100-300mm of extruded profile containing core material with potential defects or contamination.

96. Maintain steady-state extrusion with constant ram speed ensuring uniform material flow and consistent profile dimensions.

97. Monitor exit temperature using infrared sensor maintaining 480-520°C preventing excessive grain growth or surface tearing.

98. Control extrusion speed dynamically adjusting ram velocity maintaining exit temperature within optimal range.

99. Apply nitrogen gas or air-water mist cooling to die face controlling heat buildup from plastic deformation.

100. Observe profile emerging from die checking for straightness, surface quality, and dimensional conformity in real-time.

101. Support extruded profile on roller conveyor system immediately after die exit preventing bending under self-weight.

102. Extend profile to length 10-25 meters determined by press table capacity and handling system capabilities.

103. Monitor container pressure throughout extrusion tracking pressure rise indicating increasing friction from billet remnant.

104. Terminate extrusion when billet remainder reaches discard thickness 15-25mm preventing die damage and ensuring operator safety.

105. Retract ram and eject remaining billet butt (discard) for recycling recovering 5-10% material loss per cycle.

106. Clean container bore removing aluminum residue and applying graphite lubricant for next extrusion cycle.

107. Inspect die for aluminum pickup, galling, or wear after each extrusion or more frequently for complex profiles.

108. Measure die dimensional wear using coordinate measuring machine after 50-100 extrusion cycles.

109. Record extrusion parameters: billet temperature, container temperature, die temperature, ram speed, pressure profile, exit temperature.

110. Calculate extrusion productivity: 200-500 kg/hour for complex Mac unibody profiles depending on press size.

114. Measure wall thickness variation across profile cross-section maintaining tolerance ±0.3mm for precision applications.

115. Assess surface finish quality inspecting for pickup marks, die lines, or surface tearing requiring die correction.

116. Document production output tracking extrusion count, total weight, scrap percentage, and equipment efficiency.

117. Perform die changeover between profiles requiring 30-60 minutes for die removal, installation, and preheat cycle.

118. Optimize multi-cavity dies producing multiple profiles simultaneously increasing productivity 50-100%.
