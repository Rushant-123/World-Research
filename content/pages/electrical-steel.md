## Silicon Steel Production

1. Receive hot-rolled silicon steel coils containing 2-4% silicon content from upstream steel mill
2. Inspect incoming coil surface for defects including scratches, scale, and surface irregularities
3. Verify chemical composition through spectrographic analysis ensuring silicon content within specification
4. Check coil dimensions including width, thickness, and weight against purchase order specifications
5. Measure magnetic properties of incoming material using Epstein frame to establish baseline
6. Store coils in controlled environment maintaining temperature at 20°C and humidity below 60%
7. Transfer coil to decoiling station using overhead crane with magnetic lifting attachments
8. Mount coil on powered decoiler with hydraulic expanding mandrel for secure grip
9. Thread steel strip through entry bridle with dancer roll for tension control
10. Clean steel surface using alkaline degreasing solution to remove mill oils and contaminants
11. Rinse with deionized water spray at 40°C to eliminate cleaning chemical residues
12. Apply acid pickling solution (10% hydrochloric acid) to remove surface oxides and scale
13. Monitor pickling time (2-3 minutes) based on strip speed and oxide thickness
14. Rinse thoroughly with cascading deionized water to neutralize acid and prevent staining
15. Dry strip using hot air knives at 80°C to prepare surface for cold rolling

## Cold Rolling Process

16. Thread dried strip into first stand of tandem cold rolling mill configuration
17. Set work roll gap to achieve 30% thickness reduction in first pass
18. Apply rolling oil emulsion (5% concentration) for lubrication and cooling during reduction
19. Monitor roll force (800 tons) and mill power draw to ensure consistent reduction
20. Control strip tension between stands at 15 MPa using automatic tension regulators
21. Pass strip through second stand achieving additional 25% thickness reduction
22. Adjust roll speed differential to prevent slippage and surface defects
23. Apply additional rolling lubricant through high-pressure spray nozzles between stands
24. Continue reduction through third stand bringing strip to intermediate gauge
25. Monitor strip temperature (not exceeding 150°C) to prevent work hardening
26. Pass through fourth stand achieving 20% reduction approaching final thickness
27. Control strip flatness using work roll bending and intermediate roll shifting
28. Final pass through fifth stand reducing to target thickness of 0.35mm for standard grade
29. Measure exit thickness continuously using laser or X-ray thickness gauge
30. Apply final tension using exit bridle maintaining 12 MPa for coil winding

## First Annealing Process

31. Coil cold-rolled strip onto mandrel creating tight coil with consistent tension
32. Weigh finished coil and verify dimensions match production specifications
33. Transfer coil to batch annealing furnace using specialized coil handling equipment
34. Load multiple coils into bell-type annealing furnace (15-ton capacity)
35. Position inner cover over coil stack creating sealed annealing chamber
36. Lower outer heating bell over inner cover to complete furnace assembly
37. Evacuate air from annealing chamber to prevent oxidation during heating
38. Introduce protective atmosphere (75% hydrogen, 25% nitrogen) at slight positive pressure
39. Begin heating cycle ramping temperature at controlled rate of 50°C/hour
40. Hold at intermediate temperature of 650°C for 4 hours for stress relief
41. Increase temperature to 850°C for recrystallization annealing over 6 hours
42. Maintain at 850°C for 12 hours allowing complete grain growth and softening
43. Monitor atmosphere composition continuously ensuring proper reducing conditions
44. Begin controlled cooling at 30°C/hour to 400°C maintaining atmosphere protection
45. Allow furnace to cool naturally to ambient temperature over 24 hours

## Grain-Oriented Processing (for GO grades)

46. Remove coils from annealing furnace after complete cooling cycle
47. Inspect annealed surface for discoloration or oxidation indicating atmosphere problems
48. Measure magnetic properties confirming improved permeability after annealing
49. Test mechanical properties verifying adequate ductility for further processing
50. Mount coil on decoiler for second cold rolling reduction (for grain-oriented steel)

52. Control rolling parameters precisely to induce Goss texture {110}<001>
53. Monitor surface finish ensuring no scratches that could disrupt grain orientation
54. Recoil strip maintaining consistent tension to prevent buckles or telescoping
