## Manufacturing Process

### Raw Material Preparation

1. Receive silicon tetrachloride (SiCl4) in ISO tank containers at 99.5% minimum purity
2. Transfer SiCl4 to stainless steel storage tanks with nitrogen blanket protection
3. Maintain storage temperature at 15-25°C to prevent hydrolysis from atmospheric moisture
4. Monitor SiCl4 purity using gas chromatography, checking for chlorosilane impurities
5. Verify hydrogen gas supply at 99.999% purity from tube trailer manifolds
6. Connect hydrogen supply to flame reactors through mass flow controllers
7. Establish oxygen supply from liquid oxygen tanks at 99.5% purity minimum
8. Install oxygen vaporizers to convert liquid O2 to gaseous phase at controlled rates
9. Set up nitrogen purge system for reactor startup and shutdown procedures
10. Calibrate all gas mass flow controllers to ±0.5% accuracy for precise stoichiometry

### Fumed Silica Production - Flame Hydrolysis

11. Preheat the flame hydrolysis reactor chamber to 300°C using electric heating elements
12. Initiate hydrogen-oxygen pilot flame at stoichiometric ratio (2:1) in burner assembly
13. Verify flame temperature reaches 1800-2000°C using optical pyrometry
14. Begin feeding silicon tetrachloride vapor into the hydrogen flame core
15. Maintain SiCl4 feed rate at 50-200 kg/hr depending on reactor capacity
16. Control hydrogen flow at 2-3 times stoichiometric requirement for complete hydrolysis
17. Monitor oxygen flow to maintain flame temperature in optimal 1800-2200°C range
18. Observe immediate hydrolysis reaction: SiCl4 + 2H2 + O2 → SiO2 + 4HCl
19. Achieve residence time of 0.1-0.5 seconds in high-temperature flame zone
20. Control particle nucleation by adjusting flame temperature and gas velocities

### Primary Particle Formation

21. Monitor primary particle formation in flame, creating spheres of 5-50 nm diameter
22. Control particle size by adjusting SiCl4 concentration and flame temperature
23. Maintain turbulent flow conditions for uniform particle distribution
24. Allow particles to collide and fuse in high-temperature zone forming aggregates
25. Achieve aggregate sizes of 100-500 nm through controlled collision kinetics
26. Monitor aggregate formation using in-situ laser light scattering
27. Control agglomerate structure by managing cooling rate after flame zone
28. Establish specific surface area through primary particle size control
29. Target BET surface areas ranging from 50 m²/g to 400 m²/g based on grade
30. Create hydrophilic silica with silanol (Si-OH) groups at 40-60% surface coverage

### Product Collection - Cyclone Separation

31. Direct hot gas stream containing silica into primary cyclone separator
32. Maintain cyclone inlet temperature at 150-200°C to prevent moisture condensation
33. Separate coarse silica particles (>10 μm agglomerates) in primary cyclone
34. Achieve 70-80% collection efficiency in primary cyclone stage
35. Discharge collected silica through rotary valve into pneumatic conveying system
36. Route remaining gas stream to secondary cyclone for fine particle recovery
37. Capture additional 15-20% of product in secondary cyclone separator
38. Maintain cyclone pressure drop at 500-1000 Pa for optimal separation
39. Monitor cyclone wall temperature to prevent silica buildup and plugging
40. Combine primary and secondary cyclone products in blending system

### HCl Gas Scrubbing

41. Route exhaust gas containing HCl to wet scrubbing system
42. Contact gas with water in packed column scrubber at countercurrent flow
43. Absorb HCl gas into water forming hydrochloric acid solution
44. Maintain scrubber liquid pH at 0-2 indicating efficient HCl capture
45. Achieve >99% HCl removal efficiency in scrubbing system
46. Monitor scrubber outlet gas for HCl using continuous analyzers (<10 ppm target)
47. Collect hydrochloric acid byproduct at 20-32% concentration
48. Transfer HCl solution to storage for sale or neutralization
49. Neutralize remaining traces with sodium hydroxide solution if required
50. Release cleaned exhaust gas through stack after pH monitoring

### Bag Filter Collection

51. Install tertiary bag filter system for final fine particle capture
52. Use polyester or PTFE filter bags rated for 150-180°C continuous operation
53. Maintain filtration velocity at 1-2 m/min for optimal cake formation
54. Collect remaining 5-10% of ultra-fine silica particles on filter bags
55. Monitor pressure drop across filters, triggering pulse-jet cleaning at 1500-2000 Pa
56. Execute pulse cleaning using compressed air at 6-8 bar pressure
57. Dislodge filter cake into collection hoppers during pulse cycles
58. Achieve overall collection efficiency >99.5% across all separation stages
59. Combine bag filter catch with cyclone products in final blending
60. Maintain filter chamber temperature above dew point to prevent moisture issues

### Deaeration and Densification

61. Transfer collected fluffy silica (bulk density 30-50 kg/m³) to deaeration system
62. Apply mechanical compaction using rotating paddle mixers
63. Increase bulk density to 50-80 kg/m³ for easier handling and packaging
64. Control compaction to avoid destroying aggregate structure
65. Add air classification step to remove oversized agglomerates if required
66. Screen product through 100-200 mesh screens to remove hard lumps
67. Recycle oversized material back to deaeration system for re-processing
68. Monitor particle size distribution using laser diffraction (target d50: 5-15 μm)
69. Verify aggregate structure integrity using transmission electron microscopy
70. Measure tapped density achieving 60-100 kg/m³ depending on grade

### Surface Area Analysis

71. Collect representative samples from deaerated product for BET analysis
72. Degas samples at 150°C under vacuum for 2-4 hours to remove adsorbed moisture
73. Perform nitrogen adsorption at -196°C (liquid nitrogen temperature)
74. Generate complete adsorption-desorption isotherms using automated analyzer
75. Calculate BET specific surface area from isotherm data (typically 50-400 m²/g)
76. Verify hydrophilic grades achieve 150-250 m²/g surface area range
77. Confirm high surface area grades exceed 300 m²/g for specialized applications
78. Measure total pore volume finding negligible internal porosity (<0.1 cm³/g)
79. Determine external surface area matches BET area confirming non-porous structure
80. Document surface area results for product certification and lot tracking

### Precipitated Silica Production - Alternative Process

81. Prepare sodium silicate solution (water glass) at 3-4% SiO2 concentration
82. Heat dilute silicate solution to 80-95°C in stirred reactor vessel
83. Adjust pH to 10-11 using sodium hydroxide if required
84. Begin simultaneous addition of sulfuric acid (8-15% concentration)
85. Maintain controlled precipitation conditions with vigorous agitation
86. Monitor pH continuously, targeting specific profiles (pH 7-10) during precipitation
87. Control precipitation rate to achieve desired particle size (5-100 nm primaries)
88. Continue acid addition over 30-90 minutes depending on batch size
89. Reach endpoint at pH 6-7 when precipitation is complete
90. Age precipitate for 10-30 minutes at 80-95°C to control structure

### Precipitated Silica - Filtering and Washing

91. Filter precipitated silica slurry using filter press or rotary vacuum filter
92. Achieve filter cake with 15-25% solids content after initial dewatering
93. Wash filter cake with deionized water to remove sodium sulfate byproduct
94. Monitor wash water conductivity, continuing until <500 μS/cm achieved
95. Verify sulfate removal using ion chromatography (<0.5% Na2SO4 residual)
96. Re-slurry washed cake if higher purity required for specialty grades
97. Perform second washing cycle for low-sodium grades (<1% Na2O content)
98. Measure final cake pH targeting 6.0-7.5 for neutral precipitated silica
99. Achieve final cake moisture content of 75-85% after washing completion
100. Transfer washed cake to drying systems for moisture removal

### Spray Drying - Precipitated Silica

101. Prepare silica slurry at 15-20% solids for spray drying operation
102. Pump slurry through high-pressure nozzles or rotary atomizers
103. Atomize slurry into fine droplets (10-100 μm) in spray dryer chamber
104. Contact droplets with hot air at 300-500°C inlet temperature
105. Evaporate water rapidly forming spherical agglomerates of 50-200 μm
106. Maintain outlet temperature at 100-140°C to control final moisture
107. Achieve powder moisture content of 3-7% after spray drying
108. Collect dried powder using cyclone and bag filter combination
109. Control agglomerate density through atomization and drying parameters
110. Measure bulk density achieving 150-350 kg/m³ for precipitated grades

### Surface Modification - Hydrophobic Treatment

111. Select fumed silica for hydrophobic modification via silylation
112. Transfer silica to heated mixer vessel with temperature control
113. Heat silica to 150-200°C under nitrogen atmosphere to activate surface
114. Add silylating agent (hexamethyldisilazane, dimethyldichlorosilane, or siloxanes)
115. Maintain treatment temperature at 150-250°C for 1-3 hours
116. Achieve reaction: Si-OH + silylating agent → Si-O-Si(CH3)3 + byproduct
117. Replace hydrophilic silanol groups with hydrophobic trimethylsilyl groups
118. Control degree of hydrophobicity by adjusting silylating agent dosage
119. Achieve carbon content of 1-4% indicating 50-90% silanol coverage
120. Purge reaction vessel with nitrogen to remove reaction byproducts

### Hydrophobic Silica Processing

121. Cool treated silica to ambient temperature under inert atmosphere
122. Remove any unreacted silylating agent through vacuum stripping
123. Deagglomerate product using air jet mill or pin mill if required
124. Verify hydrophobicity using methanol wettability test (>50% for hydrophobic)
125. Measure water contact angle achieving >90° for hydrophobic surface
126. Test oil absorption capacity finding 150-300 mL/100g depending on surface area
127. Verify lower bulk density of 30-60 kg/m³ for hydrophobic fumed silica
128. Confirm improved dispersibility in non-polar solvents and polymers
129. Package hydrophobic grades separately from hydrophilic products
130. Label clearly indicating surface treatment type and handling requirements

### Particle Size Analysis

131. Collect samples from production lots for comprehensive particle characterization
132. Prepare dilute dispersions (0.01-0.1%) in water or appropriate solvent
133. Apply ultrasonic dispersion for 2-5 minutes at controlled power
134. Measure particle size distribution using laser diffraction analyzer
135. Verify agglomerate d10 typically 2-5 μm, d50 at 5-15 μm, d90 at 15-40 μm
136. Perform dynamic light scattering for primary particle size estimation
137. Calculate primary particle diameter from BET: d = 6/(ρ × BET) in nanometers
138. Verify primary particles range from 7 nm (400 m²/g) to 40 nm (50 m²/g)
139. Examine aggregate structure using transmission electron microscopy
140. Image agglomerate morphology using scanning electron microscopy

### Thixotropic Properties Testing

141. Prepare silica dispersions in reference oils or test fluids
142. Mix silica at 2-8% concentration using high-shear mixer
143. Allow dispersion to rest forming three-dimensional network structure
144. Measure viscosity at low shear rates (0.1-1 s⁻¹) using rotational rheometer
145. Observe high viscosity at rest due to particle-particle interactions
146. Apply increasing shear rate observing viscosity decrease (shear thinning)
147. Demonstrate viscosity drop of 10-100× from rest to high shear conditions
148. Measure viscosity recovery after shear removal (thixotropic recovery)
149. Achieve 80-95% viscosity recovery within 1-5 minutes for fumed silica
150. Document thixotropic index (ratio of low to high shear viscosity)

### Rheology and Flow Behavior

151. Test silica reinforcement in liquid systems (coatings, adhesives, sealants)
152. Measure yield stress development in filled systems (1-50 Pa typical)
153. Evaluate anti-settling properties preventing pigment sagging
154. Test anti-sagging performance on vertical surfaces (no flow <1 mm/hr)
155. Measure sag resistance in coatings applied at 200-500 μm wet thickness
156. Verify leveling properties allowing smooth surface formation after application
157. Test spray application properties ensuring good atomization
158. Evaluate brush drag and application feel in paints and coatings

160. Document recommended dosage levels (0.5-5%) for different applications

### Oil Absorption Testing

161. Perform oil absorption test following ISO 787-5 standard method
162. Weigh 5-10 g silica sample accurately to ±0.01 g
163. Add linseed oil or dibutyl phthalate dropwise while mixing manually
164. Continue addition until paste transforms to putty-like consistency
165. Record oil volume required to reach defined endpoint consistency
166. Calculate oil absorption: mL oil / 100g silica (typical range 100-330 mL/100g)
167. Verify high surface area grades (300-400 m²/g) show 250-330 mL/100g absorption
168. Confirm medium grades (150-250 m²/g) achieve 150-250 mL/100g absorption
169. Correlate oil absorption with BET surface area and application performance
170. Use oil absorption data to predict filler loading limits in formulations

### pH and Moisture Analysis

171. Prepare 4% aqueous dispersion of silica for pH measurement
172. Agitate dispersion vigorously ensuring complete wetting of particles
173. Allow dispersion to settle for 30 minutes before pH measurement
174. Measure pH using calibrated pH meter with temperature compensation
175. Verify hydrophilic fumed silica shows pH 3.5-4.5 (acidic due to silanol groups)
176. Confirm precipitated silica grades achieve pH 6.0-7.5 (near neutral)
177. Perform moisture analysis using loss on drying method at 105°C for 2 hours
178. Verify moisture content <1.5% for fumed silica grades after production
179. Confirm precipitated silica moisture at 3-7% after spray drying
180. Monitor moisture content during storage ensuring no significant increase
