## MASS RECRUITMENT OPERATIONS

1. **Regional Recruitment Planning**: Analyze production forecasts to determine workforce requirements across manufacturing facilities, typically planning for 50,000-200,000 workers per major campus during peak production seasons.

2. **Recruitment Agency Partnerships**: Establish relationships with labor agencies in rural provinces (Henan, Sichuan, Anhui), negotiating placement fees of 500-1,000 RMB per worker recruited.

3. **Job Fair Organization**: Host large-scale recruitment events in provincial capitals, setting up registration stations that can process 2,000-5,000 applicants per day.

4. **Online Application Systems**: Deploy mobile-friendly recruitment platforms allowing candidates to submit applications, upload ID documents, and schedule interviews from smartphones.

5. **Age and Education Verification**: Screen applicants for basic requirements: 18-45 years old, junior high school education minimum, valid national ID card, no criminal record.

6. **Skills Assessment Testing**: Conduct basic dexterity tests including small parts assembly, visual inspection tasks, and hand-eye coordination exercises taking 15-20 minutes per candidate.

7. **Physical Health Screening**: Perform initial health checks including vision tests (corrected to 20/40 minimum), hearing tests, and basic mobility assessments to ensure fitness for production work.

8. **Background Verification**: Submit candidate information to local police databases for criminal history checks, processing 500-1,000 verifications daily through automated systems.

9. **Interview Processing**: Conduct brief 10-15 minute interviews covering work history, availability for overtime, willingness to work nights/weekends, and dormitory residence acceptance.

10. **Offer Letter Generation**: Issue conditional employment offers within 48 hours of interview, specifying base wages (2,200-2,800 RMB/month), overtime rates (1.5x-2x), and probation period (1-3 months).

11. **Document Collection**: Gather required paperwork including original ID cards, education certificates, health examination reports (valid within 6 months), and emergency contact information.

12. **Labor Contract Signing**: Execute standardized employment contracts complying with China Labor Contract Law, typically 1-year terms with renewal options, processed through digital signature systems.

13. **Onboarding Scheduling**: Assign new hires to orientation cohorts of 50-100 workers, scheduling start dates to align with production ramp-up schedules.

14. **Transportation Coordination**: Arrange charter buses from recruitment locations to factory campuses, often covering 500-1,500 km journeys taking 8-20 hours.

15. **Welcome Package Distribution**: Provide new worker kits including factory ID cards, meal cards, dormitory keys, uniform vouchers, and orientation schedules upon arrival.

## ASSEMBLY LINE TRAINING

31. **Workstation Assignment**: Place trainees at specific production stations based on product line requirements, physical capabilities, and skills assessment results.

32. **Standard Operating Procedure Distribution**: Issue laminated SOP cards with step-by-step instructions, photos, and quality checkpoints for assigned tasks.

33. **Trainer Pairing System**: Match each new worker with experienced mentor (2+ years experience) for initial 3-5 day hands-on training period.

34. **Demonstration Phase**: Have trainers perform operation 5-10 times at full speed while explaining each action, tool usage, and quality verification steps.

35. **Slow-Speed Practice**: Allow trainees to attempt operations at 50% target speed, focusing on correct technique and quality rather than cycle time.

36. **Error Identification Training**: Intentionally introduce defective parts for trainees to detect, developing visual inspection skills and quality awareness.

37. **Tool Handling Instruction**: Train on proper use of screwdrivers, hex drivers, torque tools, tweezers, and specialized assembly equipment specific to workstation.

38. **Parts Recognition**: Familiarize workers with component identification through visual guides, part numbers, and physical samples to prevent assembly errors.

39. **Sequence Memorization**: Drill assembly sequences through repetition until workers can perform operations without referring to procedure cards.

40. **Speed Building Exercises**: Gradually increase expected cycle time performance from 50% to 70% to 85% to 100% of target rates over 2-3 weeks.

41. **Quality Self-Check Training**: Teach workers to inspect their own work using checklists, measuring tools, and functional tests before passing to next station.

42. **Workstation Changeover Practice**: Train on transitioning between different product variants, understanding part differences and SOP changes.

43. **Material Handling Procedures**: Instruct on proper methods for retrieving components from bins, managing inventory at workstation, and signaling material shortages.

44. **Ergonomic Positioning**: Ensure correct posture, seat height adjustment, tool placement, and repetitive motion techniques to prevent strain injuries.

45. **Cycle Time Monitoring**: Introduce workers to electronic display systems showing real-time performance against targets, encouraging consistent pacing.

46. **Line Stop Procedures**: Train on when and how to activate emergency stop buttons (andon system) when quality issues or safety concerns arise.

47. **Teamwork Coordination**: Practice handoffs between adjacent stations, maintaining line balance, and assisting neighbors during temporary difficulties.

48. **Multi-Skill Development**: After mastering primary station (2-3 months), begin cross-training on adjacent workstations to build flexibility.

49. **Performance Certification**: Conduct formal evaluation after training period, requiring demonstration of target cycle time with <0.5% defect rate for 4 consecutive hours.

50. **Ongoing Coaching**: Provide continuous feedback from supervisors during first 90 days, conducting weekly one-on-one reviews of quality and productivity metrics.

## ESD AND CONTAMINATION TRAINING

51. **Static Electricity Education**: Explain electrostatic discharge fundamentals, how invisible static can damage sensitive electronic components (ICs, displays, sensors).

52. **ESD Garment Procedures**: Train workers to wear anti-static smocks, wrist straps, heel grounders, and ESD-safe footwear before entering controlled production areas.

53. **Wrist Strap Testing**: Demonstrate daily verification of grounding straps using ohmmeter testers, ensuring 1-10 megohm resistance before starting shifts.

54. **Ionization Systems**: Show proper use of ionizing air blowers at workstations to neutralize static charges on parts and packaging materials.

55. **ESD-Safe Material Handling**: Instruct on using only approved pink/black conductive trays, bags, and containers for transporting circuit boards and components.

56. **Cleanroom Protocols**: For sensitive operations (camera modules, displays), train on gowning procedures: hairnets, face masks, gloves, shoe covers, and cleanroom suits.

57. **Hand Washing Standards**: Enforce frequent hand washing with ESD-safe soap, especially after breaks, before handling bare circuit boards or optical components.

58. **Particle Generation Awareness**: Educate workers on minimizing contamination sources: no paper at workstations, controlled air movements, proper tool handling.

59. **Glove Usage Protocols**: Train on when finger cots, nitrile gloves, or ESD gloves are required based on component sensitivity and contamination risk.

60. **Tool Grounding Verification**: Ensure all metal tools, fixtures, and equipment at workstation are properly grounded through visual checks and periodic testing.

61. **Environmental Monitoring**: Explain humidity controls (40-60% RH) and temperature regulation (20-26°C) that support ESD protection in production areas.

62. **Prohibited Items Policy**: Strictly enforce ban on personal electronics, metal jewelry, and non-ESD materials in controlled manufacturing zones.

63. **Violation Consequences**: Communicate that ESD protocol breaches result in immediate line removal, retraining requirements, and potential termination for repeat offenses.

64. **Continuous Awareness**: Display ESD reminder posters, conduct monthly refresher trainings, and share case studies of damage caused by static discharge.

65. **Audit Participation**: Prepare workers for ESD compliance audits by internal teams and customer representatives, understanding documentation and verification requirements.

67. **Extinguisher Training**: Provide hands-on practice with fire extinguishers using controlled training fires, teaching PASS method (Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep).

68. **Emergency Evacuation Drills**: Execute quarterly evacuation exercises, requiring complete factory floor clearance to designated assembly points within 5 minutes.

69. **Chemical Hazard Communication**: Train workers handling solvents, adhesives, or cleaning agents on MSDS interpretation, proper storage, and spill response procedures.

70. **Personal Protective Equipment**: Issue and train on appropriate PPE: safety glasses for assembly areas, cut-resistant gloves for sharp parts, ear protection in high-noise zones (>85 dB).

71. **Lockout/Tagout Procedures**: Instruct maintenance workers and machine operators on energy isolation protocols when servicing equipment, preventing accidental startup.

72. **Electrical Safety Awareness**: Educate production staff on electrical hazards, proper cord management, wet floor precautions, and restrictions on electrical panel access.

73. **Ergonomic Injury Prevention**: Teach proper lifting techniques (squat lifting, team lifts for >20 kg), stretching exercises during breaks, and early reporting of discomfort.

74. **Machine Guarding Compliance**: Ensure workers understand that safety guards must remain in place, training on automatic shutoff sensors and prohibited bypass attempts.

75. **First Aid Training**: Certify team leaders and selected workers in basic first aid, CPR, and AED usage through 8-hour courses with annual recertification.

76. **Incident Reporting Systems**: Establish clear procedures for reporting injuries, near-misses, and unsafe conditions through digital apps and physical forms within 1 hour of occurrence.

77. **Hazard Identification Programs**: Encourage worker participation in spotting risks through safety suggestion systems, offering small rewards (50-100 RMB) for actionable reports.

78. **Confined Space Safety**: For maintenance and facilities workers, provide specialized training on atmospheric testing, ventilation, and rescue procedures for tanks and utility spaces.

79. **Material Handling Equipment**: Certify forklift and pallet jack operators through classroom and practical testing, requiring license renewal every 3 years.

80. **Heat Stress Prevention**: During summer months, implement monitoring for production areas, scheduling additional breaks, providing cooling stations, and recognizing heat illness symptoms.

81. **Noise Exposure Management**: Conduct audiometric testing annually for workers in high-noise areas, enforcing hearing protection requirements and job rotation to limit exposure.

82. **Safety Performance Metrics**: Track leading indicators (safety observations, near-miss reports) and lagging indicators (injury rates, lost time incidents), posting results prominently.

83. **Behavioral Safety Observations**: Train supervisors to conduct peer-to-peer safety observations, providing constructive feedback on unsafe acts and at-risk conditions.

84. **Contractor Safety Integration**: Ensure maintenance contractors, cleaning crews, and temporary workers receive site-specific safety orientation before accessing production areas.

85. **Safety Committee Participation**: Establish worker-management safety committees meeting monthly to review incidents, discuss concerns, and implement corrective actions.
