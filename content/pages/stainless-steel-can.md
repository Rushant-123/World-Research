## Material Preparation and Selection

1. Source 18-8 stainless steel strip (AISI 304) with 18% chromium and 8% nickel composition
2. Verify material certification from steel mill including chemical composition analysis
3. Inspect incoming steel strip for surface defects using visual inspection
4. Measure strip thickness with precision micrometer to confirm 0.25mm ± 0.01mm specification
5. Check strip width uniformity across entire coil length
6. Verify strip flatness using straightness gauge to ensure <0.5mm deviation per meter
7. Test material hardness using Rockwell hardness tester (target: HRB 80-85)
8. Conduct tensile strength testing on sample strips (minimum 520 MPa)
9. Verify elongation properties (minimum 40% elongation at break)
10. Check surface finish quality Ra value (target: Ra 0.4-0.8 μm)
11. Clean steel strip surface using alkaline degreasing solution
12. Rinse strip with deionized water to remove cleaning residue
13. Apply temporary corrosion protection oil film (thickness 1-2 μm)
14. Store steel strip coils in climate-controlled warehouse (20°C, 50% RH)
15. Rotate inventory using FIFO system to prevent material aging

## Coil Unwinding and Blanking

16. Load steel strip coil onto powered decoiler with mandrel expansion
17. Thread strip through straightening roller system (7 rollers, alternating pattern)
18. Adjust straightener gap to 0.23mm for optimal flattening
19. Set strip tension to 150N using dancer roller system
20. Install precision blanking die set (hardened tool steel, 62 HRC)
21. Align blanking punch with die opening to 0.01mm concentricity
22. Set blanking press stroke rate to 200 strokes per minute
23. Configure blank diameter to 52.0mm ± 0.05mm for 18650 can production
24. Activate automatic lubrication system for punch and die
25. Engage strip feeding mechanism with servo-controlled accuracy
26. Monitor strip feed length using laser displacement sensor
27. Execute blanking operation to create circular discs
28. Collect blanked discs on conveyor system with automatic counting
29. Inspect first 10 blanks for dimensional accuracy using CMM
30. Check blank edge quality for burr height (<0.02mm acceptable)
31. Verify blank flatness using surface plate and feeler gauges
32. Remove any detected burrs using vibratory deburring process
33. Apply forming lubricant to blank surfaces (water-soluble synthetic oil)
34. Stack blanks in magazines with anti-scratch separators
35. Transfer blank magazines to cup drawing station

## First Draw Operation (Cup Drawing)

36. Load drawing die set into hydraulic press (400 kN capacity)
37. Install blank holder with programmable cushion pressure control
38. Set blank holder pressure to 180 kN for initial draw
39. Heat die surfaces to 40°C to improve material flow
40. Position first blank over die cavity using vacuum pickup
41. Activate blank holder to secure material with controlled force
42. Advance drawing punch at controlled speed (50 mm/s)
43. Draw blank into cylindrical cup with 50mm depth
44. Control drawing force to prevent material tearing (peak 320 kN)
45. Monitor punch load curve for irregularities indicating defects
46. Apply draw bead geometry to control material flow into die
47. Maintain draw ratio of 1.95:1 (blank diameter to cup diameter)
48. Hold drawn cup under pressure for 0.5 seconds for elastic recovery
49. Retract punch and activate ejector pins to release cup
50. Remove drawn cup and inspect for surface cracks using dye penetrant
51. Measure cup outer diameter (target: 26.65mm ± 0.05mm)
52. Check cup height uniformity (target: 50mm ± 0.2mm)
53. Verify wall thickness distribution using ultrasonic thickness gauge
54. Inspect cup bottom for wrinkles or material thinning
55. Calculate wall thickness reduction percentage (target: <15%)
56. Apply additional lubricant to cup surfaces for subsequent operations
57. Transfer acceptable cups to ironing press station
58. Reject defective cups for recycling and material recovery
59. Clean die surfaces every 1000 strokes to prevent buildup
60. Record first draw process parameters in quality control system

## Ironing Operations (Wall Thinning)

61. Load cup into ironing press alignment fixture
62. Install three-stage ironing die set with progressive reduction
63. Set first ironing ring reduction to 20% (wall thickness to 0.20mm)
64. Apply high-pressure lubricant system (50 bar pressure)
65. Advance ironing punch at 100 mm/s through first ring
66. Monitor ironing force on load cell (target: 280 kN)
67. Pass cup through second ironing ring (30% reduction, 0.14mm wall)
68. Control material temperature during ironing (<80°C to prevent galling)
69. Apply cooling lubricant spray to punch and die surfaces
70. Execute third ironing pass for final wall thickness (0.10mm ± 0.005mm)
71. Extend cup length during ironing to 65mm target height
72. Maintain bottom thickness at 0.25mm (no reduction in base)
73. Monitor for ironing defects: galling, scoring, or material pickup
74. Check wall thickness uniformity at 4 locations around circumference
75. Measure ironed cup length with digital height gauge
76. Verify cup straightness (concentricity within 0.03mm TIR)
77. Inspect internal surface finish using borescope camera
78. Test wall strength by applying radial compression force
79. Remove excess lubricant using warm alkaline wash solution
80. Rinse ironed cups with high-pressure deionized water spray
81. Dry cups in hot air tunnel at 120°C for 2 minutes
82. Inspect for watermarks or residual contamination
83. Apply temporary corrosion inhibitor coating
84. Transfer ironed cups to trimming operation
85. Clean ironing dies with solvent and inspect for wear patterns

## Trimming and Edge Finishing

86. Load ironed cup into precision trimming fixture with mandrel support
87. Install carbide trimming tool with sharp cutting edge (0.01mm radius)
88. Set trimming height to 65.0mm ± 0.05mm from cup bottom
89. Rotate cup at 1200 RPM while advancing cutting tool radially
90. Apply cutting fluid to trimming zone to prevent edge burrs
91. Execute trimming cut in single pass to remove excess material
92. Measure trimmed height using laser height sensor
93. Inspect cut edge for perpendicularity to axis (within 0.5°)
94. Check edge burr height using fingernail test (<0.01mm acceptable)
95. Apply chamfer to outer edge (0.1mm × 45°) using form tool
96. Deburr inner edge using rotating abrasive tool
97. Verify edge smoothness by running cotton swab along rim
98. Measure wall thickness at trimmed edge (target: 0.10mm ± 0.005mm)
99. Clean trimmed cups in ultrasonic cleaning bath (40 kHz frequency)
100. Rinse with deionized water cascade system
101. Dry in clean air blast chamber
102. Inspect 100% of trimmed edges using automated vision system
103. Reject cups with edge defects exceeding 0.02mm tolerance
104. Collect trimming scrap for recycling
105. Transfer trimmed cups to laser welding station

## Laser Welding Process

106. Load trimmed cup into welding fixture with precision centering
107. Position bottom cap disc in cup base (0.25mm thickness, 26.5mm diameter)
108. Apply mechanical pressure to hold cap against cup bottom (50N force)
109. Install fiber laser welding head (1070nm wavelength, 500W power)
110. Focus laser beam to 0.15mm spot diameter at weld interface
111. Set laser welding parameters: 450W power, 50 mm/s travel speed
112. Purge weld zone with argon shielding gas (15 L/min flow rate)
113. Establish argon atmosphere inside cup to prevent oxidation
114. Monitor oxygen level in purge gas (<50 ppm)
115. Initiate laser welding with controlled beam positioning
116. Execute circumferential weld path with overlap at start/end point
117. Control weld penetration depth to 0.20mm (80% of wall thickness)
118. Monitor weld pool temperature using infrared pyrometer (target: 1600°C)
119. Maintain consistent welding speed using CNC motion control
120. Prevent burn-through by real-time power modulation
121. Complete weld seam with smooth tie-in to starting point
122. Measure weld bead width (target: 0.4mm ± 0.05mm)
123. Inspect weld surface for porosity using magnified camera (10× magnification)
124. Check for weld spatter or discoloration indicating contamination
125. Verify weld continuity by examining 100% of seam length
126. Test weld strength using sample cans with pull testing (minimum 200N)
127. Allow welded assembly to cool naturally for 30 seconds
128. Remove from welding fixture and transfer to inspection station
129. Mark laser welding parameters in database for traceability
130. Clean laser optics every 4 hours to maintain beam quality

163. Test weld seam integrity by scanning detector probe along seam
164. Check bottom cap seal for any helium escape
165. Verify no leak signal above background noise level
166. Hold test pressure for 10 seconds to confirm stable seal
167. Record leak test result as pass/fail in database
