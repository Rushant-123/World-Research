# PCB Board Connectors Manufacturing Process

**Market Leader:** [Molex](/companies/molex)



## Introduction to PCB Connectors for Mac Logic Boards

PCB board connectors are critical components that enable electrical and mechanical connections between different circuit boards and components in Mac computers. These precision-engineered connectors must provide reliable signal transmission, mechanical durability, and resistance to environmental factors while maintaining compact form factors essential for modern electronics.

## Phase 1: Material Preparation and Base Metal Processing (Steps 1-25)

### Raw Material Selection and Inspection

**Step 1**: Receive phosphor bronze coil stock material from supplier, typically C5191 or C5210 alloy with 94.8% copper, 5% tin, and 0.2% phosphorus composition.

**Step 2**: Inspect incoming coil dimensions using precision micrometers, verifying thickness tolerance of ±0.005mm for 0.25mm base material.

**Step 3**: Perform hardness testing on random coil samples using Vickers hardness tester, targeting HV 180-220 range for optimal springback characteristics.

**Step 4**: Conduct tensile strength testing on material samples, verifying minimum 520 MPa tensile strength and 450 MPa yield strength.

**Step 5**: Check material surface finish for defects including scratches, pits, oxidation, or contamination using optical microscopy at 50x magnification.

**Step 6**: Measure coil width precision using laser measurement systems, ensuring ±0.01mm tolerance across entire coil length.

**Step 7**: Verify material certificate of conformance (CoC) documenting alloy composition, mechanical properties, and manufacturing traceability.

**Step 8**: Store coil stock in climate-controlled environment maintaining 20-25°C temperature and 45-55% relative humidity to prevent oxidation.

**Step 9**: Load coil onto precision decoiler with adjustable tension control to maintain consistent material feed during stamping operations.

**Step 10**: Thread material through straightening rollers to remove coil set and ensure flatness within 0.05mm over 100mm span.

### Material Pre-treatment and Surface Preparation

**Step 11**: Clean phosphor bronze strip using alkaline degreasing solution at 60°C to remove manufacturing oils and surface contaminants.

**Step 12**: Rinse material in deionized water cascade system with resistivity >15 MΩ·cm to eliminate alkaline residues.

**Step 13**: Apply mild acid pickle (5% sulfuric acid solution) for 15-30 seconds to remove light surface oxides and improve plating adhesion.

**Step 14**: Perform secondary rinse in flowing deionized water to neutralize acid and prevent staining.

**Step 15**: Dry material using forced air knife system at 80°C to eliminate moisture before stamping operations.

**Step 16**: Apply light anti-tarnish coating if material will be stored before processing to prevent oxidation during interim storage.

**Step 17**: Perform surface roughness measurement using profilometer, verifying Ra value <0.4 μm for optimal plating adhesion.

**Step 18**: Inspect material for surface cleanliness using water break test to confirm hydrophilic surface condition.

**Step 19**: Cut material to appropriate width if required using precision slitting equipment with edge quality control.

**Step 20**: Recoil prepared material onto processing reels with interleaving paper to protect surface finish.

### Stamping Die Preparation

**Step 21**: Select appropriate progressive stamping die set designed for specific connector contact geometry and pitch.

**Step 22**: Install die set in high-speed progressive stamping press with 80-200 ton capacity depending on connector size.

**Step 23**: Verify die clearance settings using feeler gauges, maintaining 5-8% of material thickness for clean shearing.

**Step 24**: Adjust stripper pressure to provide adequate material hold-down without causing surface deformation.

**Step 25**: Perform die inspection for wear, verifying cutting edge sharpness and alignment of all stations within 0.005mm tolerance.

## Phase 2: Contact Stamping and Forming (Steps 26-65)

### Progressive Stamping Operations

**Step 26**: Feed prepared phosphor bronze strip into progressive die at controlled speed of 200-400 strokes per minute.

**Step 27**: Execute pilot hole punching in first die station to establish precise registration for subsequent operations.

**Step 28**: Perform carrier strip notching to maintain accurate material advance and prevent skewing during progression.

**Step 29**: Stamp initial contact outline geometry, creating rough blank shape with material still attached to carrier strip.

**Step 30**: Pierce signal contact areas using precision punches with 0.002mm edge radius to minimize burr formation.

**Step 31**: Create retention features including barbs, lances, or tabs that will secure contact in plastic housing.

**Step 32**: Form primary bending operations to establish initial contact beam geometry and spring characteristics.

**Step 33**: Execute coining operations to compress material in critical contact areas, increasing hardness and dimensional stability.

**Step 34**: Stamp mating interface geometry with precise contact point location for optimal normal force distribution.

**Step 35**: Form secondary bends to create 3D contact geometry including offset sections and alignment features.

### Fine Forming and Contact Point Creation

**Step 36**: Generate contact point crown radius using precision forming punches, typically 25-100mm radius depending on application.

**Step 37**: Create contact beam cantilever spring geometry with calculated free length and thickness for target normal force.

**Step 38**: Form solder tail or press-fit section with appropriate geometry for PCB mounting method.

**Step 39**: Execute final bending operations to achieve specified contact gap and engagement dimensions.

**Step 40**: Perform embossing operations on contact surfaces to create localized high-points that concentrate contact force.

**Step 41**: Form anti-overstress features including stops or mechanical limits to prevent permanent deformation during mating.

**Step 42**: Create wiping action geometry to ensure self-cleaning contact motion that breaks through surface films.

**Step 43**: Stamp identification features or orientation markers for assembly process control.

**Step 44**: Form shielding tabs or grounding springs if connector design includes EMI suppression features.

**Step 45**: Execute stress relief bends to minimize residual stresses that could cause contact relaxation over time.

### Contact Separation and Edge Finishing

**Step 46**: Blank individual contacts from carrier strip in final stamping station, leaving controlled break-off points.

**Step 47**: Collect separated contacts in vibratory bowl feeder system for orientation and sequencing.

**Step 48**: Inspect separated contacts for burr height using optical comparator, verifying <0.02mm maximum burr.

**Step 49**: Perform tumble deburring in ceramic media if required to smooth sharp edges without affecting critical dimensions.

**Step 50**: Sort contacts by orientation using vision-guided bowl feeder system for automated plating line loading.

### Quality Control and Dimensional Verification

**Step 51**: Measure contact overall length using coordinate measuring machine (CMM) with 0.001mm resolution.

**Step 52**: Verify contact beam thickness after forming to ensure no excessive thinning occurred during bending operations.

**Step 53**: Check contact point location and height relative to datum surfaces using optical measurement system.

**Step 54**: Measure solder tail or press-fit zone dimensions to verify PCB compatibility and retention specifications.

**Step 55**: Inspect retention barb height and angle to ensure proper engagement with housing retention features.

**Step 56**: Verify contact gap dimensions that determine engagement depth and mating force characteristics.

**Step 57**: Check material hardness after forming operations to confirm work hardening effects, targeting HV 200-250.

**Step 58**: Perform visual inspection for forming defects including cracks, tears, excessive thinning, or wrinkles.

**Step 59**: Test contact spring force using calibrated force gauge, verifying compliance with normal force specifications.

**Step 60**: Measure contact electrical resistance using 4-wire Kelvin method at 100mA test current, typically <10 milliohms.

### Contact Force Characterization

**Step 61**: Perform normal force measurement on statistical sample using automated test fixture with load cell.

**Step 62**: Generate force-displacement curve to characterize spring behavior throughout engagement range.

**Step 63**: Verify minimum contact force at maximum engagement depth meets specification, typically 20-100 grams force.

**Step 64**: Check maximum insertion force to ensure acceptable mating effort, generally <500 grams per contact.

**Step 65**: Test contact retention force in housing using pull-out fixture, verifying minimum 2-5 kg retention depending on design.

## Phase 3: Electroplating and Surface Finishing (Steps 66-100)

### Pre-plating Surface Preparation

**Step 66**: Load formed contacts into plating barrel or rack fixture depending on plating method and volume requirements.

**Step 67**: Clean contacts in alkaline electrocleaner at 60°C with 3-5 A/dm² cathodic current for 30-60 seconds.

**Step 68**: Rinse in ambient temperature deionized water to remove alkaline cleaner residues.

**Step 69**: Perform anodic electrocleaning at 50°C with 2-4 A/dm² to remove residual oxides and activate surface.

**Step 70**: Rinse again in flowing deionized water cascade system with minimum 3 rinse stages.

**Step 71**: Execute acid activation dip in 5-10% sulfuric acid for 10-20 seconds to ensure oxide-free surface.

**Step 72**: Perform final pre-plate rinse in high-purity deionized water immediately before entering plating bath.

**Step 73**: Inspect contact surface using microscopy to verify clean, uniform appearance free from contamination.

### Nickel Underplating

**Step 74**: Immerse contacts in nickel strike plating bath containing nickel sulfate and chloride at pH 3-4.

**Step 75**: Apply 3-5 A/dm² current density for 1-3 minutes to deposit thin nickel strike layer of 0.5-1.0 μm thickness.

**Step 76**: Transfer contacts to bright nickel plating bath with leveling and brightening additives.

**Step 77**: Plate nickel barrier layer at 4-6 A/dm² for 5-15 minutes, achieving 1.5-2.5 μm nickel thickness.

**Step 78**: Rinse plated contacts in flowing deionized water to remove drag-out electrolyte.

**Step 79**: Verify nickel thickness using X-ray fluorescence (XRF) measurement on sample contacts from batch.

**Step 80**: Inspect nickel surface for uniformity, brightness, and absence of pitting or roughness defects.

### Gold Flash Plating

**Step 81**: Transfer contacts to gold strike bath containing dilute gold cyanide solution at 40-50°C.

**Step 82**: Apply 0.5-1.0 A/dm² current density for 30-60 seconds to deposit gold strike layer ensuring adhesion.

**Step 83**: Move contacts to production gold plating bath with hard gold chemistry containing cobalt or nickel co-deposit.

**Step 84**: Plate hard gold layer at 1-2 A/dm² for 2-5 minutes, targeting 0.5-1.0 μm gold thickness on contact areas.

**Step 85**: Use selective plating techniques or masking to restrict gold to critical contact zones, reducing material cost.

**Step 86**: Maintain gold bath temperature at 55-65°C for optimal deposition rate and hardness characteristics.

**Step 87**: Monitor bath pH at 4.0-5.0 and adjust using sulfuric acid or potassium hydroxide as needed.

**Step 88**: Control agitation using air sparging or mechanical agitation to ensure uniform current distribution.

**Step 89**: Remove contacts from plating bath and rinse immediately in flowing deionized water.

**Step 90**: Perform final rinse in hot deionized water at 60-70°C to aid drying and prevent water spotting.

### Post-plating Processing

**Step 91**: Dry plated contacts using forced air drying at 80-100°C to remove all moisture.

**Step 92**: Bake contacts at 150°C for 1-2 hours to relieve hydrogen embrittlement from plating process if required.

**Step 93**: Measure gold thickness using XRF analyzer at multiple locations to verify uniform plating distribution.

**Step 94**: Perform adhesion testing on sample contacts using tape test or bend test to verify plating bond strength.

**Step 95**: Test gold hardness using micro-hardness tester, verifying 150-200 HK for hard gold deposits.

**Step 96**: Measure contact resistance on plated samples using 4-wire method at 100mA, typically <5 milliohms.

**Step 97**: Inspect plated surface using optical and electron microscopy to verify uniform, pore-free coverage.

**Step 98**: Check for plating defects including pitting, roughness, discoloration, or incomplete coverage.

**Step 99**: Perform corrosion resistance testing on sample contacts using salt spray or mixed flowing gas exposure.

**Step 100**: Package plated contacts in anti-static trays with protective covering to prevent handling damage and contamination.

## Phase 4: Plastic Housing Injection Molding (Steps 101-130)

### Mold Preparation and Material Selection

**Step 101**: Select injection molding tool designed for connector housing geometry with appropriate cavity configuration.

**Step 102**: Install mold in injection molding machine with 50-200 ton clamping force depending on part size.

**Step 103**: Verify mold cooling line connections and water flow to ensure uniform temperature control.

**Step 104**: Set mold temperature to 80-120°C for liquid crystal polymer (LCP) or 60-80°C for PBT material.

**Step 105**: Check mold for cleanliness, removing any residual material or contamination from previous runs.

**Step 106**: Verify ejector pin function and alignment to prevent part damage during ejection.

**Step 107**: Inspect cavity surfaces for wear, scratches, or damage that could affect part quality.

**Step 108**: Apply mold release agent to cavity surfaces if required for difficult-to-release geometries.

### Material Preparation and Drying

**Step 109**: Select high-temperature thermoplastic material, typically LCP (Vectra, Zenite) or high-flow PBT (Valox, Crastin).

**Step 110**: Load virgin resin pellets into hopper dryer to remove moisture before processing.

**Step 111**: Dry LCP material at 150°C for 4-6 hours to reduce moisture content below 200 ppm.

**Step 112**: Dry PBT material at 120°C for 3-4 hours if using glass-filled grades for enhanced mechanical properties.

**Step 113**: Verify drying effectiveness using moisture analyzer to confirm specification compliance.

**Step 114**: Transfer dried material to sealed hopper with desiccant air purge to maintain dryness during molding.

**Step 115**: Mix virgin material with regrind (typically 10-20% maximum) if process allows for cost optimization.

### Injection Molding Process Setup

**Step 116**: Set injection barrel temperature profile: 300-340°C for LCP, 240-270°C for PBT across heating zones.

**Step 117**: Configure injection speed profile to fill cavity rapidly while preventing air entrapment or material degradation.

**Step 118**: Set injection pressure to 100-180 MPa to ensure complete cavity filling and pack thin-wall sections.

**Step 119**: Program holding pressure at 40-60% of injection pressure for 3-8 seconds to compensate for material shrinkage.

**Step 120**: Establish cooling time of 15-30 seconds depending on wall thickness and material thermal conductivity.

**Step 121**: Set screw rotation speed and back pressure to achieve proper material melting and homogenization.

**Step 122**: Configure mold open time, part ejection timing, and mold close sequence for cycle optimization.

### Molding Operation and Process Control

**Step 123**: Execute short shots to verify material flow pattern and identify potential filling issues before full production.

**Step 124**: Optimize gate freeze time through pressure decay analysis to prevent sink marks and dimensional instability.

**Step 125**: Monitor injection pressure, velocity, and cavity pressure using process sensors for statistical process control.

**Step 126**: Perform first article inspection measuring critical housing dimensions using CMM or optical comparator.

**Step 127**: Verify contact cavity location and pitch dimensions to ensure compatibility with contact insertion tooling.

**Step 128**: Check housing warp using flatness gauge or coordinate measurement to ensure PCB mounting coplanarity.

**Step 129**: Inspect gate break quality and runner system separation to minimize secondary trimming operations.

**Step 130**: Collect molded housings in protective packaging to prevent damage to delicate features before contact assembly.

## Phase 5: Contact Insertion and Assembly (Steps 131-160)

### Contact Insertion Preparation

**Step 131**: Load plated contacts into vibratory bowl feeder system for orientation and singulation.

**Step 132**: Program contact presentation tooling to deliver contacts in correct orientation for insertion sequence.

**Step 133**: Load molded housings into precision nest fixtures that locate housing on multiple datum surfaces.

**Step 134**: Verify housing orientation using vision system to ensure correct cavity alignment with insertion tooling.

**Step 135**: Inspect housing contact cavities using automated optical inspection (AOI) for blockages or molding defects.

### Automated Contact Insertion

**Step 136**: Position first contact using pick-and-place mechanism with vacuum pickup or mechanical gripper.

**Step 137**: Align contact retention features with housing cavity using precision guidance system with ±0.05mm accuracy.

**Step 138**: Insert contact using controlled-force insertion head with real-time force monitoring.

**Step 139**: Monitor insertion force profile to detect housing damage, contact deformation, or insertion errors.

**Step 140**: Verify insertion force remains within specification window, typically 2-10 kg depending on retention design.

**Step 141**: Check insertion depth using position sensor or end-of-travel switch to confirm full seating.

**Step 142**: Verify contact retention by performing slight pull-back test with 0.5-1 kg force to confirm locking engagement.

**Step 143**: Repeat insertion process for all contacts in housing, maintaining proper position sequence.

**Step 144**: Track insertion sequence and force data for each position to enable defect traceability.

**Step 145**: Detect missing contacts or insertion failures using machine vision inspection after each insertion.

### Retention Verification and Inspection

**Step 146**: Perform optical inspection of completed assembly to verify all contact positions filled correctly.

**Step 147**: Check contact coplanarity at mating interface using laser scanning or vision measurement system.

**Step 148**: Verify contact-to-contact spacing (pitch) using precision measurement fixture to ensure mating compatibility.

**Step 149**: Inspect contact alignment within housing cavities to prevent interference during mating operation.

**Step 150**: Test contact retention force on statistical sample using automated pull-out testing fixture.

**Step 151**: Verify minimum retention force meets specification, typically 2-5 kg per contact depending on application.

**Step 152**: Check contact electrical continuity using automated test fixture with flying probe or bed-of-nails.

**Step 153**: Measure contact resistance from solder tail to mating interface, verifying <10 milliohms per contact.

**Step 154**: Perform high-voltage dielectric strength test between adjacent contacts at 500-1000 VDC for 1 second.

**Step 155**: Check insulation resistance between contacts using megohmmeter, verifying >1000 megohms minimum.

### Contact Position and Geometry Verification

**Step 156**: Scan mating interface using 3D laser profiler to generate point cloud of all contact positions.

**Step 157**: Compare measured contact positions to CAD model using best-fit alignment and deviation analysis.

**Step 158**: Verify contact height variation across all positions within ±0.1mm to ensure uniform normal force.

**Step 159**: Check contact centerline location within ±0.05mm of nominal to prevent mating misalignment.

**Step 160**: Inspect contact tip geometry and crown radius to confirm proper contact point formation.

## Phase 6: Mechanical and Electrical Testing (Steps 161-185)

### Mechanical Durability Testing

**Step 161**: Perform insertion-extraction cycling test with sample connectors mated to test fixture at specified engagement depth.

**Step 162**: Execute 10-100 mating cycles at controlled rate of 25mm/minute with force monitoring throughout test.

**Step 163**: Measure contact resistance at initial state and after every 10 cycles to detect wear or degradation.

**Step 164**: Monitor insertion force throughout cycling to identify contact deformation or housing wear.

**Step 165**: Inspect contact surfaces after cycling using optical microscopy to assess wear patterns and plating integrity.

**Step 166**: Perform contact retention force test after cycling to verify retention features maintain specified force.

**Step 167**: Conduct vibration testing per USCAR-21 or IEC 60512 standards to verify contact retention under dynamic loading.

**Step 168**: Execute mechanical shock testing using half-sine pulse of 50-100G to simulate drop or impact conditions.

**Step 169**: Test housing mechanical strength using specified load application to mounting features and connector body.

**Step 170**: Verify contact normal force after vibration and shock exposure to ensure spring characteristics maintained.

### Electrical Performance Characterization

**Step 171**: Measure contact resistance using 4-wire Kelvin method at multiple current levels (10mA, 100mA, 1A).

**Step 172**: Verify low-level contact resistance <10 milliohms at 100mA for signal integrity applications.

**Step 173**: Test power contact resistance <5 milliohms for high-current applications carrying 1-5 amps.

**Step 174**: Perform current-carrying capacity test by passing rated current through contacts and monitoring temperature rise.

**Step 175**: Verify temperature rise <30°C above ambient at rated current with proper thermal management.

**Step 176**: Execute voltage drop measurement at rated current, ensuring <50mV drop per contact pair.

**Step 177**: Test dielectric withstanding voltage between adjacent contacts at 500-1000 VAC or 1500 VDC for 1 minute.

**Step 178**: Measure insulation resistance between non-mated contacts at 500 VDC, verifying >1000 megohms.

**Step 179**: Check contact-to-ground insulation resistance at 500 VDC through housing material, typically >100 megohms.

**Step 180**: Perform electrical continuity test across all positions using automated test fixture.

### High-Speed Signal Integrity Testing

**Step 181**: Measure single-ended impedance using time-domain reflectometry (TDR) to verify 50-ohm characteristic impedance.

**Step 182**: Test differential impedance for high-speed data pairs, targeting 85-100 ohms depending on application.

**Step 183**: Characterize insertion loss using vector network analyzer (VNA) across frequency range up to 20 GHz.

**Step 184**: Verify insertion loss <0.5 dB at 5 GHz for high-speed USB, PCIe, or DisplayPort applications.

**Step 185**: Measure return loss and VSWR to assess impedance matching quality across operating bandwidth.

## Phase 7: Environmental and Reliability Testing (Steps 186-195)

### Environmental Exposure Testing

**Step 186**: Perform temperature cycling test from -40°C to +85°C for 500-1000 cycles per JEDEC standards.

**Step 187**: Monitor contact resistance throughout temperature cycling to detect thermal expansion effects or plating degradation.

**Step 188**: Execute humidity bias testing at 85°C and 85% relative humidity with voltage bias for 1000 hours.

**Step 189**: Check for corrosion, dendritic growth, or insulation resistance degradation after humidity exposure.

**Step 190**: Conduct mixed flowing gas (MFG) testing with H2S, NO2, Cl2, and SO2 to simulate industrial atmospheres.

**Step 191**: Perform salt spray exposure testing per ASTM B117 for 96-240 hours to evaluate corrosion resistance.

**Step 192**: Execute thermal shock testing with rapid temperature transitions between -40°C and +85°C.

**Step 193**: Verify mechanical and electrical performance after all environmental exposure testing to confirm reliability.

### Final Quality Assurance and Release

**Step 194**: Perform final dimensional and electrical inspection on production samples using calibrated measurement equipment.

**Step 195**: Generate Certificate of Compliance documenting all test results, material certifications, and process parameters before releasing product to shipping.
