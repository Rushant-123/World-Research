## Aluminum Power Conductor Manufacturing Process

### Raw Material Reception and Preparation (Steps 1-15)

1. Receive aluminum ingots at conductor manufacturing facility with incoming quality inspection
2. Verify aluminum purity specification (typically 99.5-99.7% for electrical conductor grade EC aluminum)
3. Check ingot dimensions and weight against purchase order specifications
4. Inspect ingots for surface defects, cracks, or contamination that could affect drawing
5. Store ingots in climate-controlled warehouse to prevent oxidation and moisture absorption
6. Select ingots based on production schedule and conductor specification requirements
7. Load ingots onto automated conveyor system feeding the melting and casting area
8. Preheat ingots to 200-250°C to remove surface moisture and reduce thermal shock
9. Clean ingot surfaces using wire brushing or shot blasting to remove oxide layer
10. Apply flux coating to ingot surfaces to prevent oxidation during melting process
11. Stage cleaned ingots in holding area adjacent to continuous casting furnace
12. Verify traceability documentation linking ingot batch to source smelter and composition
13. Input production parameters into manufacturing execution system for this conductor batch
14. Prepare continuous casting equipment with appropriate die sizes for target rod diameter
15. Conduct pre-production safety check of furnace, casting system, and cooling equipment

### Melting and Continuous Rod Casting (Steps 16-35)

16. Load aluminum ingots into electric resistance or induction melting furnace
17. Heat aluminum to 720-750°C, above melting point to achieve complete liquefaction
18. Monitor melt temperature continuously using calibrated thermocouple sensors
19. Skim dross and impurities from surface of molten aluminum bath
20. Add degassing agents (nitrogen or argon) to remove dissolved hydrogen gas
21. Stir molten aluminum to ensure uniform temperature and composition throughout bath
22. Test molten aluminum sample for conductivity (minimum 61% IACS for EC grade)
23. Test for impurities including iron, silicon, copper that reduce electrical performance
24. Adjust melt chemistry if necessary by adding high-purity aluminum to meet specifications
25. Transfer molten aluminum to holding furnace maintaining precise temperature control
26. Feed molten aluminum into continuous casting system through ceramic filter system
27. Cast aluminum through water-cooled graphite die to form continuous rod (typically 9.5mm diameter)
28. Apply direct chill casting technique with precise water spray cooling pattern
29. Monitor casting speed (typically 2-5 meters per minute) to ensure proper solidification
30. Inspect cast rod surface for defects, cracks, or irregular cooling patterns
31. Measure rod diameter continuously using laser micrometry system
32. Coil cast aluminum rod onto large diameter take-up reel
33. Mark each rod coil with heat number and traceability information
34. Test rod samples for mechanical properties including tensile strength and elongation
35. Store cast rod coils in controlled environment awaiting wire drawing operations

### Wire Drawing Preparation (Steps 36-50)

36. Transfer aluminum rod coils to wire drawing area using overhead crane system
37. Load rod coil onto payoff reel of wire drawing machine
38. Thread rod end through rod breakdown machine to reduce initial diameter
39. Apply drawing lubricant (typically soap solution or oil-based compound) to rod surface
40. Pass rod through series of carbide drawing dies with progressively smaller diameters
41. Reduce rod from 9.5mm to approximately 4-6mm in breakdown drawing operation
42. Monitor drawing speed and tension to prevent rod breakage or excessive work hardening
43. Cool drawn wire using water spray or air cooling to prevent overheating
44. Inspect drawn wire for surface quality and diameter uniformity
45. Coil intermediate wire onto storage reels between drawing stages
46. Clean wire surface to remove drawing lubricant and aluminum particles
47. Apply fresh lubricant appropriate for fine wire drawing operations
48. Load intermediate wire onto multi-die fine wire drawing machine
49. Set up drawing dies according to final conductor wire diameter specification
50. Calibrate drawing machine tension controls and speed for aluminum wire properties

### Fine Wire Drawing (Steps 51-70)

51. Draw wire through first die of fine drawing machine reducing diameter by 15-20%
52. Monitor drawing force to ensure it remains within acceptable range for aluminum
53. Cool wire after each die pass to dissipate heat generated by plastic deformation
54. Pass wire through second die for additional diameter reduction
55. Continue progressive drawing through series of 8-12 dies depending on final size
56. Achieve final wire diameter according to conductor specification (typically 2.5-4.5mm for individual strands)
57. Maintain drawing speed of 10-20 meters per second depending on wire size
58. Monitor wire temperature to prevent excessive heating that could affect properties
59. Inspect wire surface continuously for scratches, die marks, or lubricant buildup
60. Measure wire diameter using laser gauge every few meters to ensure consistency
61. Adjust die alignment if diameter variation exceeds tolerance specifications
62. Monitor drawing lubricant flow rate and concentration for optimal performance
63. Collect wire onto high-speed take-up reel with proper tension control
64. Inspect wire surface quality under magnification for microscopic defects
65. Test wire samples for electrical conductivity (minimum 61.2% IACS typical)
66. Measure wire tensile strength (typically 160-200 MPa for fully hard aluminum)
67. Check wire elongation at break (typically 1.5-3% for as-drawn wire)
68. Verify wire diameter meets ASTM B230 or equivalent international standards
69. Mark each wire reel with production batch number and wire diameter
70. Transfer drawn wire reels to annealing furnace area for softening treatment

### Wire Annealing (Steps 71-85)

71. Load wire reels into continuous or batch annealing furnace
72. Program furnace to heat wire to 300-400°C annealing temperature
73. Control heating rate to prevent thermal shock and ensure uniform temperature
74. Maintain annealing temperature for specified time (typically 2-4 hours for batch process)
75. Create reducing atmosphere in furnace using nitrogen or forming gas to prevent oxidation
76. Monitor furnace atmosphere composition using oxygen sensors
77. Ensure uniform temperature distribution throughout furnace using multiple thermocouples
78. Hold wire at annealing temperature to allow recrystallization and grain growth
79. Cool wire gradually in controlled manner to room temperature
80. Remove annealed wire reels from furnace after complete cooling cycle
81. Test annealed wire for increased elongation (typically 20-30% after annealing)
82. Measure residual tensile strength (reduced to 90-110 MPa for soft aluminum)
83. Verify electrical conductivity maintained or slightly improved after annealing
84. Inspect wire surface for oxidation or discoloration from annealing process
85. Store annealed wire reels in dry environment awaiting stranding operations

### Stranding Setup and Preparation (Steps 86-100)

86. Transfer annealed wire reels to stranding machine area
87. Select number of wire strands based on conductor specification (typically 7, 19, 37, or 61 strands)
88. Load individual wire reels onto stranding machine payoff stands
89. Thread each wire through individual guides and tension controls
90. Set stranding machine for appropriate lay length (pitch of helical wrapping)
91. Configure stranding direction (right-hand or left-hand lay as specified)
92. Adjust tension on each wire strand to ensure uniform compression in finished conductor
93. Program stranding machine speed based on conductor diameter and lay length
94. Prepare closing die through which all strands will be compressed together
95. Set up diameter measuring system to monitor finished conductor size
96. Position take-up reel for collecting finished stranded conductor
97. Thread all wire strands through preformer plates that initiate helical shape
98. Pass grouped strands through compacting rollers to reduce overall diameter
99. Verify all tension sensors and speed controls functioning properly
100. Conduct test run to verify proper strand formation and conductor appearance

### Conductor Stranding Operation (Steps 101-120)

101. Start stranding machine and establish proper wire tension on all strands
102. Form inner core wire as straight central element of conductor
103. Wrap first layer of 6 wires helically around central core wire
104. Maintain consistent lay length (typically 10-16 times conductor diameter)
105. Add second layer of 12 wires around first layer for 19-strand conductor
106. Continue adding layers for larger conductor sizes (37, 61, or 91 strands)
107. Rotate payoff reels (planetary motion) around central conductor axis
108. Monitor individual strand tensions to prevent loose or over-tight strands
109. Pass stranded conductor through compacting die to achieve final diameter
110. Compress strands to 95-97% of theoretical maximum density
111. Cool conductor after compacting to stabilize strand positions
112. Measure conductor diameter continuously using multi-axis laser gauge
113. Monitor stranding pitch to ensure consistency along conductor length
114. Inspect conductor surface for proud wires (strands not properly seated)
115. Check for crossed strands or other stranding defects
116. Verify conductor flexibility by measuring minimum bending radius
117. Test conductor tension to ensure proper mechanical integration of strands
118. Collect finished conductor onto large diameter storage reel
119. Maintain proper spooling tension to prevent conductor deformation on reel
120. Mark conductor with sequential length markings every meter

### ACSR Core Installation (for ACSR conductors) (Steps 121-135)

121. Prepare steel core strand if manufacturing aluminum conductor steel reinforced (ACSR)
122. Load galvanized steel core wire onto central payoff of stranding machine
123. Verify steel core tensile strength (typically 1200-1700 MPa minimum)
124. Check galvanizing quality to ensure corrosion protection
125. Thread steel core through center of stranding machine as central element
126. Wrap first layer of aluminum strands around steel core
127. Maintain proper tension on steel core to prevent slack or excessive stretch
128. Ensure aluminum-to-steel contact is intimate for good heat dissipation
129. Monitor differential thermal expansion between aluminum and steel during stranding
130. Add subsequent layers of aluminum strands around steel-cored center
131. Compact ACSR conductor to ensure aluminum strands support steel core
132. Test ACSR conductor for proper strength-to-weight ratio
133. Verify steel core positioned exactly on conductor centerline
134. Measure ACSR conductor diameter and verify against specification
135. Inspect aluminum-steel interface for proper contact without gaps

### Conductor Surface Treatment (Steps 136-150)

136. Clean conductor surface to remove drawing lubricant and aluminum particles
137. Apply degreasing solution using spray manifold on moving conductor
138. Rinse conductor with deionized water to remove cleaning agent residue
139. Dry conductor using hot air blowers to prevent water spots
140. Inspect cleaned conductor surface for oxidation or corrosion
141. Apply optional corrosion inhibitor coating if specified for harsh environments
142. Coat conductor with protective polymer layer for underground applications (if specified)
143. Apply conductor identification markings using inkjet or laser marking
144. Print manufacturer name, conductor size, and standard designation on conductor
145. Add sequential length markings every meter for installation reference
146. Apply color coding if required for phase identification in distribution systems
147. Cure any applied coatings using infrared or convection heating
148. Verify coating adhesion through tape test or scratch testing
149. Inspect final conductor appearance for cosmetic quality
150. Measure conductor surface roughness if critical for application

### Spooling and Packaging (Steps 151-170)

151. Transfer finished conductor to spooling area for final packaging
152. Select appropriate reel size based on conductor diameter and ordered length
153. Load wooden or steel reel onto powered spooling arbor
154. Attach conductor end to reel hub using mechanical fastener or wire tie
155. Rotate reel while maintaining proper back-tension on conductor
156. Layer conductor onto reel with consistent pitch to prevent crossing layers
157. Traverse conductor across reel width for uniform distribution
158. Monitor spooling tension to prevent conductor deformation or reel flange damage
159. Fill reel to specified capacity leaving clearance from flange edges
160. Secure conductor end to prevent unwinding during transportation
161. Install protective lagging (wood strips) over conductor layers on reel
162. Wrap entire reel with plastic film or shrink wrap for weather protection
163. Install reel flange protectors to prevent damage during handling
164. Attach metal information tag to reel with conductor specifications
165. Mark reel with gross weight, net weight, and conductor length
166. Add handling instructions and arrows indicating rolling direction
167. Apply barcodes or RFID tags for inventory tracking
168. Photograph reel for documentation and shipment verification
169. Move packaged reel to finished goods warehouse using forklift
170. Store reels in covered area protecting from weather and contamination

### Quality Testing and Inspection (Steps 171-185)

171. Sample conductor from each production reel for laboratory testing
172. Measure conductor DC resistance per unit length (typically ohms per kilometer)
173. Calculate electrical conductivity as percentage of IACS (International Annealed Copper Standard)
174. Verify conductivity meets minimum specification (61.2% IACS minimum for EC aluminum)
175. Test conductor tensile strength by pulling sample to failure
176. Measure ultimate tensile strength (typically 140-180 MPa for stranded conductor)
177. Record elongation at break to verify ductility (typically 10-20%)
178. Perform conductor flexibility test by wrapping around mandrel
179. Measure minimum bending radius without strand breakage
180. Conduct strand adhesion test to verify proper compacting
181. Inspect individual strand surfaces under microscope for defects
182. Measure actual conductor diameter at multiple points and calculate average
183. Verify diameter within tolerance (typically ±0.5% of nominal)
184. Test conductor weight per unit length and compare to calculated value
185. Perform accelerated corrosion testing on representative samples

### Final Documentation and Shipping (Steps 186-195)

186. Generate certificate of compliance documenting all test results
187. Record production batch number, date, and manufacturing location
188. Document raw material traceability from aluminum ingot to finished conductor
189. Create mill test report with mechanical and electrical properties
190. Photograph conductor cross-section showing strand arrangement
191. Prepare shipping documentation including commercial invoice and packing list
192. Generate bill of lading for freight carrier
193. Coordinate logistics for conductor reel transportation to customer site
194. Load reels onto truck trailer using crane or forklift with proper securing
195. Ship conductor with handling instructions and provide tracking information to customer
