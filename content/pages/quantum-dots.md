# Quantum Dot Enhancement Film Manufacturing Process

**Market Leader:** [Nanosys](/companies/nanosys)



## Phase 1: Quantum Dot Synthesis (Steps 1-40)

### CdSe Quantum Dot Core Synthesis

1. **Precursor Preparation**: Prepare cadmium oleate precursor by dissolving 0.5 M cadmium oxide in oleic acid at 150°C under nitrogen atmosphere for 2 hours until clear solution forms.

2. **Selenium Solution**: Dissolve selenium powder in trioctylphosphine (TOP) to create 1 M Se-TOP solution in oxygen-free glovebox environment.

3. **Reactor Setup**: Set up three-neck round-bottom flask with temperature controller, nitrogen inlet, septum port, and condenser system.

4. **Solvent Loading**: Add 20 mL octadecene solvent to reactor flask and degas at 120°C under vacuum for 30 minutes.

5. **Temperature Ramping**: Heat reactor to 310°C under nitrogen flow at controlled rate of 10°C/min to ensure uniform heating.

6. **Injection Stage**: Rapidly inject 2 mL Se-TOP solution into hot cadmium precursor solution causing immediate nucleation burst.

7. **Temperature Drop Control**: Monitor temperature drop to 290°C immediately after injection and maintain this growth temperature.

8. **Growth Phase Monitoring**: Allow quantum dots to grow for 3-8 minutes depending on target emission wavelength (shorter time for blue, longer for red).

9. **Size Monitoring**: Take aliquots every 30 seconds and measure photoluminescence emission peak to track particle size growth.

10. **Growth Termination**: Cool reaction rapidly to 50°C by removing heating mantle when desired emission wavelength achieved.

### InP Quantum Dot Alternative Synthesis

11. **Indium Precursor Prep**: Prepare indium myristate by reacting indium acetate with myristic acid at 100°C for 3 hours.

12. **Phosphorus Solution**: Prepare tris(trimethylsilyl)phosphine (P(TMS)₃) solution in anhydrous octadecene at 0.2 M concentration.

13. **Zinc Addition**: Add zinc stearate to indium precursor to improve crystallinity and quantum yield of final InP cores.

14. **InP Nucleation**: Heat indium precursor mixture to 300°C and inject phosphorus solution for InP core formation.

15. **Extended Growth**: Continue InP growth at 280°C for 10-15 minutes as InP requires longer growth time than CdSe.

16. **Fluorescence Optimization**: Add additional zinc precursor during growth to enhance photoluminescence quantum yield above 60%.

17. **Core Completion**: Cool InP cores to room temperature under nitrogen and transfer to glovebox for shell growth.

### Shell Growth for Enhanced Stability

18. **Shell Precursor Preparation**: Prepare zinc oleate and sulfur-TOP solutions for ZnS shell growth.

19. **Core Purification**: Purify quantum dot cores by precipitation with ethanol and redispersion in hexane (3x cycles).

20. **Shell Reactor Setup**: Redissolve purified cores in octadecene with oleylamine ligands at 100°C.

21. **Temperature Equilibration**: Heat core solution to 220°C for shell growth at lower temperature than core synthesis.

22. **Slow Shell Addition**: Add zinc and sulfur precursors dropwise at 0.5 mL/hr using syringe pump for uniform shell growth.

23. **Layer-by-Layer Growth**: Grow shell in multiple steps with annealing periods to create defect-free interface.

24. **Shell Thickness Control**: Monitor shell growth by UV-vis absorption shift and aim for 2-3 monolayers of ZnS.

25. **Quantum Yield Enhancement**: Achieve photoluminescence quantum yield >85% through optimized shell growth.

26. **Final Annealing**: Anneal core-shell structures at 220°C for 30 minutes to improve crystallinity.

27. **Cooling Protocol**: Cool to room temperature at controlled rate of 5°C/min to prevent thermal stress.

### Size Selection and Purification

28. **Initial Precipitation**: Precipitate quantum dots with ethanol-methanol mixture (1:1) and centrifuge at 8000 rpm.

29. **Size-Selective Precipitation**: Use gradual addition of non-solvent to separate size distributions by differential solubility.

30. **Red Emitter Isolation**: Precipitate largest red-emitting dots (620-650 nm) first with minimal non-solvent.

31. **Green Emitter Isolation**: Add more non-solvent to supernatant to precipitate medium-sized green dots (520-550 nm).

32. **Dispersibility Testing**: Redisperse each size fraction in toluene and test for long-term colloidal stability.

33. **Concentration Measurement**: Measure quantum dot concentration by absorption spectroscopy using size-dependent extinction coefficients.

34. **Quantum Yield Testing**: Measure absolute photoluminescence quantum yield using integrating sphere setup.

35. **Size Distribution Analysis**: Perform transmission electron microscopy on each batch to confirm size uniformity <5% standard deviation.

36. **Emission Bandwidth**: Verify full-width half-maximum (FWHM) of emission peaks <30 nm for narrow color gamut.

37. **Stock Solution Preparation**: Prepare 50 mg/mL stock solutions in anhydrous toluene for film fabrication.

38. **Stability Testing**: Store solutions in sealed vials under nitrogen and test stability over 6 months.

39. **Quality Control Batching**: Batch quantum dots by emission wavelength (±2 nm) and quantum yield (±3%) for consistency.

40. **Documentation**: Record synthesis parameters, optical properties, and lot numbers for complete traceability.

## Phase 2: Surface Ligand Engineering (Steps 41-70)

### Ligand Exchange Procedures

41. **Original Ligand Analysis**: Characterize native oleic acid/oleylamine ligands by FTIR and thermogravimetric analysis.

42. **Exchange Solution Prep**: Prepare 0.1 M solution of short-chain ligands (octanoic acid or hexylamine) in toluene.

43. **Ligand Exchange Reaction**: Mix quantum dot solution with excess exchange ligands (10:1 molar ratio) at 60°C.

44. **Stirring Protocol**: Stir exchange mixture for 12 hours at 300 rpm to ensure complete ligand replacement.

45. **Washing Cycles**: Precipitate exchanged dots with methanol and redisperse in fresh toluene (5x cycles).

46. **Exchange Verification**: Confirm ligand exchange by NMR spectroscopy showing new ligand peaks.

47. **Interparticle Spacing**: Measure reduction in particle-particle distance by small-angle X-ray scattering.

48. **Quantum Yield Check**: Verify quantum yield retention >95% after ligand exchange process.

49. **Solubility Testing**: Test quantum dot dispersibility in target film matrix solvents.

50. **Aggregation Assessment**: Monitor for aggregation by dynamic light scattering showing single particle size.

### Cross-Linking Chemistry

51. **Bifunctional Ligand Synthesis**: Prepare dithiol or diamine cross-linking ligands with controlled chain length.

52. **Partial Exchange**: Replace 20-30% of surface ligands with bifunctional cross-linkers while maintaining colloid stability.

53. **Cross-Link Density Control**: Optimize cross-linker concentration for mechanical stability without quenching fluorescence.

54. **UV Cross-Linking Groups**: Attach acrylate or methacrylate groups for photo-induced cross-linking in film.

55. **Thermal Stability Enhancement**: Introduce high-bond-energy ligands (thiols on ZnS) for thermal stability to 150°C.

56. **Moisture Barrier Ligands**: Attach hydrophobic fluorinated ligands to improve moisture resistance.

57. **Matrix Compatibility**: Functionalize with polymer-compatible groups for better dispersion in film matrix.

58. **Surface Coverage**: Achieve >95% surface coverage verified by elemental analysis to prevent oxidation.

59. **Ligand Shell Thickness**: Maintain 1-2 nm ligand shell thickness for optimal packing density in film.

60. **Photostability Testing**: Expose ligand-modified dots to high-intensity blue LED for 1000 hours and measure quantum yield retention.

### Surface Passivation Optimization

61. **Defect Identification**: Use photoluminescence lifetime measurements to identify surface trap states.

62. **Passivation Layer**: Apply additional thin ZnS layer (0.5 monolayer) to passivate surface defects.

63. **Halide Passivation**: Treat with zinc halide salts to passivate surface anionic vacancies.

64. **Aluminum Oxide Coating**: Deposit ultrathin Al₂O₃ shell via atomic layer deposition for ultimate stability.

65. **Silica Shell Growth**: Grow 2-5 nm SiO₂ shell using reverse microemulsion method for aqueous stability.

66. **Organic-Inorganic Hybrid**: Create hybrid organic-inorganic shell combining polymer and oxide layers.

67. **Blinking Suppression**: Achieve non-blinking behavior at single-particle level through complete surface passivation.

68. **Trap State Elimination**: Reduce non-radiative recombination pathways to <5% through optimal passivation.

69. **Chemical Stability**: Test resistance to oxidation, moisture, and temperature cycling per reliability standards.

70. **Final QD Characterization**: Complete optical, structural, and chemical characterization before film integration.

## Phase 3: Film Matrix Development (Steps 71-100)

### Polymer Matrix Selection

71. **Material Screening**: Evaluate polymer candidates including acrylates, silicones, and epoxies for optical clarity.

72. **Refractive Index Matching**: Select polymers with refractive index 1.48-1.52 to match quantum dot cores.

73. **Glass Transition Temperature**: Choose polymers with Tg >85°C for thermal stability during display operation.

74. **Oxygen Permeability**: Measure oxygen transmission rate <0.1 cc/m²/day for long-term quantum dot stability.

75. **Moisture Permeability**: Verify water vapor transmission rate <0.01 g/m²/day through film matrix.

76. **UV Stability**: Test polymer matrix UV absorption showing no transmission below 420 nm to protect quantum dots.

77. **Mechanical Properties**: Confirm tensile strength >50 MPa and elongation >100% for handling durability.

78. **Adhesion Testing**: Verify strong adhesion to barrier films through peel strength testing >100 g/cm.

79. **Thermal Cycling**: Subject polymer to 1000 cycles from -40°C to 85°C showing no cracking or delamination.

80. **Optical Clarity**: Measure haze <1% and total transmission >92% for visible wavelengths.

### Quantum Dot Dispersion

81. **Concentration Optimization**: Prepare formulations with 0.5-2.0 wt% quantum dots in polymer matrix.

82. **Red/Green Ratio**: Mix red and green emitting quantum dots in optimal 1:2 ratio for white point correction.

83. **Solvent Selection**: Use mixed solvent system (toluene/cyclohexanone) for uniform quantum dot and polymer dissolution.

84. **Dispersion Method**: Employ high-shear mixing at 5000 rpm for 30 minutes to break up aggregates.

85. **Ultrasonication**: Apply bath sonication at 40 kHz for 10 minutes to ensure complete dispersion.

86. **Filtration**: Filter formulation through 0.45 μm PTFE filter to remove any large aggregates.

87. **Viscosity Adjustment**: Adjust formulation viscosity to 200-500 cP for optimal coating process.

88. **Stability Testing**: Monitor dispersion stability over 30 days showing no sedimentation or phase separation.

89. **Degassing**: Vacuum degas formulation at 50 mbar for 20 minutes to remove dissolved gases and bubbles.

90. **Quality Control**: Measure photoluminescence spectrum and quantum yield of formulation before coating.

### Barrier Film Integration

91. **Barrier Film Selection**: Source multilayer barrier films with <10⁻⁴ g/m²/day WVTR specification.

92. **Surface Treatment**: Corona or plasma treat barrier film surface to improve wetting and adhesion.

93. **Primer Application**: Apply thin primer layer to enhance bonding between barrier film and quantum dot layer.

94. **Coating Method Selection**: Evaluate slot-die, gravure, and precision blade coating methods.

95. **Slot-Die Setup**: Configure slot-die coater with 25 μm gap height and 50 mm coating width.

96. **Web Speed Optimization**: Set coating speed at 10 m/min for uniform wet film deposition.

97. **Wet Thickness Control**: Target 50 μm wet thickness for 10-15 μm final dry film thickness.

98. **Coating Uniformity**: Achieve thickness variation <±3% across web width through precision flow control.

99. **Edge Bead Management**: Implement edge guides and vacuum to minimize edge thickness variations.

100. **First Layer Coating**: Coat quantum dot formulation onto barrier film under yellow light to prevent photo-degradation.

## Phase 4: Film Fabrication Process (Steps 101-140)

### Coating and Drying

101. **Drying Zone Entry**: Pass coated film through IR heating zone at 60°C for initial solvent flash-off.

102. **Multi-Stage Drying**: Employ three-stage drying at 60°C, 80°C, and 100°C to prevent film defects.

103. **Residence Time**: Maintain 2-minute residence time in each drying zone for complete solvent removal.

104. **Solvent Recovery**: Capture evaporated solvents in condenser system for environmental compliance and reuse.

105. **Film Tension Control**: Maintain web tension at 30 N/m to prevent wrinkles during drying.

106. **Temperature Monitoring**: Use IR sensors to monitor film surface temperature in real-time.

107. **Residual Solvent Testing**: Verify residual solvent <100 ppm by gas chromatography analysis.

108. **Film Appearance**: Inspect for coating defects including streaks, pinholes, or particles.

109. **Thickness Measurement**: Use non-contact optical sensors to measure film thickness continuously.

110. **First Layer Curing**: UV cure polymer matrix at 2 J/cm² using 365 nm LED arrays if using photo-curable formulation.

### Top Barrier Layer Application

111. **Film Flip Station**: Flip coated film for application of top barrier layer protecting quantum dot film.

112. **Adhesive Selection**: Select optical-grade pressure-sensitive adhesive with >99% transmission.

113. **Adhesive Coating**: Coat 5-10 μm adhesive layer on second barrier film for lamination.

114. **Lamination Temperature**: Heat lamination rolls to 60°C for optimal adhesive flow and bonding.

115. **Nip Pressure**: Apply 3-5 bar pressure through lamination nip for bubble-free bonding.

116. **Lamination Speed**: Match lamination speed to coating line at 10 m/min for continuous process.

117. **Bubble Detection**: Use optical inspection to detect and reject any films with trapped air bubbles.

118. **Edge Sealing**: Apply additional sealant at film edges to create hermetic seal preventing moisture ingress.

119. **Post-Lamination Cure**: Age laminated films at 50°C for 24 hours to develop full adhesive bond strength.

120. **Seal Integrity Testing**: Sample seal quality using dye penetration test verifying hermetic encapsulation.

### Optical Performance Optimization

121. **Scattering Layer Design**: Add optional light-scattering particles for diffuse reflection and efficiency.

122. **Refractive Index Matching**: Use optical coupling fluids between layers to minimize Fresnel reflections.

123. **Anti-Reflection Coating**: Apply nano-porous AR coating on outer surfaces reducing reflection to <0.5%.

124. **Color Filter Integration**: Integrate optional color filters for further gamut enhancement beyond Rec. 2020.

125. **Brightness Enhancement Film**: Laminate with BEF film for improved on-axis brightness and efficiency.

126. **Light Recycling**: Design film stack to recycle unconverted blue light back to quantum dots.

127. **Quantum Dot Density Mapping**: Create zone-coated films with varying QD concentration for uniformity correction.

128. **Spectral Tuning**: Fine-tune red/green ratio across film area to achieve uniform white point.

129. **Efficiency Testing**: Measure photon conversion efficiency >85% from blue excitation to red/green emission.

### Quality Control and Inspection

132. **Thickness Profiling**: Create 2D thickness maps using beta-ray or capacitance sensors.

133. **Optical Density**: Measure optical density at peak emission wavelengths to verify quantum dot concentration.
