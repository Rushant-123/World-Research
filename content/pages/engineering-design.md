# Engineering Design Services Manufacturing Process

## Requirements Analysis Phase

1. Receive project brief and initial specifications from client stakeholders
2. Conduct kickoff meeting with client to understand project objectives and constraints
3. Review existing facilities, systems, or legacy designs if applicable
4. Identify regulatory requirements and applicable codes (ASME, IEEE, ISO, etc.)
5. Document functional requirements including performance metrics and capacity targets
6. Define operational parameters including temperature, pressure, flow rates, and loads
7. Establish safety requirements and hazard identification protocols
8. Determine environmental constraints and sustainability goals
9. Review site conditions including geological surveys and climate data
10. Assess utility availability including power, water, and telecommunications
11. Identify interfacing systems and integration requirements
12. Document maintenance and accessibility requirements
13. Establish lifecycle and reliability targets (MTBF, MTTR specifications)
14. Define budget constraints and cost optimization priorities
15. Create project schedule with major milestones and deliverable dates
16. Identify key stakeholders and establish communication protocols
17. Review intellectual property considerations and proprietary technologies
18. Assess supply chain constraints and material availability
19. Document quality assurance and testing requirements
20. Establish design basis memorandum summarizing all requirements

## Conceptual Design Phase

21. Brainstorm multiple design concepts through engineering team workshops
22. Create preliminary sketches and block diagrams for each concept
23. Perform high-level feasibility analysis for each alternative
24. Conduct technology selection comparing proven vs. emerging solutions
25. Evaluate modular vs. integrated design approaches
26. Assess scalability and future expansion capabilities
27. Perform preliminary cost estimation using parametric methods
28. Create concept-level process flow diagrams (PFDs)
29. Develop equipment lists with approximate sizes and capacities
30. Perform space allocation and preliminary layout studies
31. Evaluate construction methodology and sequencing
32. Assess transportation and logistics constraints for major components
33. Create preliminary single-line electrical diagrams
34. Develop instrumentation and control philosophy
35. Perform risk assessment for each concept using FMEA methodology
36. Conduct value engineering workshops to optimize concepts
37. Create 3D massing models to visualize spatial relationships
38. Perform preliminary structural load analysis
39. Evaluate energy efficiency and operational cost estimates
40. Present concept alternatives to client with recommendations
41. Facilitate client decision-making workshops
42. Document selected concept with justification in concept design report
43. Obtain client approval to proceed to detailed design phase

## Detailed Design Phase - Mechanical Systems

44. Create detailed 3D CAD models in SolidWorks or CATIA
45. Design pressure vessels per ASME Section VIII Division 1
46. Calculate wall thickness using Barlow's formula and stress analysis
47. Design nozzle reinforcements and openings per UG-37 requirements
48. Select materials based on temperature, corrosion, and strength requirements
49. Design piping systems per ASME B31.3 process piping code
50. Perform pipe stress analysis using CAESAR II software
51. Design pipe supports, hangers, and restraints
52. Size relief valves and pressure safety devices
53. Design heat exchangers using TEMA standards
54. Calculate heat transfer coefficients and thermal effectiveness
55. Design shell and tube configurations with baffle arrangements
56. Size pumps based on system curves and NPSH requirements
57. Select pump types (centrifugal, positive displacement, etc.)
58. Design pump foundations and vibration isolation
59. Size compressors using thermodynamic cycle analysis
60. Design cooling and lubrication systems for rotating equipment
61. Create detailed assembly drawings with bill of materials
62. Design static equipment including tanks, columns, and reactors
63. Perform FEA stress analysis on critical components
64. Design mechanical connections including flanges, gaskets, and bolting
65. Create maintenance access provisions and lifting lug designs

## Detailed Design Phase - Structural Systems

66. Develop structural design basis including load combinations
67. Calculate dead loads from equipment, piping, and permanent structures
68. Determine live loads from maintenance, snow, and operational activities
69. Calculate wind loads per ASCE 7 using velocity pressure method
70. Perform seismic analysis using response spectrum or equivalent static methods
71. Model structures in SAP2000 or ETABS finite element software
72. Design steel frames using AISC 360 specifications
73. Size beams using moment and shear capacity checks
74. Design beam-column connections (moment, shear, braced frame)
75. Size columns for axial and combined loading conditions
76. Design base plates and anchor bolt configurations
77. Check stability including lateral-torsional buckling and frame stability
78. Design concrete foundations using ACI 318 code
79. Calculate bearing capacity and settlement using geotechnical reports
80. Size footings, pile caps, and mat foundations
81. Design reinforcement layouts with proper cover and spacing
82. Detail concrete reinforcement drawings with bar schedules
83. Design platforms, stairways, and ladders per OSHA requirements
84. Create structural steel fabrication drawings with weld symbols
85. Perform connection design including welds and bolted connections
86. Design crane support structures and runway beams
87. Calculate deflection limits for serviceability requirements

## Detailed Design Phase - Electrical Systems

88. Develop single-line diagrams showing power distribution architecture
89. Perform load analysis and demand calculations per NEC Article 220
90. Size main switchgear and distribution panels
91. Calculate short circuit currents using impedance method
92. Select circuit breakers and protective devices for coordination
93. Create protective device coordination study with time-current curves
94. Design grounding system per IEEE 80 standards
95. Calculate ground grid resistance and step/touch potentials
96. Size cables and conductors based on ampacity and voltage drop
97. Route cable trays and conduit systems
98. Design lighting systems with illumination calculations per IES standards
99. Select lighting fixtures for hazardous and non-hazardous areas
100. Design emergency lighting and egress systems
101. Create lighting control strategies including occupancy sensors
102. Design motor control centers (MCC) with VFD integration
103. Size motor starters and overload protection
104. Create motor connection diagrams and control schematics
105. Design UPS systems for critical loads with battery sizing
106. Calculate battery backup duration and recharge times
107. Design fire alarm systems per NFPA 72
108. Create cable schedules and interconnection diagrams

## Detailed Design Phase - Control Systems

109. Develop control system architecture (DCS, PLC, SCADA)
110. Create process and instrumentation diagrams (P&IDs)
111. Assign instrument tag numbers per ISA standards
112. Specify instruments including transmitters, analyzers, and switches
113. Size control valves using Cv calculations
114. Develop control valve specifications including trim and actuators
115. Design control loops with PID tuning parameters
116. Create cause and effect matrices for interlocks and safety systems
117. Develop alarm management philosophy per ISA 18.2
118. Create HMI screen layouts and navigation structures
119. Design safety instrumented systems (SIS) per IEC 61511
120. Perform SIL verification calculations for safety loops
121. Create functional safety requirements specifications
122. Design instrument cable routing and junction box layouts
123. Specify instrument air systems and pneumatic distribution
124. Create instrument installation details and typical drawings
125. Develop control system network architecture
126. Specify cybersecurity measures for industrial control systems

## FEA Simulation and Analysis

127. Create finite element meshes using tetrahedral or hexahedral elements
128. Define material properties including elastic modulus and Poisson's ratio
129. Apply boundary conditions representing supports and constraints
130. Apply loads including pressure, thermal, and inertial forces
131. Run static stress analysis to determine von Mises stress distributions
132. Evaluate stress concentrations at geometric discontinuities
133. Check stresses against allowable values per design codes
134. Perform fatigue analysis using S-N curves or fracture mechanics
135. Calculate fatigue life and damage accumulation
136. Conduct thermal analysis to determine temperature distributions
137. Analyze thermal expansion and thermal stress effects
138. Perform modal analysis to determine natural frequencies
139. Evaluate vibration modes and resonance risks
140. Conduct harmonic response analysis for cyclic loading
141. Perform buckling analysis for compression members and shells
142. Analyze nonlinear effects including plasticity and contact
143. Run transient dynamic analysis for impact or seismic events
144. Optimize designs using topology optimization techniques
145. Validate FEA results against hand calculations and benchmarks
146. Document analysis results in detailed calculation reports

## CFD Analysis

147. Create fluid domain geometry and flow boundaries
148. Generate computational mesh with appropriate refinement
149. Define fluid properties including density, viscosity, and thermal properties
150. Specify boundary conditions at inlets, outlets, and walls
151. Select turbulence model (k-epsilon, k-omega, LES, etc.)
152. Run steady-state flow simulations for baseline conditions
153. Analyze velocity fields and identify recirculation zones
154. Evaluate pressure distributions and pressure drop predictions
155. Calculate heat transfer coefficients and temperature profiles
156. Assess mixing effectiveness and residence time distributions
157. Perform particle tracking for multiphase flow systems
158. Analyze combustion processes with chemical kinetics
159. Run transient simulations for time-varying conditions
160. Evaluate flow-induced vibration risks
161. Optimize geometries to reduce pressure drop or improve efficiency
162. Validate CFD results against experimental data or correlations
163. Document CFD methodology and results in technical reports

## Design Reviews and Validation

164. Conduct 30% design review with internal team
165. Review design against requirements checklist
166. Identify design conflicts and interferences using 3D model reviews
167. Perform constructability review with construction team input
168. Conduct operability review with operations personnel
169. Review maintainability and accessibility provisions
170. Conduct 60% design review with client participation
171. Present design progress and address client comments
172. Perform hazard and operability study (HAZOP) for process systems
173. Identify deviations and implement risk mitigation measures
174. Conduct 90% design review for final coordination
175. Verify all design calculations and analysis results
176. Check drawing coordination across disciplines
177. Review equipment datasheets for completeness and accuracy
178. Conduct design verification per quality procedures
179. Perform independent checking of critical calculations
180. Review designs against applicable codes and standards checklist

## Documentation and Deliverables

181. Create design basis document summarizing criteria and methodology
182. Compile calculation packages with signed and sealed calculations
183. Produce final 3D CAD models with all disciplines integrated
184. Generate fabrication drawings with dimensions and tolerances
185. Create assembly drawings showing component relationships
186. Produce installation drawings with field construction details
187. Develop equipment specifications with technical requirements
188. Create material requisitions with approved vendor lists
189. Compile instrument datasheets and loop drawings
190. Produce operations and maintenance manuals
191. Create commissioning and startup procedures
192. Develop training materials for operations personnel
193. Compile as-designed documentation package
194. Obtain professional engineer stamps and seals on drawings
195. Submit final deliverables package to client for approval and construction
