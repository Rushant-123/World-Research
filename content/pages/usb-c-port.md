# Manufacturing Process: USB-C Port Connector

**Market Leader:** [JAE Electronics](/companies/jae-electronics)



## Step 1: Design Specification and Requirements Analysis
Review USB-C specifications including USB Type-C Cable and Connector Specification 2.1, USB Power Delivery 3.0 requirements, and mechanical durability standards for 10,000 insertion cycles.

## Step 2: 24-Pin Layout Design
Create CAD drawings for the 24-pin configuration with A1-A12 and B1-B12 pin arrangements ensuring reversible orientation capability and proper signal path routing.

## Step 3: Differential Pair Identification
Map out the four differential pairs for SuperSpeed data: TX1+/TX1-, TX2+/TX2-, RX1+/RX1-, and RX2+/RX2- with impedance matching requirements at 90 ohms.

## Step 4: Power Pin Specification
Design VBUS pins (A4, A9, B4, B9) to handle up to 100W power delivery with current capacity of 5A at 20V for USB PD 3.0 compliance.

## Step 5: Ground Pin Distribution
Allocate ground pins (A1, A12, B1, B12) symmetrically for optimal shielding and return path for high-speed signals across the reversible design.

## Step 6: Configuration Channel Design
Integrate CC1 (A5) and CC2 (B5) pins for connection detection, cable orientation detection, and USB Power Delivery communication protocol.

## Step 7: Sideband Use Pins
Designate SBU1 (A8) and SBU2 (B8) pins for alternate mode support including DisplayPort, HDMI, and Thunderbolt alternate modes.

## Step 8: Contact Pin Material Selection
Select copper alloy C7025 (copper-nickel-silicon) for contact pins providing excellent spring characteristics, electrical conductivity, and resistance to stress relaxation.

## Step 9: Contact Pin Forming
Stamp contact pins from copper alloy strip using progressive die stamping to create the complex geometry including contact beams, retention features, and solder tails.

## Step 10: Pin Surface Preparation
Clean contact pins using ultrasonic cleaning in alkaline solution followed by deionized water rinse to remove stamping oils and contaminants.

## Step 11: Nickel Underplating
Apply nickel underplating layer of 1.27-2.54 micrometers thickness via electroplating to provide corrosion resistance and barrier layer for subsequent gold plating.

## Step 12: Gold Contact Plating
Electroplate gold layer of 0.76-1.27 micrometers on contact areas to ensure low contact resistance, durability through 10,000 insertion cycles, and oxidation resistance.

## Step 13: Tin Solder Tail Plating
Apply tin plating to PCB mounting solder tails with thickness of 2.54-5.08 micrometers for excellent solderability and lead-free solder compatibility.

## Step 14: Pin Inspection
Conduct automated optical inspection of plated contact pins checking for plating uniformity, thickness compliance, surface defects, and dimensional accuracy.

## Step 15: Contact Force Calibration
Test and adjust spring force of contact beams to achieve 0.3-0.8N contact force per pin ensuring reliable electrical connection without excessive insertion force.

## Step 16: Metal Shell Material Selection
Select stainless steel SUS301 or SUS304 for outer shell providing electromagnetic shielding, mechanical strength, and corrosion resistance.

## Step 17: Shell Blank Preparation
Cut stainless steel sheet to required dimensions using precision shearing or laser cutting for shell component fabrication.

## Step 18: Shell Drawing Operation
Form shell body through deep drawing process creating the rectangular cavity that houses the contact pin assembly and provides external mating interface.

## Step 19: Shell Trimming and Piercing
Trim excess material and pierce mounting holes, cable entry slots, and ventilation features using progressive die operations.

## Step 20: Shell Edge Deburring
Deburr all cut edges using tumbling, brushing, or electrochemical deburring to eliminate sharp edges that could damage mating connectors or cables.

## Step 21: Shell EMI Shielding Enhancement
Create textured interior surface through bead blasting or etching to improve electromagnetic interference shielding effectiveness across frequency range.

## Step 22: Shell Nickel Plating
Apply nickel plating to shell exterior and interior surfaces with thickness of 2.54-5.08 micrometers for corrosion protection and improved shielding.

## Step 23: Shell Grounding Features
Form spring fingers or grounding tabs on shell to ensure reliable electrical contact with mating connector shell for EMI shielding continuity.

## Step 24: Shell Dimensional Verification
Measure critical dimensions including cavity depth, width, mounting hole positions, and profile tolerances using coordinate measuring machine (CMM).

## Step 25: Insulator Material Selection
Choose liquid crystal polymer (LCP) with glass fiber reinforcement (30-40%) for insulator providing high temperature resistance, dimensional stability, and excellent dielectric properties.

## Step 26: LCP Compound Preparation
Prepare LCP molding compound by drying resin pellets at 150-180°C for 4-6 hours to remove moisture ensuring optimal flow characteristics and preventing defects.

## Step 27: Injection Mold Design
Design precision injection mold with 24 pin cavities, gate locations optimized for balanced filling, and cooling channels for thermal management.

## Step 28: Mold Temperature Control
Set mold temperature to 120-150°C to ensure proper LCP crystallization, dimensional stability, and surface finish quality.

## Step 29: Injection Molding Parameters
Configure injection molding machine with barrel temperature profile of 300-360°C, injection pressure of 100-150 MPa, and holding pressure for dimensional accuracy.

## Step 30: Pin Insert Molding Setup
Position contact pins in mold using precision fixtures ensuring accurate pin pitch of 0.5mm and perpendicularity within 0.05mm tolerance.

## Step 31: Insert Molding Process
Inject molten LCP around positioned contact pins, encapsulating retention features while maintaining pin position and electrical isolation between contacts.

## Step 32: Insulator Cooling
Allow molded insulator assembly to cool in controlled manner preventing warpage and ensuring dimensional stability of 24-pin array.

## Step 33: Ejection and Handling
Eject molded insulator assembly from mold using ejector pins without damaging delicate contact pins or insulator body.

## Step 34: Gate Removal
Remove injection molding gate remnants through trimming or grinding without affecting insulator surface quality or pin alignment.

## Step 35: Insulator Inspection
Inspect molded insulator for flash, voids, short shots, pin misalignment, and surface defects using automated vision systems.

## Step 36: Pin Pitch Verification
Measure pin pitch across all 24 positions verifying 0.5mm spacing with tolerance of ±0.05mm using high-precision optical measurement.

## Step 37: Insulator Dielectric Testing
Test dielectric strength of insulator material between adjacent pins ensuring minimum 500V breakdown voltage and proper electrical isolation.

## Step 38: Pin Retention Test
Apply axial force to contact pins verifying minimum retention force of 10N preventing pin dislodgment during mating and unmating cycles.

## Step 39: Tongue Design
Create central tongue feature on insulator that carries all 12 A-side pins with thickness of 0.55-0.65mm allowing reversible insertion.

## Step 40: Outer Pin Row Design
Design outer pin row on insulator body carrying all 12 B-side pins positioned to make contact when connector is inserted in either orientation.

## Step 41: Pin Height Adjustment
Set precise pin heights with A-side pins protruding 0.15-0.25mm beyond tongue surface and B-side pins positioned for proper contact timing.

## Step 42: Insulator Chamfer Formation
Form chamfer on tongue leading edge and outer edges facilitating smooth insertion and reducing risk of pin damage during mating.

## Step 43: PCB Mounting Footprint Design
Design through-hole or surface-mount footprint on printed circuit board with pad dimensions, hole sizes, and spacing matching connector pin layout.

## Step 44: Solder Tail Configuration
Configure contact pin solder tails as through-hole type with tail length of 2-3mm and diameter of 0.4-0.6mm for robust mechanical and electrical connection.

## Step 45: SMT Pad Design
For surface-mount variants, design contact pad geometry on PCB providing sufficient solder joint area for reliable connection and mechanical strength.

## Step 46: Anchor Post Addition
Integrate mechanical anchor posts on connector housing with corresponding holes in PCB providing structural support independent of electrical contacts.

## Step 47: Anchor Post Material
Fabricate anchor posts from brass or stainless steel with length matching PCB thickness plus solder joint height for optimal mechanical support.

## Step 48: Thermal Relief Design
Incorporate thermal relief features on PCB pads for through-hole pins facilitating hand soldering while maintaining adequate electrical connection.

## Step 49: PCB Copper Thickness
Specify PCB copper weight of 2oz (70 micrometers) or greater for VBUS power pins handling 5A current without excessive temperature rise.

## Step 50: Via Stitching Pattern
Design via stitching pattern around connector footprint connecting ground pins to internal ground planes for improved signal integrity and EMI control.

## Step 51: Shell Assembly Preparation
Clean and inspect all component parts including contact pin subassembly, insulator, metal shell, and mounting hardware before final assembly.

## Step 52: Pin Subassembly Insertion
Insert contact pin and insulator subassembly into metal shell ensuring proper alignment and seating of components within shell cavity.

## Step 53: Shell Crimping
Crimp metal shell around insulator assembly using precision tooling creating mechanical interlock while maintaining shell dimensional accuracy.

## Step 54: Ground Tab Forming
Form and position shell ground tabs to contact PCB ground pads providing EMI shielding connection and mechanical stability.

## Step 55: Retention Clip Installation
Install retention clips or latches on shell exterior providing audible/tactile feedback during cable insertion and retention force of 10-40N.

## Step 56: Connector Housing Assembly
Assemble any additional housing components including plastic covers, strain relief features, or mounting brackets to shell assembly.

## Step 57: Dimensional Inspection
Verify assembled connector dimensions including overall height, width, depth, tongue protrusion, and pin positions against USB-C specification requirements.

## Step 58: Pin Continuity Testing
Test electrical continuity of all 24 pins from contact point to solder tail ensuring resistance below 30 milliohms per pin.

## Step 59: Differential Pair Impedance
Measure characteristic impedance of differential pairs verifying 90±10 ohm impedance across SuperSpeed signal pairs using TDR (Time Domain Reflectometry).

## Step 60: Insertion Force Testing
Measure connector insertion force using calibrated force gauge verifying maximum force of 35N for initial insertion meeting USB-C specification.

## Step 61: Withdrawal Force Testing

## Step 62: Durability Cycle Testing

## Step 63: Contact Resistance Monitoring

## Step 64: USB Power Delivery Testing

## Step 65: Current Carrying Verification

## Step 66: Temperature Rise Testing
