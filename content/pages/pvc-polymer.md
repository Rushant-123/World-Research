# PVC Polymer Production for Cable Insulation and Components

**Market Leader:** [Formosa Plastics](/companies/formosa-plastics)



## Raw Material Preparation and Quality Control

1. Receive vinyl chloride monomer (VCM) in dedicated storage tanks with refrigeration systems maintaining -13°C to -10°C
2. Test incoming VCM for purity specification of minimum 99.95% with gas chromatography analysis
3. Verify VCM inhibitor content (phenol or hydroquinone) to prevent premature polymerization during storage
4. Sample VCM for moisture content analysis ensuring less than 100 ppm water
5. Check VCM storage tank pressure maintaining 0.2-0.5 MPa gauge pressure with nitrogen blanketing
6. Inspect storage tank cooling system functionality and refrigerant levels
7. Calibrate VCM flow meters and mass flow controllers for accurate dosing
8. Receive suspension stabilizer (typically polyvinyl alcohol or cellulose derivatives) in powder or solution form
9. Verify suspension stabilizer molecular weight specification (typically 20,000-100,000 Da)
10. Test stabilizer hydrolysis degree (87-89% for optimal performance in PVC suspension polymerization)
11. Prepare suspension stabilizer solution by dissolving in deionized water at 80-95°C with agitation
12. Filter stabilizer solution through 50-100 micron filters to remove undissolved particles
13. Store prepared stabilizer solution at controlled temperature with continuous circulation
14. Receive organic peroxide initiator (typically lauroyl peroxide or diisopropyl peroxydicarbonate)
15. Verify initiator purity and half-life temperature specifications (10-hour half-life at 55-65°C)
16. Store initiator in refrigerated compartment at 0-5°C to prevent decomposition
17. Test initiator active oxygen content to confirm polymerization activity
18. Prepare initiator dispersion or solution according to formulation requirements
19. Receive auxiliary additives including chain transfer agents for molecular weight control
20. Verify buffer systems (typically sodium bicarbonate or phosphate buffers) for pH control during polymerization
21. Test deionized water quality ensuring conductivity less than 5 microsiemens/cm
22. Check demineralized water supply with total dissolved solids less than 10 ppm
23. Prepare antioxidant solutions to prevent polymer degradation during processing
24. Verify anti-scaling agents to prevent polymer buildup on reactor walls
25. Inspect all raw material containers for proper labeling and material compatibility

## Reactor Charging and Initial Conditions

51. Establish nitrogen atmosphere in clean reactor by purging with high-purity nitrogen
52. Evacuate reactor to vacuum (typically 50-100 mbar) to remove residual oxygen
53. Charge demineralized water to reactor (typically 40-50% of reactor volume)
54. Add suspension stabilizer solution to water phase with agitation at low speed
55. Verify stabilizer concentration in water phase (typically 0.03-0.15% based on water)
56. Add buffer solution (sodium bicarbonate or phosphate) to maintain pH 3.5-4.5
57. Add anti-scaling agent (typically 0.01-0.05% based on water) to prevent reactor fouling
58. Adjust water phase pH using dilute acid or base as needed
59. Activate reactor agitation system at initial low speed (30-50 RPM)
60. Start jacket cooling circulation with chilled water or refrigerant at set temperature
61. Begin VCM charging to reactor through bottom feed line under controlled flow
62. Monitor reactor pressure increase as VCM is charged indicating vapor phase formation
63. Control VCM feed rate to manage temperature rise from condensation heat
64. Charge calculated amount of VCM (typically 50-70% of reactor volume when liquid)
65. Verify VCM charge weight using load cells or volumetric measurement systems
66. Add chain transfer agent if required for molecular weight control (typically 0.01-0.1%)
67. Prepare initiator charge calculating dosage based on VCM weight (typically 0.02-0.08%)
68. Heat reactor contents to polymerization temperature (typically 50-57°C for suspension PVC)
69. Monitor temperature approach to set point with controlled heating rate
70. Stabilize reactor temperature at target set point before initiator addition
71. Increase agitator speed to polymerization operating speed (typically 80-120 RPM)
72. Verify agitator power draw and torque within normal operating range
73. Check for proper droplet formation and suspension stability visually through sight glass
74. Confirm all safety interlocks and emergency shutdown systems active
75. Verify reactor pressure relief systems set at appropriate values (typically 12-14 bar)

## VCM Stripping and Recovery

101. Stop polymerization by discontinuing heat input when target conversion reached
102. Cool reactor contents to 40-50°C to reduce VCM vapor pressure
103. Begin VCM recovery by venting vapor phase to VCM recovery condenser system
104. Condense recovered VCM vapor using refrigerated condenser at -15 to -20°C
105. Return condensed VCM to storage tank for reuse in subsequent batches
106. Monitor VCM concentration in reactor decreasing as stripping proceeds
107. Apply vacuum to reactor (typically 200-500 mbar) to enhance VCM removal
108. Heat reactor contents to 60-70°C under vacuum to strip residual VCM from polymer
109. Maintain agitation during stripping to expose all polymer surfaces
110. Strip VCM to target residual level (typically less than 1 ppm in final resin)
111. Pass reactor vapor through VCM scrubbing system removing traces with caustic solution
112. Monitor VCM concentration in vent gas using online analyzer or periodic sampling
113. Continue stripping until VCM content in slurry meets specification
114. Verify no VCM odor remains in reactor headspace indicating complete removal
115. Sample slurry for residual VCM analysis using gas chromatography
116. Record total VCM recovered and calculate material balance for batch
117. Cool reactor contents to 40-50°C for slurry discharge operations
118. Release reactor vacuum and return to atmospheric pressure with nitrogen
119. Prepare slurry transfer system for discharge to downstream processing
120. Verify slurry homogeneity with final agitation before discharge
