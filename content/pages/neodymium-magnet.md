# Neodymium Magnet Production Process

**Market Leader:** [Hitachi Metals](/companies/hitachi-metals)



## Raw Material Preparation and Inspection

1. Receive neodymium oxide (Nd2O3) shipment in sealed containers from rare earth processing facility
2. Verify certificate of analysis for neodymium oxide purity (minimum 99.5%)
3. Check moisture content of neodymium oxide using thermogravimetric analysis
4. Store neodymium oxide in climate-controlled warehouse at <20% relative humidity
5. Receive high-purity iron powder (99.9% Fe) in 25kg drums
6. Measure iron powder particle size distribution using laser diffraction analyzer
7. Verify iron powder oxygen content is below 1000 ppm
8. Receive ferro-boron or pure boron powder (99.5% purity minimum)
9. Inspect boron powder for contamination using spectroscopic analysis
10. Weigh neodymium oxide batch using precision scale (±0.1g accuracy)
11. Calculate stoichiometric ratios for Nd2Fe14B composition (typical: 32% Nd, 64% Fe, 1% B)
12. Weigh iron powder according to formulation requirements
13. Weigh boron powder or ferro-boron to achieve target composition
14. Prepare dopant materials including dysprosium, praseodymium, or cobalt for property enhancement
15. Weigh dopant materials to achieve 1-5% substitution levels

## Alloy Melting and Casting

16. Clean vacuum induction melting furnace chamber with isopropanol
17. Install graphite crucible in melting furnace
18. Load weighed neodymium oxide, iron powder, and boron powder into crucible
19. Seal melting furnace chamber and evacuate to <10^-3 Torr
20. Backfill chamber with high-purity argon gas (99.999%)
21. Establish argon flow rate at 5-10 L/min during melting
22. Initiate induction heating at controlled ramp rate of 50°C/min
23. Monitor melt temperature using optical pyrometer
24. Heat materials to 1400-1500°C to achieve complete melting
25. Reduce neodymium oxide to metallic neodymium through reaction with carbon/boron
26. Stir molten alloy electromagnetically for 10-15 minutes to ensure homogeneity
27. Check melt temperature stability (±10°C) before casting
28. Tilt furnace to pour molten alloy into water-cooled copper mold
29. Achieve rapid solidification rate of 100-1000°C/sec through mold cooling
30. Allow cast ingot to cool to room temperature in controlled atmosphere
31. Remove solidified ingot from casting mold using mechanical extractor
32. Visually inspect ingot for cracks, porosity, or surface defects
33. Measure ingot dimensions and weight for yield calculation
34. Cut sample from ingot for chemical composition verification
35. Analyze sample composition using inductively coupled plasma mass spectrometry
36. Verify Nd2Fe14B phase formation using X-ray diffraction analysis
37. Measure grain size in as-cast ingot using metallographic examination
38. Document melting parameters and ingot properties in batch record

## Hydrogen Decrepitation Process

39. Transfer cast ingot to hydrogen decrepitation chamber
40. Secure ingot in stainless steel reaction vessel
41. Evacuate hydrogen decrepitation chamber to <10^-2 Torr
42. Leak test chamber using helium mass spectrometer
43. Heat chamber to 200-400°C using resistance heating elements
44. Introduce hydrogen gas (99.99% purity) at controlled pressure of 1-3 bar
45. Monitor hydrogen absorption rate using pressure transducer
46. Allow hydrogen to diffuse into alloy lattice causing volumetric expansion
47. Observe spontaneous decrepitation as ingot fractures into coarse powder
48. Maintain hydrogen atmosphere for 2-4 hours to ensure complete reaction
49. Monitor temperature exotherm during hydrogen absorption
50. Allow chamber to cool to <100°C while maintaining hydrogen atmosphere
51. Evacuate hydrogen gas and purge chamber with argon
52. Transfer decrepitated powder to inert atmosphere glove box
53. Inspect powder for particle size reduction (typical 1-10mm fragments)
54. Separate any unbroken ingot pieces for re-processing
55. Weigh decrepitated powder and calculate yield percentage
56. Store hydrogen-treated powder in sealed containers under argon
57. Label containers with batch number, date, and hydrogen treatment parameters

## Jet Milling and Fine Powder Production

58. Transfer decrepitated powder to jet mill feed hopper in inert atmosphere
59. Purge jet mill system with nitrogen gas to remove oxygen (<100 ppm O2)
60. Set jet mill operating pressure to 6-10 bar nitrogen
61. Calibrate powder feed rate to 1-5 kg/hour depending on mill size
62. Adjust classifier wheel speed to control particle size distribution
63. Start jet mill operation and monitor feed hopper level
64. Allow opposing nitrogen jets to create high-velocity particle collisions
65. Achieve fine powder with median particle size of 3-5 micrometers
66. Monitor mill chamber temperature to prevent oxidation (<50°C)
67. Maintain oxygen content in milling chamber below 200 ppm
68. Collect milled powder in cyclone separator under inert atmosphere
69. Check powder collection efficiency and minimize material loss
70. Sample milled powder for particle size analysis using laser diffraction
71. Verify particle size distribution meets specification (D50: 3-5 μm)
72. Inspect powder morphology using scanning electron microscopy
73. Measure specific surface area using BET gas adsorption method
74. Test powder flowability using Hall flowmeter apparatus
75. Store jet-milled powder in hermetically sealed containers
76. Maintain storage environment with <50 ppm oxygen and <20% RH
77. Implement first-in-first-out inventory management for powder batches
78. Blend multiple powder batches if required for production consistency

## Magnetic Alignment and Pressing

79. Transfer jet-milled powder to alignment press feed system
80. Install alignment die set in hydraulic or mechanical press
81. Clean die cavity and punches with non-contaminating solvent
82. Apply release agent to die surfaces (e.g., zinc stearate suspension)
83. Energize electromagnetic alignment coils to generate 1.5-2.5 Tesla field
84. Verify magnetic field strength and uniformity using gaussmeter
85. Set magnetic field direction perpendicular to pressing direction
86. Fill die cavity with measured quantity of powder (typically 50-500g)
87. Activate magnetic field before applying mechanical pressure
88. Allow powder particles to rotate and align with magnetic field direction
89. Monitor alignment time (typically 1-3 seconds) for optimal orientation
90. Apply initial low pressure of 20-50 MPa to consolidate aligned powder
91. Increase pressing pressure gradually to 100-200 MPa
92. Maintain maximum pressure for 5-10 seconds for density uniformity
93. Achieve green compact density of 50-60% of theoretical density
94. Release pressing pressure slowly to minimize compact cracking
95. Deactivate magnetic field after pressure release
96. Eject green compact from die using mechanical ejection system
97. Inspect green compact for surface defects, cracks, or delamination
98. Measure green compact dimensions using digital calipers
99. Weigh green compact to calculate green density
100. Check alignment quality by measuring magnetic orientation using flux meter
101. Mark green compact with batch identification code
102. Transfer green compacts to sintering furnace loading trays
103. Arrange compacts on trays with adequate spacing for uniform heating
104. Cover compacts with protective powder bed (e.g., alumina) if required

## Vacuum Sintering Process

105. Load sintering trays with green compacts into vacuum sintering furnace
106. Seal furnace chamber and initiate vacuum pumping sequence
107. Achieve vacuum level of <10^-3 Torr before heating begins
108. Monitor vacuum level continuously during sintering cycle
109. Start heating cycle with controlled ramp rate of 3-5°C/min
110. Heat to debinding temperature of 200-400°C to remove pressing lubricants
111. Hold at debinding temperature for 30-60 minutes
112. Continue heating to sintering temperature of 1000-1100°C
113. Monitor temperature uniformity across furnace hot zone (±10°C)
114. Maintain sintering temperature for 2-6 hours depending on compact size
115. Achieve liquid phase sintering as Nd-rich phase melts at grain boundaries
116. Allow grain growth and densification to reach >98% theoretical density
117. Begin cooling cycle at controlled rate of 3-5°C/min
118. Cool to 600-700°C for aging/annealing treatment
119. Hold at aging temperature for 1-3 hours to optimize microstructure
120. Precipitate Nd-rich grain boundary phase during aging
121. Continue cooling to room temperature at controlled rate
122. Backfill furnace chamber with argon gas when temperature reaches <100°C
123. Open furnace and unload sintered magnets
124. Inspect magnets for sintering defects (cracks, warping, discoloration)
125. Measure sintered magnet dimensions and compare to green dimensions
126. Calculate linear shrinkage (typically 15-20% in each direction)
127. Weigh sintered magnets to verify density achievement
128. Measure sintered density using Archimedes principle (water immersion)
129. Cut sample from sintered magnet for microstructure analysis
130. Polish sample and examine grain structure using optical microscopy
131. Verify Nd2Fe14B grain size of 5-20 micrometers
132. Check for abnormal grain growth or porosity using image analysis

## Machining and Dimensional Finishing

133. Transfer sintered magnets to precision grinding department
134. Mount magnets on magnetic chuck of surface grinder
135. Select diamond grinding wheel appropriate for NdFeB hardness
136. Set grinding parameters (wheel speed, feed rate, depth of cut)
137. Grind magnets to specified thickness with tolerance of ±0.05mm
138. Use flood coolant during grinding to prevent oxidation and heat damage
139. Measure ground surface thickness using digital micrometer
140. Grind magnet length and width dimensions to specified tolerances
141. Create flat, parallel surfaces with flatness <0.02mm
142. Achieve surface roughness Ra <0.8 μm through fine grinding
143. Transfer magnets to wire EDM or slicing saw for shape cutting
144. Program CNC wire EDM machine with magnet geometry specifications
145. Cut complex shapes (arcs, segments, rings) using wire EDM process
146. Use deionized water as EDM dielectric fluid
147. Minimize heat-affected zone during EDM cutting (<0.1mm)
148. Alternatively, use diamond blade slicing saw for simple rectangular cuts
149. Clean magnets ultrasonically in isopropanol after machining
150. Dry magnets in vacuum oven at 60-80°C for 30 minutes
151. Inspect machined dimensions using coordinate measuring machine (CMM)
152. Verify dimensional tolerances meet customer specifications
153. Check magnet edges for chips or micro-cracks using magnification
154. Deburr sharp edges using tumbling or manual methods if required
155. Sort magnets by dimensional categories for inventory management

## Surface Coating Application

156. Clean machined magnets in alkaline cleaning solution to remove oils
157. Rinse magnets thoroughly with deionized water
158. Dry magnets completely in hot air oven at 80-100°C
159. Inspect magnet surfaces for cleanliness and contamination
160. Suspend magnets on rack for electroplating preparation
161. Prepare nickel electroplating bath with nickel sulfate solution
162. Set bath temperature to 50-60°C and pH to 4.0-5.0
163. Immerse magnets in electroplating bath as cathode
164. Apply current density of 2-5 A/dm² for nickel plating
165. Deposit first nickel layer with thickness of 10-15 micrometers
166. Monitor plating time to achieve specified nickel thickness
167. Remove magnets from nickel bath and rinse with deionized water
168. Transfer magnets to copper electroplating bath
169. Plate copper interlayer with thickness of 5-10 micrometers
170. Rinse magnets and transfer to final nickel plating bath
171. Deposit outer nickel layer with thickness of 5-10 micrometers
172. Achieve total Ni-Cu-Ni coating thickness of 20-35 micrometers
173. Remove magnets from plating bath and rinse thoroughly
174. Dry coated magnets in oven at 80-100°C
175. Bake coated magnets at 150-200°C for 1-2 hours to improve adhesion
176. Inspect coating for uniformity, coverage, and defects
177. Measure coating thickness using eddy current thickness gauge
178. Perform salt spray testing on sample magnets (per ASTM B117)
179. Verify coating passes 48-72 hour salt spray corrosion test
180. Alternatively, apply epoxy coating using electrophoretic deposition
181. Or apply zinc coating followed by chromate conversion coating

## Magnetization and Final Testing

182. Transfer coated magnets to magnetization fixture
183. Select magnetization coil appropriate for magnet geometry
184. Place magnet in coil with easy axis aligned to field direction
185. Connect magnetization capacitor bank (stored energy 5-50 kJ)
186. Set charging voltage to achieve field strength of 3-5 Tesla
187. Trigger capacitor discharge through magnetization coil
188. Generate pulsed magnetic field with rise time <1 millisecond
189. Saturate all magnetic domains in magnet parallel to easy axis
190. Remove magnetized magnet from coil using non-magnetic tools
191. Measure magnetic flux density using gaussmeter or fluxmeter
192. Test magnet in closed circuit using permeameter
193. Measure intrinsic coercivity (Hci), remanence (Br), and maximum energy product (BHmax)
194. Verify magnetic properties meet grade specifications (e.g., N52: Br=14.5kG, BHmax=52MGOe)
195. Package finished magnets with magnetic field shielding and ship to customers for use in Mac speakers and motor applications
