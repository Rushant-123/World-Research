## Water Intake and Initial Screening

1. Connect to municipal water supply line at 500-800 kPa supply pressure
2. Install isolation valve and pressure gauge at intake point
3. Route water through 3-inch diameter PVC intake piping
4. Pass water through coarse bar screen (25mm spacing) to remove large debris
5. Monitor intake flow rate at 500-1000 gallons per minute (GPM) using magnetic flowmeter
6. Record initial water temperature (typically 10-25°C depending on season)
7. Measure raw water pH using inline pH probe (typical range 6.5-8.5)
8. Check initial total dissolved solids (TDS) level (typically 200-500 mg/L from municipal supply)
9. Screen for turbidity using nephelometric turbidity unit (NTU) sensor (acceptable <5 NTU)
10. Install fine screen (5mm spacing) for secondary debris removal
11. Collect screen overflow in debris collection basket
12. Clean coarse screen weekly and fine screen daily to prevent clogging
13. Monitor differential pressure across screens (should not exceed 15 kPa)
14. Route screened water to equalization tank

## Equalization and Storage

15. Fill 50,000-gallon concrete equalization tank to buffer flow variations
16. Install submersible level sensors to monitor tank depth (maintain 60-80% full)
17. Mix tank contents using 5 HP submersible mixer to prevent stratification
18. Run mixer continuously at 200 RPM to ensure uniform water quality
19. Monitor tank temperature with RTD sensors at three depths
20. Install overflow pipe at top of tank directing to storm drain
21. Add sampling port at mid-depth for water quality testing
22. Test equalized water every 4 hours for pH, conductivity, and turbidity
23. Record tank residence time (typically 2-4 hours at design flow)
24. Install high-level alarm to prevent tank overflow
25. Clean tank annually and inspect for cracks or deterioration
26. Pump water from equalization tank using variable frequency drive (VFD) pumps
27. Maintain steady flow of 600 GPM to treatment trains
28. Monitor pump discharge pressure (target 400 kPa)

## Coagulation Process

29. Inject aluminum sulfate (alum) coagulant at 20-50 mg/L dosing rate
30. Use metering pump with 0.1% accuracy for precise chemical addition
31. Inject coagulant immediately before rapid mix chamber
32. Monitor coagulant feed rate using flow totalizer (typical 30-40 mg/L)
33. Rapidly mix coagulant in 500-gallon flash mix tank
34. Use high-speed mixer at 150-200 RPM for 30-60 seconds
35. Apply velocity gradient (G value) of 700-1000 s⁻¹ for effective mixing
36. Monitor mixer power consumption (typically 2-3 HP)
37. Adjust coagulant dose based on jar testing results performed weekly
38. Test pH after coagulant addition (optimal range 6.0-7.5 for alum)
39. Add sulfuric acid or sodium hydroxide if pH adjustment needed
40. Maintain flash mix detention time of 30-45 seconds
41. Observe floc formation beginning in flash mix chamber
42. Monitor turbidity reduction (should decrease by 20-30%)
43. Route coagulated water to flocculation basin

## Flocculation

44. Transfer water to three-stage flocculation basin (15,000 gallons total)
45. Mix gently in first stage at 40-50 RPM with paddle flocculators
46. Apply G value of 70-90 s⁻¹ in first flocculation chamber
47. Maintain 10-minute residence time in first stage
48. Add anionic polyelectrolyte flocculant aid at 0.5-2.0 mg/L
49. Inject flocculant using precision metering pump
50. Mix in second stage at 30-40 RPM with reduced intensity
51. Apply G value of 40-60 s⁻¹ in second chamber
52. Observe floc growth to 2-5mm diameter particles
53. Maintain 10-minute residence time in second stage
54. Mix gently in third stage at 20-30 RPM to avoid floc breakup
55. Apply G value of 20-30 s⁻¹ in final flocculation chamber
56. Maintain 10-minute residence time in third stage
57. Monitor floc blanket formation and density
58. Test settled water turbidity (target <2 NTU before sedimentation)
59. Adjust mixer speeds based on floc characteristics
60. Total flocculation time: 30 minutes across all stages
61. Route flocculated water to sedimentation basin via baffle walls

## Sedimentation

62. Direct water to 40,000-gallon rectangular sedimentation basin
63. Design for surface loading rate of 600-800 gallons/day/sq ft
64. Maintain horizontal flow velocity below 0.5 feet per minute
65. Install inclined plate settlers at 60-degree angle for enhanced settling
66. Plate spacing of 2 inches with effective settling length of 4 feet
67. Allow 2-3 hour detention time in sedimentation basin
68. Monitor settled water turbidity continuously (target <1 NTU)
69. Install sludge collection mechanism with scrapers at basin floor
70. Move scrapers at 1-2 feet per minute across basin length
71. Collect settled sludge in hopper at basin inlet end
72. Pump sludge to thickener tank using progressive cavity pump
73. Remove sludge every 4-6 hours to prevent resuspension
74. Monitor sludge blanket depth using ultrasonic level sensor
75. Maintain sludge blanket at 12-18 inches from basin floor
76. Skim floating materials from surface using mechanical skimmer
77. Collect clarified water from basin outlet via weir
78. Install adjustable weir plates to maintain uniform flow distribution
79. Test clarified water: pH 6.8-7.2, turbidity <1 NTU, suspended solids <5 mg/L
80. Route clarified water to multimedia filter system

## Multimedia Filtration

81. Operate four parallel pressure filters (8 feet diameter, 12 feet height)
82. Load anthracite coal (24 inches depth, 0.8-1.6mm effective size) as top layer
83. Load sand (12 inches depth, 0.4-0.6mm effective size) as middle layer
84. Load garnet (6 inches depth, 0.2-0.4mm effective size) as bottom layer
85. Install gravel support layer (12 inches depth, graded 2-25mm)
86. Design for filtration rate of 5 gallons/minute/sq ft
87. Apply downflow filtration with inlet pressure of 300 kPa
88. Distribute water evenly across filter surface using distribution manifold
89. Monitor pressure drop across filter bed (initial 30-40 kPa)
90. Run filtration cycle for 24-48 hours until pressure drop reaches 100 kPa
91. Measure filtered water turbidity continuously (target <0.1 NTU)
92. Test for total suspended solids (should be <1 mg/L)
93. Monitor filter effluent quality every 2 hours
94. Initiate backwash when pressure differential exceeds 70 kPa
95. Stop forward flow and drain filter to top of media bed
96. Introduce backwash water at 15 GPM/sq ft for 10-15 minutes
97. Expand filter bed by 30-40% during backwash
98. Add air scour at 3-5 CFM/sq ft simultaneously for enhanced cleaning
99. Rinse filter with forward flow at 5 GPM for 5 minutes
100. Return first 1000 gallons of filtered water to equalization tank
101. Resume normal filtration once turbidity drops below 0.1 NTU
102. Monitor backwash water consumption (typically 2-4% of production)
103. Clean filters completely every 3-6 months with chemical cleaning

## Water Softening - Ion Exchange

104. Route 50% of filtered water to ion exchange softening system
105. Use two parallel softener vessels (48 inches diameter, 72 inches height)
106. Fill with strong acid cation resin (40 cubic feet per vessel)
107. Operate in sodium cycle for calcium and magnesium removal
108. Design for service flow rate of 10 GPM per cubic foot of resin
109. Monitor inlet hardness (typically 150-250 mg/L as CaCO₃)
110. Reduce hardness to <1 mg/L as CaCO₃ in softened water
111. Measure hardness every 4 hours using titration method
112. Monitor effluent conductivity (increases as hardness ions removed)
113. Run service cycle until hardness breakthrough detected (>1 mg/L)
114. Typical service cycle length: 8-12 hours depending on inlet hardness
115. Initiate regeneration cycle when hardness exceeds specification
116. Backwash resin bed at 5 GPM/sq ft for 10 minutes to remove fines
117. Draw brine solution (10% sodium chloride) from 1000-gallon brine tank
118. Inject brine at 0.5 GPM per cubic foot of resin downflow
119. Use 10-15 pounds of salt per cubic foot of resin for regeneration
120. Allow 30-minute slow brine injection followed by 30-minute soak
121. Rinse resin bed with filtered water at 1.5 GPM/cu ft downflow
122. Continue rinse for 30-40 minutes until hardness and conductivity stabilize
123. Return first 500 gallons of rinse water to equalization tank
124. Return softener to service once effluent hardness <1 mg/L
125. Monitor salt usage and maintain brine tank saturation
126. Replace resin every 5-7 years or when capacity decreases

## pH Adjustment and Chemical Conditioning

127. Measure pH of softened water (typically 7.0-7.5)
128. Inject sodium hydroxide (50% solution) to increase pH to 8.0-8.5
129. Use dosing rate of 5-20 mg/L depending on inlet pH and alkalinity
130. Mix caustic thoroughly in 1000-gallon static mixer tank
131. Monitor pH continuously using online pH analyzer with ATC probe
132. Add sodium hexametaphosphate at 1-3 mg/L as corrosion inhibitor
133. Inject phosphate using diaphragm metering pump
134. Add sodium hypochlorite at 0.5-1.0 mg/L for biofouling control
135. Monitor free chlorine residual continuously (target 0.2-0.5 mg/L)
136. Test for calcium carbonate precipitation potential (Langelier Saturation Index)
137. Maintain LSI between -0.5 and +0.5 for balanced water
138. Blend softened water with filtered water for optimized mineral balance
139. Mix 50% softened with 50% filtered to achieve 75-100 mg/L hardness
140. Route conditioned water to process water storage tank

## Cooling Tower Water Treatment

141. Pump 40% of treated water to cooling tower makeup tank (20,000 gallons)
142. Monitor cooling tower cycles of concentration (target 4-6 cycles)
143. Measure conductivity in tower basin (should be 4-6× makeup water)
144. Test makeup water TDS: 200-300 mg/L
145. Monitor tower water TDS: 800-1800 mg/L at design cycles
146. Add corrosion inhibitor (zinc/phosphate blend) at 5-10 mg/L
147. Inject scale inhibitor (polymer-based) at 10-20 mg/L
148. Maintain biocide (isothiazolinone) at 10-15 mg/L
149. Monitor pH in tower basin (maintain 7.5-8.5)
150. Test for calcium hardness, alkalinity, and chlorides weekly
151. Control blowdown rate to maintain cycles of concentration
152. Calculate blowdown: Blowdown = Evaporation / (Cycles - 1)
153. Evaporation rate approximately 0.5% of recirculation flow per 5°C cooling
154. Install conductivity controller to automatically regulate blowdown
155. Monitor cooling tower efficiency (approach temperature 5-7°C)
156. Collect blowdown water for potential reuse or discharge
157. Test blowdown water quality before discharge: pH 6.5-9.0, TDS <2000 mg/L

## Boiler Feedwater Treatment

158. Route 30% of softened water to boiler feedwater system
159. Further treat through reverse osmosis (RO) unit for demineralization
160. Operate RO at 1200-1500 kPa with 75-80% recovery rate
161. Reduce TDS from 300 mg/L to <10 mg/L in permeate
162. Monitor RO permeate quality: conductivity <20 μS/cm
163. Polish permeate through mixed bed deionizer for ultrapure water
164. Achieve final feedwater quality: TDS <1 mg/L, conductivity <1 μS/cm
165. Deaerate feedwater to remove dissolved oxygen (target <7 ppb O₂)
166. Heat feedwater to 105°C in deaerator vessel
167. Strip oxygen using steam injection in deaerator
168. Add oxygen scavenger (sodium sulfite) at 10× dissolved oxygen concentration
169. Inject filming amine at 5-10 mg/L for condensate system protection
170. Monitor iron and copper corrosion products (<10 ppb each)
171. Test boiler water alkalinity (maintain 200-400 mg/L as CaCO₃)
172. Add phosphate for internal boiler treatment (30-50 mg/L as PO₄)
173. Maintain boiler water pH at 10.5-11.5 for optimal conditions
174. Monitor boiler blowdown to control solids concentration
175. Target boiler water TDS: 2500-3500 mg/L for low-pressure boilers

179. Install redundant pumps (duty/standby configuration) for reliability

## Wastewater Collection and Treatment

185. Collect industrial wastewater from manufacturing processes (300 GPM average)
186. Neutralize acidic or alkaline streams before biological treatment
187. Add caustic or acid to maintain pH 6.5-8.5 in collection system
188. Screen wastewater for debris and large particles (5mm screen)
189. Measure wastewater characteristics: BOD 200-400 mg/L, COD 400-800 mg/L
190. Treat in activated sludge biological reactor with 8-12 hour detention time
191. Maintain mixed liquor suspended solids (MLSS) at 2500-3500 mg/L
192. Aerate at 2.0-2.5 kg O₂ per kg BOD removed
193. Monitor dissolved oxygen in aeration basin (maintain 2-4 mg/L)
194. Settle treated wastewater in clarifier to remove biomass
195. Test final effluent quality: BOD <20 mg/L, suspended solids <30 mg/L, pH 6.5-8.5, discharge to municipal sewer system within permit limits or reuse for non-critical applications
