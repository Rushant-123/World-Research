# Manufacturing Process: High Voltage Ceramic Insulators

**Top Market Leaders:**
- [NGK](/companies/ngk)
- [Lapp Insulator](/companies/lapp-insulator)



## Raw Material Preparation

1. Receive porcelain clay from ceramic slurry supplier in 1-ton super sacks
2. Inspect incoming clay shipment for moisture content using halogen moisture analyzer
3. Verify clay purity meets >98% specification using XRF spectroscopy
4. Store clay in climate-controlled warehouse at 20°C and 50% relative humidity
5. Transport clay bags to raw material preparation area using forklift
6. Load porcelain clay into industrial screw feeder system
7. Receive alumina powder (Al2O3) in sealed containers from alumina substrate supplier
8. Test alumina powder particle size distribution using laser diffraction analyzer
9. Verify alumina purity exceeds 99.5% using ICP-OES analysis
10. Store alumina powder in moisture-proof containers with desiccant packs
11. Weigh porcelain clay using 5000 kg capacity industrial scale (±0.1 kg accuracy)
12. Weigh alumina powder using precision balance for exact formulation ratio
13. Receive kaolin clay additive for plasticity enhancement
14. Test kaolin for iron content to ensure <0.5% for white insulator body
15. Receive feldspar flux material to lower firing temperature
16. Screen feldspar through 200-mesh sieve to remove oversized particles

## Batch Formulation

17. Calculate batch composition: 50% porcelain clay, 25% alumina, 15% kaolin, 10% feldspar
18. Load ingredients into 2000-liter industrial planetary mixer
19. Add deionized water to achieve 35% moisture content for workability
20. Add 0.5% sodium silicate as deflocculant to improve flow properties
21. Mix ingredients at 60 RPM for 45 minutes to achieve homogeneous blend
22. Monitor mixing viscosity using Brookfield viscometer (target: 800-1200 cP)
23. Test batch plasticity using Atterberg limits apparatus
24. Adjust water content if plasticity index falls outside 15-25% range
25. Transfer mixed batch to aging tank for 48-hour rest period
26. Cover aging tank to prevent moisture evaporation and surface drying
27. Periodically remix batch during aging to maintain consistency
28. Test aged batch for workability using compression molding test sample

## Slip Casting Preparation

29. Pump aged ceramic batch into slip preparation vessel
30. Add additional water to reduce viscosity to 300-500 cP for casting
31. Add 0.2% organic binder (methylcellulose) to improve green strength
32. Mix slip in high-shear mixer at 1200 RPM for 30 minutes
33. Remove air bubbles using vacuum de-airing chamber at -0.9 bar for 20 minutes
34. Test slip specific gravity using density cup (target: 1.75-1.80 g/cm³)
35. Measure slip viscosity with flow cup to ensure proper casting characteristics
36. Filter slip through 100-mesh screen to remove any agglomerated particles
37. Prepare plaster molds by cleaning surfaces with compressed air
38. Inspect molds for cracks or defects that could affect casting quality
39. Apply mold release agent (silicone-based) to facilitate demolding
40. Arrange molds on casting line conveyor system
41. Preheat molds to 25°C for consistent water absorption rate
42. Calibrate slip pumping system for accurate fill volume

## Slip Casting Process

43. Pour ceramic slip into plaster molds using automated dosing system
44. Fill molds to slightly above final level to account for settling
45. Allow slip to dwell in mold for 45 minutes while water absorbs into plaster
46. Monitor cast wall thickness development using ultrasonic thickness gauge
47. Achieve target wall thickness of 12-15 mm for standard insulator design
48. Drain excess slip from mold center back into slip reservoir for reuse
49. Allow cast piece to continue drying in mold for additional 30 minutes
50. Observe surface finish for defects like air bubbles or delamination
51. Carefully open plaster mold halves using pneumatic mold separator
52. Extract green ceramic insulator body from mold cavity
53. Inspect cast piece for dimensional accuracy using caliper measurements
54. Check for surface defects, cracks, or warpage that would require rejection
55. Place cast insulators on drying racks with adequate spacing for air circulation
56. Clean plaster molds and prepare for next casting cycle
57. Monitor mold moisture content and rotate molds to ensure consistent absorption

## Green Body Drying

58. Transport green insulators to controlled drying chamber
59. Set initial drying temperature to 40°C with 70% relative humidity
60. Slowly reduce humidity by 5% per hour over 8-hour period
61. Gradually increase temperature to 60°C over next 4 hours
62. Monitor moisture content using moisture meter inserted into test pieces
63. Maintain slow drying rate to prevent thermal shock and cracking
64. Rotate insulator positions on racks every 4 hours for uniform drying
65. Check for surface crazing or edge cracking during drying process
66. Continue drying until moisture content reduces below 2%
67. Increase temperature to 80°C for final drying phase (4 hours)
68. Verify final moisture content using weight loss measurement method
69. Allow dried pieces to equilibrate to room temperature over 2-hour period
70. Inspect dried insulators for shrinkage uniformity and dimensional tolerance
71. Measure key dimensions (height, diameter, shed spacing) with digital calipers
72. Record weight of dried pieces for quality control documentation

## Green Machining and Finishing

73. Mount dried insulator on precision lathe for surface finishing
74. Machine outer profile to exact dimensions using carbide cutting tools
75. Create precise shed geometry with specified overhang and spacing
76. Drill mounting holes for metal end fittings using diamond core drill bits
77. Create threaded sections for hardware attachment using thread-forming tools
78. Smooth all surfaces with fine-grit abrasive sponges (320 grit)
79. Remove machining dust using soft brushes and compressed air
80. Inspect machined features for dimensional accuracy within ±0.5 mm tolerance
81. Check surface finish quality using surface roughness tester (Ra < 3.2 μm)
82. Verify hole alignments and perpendicularity using coordinate measuring machine
83. Mark insulators with production batch number using ceramic ink marker
84. Place finished green bodies on kiln-ready shelves with alumina setters

## Bisque Firing Preparation

85. Load green insulators onto refractory kiln furniture (silicon carbide shelves)
86. Space insulators minimum 50 mm apart to allow gas circulation
87. Support tall insulators with ceramic props to prevent sagging during firing
88. Stack kiln furniture in kiln car maintaining uniform height distribution
89. Verify proper loading pattern for optimal heat distribution
90. Install thermocouples at multiple locations to monitor temperature uniformity
91. Close kiln door and verify seal integrity to prevent heat loss
92. Purge kiln atmosphere with nitrogen to remove moisture
93. Program kiln controller for bisque firing cycle profile

## Bisque Firing Process

94. Begin heating at slow rate of 50°C/hour to 200°C to remove residual moisture
95. Hold temperature at 200°C for 2 hours for complete water evaporation
96. Increase heating rate to 100°C/hour up to 600°C
97. Hold at 600°C for 1 hour to allow organic binder burnout
98. Continue heating at 150°C/hour to reach 950°C bisque firing temperature
99. Monitor kiln atmosphere and adjust ventilation to prevent reducing conditions
100. Hold at 950°C peak temperature for 3 hours for uniform heat soaking
101. Observe pyrometric cones to verify temperature accuracy
102. Begin controlled cooling at 100°C/hour down to 600°C
103. Allow natural cooling below 600°C to prevent thermal shock
104. Monitor cooling rate to avoid crack formation from thermal gradients
105. Wait until kiln temperature drops below 100°C before opening
106. Inspect bisque-fired insulators for cracks, warping, or bloating defects
107. Test bisque strength by attempting to scratch surface with steel tool
108. Measure shrinkage from green to bisque state (typically 6-8%)
109. Record bisque density using water displacement method (target: 2.2-2.4 g/cm³)
110. Sort insulators by quality grade for glazing operations

## Glaze Preparation

111. Receive glazing materials from ceramic coating supplier in powder form
112. Weigh glaze batch components: 40% feldspar, 25% silica, 20% kaolin, 15% zinc oxide
113. Add colorant oxides if required for specific insulator appearance
114. Load dry glaze materials into ball mill with alumina grinding media
115. Add water to achieve 60% solids content by weight
116. Add 0.3% CMC gum as suspension agent to prevent settling
117. Mill glaze mixture for 8 hours to achieve fine particle size (<10 microns)
118. Remove grinding media using vibrating screen separator
119. Test glaze specific gravity (target: 1.55-1.65 g/cm³)
120. Adjust viscosity by adding water or allowing evaporation as needed
121. Measure glaze flow properties using flow cup method
122. Age glaze for 24 hours to allow full hydration of clay components
123. Remix glaze before use to ensure homogeneous suspension
124. Filter glaze through 80-mesh screen to remove any coarse particles

## Glazing Application

125. Clean bisque insulators using compressed air to remove dust particles
126. Pre-warm insulators to 50°C to improve glaze adhesion
127. Dip small insulators completely into glaze bath using tongs
128. Hold inverted for 10 seconds to allow excess glaze to drain
129. For larger insulators, apply glaze using airless spray system
130. Set spray pressure to 40 bar with 1.5 mm nozzle diameter
131. Apply glaze in multiple passes to achieve 0.3-0.5 mm dry thickness
132. Ensure complete coverage of all surfaces including recessed areas
133. Wipe glaze from mounting hole areas using damp sponge
134. Clean metal fitting contact surfaces to bare bisque for proper bonding
135. Inspect glaze coverage for bare spots or excessive thickness
136. Touch up any missed areas using brush application method
137. Allow glazed insulators to air dry for 4 hours before firing
138. Place on refractory setters coated with kiln wash to prevent sticking
139. Support glazed pieces to prevent distortion during high-temperature firing

## High-Temperature Firing Preparation

140. Load glazed insulators onto kiln furniture with increased spacing (75 mm minimum)
141. Use high-purity alumina setters rated for 1400°C operation
142. Install kiln props to support shelves at uniform heights
143. Position insulators to avoid direct flame impingement from burners
144. Install witness cones (Orton cone 10-12) to verify firing temperature
145. Place test rings to monitor firing atmosphere effects
146. Verify kiln burner function and gas pressure settings
147. Check oxygen sensor calibration for atmosphere control
148. Program multi-zone temperature controller for vitrification cycle

## Vitrification Firing Process

149. Begin heating at 100°C/hour to minimize thermal stress
150. Heat to 400°C and hold for 1 hour to complete glaze drying
151. Increase heating rate to 200°C/hour up to 1000°C
152. Slow heating rate to 100°C/hour between 1000-1200°C for quartz inversion
153. Continue heating to peak firing temperature of 1350°C
154. Monitor kiln atmosphere maintaining slightly oxidizing conditions
155. Hold at 1350°C for 4 hours to achieve complete vitrification
156. Verify glaze melting and fusion to bisque body through observation ports
157. Check witness cone deformation to confirm proper heat work
158. Begin controlled cooling at 150°C/hour down to 1000°C
159. Slow cooling rate to 50°C/hour through 1000-800°C range
160. Maintain controlled cooling to 500°C to prevent glaze crazing
161. Allow natural cooling below 500°C (approximately 8 hours)
162. Wait for kiln to cool below 80°C before unloading
163. Remove fired insulators from kiln and inspect for defects
164. Check glaze surface for smoothness, gloss, and color uniformity
165. Inspect for firing defects: pinholes, crawling, or orange peel texture
166. Test glaze adhesion by attempting to chip with metal tool
167. Measure final shrinkage from green to fired state (typically 10-12% total)
168. Verify dimensional accuracy against engineering drawings

## Metal Fitting Preparation

169. Receive forged steel end fittings from steel alloys supplier
170. Inspect metal caps for dimensional accuracy and surface finish
171. Clean metal fittings in alkaline degreasing solution at 70°C
172. Rinse thoroughly with deionized water to remove cleaning residues
173. Apply zinc-rich primer to interior surfaces for corrosion protection
174. Bake primer coating at 150°C for 30 minutes to cure
175. Thread metal caps onto insulator ends for fit verification
176. Prepare zinc-based cement (zinc oxide + sodium silicate) for bonding
177. Mix cement to thick paste consistency for gap-filling capability
178. Apply cement generously to interior of metal cap

## Metal Cap Assembly

179. Heat insulators to 60°C to reduce viscosity of cement during assembly
180. Thread metal cap onto insulator end with rotating motion
181. Apply torque to seat cap firmly against shoulder of insulator body
182. Wipe excess cement squeeze-out with damp cloth
183. Verify alignment of cap using straightness gauge
184. Allow cement to cure at room temperature for 24 hours
185. Apply additional cement to external interface as mechanical reinforcement
186. Heat assembled unit to 80°C for 2 hours to accelerate cement curing
187. Inspect cement bond line for voids or gaps
188. Test mechanical pull strength on sample units (minimum 50 kN required)

## Quality Testing and Inspection

189. Perform visual inspection under bright LED lighting for surface defects
190. Conduct dimensional verification using CMM for critical features
191. Test dry flashover voltage in high-voltage laboratory (minimum 150 kV)
192. Perform wet withstand voltage test by spraying with water during energization
193. Test mechanical cantilever strength by applying specified bending load
194. Verify cement bond integrity using ultrasonic inspection method
195. Package approved insulators in corrugated cardboard boxes with foam cushioning for shipment to utility customers
