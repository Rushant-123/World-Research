# RGB Color Filter Array Manufacturing Process

**Market Leader:** [Toppan Printing](/companies/toppan-printing)



## Phase 1: Substrate Preparation (Steps 1-20)

1. Receive Gen 8.5 glass substrates (2200mm x 2500mm) from Corning Eagle XG facility
2. Inspect incoming glass for defects using automated optical inspection (AOI) system
3. Measure substrate thickness uniformity with laser profilometry (target: 0.5mm ±5µm)
4. Check surface roughness with atomic force microscopy (Ra < 0.3nm required)
5. Load substrates into Class 10 cleanroom environment (ISO 4 specification)
6. Pre-clean substrates with deionized water spray at 60°C
7. Apply alkaline cleaning solution (pH 11.5) for organic contaminant removal
8. Rinse with ultrapure water (18.2 MΩ·cm resistivity) for 90 seconds
9. Perform ultrasonic cleaning at 40 kHz frequency to remove particles
10. Rinse again with cascading ultrapure water system
11. Dry substrates in nitrogen purge oven at 120°C for 10 minutes
12. Inspect for water marks and particles under UV illumination
13. Measure contact angle to verify hydrophilic surface (target: <10°)
14. Apply UV-ozone treatment for 5 minutes to enhance surface energy
15. Load substrates onto precision alignment carrier system
16. Calibrate carrier position with laser interferometry (±1µm accuracy)
17. Register substrate fiducial marks in manufacturing execution system
18. Verify substrate flatness across entire surface (max deviation: 50µm)
19. Transfer substrates to black matrix coating station via automated handler
20. Document substrate lot number and traceability data in MES system

## Phase 2: Black Matrix Formation (Steps 21-45)

21. Load chromium target into sputtering chamber for black matrix deposition
22. Evacuate chamber to base pressure of 5×10⁻⁷ Torr
23. Introduce argon gas at 20 sccm flow rate for plasma generation
24. Strike plasma at 300W RF power and stabilize for 30 seconds
25. Deposit chromium layer at 150nm thickness (rate: 0.5nm/s)
26. Monitor deposition uniformity with in-situ optical monitoring
27. Vent chamber and transfer substrate to photoresist coating area
28. Spin-coat positive photoresist at 1500 rpm for 30 seconds
29. Soft bake photoresist at 90°C for 90 seconds on vacuum hotplate
30. Cool substrate to room temperature on precision chuck
31. Load photomask with black matrix pattern into stepper aligner
32. Align substrate to mask using automated pattern recognition
33. Expose photoresist with i-line UV light (365nm) at 150 mJ/cm²
34. Post-exposure bake at 110°C for 60 seconds to enhance contrast
35. Develop exposed resist in 2.38% TMAH solution for 45 seconds
36. Rinse with deionized water and spin dry at 2000 rpm
37. Inspect developed pattern with optical microscope for defects
38. Hard bake patterned resist at 130°C for 5 minutes
39. Etch chromium in cerium ammonium nitrate solution (acid etchant)
40. Monitor etch endpoint with optical emission spectroscopy
41. Strip remaining photoresist in NMP-based stripper at 60°C
42. Clean substrate with oxygen plasma ashing (300W, 2 minutes)
43. Rinse thoroughly and dry with nitrogen blow-off
44. Inspect black matrix pattern for line width and alignment accuracy
45. Measure optical density of black matrix (target: OD > 3.5)

## Phase 3: Red Color Filter Formation (Steps 46-75)

46. Prepare red pigment dispersion with C.I. Pigment Red 254 and 177
47. Mix pigments with acrylic resin binder in MEK/PGMEA solvent system
48. Disperse pigment particles using high-speed bead mill for 4 hours
49. Filter dispersion through 0.2µm PTFE membrane filter
50. Adjust viscosity to 8 cP at 25°C with additional solvent
51. Measure particle size distribution (target: D50 < 100nm)
52. Check color coordinates with spectrophotometer (CIE 1931 x,y)
53. Spin-coat red color resist at 800 rpm for 40 seconds
54. Pre-bake at 70°C for 2 minutes to evaporate light solvents
55. Soft bake at 100°C for 3 minutes on proximity hotplate
56. Cool substrate gradually to prevent thermal stress
57. Load red filter photomask into i-line stepper system
58. Perform multi-point alignment using substrate fiducial marks
59. Calculate and apply lens distortion compensation
60. Expose red pattern with 200 mJ/cm² dose (365nm wavelength)
61. Step and repeat exposure across entire substrate area
62. Post-exposure bake at 120°C for 90 seconds
63. Develop in 0.4% potassium carbonate solution for 60 seconds
64. Agitate gently to ensure uniform development
65. Rinse with deionized water spray for 45 seconds
66. Inspect red pixel pattern for missing or deformed pixels
67. Cure red color filter at 230°C for 30 minutes in nitrogen atmosphere
68. Ramp temperature at controlled rate (5°C/min) to prevent cracking
69. Cool down slowly to room temperature over 60 minutes
70. Measure red filter thickness with stylus profilometer (target: 1.8µm)
71. Check color purity with transmission spectroscopy (peak: 620nm)
72. Measure luminous transmittance (target: 22-25%)
73. Verify CIE color coordinates match display specification
74. Inspect for pinholes and defects under high magnification
75. Document red filter process parameters and results

## Phase 4: Green Color Filter Formation (Steps 76-105)

76. Prepare green pigment dispersion with C.I. Pigment Green 58 and 36
77. Add yellow pigment (C.I. Pigment Yellow 150) for color tuning
78. Mix with halogenated copper phthalocyanine for brightness
79. Disperse in acrylic binder system using three-roll mill
80. Filter green dispersion through 0.1µm ceramic membrane
81. Adjust refractive index with high-index additives
82. Measure absorption spectrum (peak wavelength: 530nm target)
83. Verify pigment concentration at 25% by weight
84. Spin-coat green color resist at 750 rpm for 40 seconds
85. Pre-bake at 75°C for 2 minutes to level coating
86. Soft bake at 105°C for 3 minutes with controlled humidity
87. Allow substrate to equilibrate at room temperature
88. Load green filter photomask into stepper aligner
89. Align to existing red filter pattern with ±0.5µm accuracy
90. Check overlay alignment marks at multiple locations
91. Expose green pattern with 180 mJ/cm² dose (broadband exposure)
92. Use proximity mode for uniform exposure across large area
93. Post-exposure bake at 115°C for 2 minutes
94. Develop in dilute alkaline developer (0.3% KOH) for 55 seconds
95. Apply developer puddle and spin to enhance uniformity
96. Rinse and inspect for residue or underdevelopment
97. Cure green color filter at 240°C for 35 minutes in N₂
98. Monitor atmosphere for oxygen contamination (< 10 ppm)
99. Measure final green filter thickness (target: 2.0µm)
100. Characterize transmission spectrum with UV-Vis spectrophotometer
101. Verify luminous transmittance at 55-60% (highest of RGB)
102. Check color gamut contribution using colorimeter
103. Inspect alignment accuracy between red and green filters
104. Measure edge roughness and pixel uniformity
105. Record green filter quality metrics in database

## Phase 5: Blue Color Filter Formation (Steps 106-135)

106. Prepare blue pigment dispersion with C.I. Pigment Blue 15:6
107. Add violet pigment for enhanced color saturation
108. Optimize pigment ratio for sRGB color space compliance
109. Disperse using ultrasonic homogenizer at 20 kHz
110. Filter through dual-stage filtration system (0.2µm then 0.1µm)
111. Measure zeta potential to ensure dispersion stability
112. Adjust pH to 7.5 for optimal coating characteristics
113. Add photoinitiator package for UV sensitivity
114. Spin-coat blue color resist at 850 rpm for 35 seconds
115. Pre-bake at 70°C for 2.5 minutes with nitrogen purge
116. Soft bake at 98°C for 3 minutes on vacuum hotplate
117. Cool to room temperature in controlled environment
118. Load blue filter photomask (final RGB layer)
119. Perform global alignment using all available fiducial marks
120. Calculate best-fit alignment transformation matrix
121. Apply die-by-die alignment correction if needed
122. Expose blue pattern with 220 mJ/cm² dose (i-line)
123. Use multiple exposure passes for enhanced resolution
124. Post-exposure bake at 118°C for 2 minutes
125. Develop in 0.35% TMAH solution for 50 seconds
126. Use megasonic-assisted development for clean pixel edges
127. Rinse with ultrapure water and dry with IPA vapor
128. Inspect complete RGB pattern for alignment accuracy
129. Cure blue color filter at 235°C for 35 minutes
130. Maintain nitrogen atmosphere throughout thermal cure
131. Measure blue filter thickness (target: 1.6µm)
132. Characterize blue transmission spectrum (peak: 460nm)
133. Verify luminous transmittance at 18-20%
134. Check RGB pixel arrangement and spacing uniformity
135. Perform final defect inspection of complete color filter array

## Phase 8: Overcoat Layer Application (Steps 171-185)

172. Filter overcoat solution through 0.45µm filter

174. Spin-coat overcoat layer at 1200 rpm for 30 seconds
175. Pre-bake at 80°C for 3 minutes to remove solvents
176. Cure overcoat at 200°C for 20 minutes in nitrogen
177. Measure overcoat thickness uniformity across substrate

183. Inspect for coating defects or contamination

185. Prepare surface for ITO common electrode deposition

## Phase 9: ITO Common Electrode Deposition (Steps 186-195)

186. Load substrate into ITO sputtering chamber
187. Evacuate to base pressure < 1×10⁻⁶ Torr
188. Introduce argon/oxygen gas mixture (98:2 ratio)
189. Heat substrate to 150°C for densified ITO film
190. Sputter deposit ITO at 100nm thickness (sheet resistance: 100 Ω/sq)
191. Monitor optical transmission during deposition (target: > 85%)
192. Cool substrate and measure ITO conductivity
193. Pattern ITO if required for common electrode structure
194. Perform final inspection and electrical testing
195. Package completed color filter substrate for LCD cell assembly
