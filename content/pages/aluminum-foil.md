# Aluminum Foil Manufacturing Process

**Top Market Leaders:**

## Overview
Aluminum foil production involves rolling aluminum ingots through multiple passes to achieve ultra-thin gauges of 7-200 micrometers, suitable for cable shielding and battery current collectors. The process requires precise control of temperature, tension, and surface quality.

## Hot Rolling Stage

### 1. Ingot Preheating Preparation
Aluminum ingots are transported from storage to the preheating furnace staging area using overhead cranes with specialized lifting fixtures.

### 2. Surface Inspection Pre-Heat
Each ingot undergoes visual and automated optical inspection to detect surface defects, cracks, or contamination before heating.

### 3. Ingot Dimension Verification
Measure ingot dimensions using laser scanning systems to ensure they meet specifications for width, thickness, and length before processing.

### 4. Furnace Loading Sequence
Ingots are loaded into the preheating furnace through automated charging doors, maintaining furnace atmosphere control.

### 5. Preheating Temperature Ramp
Gradually increase ingot temperature from ambient to 450-500°C over 4-6 hours to ensure uniform heat distribution and prevent thermal shock.

### 6. Soak Time Control
Hold ingots at target temperature for 2-4 hours to achieve complete thermal equilibrium throughout the material cross-section.

### 7. Temperature Uniformity Monitoring
Use multiple thermocouples positioned at various furnace zones to verify temperature consistency within ±5°C across all ingots.

### 8. Furnace Atmosphere Management
Maintain protective atmosphere using nitrogen or forming gas to prevent oxidation during the preheating cycle.

### 9. Hot Ingot Extraction
Remove preheated ingots using heat-resistant extraction tongs and transfer quickly to the hot rolling mill to minimize heat loss.

### 10. Descaling Operation
Pass ingots through high-pressure water descaling jets to remove surface oxide layers formed during preheating.

### 11. Hot Mill Entry Threading
Position the hot ingot at the entry of the reversing hot mill, aligning it precisely with the roll gap centerline.

### 12. First Hot Rolling Pass
Execute initial reduction pass with 20-30% thickness reduction, transforming the ingot from 500mm to approximately 350mm thickness.

### 13. Roll Gap Adjustment Pass 2
Adjust roll gap hydraulically and perform second pass with additional 25% reduction to approximately 260mm thickness.

### 14. Temperature Monitoring Between Passes
Use infrared pyrometers to monitor slab temperature after each pass, maintaining optimal range of 400-480°C for aluminum alloy workability.

### 15. Third Hot Rolling Pass
Continue reduction with third pass bringing thickness to approximately 190mm while maintaining edge quality.

### 16. Edge Trimming Hot Stage
Trim irregular edges using hot edge trimming shears to ensure uniform width throughout subsequent passes.

### 17. Fourth Hot Rolling Pass
Further reduce thickness to approximately 140mm, monitoring roll force and ensuring consistent bite angle.

### 18. Roll Cooling System Check
Verify operation of internal roll cooling channels, maintaining roll surface temperature at optimal range for heat transfer.

### 19. Fifth Hot Rolling Pass
Reduce to approximately 105mm thickness, checking for surface defects and adjusting rolling parameters as needed.

### 20. Intermediate Slab Inspection
Inspect slab surface for cracks, edge defects, or surface irregularities using automated camera systems.

### 21. Sixth Hot Rolling Pass
Continue reduction to approximately 80mm, maintaining consistent reduction ratio across the width.

### 22. Roll Cambering Adjustment
Adjust roll cambering (crown) to compensate for roll deflection and ensure uniform thickness across slab width.

### 23. Seventh Hot Rolling Pass
Reduce to approximately 60mm thickness while monitoring strip flatness parameters.

### 24. Eighth Hot Rolling Pass
Further reduce to approximately 45mm, entering the range where surface quality becomes increasingly critical.

### 25. Ninth Hot Rolling Pass
Reduce to approximately 34mm thickness, carefully controlling temperature to maintain optimal microstructure.

### 26. Tenth Hot Rolling Pass
Bring thickness to approximately 26mm, approaching the transition point to cold rolling operations.

### 27. Final Hot Rolling Pass
Execute final hot reduction to approximately 20mm thickness, achieving target hot band gauge.

### 28. Hot Band Coiling Temperature
Monitor coiling temperature at 300-350°C for optimal metallurgical properties using multiple pyrometer stations.

### 29. Coiling Tension Control
Apply controlled coiling tension of 2-4 MPa to create uniformly wound hot band coils without telescoping.

### 30. Hot Coil Cooling Protocol
Allow hot coils to cool naturally in controlled cooling area with protective atmosphere to prevent oxidation.

## Cold Rolling Stage - First Pass Series

### 31. Hot Band Uncoiling
Mount hot band coil on uncoiler mandrel and perform initial threading through entry guides with tension sensors.

### 32. Cold Rolling Mill Entry Setup
Thread aluminum strip through cold rolling mill entry, establishing proper strip tracking and alignment.

### 33. Rolling Oil Application System
Activate oil application nozzles to apply rolling oil emulsion at controlled flow rate and concentration for lubrication.

### 34. First Cold Pass Reduction
Execute first cold rolling pass with 30-40% reduction, reducing from 20mm to approximately 13mm thickness.

### 35. Strip Tension Monitoring Pass 1
Monitor entry and exit tension using load cells, maintaining proper stress distribution to prevent strip breaks.

### 36. Thickness Measurement Pass 1
Use X-ray or isotope thickness gauges to measure strip thickness profile across width after first pass.

### 37. Second Cold Pass Reduction
Perform second cold pass reducing thickness to approximately 8.5mm with continued work hardening of material.

### 38. Crown Control Adjustment
Adjust work roll bending and intermediate roll shifting to control strip crown and maintain thickness uniformity.

### 39. Third Cold Pass Reduction
Execute third pass reducing to approximately 5.5mm, monitoring strip temperature rise from deformation energy.

### 40. Strip Cooling Enhancement
Increase cooling capacity using roll cooling and strip spray cooling to manage temperature rise during heavy reduction.

### 41. Fourth Cold Pass Reduction
Continue reduction to approximately 3.6mm thickness, entering the range requiring increased roll force.

### 42. Roll Force Monitoring
Monitor hydraulic roll force continuously, ensuring it remains within mill capacity and optimal rolling window.

### 43. Fifth Cold Pass Reduction
Reduce to approximately 2.3mm thickness with enhanced oil film control for surface quality.

### 44. Edge Crack Detection
Inspect strip edges using edge cameras to detect any edge cracking requiring edge trimming.

### 45. Sixth Cold Pass Reduction
Continue to approximately 1.5mm thickness, approaching the transition to intermediate annealing requirement.

### 46. Work Hardening Assessment
Evaluate degree of work hardening through hardness measurements or inline tension response monitoring.

### 47. Seventh Cold Pass Reduction
Final pass before annealing, reducing to approximately 1.0mm thickness with maximum work hardening condition.

### 48. Coiling for Intermediate Anneal
Coil the work-hardened strip with controlled tension for transport to intermediate annealing furnace.

## Intermediate Annealing Process

### 49. Coil Transport to Anneal
Transfer coils to annealing furnace using coil cars or overhead cranes with proper coil identification tracking.

### 50. Furnace Charging Sequence
Load coils into batch annealing furnace or continuous annealing line with proper spacing for heat circulation.

### 51. Atmosphere Purging
Purge furnace with nitrogen or hydrogen-nitrogen mixture to establish protective atmosphere and remove oxygen.

### 52. Heating Rate Control
Ramp furnace temperature at controlled rate of 50-100°C per hour to prevent thermal gradients and distortion.

### 53. Annealing Temperature Target
Achieve annealing temperature of 300-380°C depending on alloy composition and target mechanical properties.

### 54. Soak Time for Recrystallization
Maintain annealing temperature for 2-6 hours to allow complete recrystallization and stress relief.

### 55. Controlled Cooling Phase
Cool coils at controlled rate to prevent grain growth and achieve target grain size for subsequent rolling.

### 56. Furnace Discharge
Remove annealed coils from furnace once temperature drops below 150°C to prevent oxidation in air.

### 57. Coil Cooling to Ambient
Allow coils to cool to near-ambient temperature in controlled atmosphere storage before further processing.

### 58. Post-Anneal Inspection
Inspect coils for color consistency, indicating proper annealing, and check for surface defects.

## Cold Rolling Stage - Second Pass Series

### 59. Annealed Coil Mounting
Mount annealed coil on cold mill uncoiler and thread through entry section with renewed reduction capability.

### 60. First Post-Anneal Pass
Execute first pass after annealing with 35-45% reduction, taking advantage of restored ductility.

### 61. Reducing from 1.0mm to 0.60mm
Achieve approximately 0.60mm thickness through controlled reduction with optimized roll gap setting.

### 62. Surface Quality Monitoring
Monitor strip surface continuously using high-resolution cameras and lighting systems to detect defects.

### 63. Second Post-Anneal Pass
Continue reduction to approximately 0.38mm thickness with maintained surface quality.

### 64. Oil Film Thickness Control
Adjust rolling oil viscosity, flow rate, and concentration to maintain optimal lubrication film.

### 65. Third Post-Anneal Pass
Reduce to approximately 0.24mm thickness, entering the foil gauge range threshold.

### 66. Strip Flatness Control
Monitor and control strip flatness using shape meter, adjusting roll bending to minimize waviness.

### 67. Fourth Post-Anneal Pass
Continue to approximately 0.15mm thickness with increased attention to strip tracking stability.

### 68. Fifth Post-Anneal Pass
Reduce to approximately 0.095mm thickness, approaching thin foil gauge range.

### 69. Tension Profile Optimization
Optimize tension distribution between stands to prevent strip breaks and maintain dimensional control.

### 70. Sixth Post-Anneal Pass
Achieve approximately 0.060mm thickness suitable for some cable shielding applications.

## Foil Rolling Operations

### 71. Foil Mill Transfer
Transfer coil to specialized foil rolling mill designed for ultra-thin gauge rolling with enhanced control systems.

### 72. Foil Mill Threading
Carefully thread strip through foil mill with reduced threading speed to prevent tearing of thin material.

### 73. Double Rolling Setup
Prepare for double rolling (rolling two layers together) to achieve gauges below 0.025mm if required.

### 74. First Foil Pass
Execute first foil mill pass reducing to approximately 0.040mm with specialized foil rolling oil.

### 75. Tension Sensitivity Management
Manage extremely sensitive tension control as strip strength decreases with thickness reduction.

### 76. Second Foil Pass
Continue reduction to approximately 0.026mm thickness with enhanced strip tracking systems.

### 77. Surface Finish Specification
Control work roll surface finish (roughness) to achieve specified foil surface characteristics.

### 78. Third Foil Pass
Reduce to approximately 0.017mm for applications requiring intermediate foil thickness.

### 79. Roll Surface Maintenance
Monitor work roll surface condition continuously, scheduling roll changes based on surface quality requirements.

### 80. Fourth Foil Pass
Continue to approximately 0.011mm thickness with maximum precision control systems engaged.

### 81. Gauge Variation Control
Minimize gauge variation to ±0.5 micrometers using advanced automatic gauge control systems.

### 82. Fifth Foil Pass for Ultra-Thin
Achieve final gauge of 0.007-0.009mm (7-9 micrometers) for battery current collector applications.

### 83. Strip Break Prevention
Implement multiple break detection and prevention systems including cameras and tension monitoring.

### 84. Final Foil Gauge Achievement
Complete rolling to target thickness ranging from 7μm for battery applications to 200μm for heavy cable shielding.

## Surface Treatment Process

### 85. Foil Degreasing Preparation
Prepare degreasing solution using alkaline cleaners or solvent systems for rolling oil removal.

### 86. Degreasing Line Threading
Thread foil through continuous degreasing line with controlled web handling to prevent damage.

### 87. Hot Alkaline Cleaning Stage
Pass foil through hot alkaline cleaning solution at 60-80°C to saponify and remove rolling oils.

### 88. High-Pressure Rinse 1
Apply high-pressure water rinse to remove alkaline cleaner and emulsified oil residues.

### 89. Acid Desmut Treatment
Treat surface with dilute nitric acid or sulfuric acid solution to remove smut and residual alkaline salts.

### 90. High-Pressure Rinse 2
Apply second high-pressure rinse to remove acid treatment chemicals completely.

### 91. Deionized Water Rinse
Final rinse using deionized water to ensure no ionic contamination remains on foil surface.

### 92. Air Knife Drying
Remove surface water using precision air knives with controlled air pressure and angle.

### 93. Hot Air Drying Chamber
Pass foil through hot air drying chamber at 100-150°C to evaporate remaining moisture.

### 94. Surface Cleanliness Verification
Test surface cleanliness using water break test or contact angle measurement to verify complete oil removal.

### 95. Chemical Conversion Coating (Optional)
Apply chromate or non-chromate conversion coating for enhanced corrosion resistance if specified.

### 96. Coating Rinse and Dry
Rinse and dry conversion coated foil using controlled process to achieve uniform coating appearance.

### 97. Carbon Coating for Battery Foils
Apply carbon coating layer using slot-die or gravure coating for battery current collector applications.

### 98. Carbon Coating Thickness Control
Control carbon coating thickness to 1-3 micrometers using precise metering systems.

### 99. Carbon Coating Drying
Dry carbon coating using infrared or hot air dryers with controlled temperature profile.

### 100. Coating Adhesion Testing
Perform periodic peel tests to verify carbon coating adhesion strength meets specifications.

## Tension Control and Strip Handling

### 102. Bridle Roll Configuration

### 103. Dancer Roll Positioning

### 104. Tension Zone Balancing

### 105. Load Cell Calibration

### 107. Web Spread Control

### 108. Anti-Static Treatment

### 109. Rewind Tension Programming

### 110. Core Preparation

### 111. Core Mounting Alignment

### 112. Initial Wrap Adhesion

### 113. Tension Taper Implementation

### 114. Coil Building Monitoring

### 115. Splice Detection Flagging
Mark or flag any splices in the coil for customer notification and downstream process awareness.

Select and install slitting knives (shear, razor, or crush) based on foil thickness and edge quality requirements.

### 119. Knife Sharpness Verification

### 120. Knife Spacing Setup

### 121. Unwind Tension Setting for Slitting

### 124. Production Slitting Run

## Conclusion
