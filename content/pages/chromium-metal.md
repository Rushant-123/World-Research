# Chromium Metal Production Supply Chain

**Market Leader:** [Glencore Alloys](/companies/glencore-alloys)



## Overview
Chromium metal production begins with chromite ore extraction and proceeds through ferrochrome production in electric arc furnaces, followed by aluminothermic reduction to produce high-purity chromium metal for specialized applications.

## Key Technical Parameters

- **Chromite Ore Grade:** 40-50% Cr2O3
- **Ferrochrome Composition:** 60-70% Cr, 4-8% C (HC-FeCr)
- **Smelting Temperature:** 1600-1800°C
- **Aluminothermic Reaction Temperature:** >2500°C
- **Pure Chromium Purity:** 99-99.9%
- **Energy Consumption (FeCr):** 3.5-4.5 MWh per ton
- **Energy Consumption (Pure Cr):** 15-20 MWh per ton
- **Yield (Ore to FeCr):** 70-75%

## Environmental and Health Considerations

Chromium processing must carefully manage hexavalent chromium (Cr6+) compounds, which are toxic and carcinogenic. Modern ferrochrome plants implement:
- Enclosed handling systems to minimize dust exposure
- Bag house filtration for particulate capture
- Waste water treatment to remove soluble chromium
- Conversion of Cr6+ to less toxic Cr3+ forms
- Worker protection through PPE and monitoring
- Slag utilization in construction materials

Ferrochrome slag, rich in magnesia and alumina, finds applications in refractory manufacturing and road construction, reducing landfill requirements. Chromium recycling from stainless steel scrap is highly efficient, with over 80% of chromium in end-of-life products recovered and reused.
