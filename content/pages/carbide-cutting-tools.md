# Carbide Cutting Tools Manufacturing Process

**Market Leader:** [Sandvik Coromant](/companies/sandvik-coromant)



## Raw Material Preparation and Quality Control

1. Receive tungsten carbide powder shipment in sealed moisture-proof containers
2. Verify WC powder particle size distribution using laser diffraction analyzer
3. Check WC powder purity certification showing minimum 99.5% tungsten carbide content
4. Measure WC powder Fisher sub-sieve size (FSSS) targeting 0.8-1.5 microns
5. Store WC powder in climate-controlled warehouse at 20°C and 40% relative humidity
6. Receive cobalt binder powder in nitrogen-purged packaging
7. Verify cobalt powder purity minimum 99.8% with trace element analysis
8. Measure cobalt powder particle size distribution targeting 1-2 microns
9. Check cobalt powder oxygen content below 0.3% to prevent oxidation
10. Store cobalt powder separately in inert atmosphere storage system
11. Receive carbide grain growth inhibitors (VC, Cr3C2) in small batches
12. Verify grain growth inhibitor purity and particle size specifications
13. Prepare clean mixing equipment using ultrasonic cleaning with acetone
14. Calibrate precision weighing scales accurate to 0.01 grams
15. Dry all mixing vessels in vacuum oven at 120°C for 2 hours

## Powder Mixing and Homogenization

16. Calculate precise powder mixture composition: 88-94% WC, 6-12% Co binder
17. Add grain growth inhibitors at 0.3-0.8% by weight for fine-grain grades
18. Weigh tungsten carbide powder using calibrated analytical balance
19. Weigh cobalt binder powder according to specified grade composition
20. Add organic binder (paraffin wax or PEG) at 1-2% for pressing aid
21. Add plasticizer to improve powder flow characteristics during pressing
22. Transfer all powders into attritor mill lined with tungsten carbide media
23. Add ethanol or isopropanol as milling liquid medium
24. Seal attritor mill and purge with argon gas to prevent oxidation
25. Start attritor mill at 400 RPM for initial mixing phase
26. Mill powder mixture for 24-48 hours for complete homogenization
27. Monitor milling temperature maintaining below 40°C with cooling water
28. Sample powder periodically to check homogeneity using SEM analysis
29. Check particle size distribution every 8 hours during milling
30. Verify cobalt coating uniformity on WC particles through microscopy
31. Stop milling when Fisher sub-sieve size reaches target specification
32. Drain powder slurry into stainless steel collection trays
33. Dry powder mixture in spray dryer at 80-120°C with nitrogen atmosphere
34. Collect dried powder in sealed containers preventing moisture absorption
35. Screen dried powder through 100-mesh sieve to remove agglomerates
36. Measure powder flow rate using Hall flowmeter funnel
37. Check powder apparent density and tap density for pressing characteristics
38. Verify powder moisture content below 0.1% using moisture analyzer
39. Store mixed powder in vacuum-sealed bags with desiccant packets
40. Label powder batches with composition, date, and lot number

## Green Body Pressing Operations

41. Select appropriate pressing dies matching final tool insert geometry
42. Machine die cavities from hardened tool steel with precision CNC grinding
43. Polish die surfaces to Ra 0.2 micron surface finish
44. Apply die wall lubricant (stearic acid or zinc stearate) uniformly
45. Preheat pressing dies to 60-80°C for better powder flow
46. Calibrate hydraulic press force measurement system
47. Set press parameters: 100-200 MPa pressing pressure for standard grades
48. Program multi-stage pressing cycle with controlled pressure ramp rates
49. Fill die cavity with precisely measured powder charge
50. Vibrate die to achieve uniform powder distribution and packing
51. Apply initial low pressure (10-20 MPa) to consolidate powder
52. Hold at low pressure for 5-10 seconds allowing air escape
53. Ramp pressure to intermediate level (50-80 MPa) at controlled rate
54. Hold at intermediate pressure for 15-20 seconds for densification
55. Increase to final pressing pressure (150-200 MPa) gradually
56. Hold at maximum pressure for 30-60 seconds for full compaction
57. Release pressure gradually to prevent lamination defects
58. Eject green compact from die using controlled ejection force
59. Handle green compacts carefully to prevent edge chipping or cracking
60. Inspect green body dimensions using digital calipers and gauges
61. Measure green density using geometric method or Archimedes principle
62. Check green body for visible defects: cracks, laminations, density variations
63. Verify green strength by carefully handling without breakage
64. Apply protective coating to green compacts if extended storage required
65. Place green bodies on sintering trays with appropriate spacing
66. Document pressing parameters and green body measurements for each batch

## Pre-Sintering Preparation

67. Clean sintering furnace chamber removing any residual powder or debris
68. Inspect furnace heating elements and thermocouples for proper function
69. Calibrate furnace temperature measurement system with standard references
70. Check furnace atmosphere control system for leaks and proper flow
71. Load green compacts onto graphite or ceramic sintering trays
72. Space parts with minimum 5mm separation to prevent sticking
73. Support complex shapes with ceramic fixtures to prevent distortion
74. Place thermocouples near parts to monitor actual sintering temperature
75. Calculate dewaxing cycle time based on organic binder content
76. Program furnace controller with complete thermal cycle profile
77. Set vacuum system parameters for atmosphere control during sintering
78. Prepare hydrogen gas supply with purity verification minimum 99.99%
79. Connect cooling water circulation system to furnace jackets
80. Verify all furnace safety interlocks and emergency shutdown systems

## Dewaxing and Pre-Sintering Phase

81. Close and seal furnace chamber ensuring gas-tight operation
82. Evacuate furnace chamber to 0.1 mbar removing air and moisture
83. Begin controlled heating at slow rate 50-100°C per hour
84. Maintain temperature at 200-300°C for initial binder softening
85. Hold at dewaxing temperature for 2-4 hours allowing binder vaporization
86. Flow hydrogen gas through furnace at low rate during dewaxing
87. Monitor exhaust for organic vapor removal completion
88. Continue heating at 100-150°C per hour to 600-800°C
89. Hold at 600-800°C for 1-2 hours for complete binder removal
90. Verify carbon residue from organic binder is fully volatilized
91. Check furnace atmosphere composition ensuring reducing conditions
92. Observe parts for any distortion or slumping during pre-sinter phase
93. Continue heating toward sintering temperature at controlled rate
94. Maintain hydrogen partial pressure to prevent oxidation of metals
95. Monitor and control furnace pressure between 100-300 mbar

## High-Temperature Sintering Process

96. Increase temperature to sintering range 1350-1500°C depending on grade
97. Ramp heating rate at 200-300°C per hour in sintering temperature range
98. Reach target sintering temperature with precision +/- 10°C tolerance
99. Hold at peak sintering temperature for 30-90 minutes for densification
100. Monitor furnace atmosphere maintaining strong reducing conditions
101. Observe pyrometer readings verifying uniform temperature distribution
102. Allow liquid phase sintering to occur with cobalt binder melting at 1320°C
103. Enable WC grain growth inhibitors to control final grain size 0.5-1.5 microns
104. Achieve full densification targeting 99.5-99.8% of theoretical density
105. Control carbon potential in atmosphere preventing eta-phase formation
106. Prevent excessive grain growth maintaining fine-grain structure
107. Ensure complete pore elimination through liquid phase sintering mechanism
108. Monitor shrinkage occurring approximately 16-20% linear dimension change
109. Maintain isothermal conditions at peak temperature across all parts
110. Check for uniform densification using furnace temperature mapping data

## Controlled Cooling and Furnace Unloading

111. Begin controlled cooling at rate 200-300°C per hour initially
112. Slow cooling rate below 800°C to 50-100°C per hour
113. Maintain hydrogen atmosphere throughout entire cooling cycle
114. Prevent thermal shock by avoiding rapid temperature changes
115. Control cooling through cobalt phase transformation temperatures
116. Allow parts to cool to below 200°C before furnace opening
117. Purge furnace with argon or nitrogen before introducing air
118. Open furnace chamber carefully releasing residual atmosphere safely
119. Allow parts to equilibrate to room temperature gradually
120. Remove sintered inserts from trays using careful handling
121. Inspect sintered parts for distortion, cracks, or surface defects
122. Measure sintered dimensions verifying expected shrinkage factors
123. Weigh sintered parts checking for proper density achievement
124. Document sintering cycle data including all temperature measurements
125. Clean sintered inserts removing any tray residue or surface contamination

## Post-Sinter Grinding and Shaping

126. Mount sintered blanks on precision grinding fixtures with wax or magnetic chuck
127. Set up diamond grinding wheel on CNC grinding machine
128. Select diamond grit size based on surface finish requirement: 120-600 grit
129. Program grinding paths for top and bottom face parallel grinding
130. Grind top surface removing 0.1-0.3mm to achieve flatness within 5 microns
131. Flip and re-mount insert to grind opposite face to exact thickness tolerance
132. Measure thickness with precision comparator accurate to 1 micron
133. Grind peripheral surfaces establishing final insert shape and dimensions
134. Program CNC grinding cycles for complex insert geometries and chip breakers
135. Use formed grinding wheels to create chip breaker grooves and geometries
136. Maintain grinding coolant flow to prevent thermal damage to carbide
137. Monitor grinding forces preventing excessive pressure causing cracks
138. Measure ground dimensions using CMM or optical comparator systems
139. Verify perpendicularity and parallelism within 5 micron tolerance
140. Inspect ground surfaces for grinding marks, cracks, or subsurface damage
141. Clean ground inserts ultrasonically removing grinding debris and coolant

## Cutting Edge Preparation

142. Set up edge preparation equipment with specific brushing or grinding tools
143. Select edge preparation method: honing, brushing, or micro-blasting
144. Program edge rounding radius typically 10-30 microns for toughness
145. Apply controlled edge honing to all cutting edges uniformly
146. Use diamond-charged brushes for consistent edge preparation
147. Measure edge radius using optical microscope at 200-500X magnification
148. Verify edge quality free from micro-chipping or irregularities
149. Inspect edge preparation uniformity around entire cutting edge
150. Clean inserts thoroughly removing any edge preparation media residue
151. Dry inserts completely in warm air or nitrogen blow-off system

## Surface Preparation for Coating

152. Inspect pre-coating surface quality using optical and electron microscopy
153. Measure surface roughness targeting Ra 0.1-0.3 microns for coating adhesion
154. Clean inserts in ultrasonic bath with alkaline cleaning solution
155. Rinse inserts in deionized water removing all cleaning agent residue
156. Dry inserts in vacuum oven at 120°C eliminating all moisture
157. Perform plasma etching to activate surface and improve coating adhesion
158. Use argon ion bombardment to clean surface at atomic level
159. Check surface cleanliness using water break test or contact angle measurement
160. Handle cleaned inserts only with powder-free gloves preventing contamination
161. Load inserts into coating fixture with optimal spacing and orientation
162. Mount fixture on rotating carousel inside PVD coating chamber

## PVD Coating Application

163. Close and seal PVD coating chamber ensuring vacuum integrity
164. Evacuate chamber to high vacuum level below 0.001 mbar
165. Perform leak check verifying no significant vacuum leaks present
166. Heat substrates to coating temperature 450-550°C for TiAlN coating
167. Allow temperature equilibration across all parts for 30-60 minutes
168. Initiate argon gas flow for sputter cleaning phase
169. Apply negative bias voltage -200 to -400V to substrate fixture
170. Sputter clean substrate surfaces for 10-30 minutes removing any oxide
171. Adjust coating parameters for TiAlN or AlCrN coating composition
172. Ignite titanium, aluminum targets with nitrogen reactive gas for TiAlN
173. Or ignite aluminum, chromium targets with nitrogen for AlCrN coating
174. Control reactive gas flow maintaining optimal coating stoichiometry
175. Apply substrate bias -50 to -150V during coating for proper film density
176. Rotate substrate fixture ensuring uniform coating coverage on all surfaces
177. Monitor coating rate using quartz crystal microbalance sensor
178. Deposit coating to target thickness 2-5 microns over 60-180 minutes
179. Apply multilayer architecture if specified: TiN/TiCN/TiAlN structure
180. Deposit final top layer optimized for wear resistance and low friction
181. Reduce temperature gradually after coating deposition completion
182. Cool coated inserts to below 150°C before venting chamber
183. Vent chamber slowly with dry nitrogen preventing thermal shock

## Post-Coating Inspection and Quality Control

184. Unload coated inserts from fixture handling carefully to avoid damage
185. Inspect coating appearance for uniform color and finish
186. Measure coating thickness using ball crater grinding or XRF analysis
187. Verify coating thickness uniformity within +/- 10% across insert surfaces
188. Check coating adhesion using scratch test with progressive loading
189. Perform Rockwell indentation test evaluating coating delamination resistance
190. Measure coating hardness using nanoindentation typically 2500-3500 HV
191. Analyze coating composition and stoichiometry using EDX or XPS
192. Inspect coated edges under microscope for uniform edge coverage
193. Verify coating roughness similar to substrate roughness for good finish
194. Clean coated inserts removing any coating chamber particulate contamination
195. Package finished carbide cutting tools in protective containers with part identification and quality certification documentation for shipment to CNC machining customers
