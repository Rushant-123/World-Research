# ITO Coated Glass Manufacturing Process

**Market Leader:** [Corning](/companies/corning)


## Raw Material Preparation and Quality Control

1. Receive glass substrate shipment from cover glass manufacturer
2. Verify glass dimensions meet specification (typically 300×400 mm sheets)
3. Check glass thickness uniformity using micrometer (target: 0.5-1.1 mm ±0.05 mm)
4. Inspect for surface defects using optical microscopy at 50× magnification
5. Measure surface roughness using atomic force microscopy (target Ra < 1 nm)
6. Verify glass composition meets soda-lime or aluminosilicate specifications
7. Check thermal expansion coefficient (target: 7-9 × 10⁻⁶/K)
8. Test optical transmittance in visible range (400-700 nm, target >90%)
9. Receive ITO sputtering target from supplier (In₂O₃:SnO₂ 90:10 wt%)
10. Verify ITO target dimensions (typically 500×125 mm planar or 75 mm diameter cylindrical)
11. Measure ITO target density (target: >98% theoretical density, 7.1 g/cm³)
12. Check tin oxide doping concentration using X-ray fluorescence (target: 10 wt%)
13. Inspect target surface for cracks, chips, or contamination
14. Verify target purity specification (99.99% combined oxide purity)
15. Store ITO targets in controlled humidity environment (<30% RH)
16. Calibrate mass flow controllers for process gases
17. Verify argon gas purity (99.999% minimum)
18. Check oxygen gas purity (99.995% minimum)
19. Inspect sputtering chamber for cleanliness and prior contamination
20. Replace target shielding and ensure proper grounding

## Substrate Pre-cleaning and Surface Preparation

21. Remove glass substrates from packaging in Class 1000 cleanroom
22. Perform visual inspection under high-intensity LED lighting
23. Load substrates into ultrasonic cleaning tank with alkaline detergent solution
24. Clean at 40 kHz frequency for 10 minutes at 50°C
25. Rinse substrates in deionized water cascade (resistivity >18 MΩ·cm)
26. Transfer to second ultrasonic bath with isopropyl alcohol
27. Clean in IPA for 5 minutes to remove organic residues
28. Rinse again in flowing deionized water for 3 minutes
29. Perform final ultrasonic cleaning in deionized water for 5 minutes
30. Blow-dry substrates using filtered nitrogen gas (0.1 μm particle filter)
31. Inspect dried substrates under UV light for residual contamination
32. Measure contact angle to verify hydrophilic surface (target <10°)
33. Load substrates into plasma cleaning chamber
34. Evacuate chamber to 10⁻² Torr base pressure
35. Introduce oxygen plasma at 100 W RF power
36. Treat substrate surface for 3 minutes to remove final organic contaminants
37. Vent chamber with nitrogen and transfer substrates immediately to sputtering system
38. Handle substrates only with vacuum tweezers or clean gloves
39. Record cleaning batch number and timestamp
40. Document any rejected substrates and defect types

## Sputtering System Setup and Conditioning

41. Open sputtering chamber and install cleaned substrates on carrier
42. Position substrates on temperature-controlled holder with mechanical clamps
43. Ensure uniform spacing between substrate and target (typically 60-80 mm)
44. Close chamber and initiate vacuum pump-down sequence
45. Activate turbomolecular pump after reaching 10⁻² Torr with roughing pump
46. Monitor pressure reduction to base pressure of 5×10⁻⁶ Torr
47. Allow 2-hour pump-down to ensure thorough outgassing
48. Perform leak check using helium mass spectrometer (leak rate <10⁻⁸ Torr·L/s)
49. Heat substrate holder to process temperature (150-250°C)
50. Allow 30 minutes for thermal stabilization and temperature uniformity
51. Verify substrate temperature using infrared pyrometer
52. Install process gas lines to chamber feedthroughs
53. Purge gas lines with 3 vacuum/refill cycles
54. Set argon flow rate to 40 sccm using calibrated mass flow controller
55. Set oxygen flow rate to 1.5 sccm for reactive sputtering
56. Establish process pressure of 3 mTorr with throttle valve control
57. Allow gas flow stabilization for 10 minutes
58. Configure DC or RF power supply for magnetron sputtering
59. Set initial power density to 2-3 W/cm² target area
60. Install target shutter in closed position to protect substrate during conditioning

## Target Conditioning and Pre-sputtering

61. Ignite argon plasma at reduced power (50% of process power)
62. Observe plasma color and uniformity across target surface
63. Gradually increase power to full process level (300-500 W)
64. Monitor target voltage and current to verify stable discharge
65. Pre-sputter target for 15 minutes with shutter closed
66. Remove surface contamination and establish stable erosion profile
67. Monitor deposition rate on witness sample next to shutter
68. Verify erosion track formation in target wear pattern
69. Check for arcing events or abnormal discharge behavior
70. Measure target temperature using embedded thermocouples (maintain <150°C)
71. Adjust target cooling water flow rate if needed (typical 2-3 L/min)
72. Verify magnetic field strength and uniformity using Hall probe
73. Optimize O₂/Ar ratio by monitoring optical emission spectroscopy
74. Adjust reactive gas flow to achieve metallic mode sputtering
75. Monitor chamber pressure stability (±0.1 mTorr variation)
76. Record plasma emission lines for indium (410 nm, 451 nm)
77. Check for tin emission lines (284 nm, 317 nm)
78. Verify stoichiometric ITO formation through in-situ monitoring
79. Allow target surface to reach steady-state condition
80. Prepare to open shutter for deposition on substrate

## ITO Film Deposition Process

81. Open target shutter to expose substrate to sputtering flux
82. Monitor deposition rate using quartz crystal microbalance (target: 0.5-2 nm/s)
83. Control substrate rotation for uniform film thickness
84. Maintain rotation speed at 10-20 rpm
85. Monitor real-time film thickness using optical interference
86. Adjust power to maintain constant deposition rate
87. Control oxygen partial pressure to optimize film conductivity
88. Monitor chamber pressure continuously throughout deposition
89. Target film thickness of 150-300 nm for optimal performance
90. Calculate deposition time based on calibrated rate (typically 5-15 minutes)
91. Monitor substrate temperature to prevent overheating (keep <300°C)
92. Observe plasma stability and color during reactive sputtering
93. Check for particle generation or flaking from target
94. Monitor vacuum gauge for pressure fluctuations
95. Record power consumption and voltage-current characteristics
96. Verify gas flow rates remain stable via MFC readout
97. Check cooling water temperature for target and substrate holder
98. Monitor for electrical arcing or discharge instabilities
99. Approach target thickness with real-time monitoring
100. Slow deposition rate in final 10 nm for precise endpoint control

## Post-Deposition Process Control

101. Close target shutter when target thickness is reached
102. Ramp down sputtering power gradually over 30 seconds
103. Maintain argon flow for 2 minutes to cool target
104. Stop reactive oxygen flow
105. Continue substrate heating for post-deposition annealing (optional)
106. Hold at 200-250°C for 30 minutes to improve crystallinity
107. Monitor sheet resistance change during thermal treatment
108. Ramp down substrate temperature at controlled rate (5°C/min)
109. Stop argon flow and close gas valves
110. Backfill chamber with nitrogen to atmospheric pressure
111. Wait for substrate cooling to <50°C before opening chamber
112. Open chamber and carefully remove substrate carrier
113. Transfer coated substrates to laminar flow bench
114. Perform immediate visual inspection for color uniformity
115. Check for typical ITO appearance (slight yellow-green tint)
116. Inspect for delamination, peeling, or poor adhesion
117. Look for pinholes or uncovered areas
118. Identify any contamination particles on surface
119. Measure film thickness at 5 points using spectroscopic ellipsometry
120. Record thickness uniformity (target: ±5% across substrate)

## Electrical Characterization and Testing

121. Prepare samples for four-point probe measurement
122. Clean probe tips with isopropyl alcohol
123. Position substrate on insulating stage
124. Apply four-point probe in square configuration (probe spacing: 1 mm)
125. Measure sheet resistance at center point
126. Record resistance value and calculate using geometric correction factor
127. Measure sheet resistance at 9 points across substrate (3×3 grid)
128. Calculate average sheet resistance (target: 100-300 Ω/sq)
129. Determine uniformity across substrate area (target: <15% variation)
130. Verify sheet resistance meets specification for touch sensor application
131. Measure Hall effect mobility using van der Pauw configuration
132. Determine carrier concentration (typical range: 2-5 × 10²⁰ cm⁻³)
133. Calculate resistivity from sheet resistance and film thickness
134. Verify resistivity in range 2-4 × 10⁻⁴ Ω·cm
135. Test film adhesion using tape test (ASTM D3359 standard)
136. Apply pressure-sensitive tape and remove rapidly at 90° angle
137. Inspect for film removal or delamination (target: 5B rating)
138. Perform stylus scratch test to evaluate adhesion strength
139. Measure critical load for film delamination
140. Document electrical and mechanical test results in quality control log

## Optical Property Characterization

141. Mount ITO-coated glass in spectrophotometer sample holder
142. Calibrate instrument with uncoated reference glass
143. Measure transmittance spectrum from 300 to 2500 nm wavelength
144. Record average visible transmittance (400-700 nm)
145. Verify transmittance >85% in visible range
146. Check for interference fringes indicating uniform thickness
147. Analyze near-infrared transmittance for heat rejection properties
148. Measure reflectance spectrum from both sides
149. Calculate absorptance from transmittance and reflectance data
150. Verify low absorptance in visible range (<5%)
151. Measure haze using integrating sphere (target: <2%)
152. Evaluate color coordinates in CIE L*a*b* color space
153. Verify neutral color appearance (low b* value)
154. Check color uniformity across substrate area
155. Measure refractive index using ellipsometry (typical n ≈ 1.9-2.1)
156. Determine extinction coefficient in visible range
157. Calculate optical band gap from absorption edge (~3.5-4.0 eV)
158. Verify optical properties meet touchscreen display requirements
159. Document spectral data and optical parameters
160. Generate certificate of conformance for optical specifications

## Film Structure and Composition Analysis

161. Prepare cross-section sample for electron microscopy
162. Cleave substrate or use focused ion beam milling
163. Mount sample on SEM stub with conductive tape
164. Coat sample edge with thin carbon layer for conductivity
165. Image film cross-section using scanning electron microscopy
166. Measure film thickness from SEM image (verify ellipsometry measurement)
167. Examine film microstructure and grain morphology
168. Check for columnar grain structure typical of sputtered ITO
169. Verify uniform film thickness and sharp substrate interface
170. Analyze film composition using energy-dispersive X-ray spectroscopy
171. Measure indium, tin, and oxygen elemental ratios
172. Verify In:Sn ratio approximately 9:1 by atomic percent
173. Check oxygen content and stoichiometry
174. Perform X-ray diffraction analysis on film surface
175. Identify crystalline peaks for cubic bixbyite In₂O₃ structure
176. Determine preferred orientation from peak intensities (typically (400) or (222))
177. Calculate crystallite size using Scherrer equation (typical 20-50 nm)
178. Verify polycrystalline structure appropriate for conductive film
179. Perform X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy for surface composition
180. Analyze In 3d, Sn 3d, and O 1s core level spectra

## Photolithography Patterning for Touch Sensors

181. Apply positive photoresist by spin coating at 3000 rpm
182. Soft bake photoresist at 95°C for 90 seconds on hotplate
183. Align photomask with electrode pattern design
184. Expose resist through mask using UV light (365 nm, 150 mJ/cm²)
185. Develop exposed resist in alkaline developer for 60 seconds
186. Rinse with deionized water and dry with nitrogen
187. Inspect pattern under microscope for proper development
188. Hard bake patterned resist at 120°C for 5 minutes
189. Prepare ITO etchant solution (37% HCl + 3% HNO₃ + water, 20:1:100)
190. Immerse substrate in etchant at 40°C for 90-120 seconds
191. Agitate gently to ensure uniform etching
192. Monitor etch progress using sheet resistance probe
193. Remove substrate when ITO is completely etched in exposed areas
194. Rinse thoroughly in deionized water
195. Strip remaining photoresist in acetone and clean substrate for touch sensor assembly

## Quality Assurance and Final Packaging

Quality control verification ensures sheet resistance uniformity within ±10%, optical transmittance exceeding 85% across the visible spectrum, and proper adhesion with no delamination. Final ITO-coated glass panels are packaged in cleanroom environment with protective interleaving sheets, hermetically sealed with desiccant, and shipped to touchscreen assembly facilities. Each batch includes certification documentation of electrical, optical, and mechanical properties for traceability and quality assurance.
